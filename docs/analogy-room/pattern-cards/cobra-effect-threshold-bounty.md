## Core Pattern

In Meridian Prime, the cobra is not always an animal.

The **cobra / rat** is any problem that becomes profitable once an authority starts paying people to prove, kill, clear, report, contain, or route around it.

```text
Authority creates a reward.
People optimize for the reward.
The measured problem multiplies.
The real problem gets worse.
```

Meridian Prime version:

```text
World Works pays for proof, routes, compliance, hazards cleared, anomalies contained, and thresholds found.

Meridian Prime responds by breeding proof, routes, compliance theater, hazard reports, fake containment, and threshold rumors.
```

## Global Cobra Effect: The Threshold Bounty

### Who Pays?

**World Works** pays, directly or indirectly, through:

- contracts
- route approvals
- hazard bonuses
- research grants
- legal protection
- promotion
- access to restricted zones
- clearance upgrades
- equipment
- silence
- debt relief
- survival infrastructure
- official recognition

### What Are the Cobras / Rats?

The global cobras are **threshold-shaped evidence**.

Examples:

- Dry Port clues
- ghost signals
- buried infrastructure
- strange route alignments
- Contact Frame-adjacent readings
- Core-adjacent fragments
- old maps
- anomalous hydrocrystal behavior
- gateway-looking formations
- not-dead-enough signals
- sealed records
- misclassified artifacts

The global rat is **evidence of access**.

### Who Breeds More Cobras?

Everyone trapped in the reward system may learn to produce the metric:

- WWISSaRD researchers who need anomalies to justify budgets
- contractors who need route hazards to justify contracts
- survey teams who learn which readings get attention
- Prime Ops bureaucrats who prefer more reports to less risk
- local informants who sell old rumors as new leads
- black-market brokers who manufacture Dry Port relics
- frightened settlements that exaggerate danger to get support
- ambitious executives who need the next threshold rumor
- desperate NCI crews who need work
- Free Scabs who sell false route intelligence
- Hydropolis factions who leak or distort warnings to preserve leverage

### What Problem Gets Worse?

The world fills with false thresholds.

The real problem was:

```text
How do we safely understand impossible contact?
```

The reward system turns it into:

```text
Who can produce the most useful-looking evidence of contact?
```

Result:

- World Works becomes more reckless.
- Hydropolis becomes more defensive.
- Bought Nation territory becomes more surveilled.
- Traverse crews get sent into stranger routes.
- genuine anomalies are buried under fake ones.
- protected mysteries become corporate targets.
- people become classified as evidence.
- the Dry Port becomes more dangerous as an idea, even before it is proven real.

Core line:

```text
World Works pays for signs of a controllable second threshold.
The planet, the market, and the bureaucracy begin producing signs.
The hunt for the Dry Port breeds more Dry Ports.
```

## Local Cobra Effects

### 1. Prime Ops / Traverse Routes

**Cobras / Rats:** route hazards, false safe paths, ghost signals, emergency reroutes, clearance problems.  
**Who Pays:** Prime Ops / World Works through route-proofing contracts, hazard bonuses, equipment priority, and emergency budgets.  
**Who Breeds More:** contractors, desperate crews, corrupt surveyors, settlement brokers, Free Scab informants.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** Traverse crews, settlements, and anyone depending on route truth.

Pattern line:

```text
Prime Ops pays to prove routes safe.
Crews learn that unsafe routes create work.
The map becomes a job engine instead of a truth engine.
```

Story line:

```text
When route danger becomes billable, the map stops being a guide and becomes livestock.
```

### 2. The Bought Nation

**Cobras / Rats:** permits, debt records, labor contracts, settlement charters, fuel allotments, medical clearances, proof-of-need forms.  
**Who Pays:** World Works, Prime Ops, and administrative systems.  
**Who Breeds More:** administrators, local intermediaries, survival committees, settlement leaders, desperate families, contractors, Reclaimer-aligned activists.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** the Bought Nation, because it must keep proving its wound to receive support.

Pattern line:

```text
World Works pays for documented need.
The paperwork multiplies.
The Bought Nation becomes more real politically, but less free materially.
```

### 3. Hydropolis

**Cobras / Rats:** illegal Frame parts, unlicensed Mirror-Shell repairs, fake visitor relics, breach scares, signal contraband, counterfeit quarantine credentials.  
**Who Pays:** Hydropolis enforcement structures, World Works demand, and black-market buyers.  
**Who Breeds More:** black-market framewrights, World Works proxies, smugglers, fake visitor cults, desperate families, enforcement factions.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** Hydropolis and true Àæonos-origin visitors, because contact becomes contraband.

Pattern line:

```text
Hydropolis rewards detection of illegal contact.
World Works creates demand for illegal contact.
The city breeds a shadow visitation economy.
```

### 4. Dry Port / Claw-Region Mystery

**Cobras / Rats:** Dry Port evidence, old docking scars, impossible signal returns, contact-looking architecture, false maps, dead ports, alignment structures.  
**Who Pays:** World Works and Horus-like power structures.  
**Who Breeds More:** survey teams, map sellers, archive thieves, contractors, old-route families, fake relic dealers, frightened witnesses, executives who need the Dry Port to exist.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** everyone trying to tell real mystery from profitable false positive.

Pattern line:

```text
World Works pays for a second port.
People start finding second ports everywhere.
The real mystery gets buried under profitable false positives.
```

Guardrail:

Do not confirm the Dry Port. Belief drives action. Proof comes later.

### 5. Anticlinal Craton

**Cobras / Rats:** gate trespasses, sacred geology samples, unauthorized maps, fake Craton permissions, tunnel-borer relic claims, Blackstep docking violations, staged border incidents.  
**Who Pays:** World Works, smugglers, collectors, military planners, and access-hungry researchers.  
**Who Breeds More:** smugglers, fake guides, corporate scouts, sample thieves, border provocateurs, contractors who profit from Craton instability.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** Craton authorities, because sovereign refusal is converted into a problem to be solved.

Pattern line:

```text
World Works pays for Craton access intelligence.
Trespass becomes profitable.
The Craton closes harder, and outsiders call that closure proof of danger.
```

### 6. Eschar Slough / Free Scabs

**Cobras / Rats:** raids, stolen cargo, false distress calls, pirate sightings, Scab route rumors, black-market medicine flows, fake Scab attacks, real Scab attacks provoked by policy.  
**Who Pays:** World Works, Prime Ops, insurers, contractors, and sometimes settlements.  
**Who Breeds More:** corrupt security contractors, desperate Free Scabs, smugglers, convoy bosses, fake victims, militarized route offices.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** settlements, convoys, and the Free Scabs themselves, because the Scab problem becomes an economy.

Pattern line:

```text
Authorities pay to suppress the Free Scabs.
Suppression becomes profitable.
The Free Scabs become more necessary to the economy built to fight them.
```

### 7. Ledger Falls

**Cobras / Rats:** illegal timber, fake conservation claims, staged sabotage, permit violations, mislabeled timber, moved Green Line markers, emergency logging.  
**Who Pays:** World Works, logging interests, conservation offices, Reclaimers, survival markets.  
**Who Breeds More:** logging bosses, permit clerks, desperate workers, activists, black-market haulers, contractors, administrators.  
**Problem Gets Worse For:** Ledger Falls, because the forest becomes more valuable as conflict than as forest.

Pattern line:

```text
Institutions reward cut timber, protected timber, and contested timber.
People learn to manufacture timber conflict.
Ledger Falls becomes a sawmill for grievances.
```

**What Are the Rats:** route hazards, false safe paths, ghost signals, emergency reroutes, clearance problems.  
**Who Breeds Them:** contractors, desperate crews, corrupt surveyors, settlement brokers, Free Scab informants.  
**What Gets Worse:** travel truth.

Core line:

```text
When route danger becomes billable, the map stops being a guide and becomes livestock.
```

## Best Story Engine: The Cobra Ledger

A World Works office quietly creates an incentive program:

```text
Report any contact-adjacent route anomaly.
Verified reports receive hazard bonuses, equipment priority, and contract renewal preference.
```

At first, it works.

Then the reports multiply.

Some are real.  
Some are misread.  
Some are planted.  
Some are old.  
Some are bait.  
Some are people trying to survive.

Soon World Works has exactly what it wanted:

```text
Too many cobras to ignore.
```

And exactly what it should fear:

```text
Too many cobras to know which one is real.
```

Final pattern line:

```text
Bad incentives teach the world what shape of lie power wants to hear.
```

## Canon Use

This pattern can support Meridian Prime by making institutional pressure more coherent:

- World Works does not need to be cartoonishly evil.
- Prime Ops does not need to be incompetent.
- Contractors do not need to be universally corrupt.
- Settlements do not need to be passive victims.
- Hydropolis does not need to be purely virtuous.
- Free Scabs do not need to be simple villains.

The reward system creates the behavior.

## Risks / What Not To Copy

- Do not make the Cobra Effect explain every problem.
- Do not turn World Works into a mustache-twirling villain.
- Do not make every local actor a fraud.
- Do not confirm the Dry Port.
- Do not solve the Core or gateway mechanics.
- Do not reduce the Bought Nation, Free Scabs, Hydropolis, or Craton to allegory.
- Do not make incentives stronger than ecology, history, grief, faith, or survival.

## Final Principle

```text
Meridian Prime's Cobra Effect is not only bad incentives creating bad outcomes.

It is bad incentives teaching the world what shape of lie power wants to hear.
```
