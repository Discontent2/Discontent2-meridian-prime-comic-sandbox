## Simple Definition

The **Cobra Effect** is a perverse incentive pattern.

It happens when a reward is designed to solve a problem, but the reward accidentally encourages people to produce more of the problem.

Simple pattern:

```text
A powerful group pays people to reduce a problem.
People learn they can profit by producing more of the problem.
The measured problem grows.
The real problem gets worse.
```

## Famous Cobra Story

The common story says colonial authorities in India wanted fewer cobras, so they paid a bounty for dead cobras.

People allegedly began breeding cobras for profit.

When the bounty ended, breeders supposedly released the extra cobras.

Result:

```text
The cobra bounty may have created more cobras.
```

Important caution:

The famous cobra story is often repeated, but it is difficult to verify as documented history. Treat it as a useful teaching story, not a proven archival fact.

## Better-Documented Rat-Tail Pattern

A stronger real-world counterpart is the rat-tail bounty pattern associated with colonial Hanoi.

Authorities wanted fewer rats, so they paid for rat tails.

People could collect tails without killing all the rats. Some rats were allegedly released after their tails were cut so they could keep breeding.

Result:

```text
The reward targeted tails, not rat elimination.
The metric improved while the problem continued.
```

## Simplified Counterpart Table

| Real-World Version | Problem Authority Wanted Solved | Reward Offered | What People Optimized For | What Got Worse |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cobra bounty story | Too many cobras | Dead cobras | Producing cobras for bounty | Cobra population may increase |
| Rat-tail bounty pattern | Too many rats | Rat tails | Collecting tails, not eliminating rats | Rat breeding / rat problem continues |
| General policy version | Social or environmental problem | Payment for simple metric | Metric production | Underlying problem worsens |

## Clean Pattern Language

Use this version for analogy work:

```text
The reward was attached to the visible proof of the problem, not the disappearance of the problem.
```

Or:

```text
The system paid for evidence, so people manufactured evidence.
```

## Meridian Prime Translation

In Meridian Prime, the cobras and rats are usually not animals.

They are:

- route hazards
- anomaly reports
- Dry Port clues
- fake relics
- false distress calls
- illegal contact evidence
- proof-of-need documents
- sacred threshold trespasses
- black-market scarcity signals
- suspicious survey readings

Meridian Prime translation:

```text
World Works pays for evidence of useful danger.
People learn to produce useful danger.
The difference between danger and evidence collapses.
```

## What To Preserve In Fiction

The useful shape is not merely fraud.

The useful shape is:

```text
A survival system teaches people which lie keeps them alive.
```

This matters because Meridian Prime should avoid making every actor simply corrupt.

Some people manufacture evidence because they are greedy.  
Some do it because their settlement needs fuel.  
Some do it because the only way to get medical support is to prove crisis.  
Some do it because the official map already lied first.  
Some do it because World Works only listens to certain shapes of danger.

## Local Meridian Prime Counterparts

### World Works / Dry Port

Real-world counterpart:

```text
Pay for dead cobras.
People produce cobras.
```

Meridian Prime counterpart:

```text
Pay for contact-port evidence.
People produce contact-port evidence.
```

Problem worsens:

The real Dry Port mystery gets buried under profitable false positives.

### Prime Ops / Traverse Routes

Real-world counterpart:

```text
Pay for rat tails.
People optimize for tails, not fewer rats.
```

Meridian Prime counterpart:

```text
Pay for hazard reports.
People optimize for reportable hazards, not safer routes.
```

Problem worsens:

The map stops being truth and becomes contract livestock.

### Bought Nation

Real-world counterpart:

```text
Reward visible need.
People must remain visibly needy to receive support.
```

Meridian Prime counterpart:

```text
Reward documented dependency.
The paperwork multiplies.
```

Problem worsens:

The Bought Nation becomes more legible to the system but less materially free.

### Hydropolis

Real-world counterpart:

```text
Ban or reward seizure of contraband.
Contraband value rises.
```

Meridian Prime counterpart:

```text
Police illegal contact.
World Works creates demand for illegal contact.
```

Problem worsens:

A shadow visitation economy grows.

### Free Scabs

Real-world counterpart:

```text
Pay to suppress an outlaw problem.
Suppression becomes an industry.
```

Meridian Prime counterpart:

```text
Pay for anti-Scab security.
Everyone with a security budget needs Scabs to remain dangerous.
```

Problem worsens:

The Free Scab problem becomes economically necessary to the people paid to fight it.

### Ledger Falls

Real-world counterpart:

```text
Reward enforcement metrics.
People manufacture violations, reports, or compliance theater.
```

Meridian Prime counterpart:

```text
Reward cut timber, protected timber, and contested timber.
People manufacture timber conflict.
```

Problem worsens:

The forest becomes more valuable as grievance than as forest.

## Design Lessons For Meridian Prime

1. Do not make the incentive failure purely comic.
2. Do not make everyone a scammer.
3. Let survival pressure produce morally tangled behavior.
4. Let authorities create the metric, then lose control of it.
5. Let local actors exploit the metric for different reasons.
6. Let the real problem become harder to see because the reports multiply.
7. Keep protected mysteries protected.

## Best Meridian Prime Summary

```text
Bad incentives teach the world what shape of lie power wants to hear.
```
