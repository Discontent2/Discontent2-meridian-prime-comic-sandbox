## Core Finding

Track 2 should not reuse Track 1's descent-based grime structure.

Track 1 identity:

```text
Track 1 is dirty descent.
```

Track 2 identity:

```text
Track 2 is muted command violence.
```

This split prevents `Riot Permit Office` from sounding like `Condemned Block Intro` with new stage labels.

---

## What Fixed Track 2

The successful direction used:

```text
muted square-wave bass strikes
lower FM saw-pulse lead
staccato command-riff bursts
hard stop-start thrash phrasing
short hostile lead answers
low machine pressure
tribunal-engine mood
experimental tracker combat BGM
```

This shifted Track 2 away from downward bass gravity and into stop-start assault.

---

## Lower Lead Rule

For Track 2, use:

```text
lower FM saw-pulse lead
reverb-fogged lower FM lead
corroded and narrow lead
short hostile lead answers
```

Avoid:

```text
upper lead
bright lead
shriek lead
scream lead
cute lead
```

Lower lead language helped remove cute/novelty behavior and kept the sound dirty, narrow, and combative.

---

## Bass Rule

For Track 2, use:

```text
muted square-wave bass strikes
square bass strikes
short square bass strikes
bass strikes lock to downbeats
bass is the main impact
```

Avoid:

```text
bass descent as the main hook
chromatic walkdown language
wobble bass
filter movement
sub drop
```

Track 2 should hit in clipped strikes, not crawl downward.

---

## Rhythm Rule

Track 2 is built from command bursts and threat space.

Use:

```text
staccato command riff
short rests between riff hits
hard stop-start phrasing
dense downbeat hits
riff cuts into shorter bursts
```

This creates a different combat grammar from Track 1.
