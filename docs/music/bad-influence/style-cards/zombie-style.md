# Zombie Style

**Status:** Sandbox Music Style Card / Non-Canon / Requires Review Before Promotion  
**Album Context:** `Time Bastard - Live from the Low Road Road House`  
**Room:** Meridian Prime Bad Influence Music Room  
**Saved:** 2026-06-25

## Core Identity

Zombie Style is a raw, ugly, stop-start Low Road performance style for bad-influence tracks. It is designed for songs that feel like a live roadhouse crowd, a broken machine, and a monster groove all arguing under flickering neon.

The style should feel:

- raw rather than polished
- aggressive but rhythmically catchy
- industrial and roadhouse-dirty
- built around dead-silent pauses
- driven by heavy low-tuned guitar movement
- vocally unstable, with deliberate high-to-low cracks
- suitable for album tracks, encore closers, pit chants, wrong-door anthems, and cursed live-performance cues

This style must not name or imitate real bands or artists in output. Any outside references should be translated into traits only.


## Guitar Language

Use this wording when a track needs the signature guitar move:

```text
heavily detuned power chord tuned down to C#, then a low drop-tuned guitar slide smoothly screams up the neck to the 12th, 14th, and 17th frets on the high strings. To drop back down, the slide drags all the way back down the fretboard into heavy palm-muted low chugs.
```

Musical traits:

- C# detuned power chords
- low drop-tuned guitar slide
- dissonant pitch slides
- chromatic octave riffs
- ugly upward scream into high strings
- violent return to palm-muted low chugs
- stop-start riffing with silence used as a weapon

## Vocal Language

Use this wording when a track needs the signature vocal move:

```text
frequently transitions from high notes to low notes by intentionally triggering a vocal break or vocal flip. The singer belts an aggressive high-pitched phrase using heavy nasal compression, then suddenly lets the voice crack and collapse into a low gravelly chest register. The break is emphasized rather than hidden, creating an unhinged unstable sound.
```

Useful prompt phrases:

- extreme guttural vocals
- deep death growl
- high nasal-compressed bark
- sudden vocal crack into gravelly chest voice
- high-to-low cadence shift at bar end
- hip-hop rhythmic bark-and-drop structure
- no clean vocal

## Lyric Formatting Rules

Zombie Style lyrics should be Suno-friendly and built around hard stops.

Preferred format:

```text
WORD.
[Stop]

NEXT.
[Stop]
```

Use:

- all-caps keywords
- periods after punch words
- `[Stop]` on its own line
- short phrases instead of long melodic sentences
- section labels such as `[Intro]`, `[Verse]`, `[Pre-Chorus]`, `[Chorus]`, `[Slide Break]`, `[Breakdown]`, `[Outro]`
- performance directions in parentheses only when they help Suno

Avoid:

- long lyrical paragraphs
- smooth sing-song phrasing
- overly poetic lines that soften the impact
- country, acoustic twang, polished modern metal, clean vocal prompts
