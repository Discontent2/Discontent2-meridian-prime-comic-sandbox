## Core Identity

Pink Style is Mergenta’s haunting 80s horror hypnagogic-pop lane. It combines the surreal cassette-cartoon DNA of earlier Mergenta tracks with a darker, lower, creepier VHS-horror mood. It should feel like a bedroom-pop nightmare leaking out of a thrift-store television after midnight.

The style should be:

- haunting rather than frantic
- lower and creepier vocally than the earlier toy-punk Mergenta sound
- warm, murky, and lo-fi
- nostalgic but wrong
- suburban-horror flavored
- built around VHS hiss, fluorescent buzz, cheap drum machines, analog synth bass, and ghost harmonies
- just playful enough to feel uncanny

Pink Style should not become modern metal, country, polished pop, or clean glossy synthwave.

## Band Identity: Mergenta

Mergenta is a fictional project identity for hypnagogic horror-pop, toy-synth weirdness, and warped cassette-bedroom songs.

In Pink Style, Mergenta sounds less hyperactive and more haunted:

## Vocal Language

### Lower Creepy Lo-Fi Vocal

The lead voice should sit lower than the earlier Mergenta toy-punk sound.

Prompt phrases:

```text
lower creepy lo-fi cassette vocal, smoky low new wave croon, intimate murky vocal, slightly detached haunted delivery, soft but unsettling vocal presence
```

Effect:

- creepy without being aggressive
- tired but elegant
- bedroom-recorded
- half-awake and half-possessed

### Nasal Mid-Range Creep

Use a smaller, creepier version of the earlier cartoon whine.

Prompt phrases:

```text
slightly nasal mid-range creep, restrained nasal whine, eerie upward vocal creep, haunted throat-and-nasal placement
```

Effect:

- uncanny but not obnoxious
- rises into tension without becoming loud comedy
- gives the chorus a ghostly lift

### Layered Ghost Harmonies

The backing vocals should feel buried, pitch-shifted, and spectral.

Prompt phrases:

```text
layered pitch-shifted ghost harmonies, low and high vocal doubles buried in the mix, broken toy intercom backing voices, ghostly echo, subtle slapback delay
```

Effect:

- nostalgic
- haunted
- like an old broadcast bleeding through the walls

## Instrument Language

### VHS Horror Pop Foundation

Core sound:

```text
VHS tape hiss, fluorescent light buzz, ominous analog synth bass, pulsing minor-key synth arpeggios, cheap drum machine, dark bedroom-pop production
```

Effect:

- 80s horror mood without glossy synthwave polish
- quiet suburban dread
- warm, murky, and handmade

### Detuned Toy Melody

Use toy keys sparingly and creepily.

Prompt phrases:

```text
detuned toy keyboard melody, tiny haunted toy synth, warped nursery-like motif, cheap toy-keyboard hook
```

Effect:

- childlike but not cute
- small and cold
- a hook that feels found in a drawer

### Warbly Guitar Texture

Guitar should be atmospheric, not heavy.

Prompt phrases:

```text
warbly chorus-drenched guitar licks, out-of-tune guitar shimmer, thrift-store psych-rock texture, soft eerie guitar hooks
```

Effect:

- watery
- retro
- ghost-pop and thrift-store psych flavored

### Eerie TV-Theme Interludes

Brief poppy interruptions can happen, but they should feel wrong.

Prompt phrases:

```text
sudden eerie TV-theme interlude, warped laugh-track moment, dream-broadcast transition, broken television bumper
```

Effect:

- nostalgic interruption
- uncanny reset
- makes the song feel like a haunted program

## Lyric Formatting Rules

Pink Style lyrics should use full lines, not every-word stop commands.

Preferred structure:

```text
[Title]
TITLE HERE

[Intro: VHS Hiss]
A short eerie setup.
A small repeated phrase or toy-keyboard hook.

[Verse 1: Low Creepy Croon]
Full lyric lines with domestic or suburban dread.

[Pre-Chorus: Nasal Mid-Range Creep]
The voice rises slightly and gets stranger.

[Chorus: Low Croon + Ghost Harmonies]
A simple haunted hook.

[Interlude: Broken Toy Intercom]
Short uncanny announcement.

[Bridge: Drunken Cassette Croon]
The weirdest domestic image goes here.

[Final Chorus: Full VHS Horror Pop]
Return bigger but still murky.

[Outro: Low Croon + Tape Warble]
Repeat the hook until it feels cursed.
```

Use:

- mirrors
- lipstick
- bathroom light
- fluorescent buzz
- television snow
- medicine drawers
- empty kitchens
- suburban rooms
- old phones
- warped intercom lines
- little repeated hooks
- ghost harmonies

Avoid:

- extreme guttural vocals
- every-word `[Stop]` formatting
- modern metal
- country/twang prompts
- glossy pop or clean synthwave
- too much slapstick cartoon energy
