## Core Identity

Growlers Style is a surf-goth garage-rock palette for The Snifters. It is built around smoky slacker vocals, haunted beach-guitar tones, hypnotic bass pulse, and raw vintage looseness. It should feel like a half-lit roadhouse set drifting into a dead seaside town, with the singer sounding lazy, haunted, and slightly drunk on bad directions.

This style is not Zombie Style. It should be less metallic, less guttural, and more humanly loose. The danger comes from atmosphere, vocal sag, ghostly hooks, and abrupt structural dropouts rather than brute force.

## Vocal Language

### 1. The Slacker Croon

During verses, use a relaxed low chest register that sounds purposefully lazy.

Prompt phrases:

```text
smoky slacker croon, relaxed low chest register, airy lazy vocal delivery, loose rumble at line endings, unbothered detached garage-rock vocal
```

Effect:

- smoky
- detached
- slightly lazy
- sentence endings drag downward
- reminiscent of old garage rock without naming any real artist

### 2. The Nasal Whine / Vocal Creep

For pre-chorus and chorus transitions, the voice creeps upward through nasal/throat placement instead of cleanly leaping.

Prompt phrases:

```text
nasal psych-rock whine, elongated vocal creep into higher notes, reedy throat-and-nasal placement, unstable beach-goth drawl
```

Effect:

- haunted
- unstable
- beach-goth
- sharper than the verse voice
- the pitch climbs like it has wet shoes

### 3. The Lonesome Moan Pitch Drop

At chorus climaxes, use a big downward vocal glissando.

Prompt phrases:

```text
exaggerated lonesome moan pitch drop, downward vocal glissando, pitch sags back into low chest register, exhausted bluesy vocal collapse
```

Effect:

- mournful
- humanly flawed
- physically tired
- the chorus collapses back down instead of resolving brightly

### 4. Drunken Cadence

During bridges, the singer drags behind the beat and trails off syllables.

Prompt phrases:

```text
drunken bridge cadence, vocals slightly behind the beat, trailing syllable endings, raw human timing, loose swagger delivery
```

Effect:

- stumbling but musical
- warm and vintage
- organic high-to-low transitions
- no sharp studio polish

## Instrument Language

### Surf-Goth Rhythm

Core groove:

```text
dark hypnotic surf rhythm, steady eighth-note pulsing bassline, crisp snare, tight hi-hats, garage-rock march
```

Effect:

- steady anchor under loose vocals
- mid-tempo drive
- hypnotic and dark

### Drowny Reverb Lead Guitar

Lead guitar:

```text
drowny spring-reverb lead guitar, slight tremolo guitar, watery haunted high notes, distant vintage beach-guitar tone
```

Effect:

- spooky
- psychedelic
- distant and wet
- melodic without sounding polished

### Gritty Lo-Fi Rhythm Guitar

Rhythm guitar:

```text
gritty lo-fi rhythm guitar chords, light overdrive, minor-key surf-noir chord progressions, warm dirty lower-mid texture
```

Effect:

- vintage dirt
- organic shifts
- no modern metal distortion

### Abrupt Stop-and-Start Breaks

Breaks:

```text
abrupt stop-and-start dynamics, instruments suddenly drop out, stripped-back beat, isolated guitar riff, stumbling swagger transitions
```

Effect:

- uneven and haunted
- complements zombie-shuffle lyrical energy
- silence becomes the hook without turning into metal hard-stop style

## Lyric Formatting Rules

Growlers Style lyrics should be more lyrical than Zombie Style. Use full lines, not every-word stop commands.

Preferred format:

```text
[Verse 1: Slacker Croon]
I took the empty routes again
Dragging my shoes through the radio sand

[Pre-Chorus: Nasal Vocal Creep]
And it creeps up slow
Through the back of my nose

[Chorus]
Empty routes
Empty routes
Hear my lonesome moans

*(Vocal slides downward on "moans")*
```

Use:

- full lyric lines
- section labels that indicate vocal delivery
- occasional `[Stop]` for dramatic silence
- performance notes for vocal slides and stripped-down sections
- surf, road, motel, static, moon, map, empty route, signpost, tide, and neon imagery

Avoid:

- every-word `[Stop]` formatting
- extreme guttural vocals
- modern metal language
- polished pop vocals
- country/twang prompts
- overexplaining lore
