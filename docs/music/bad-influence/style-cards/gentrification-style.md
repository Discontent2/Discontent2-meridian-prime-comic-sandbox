## Core Identity

Gentrification is the underproduced Beach Goth creature-track band for Meridian Prime.

The sound should feel like:

- a homemade CD-R from a foggy beach-town garage
- a water-damaged cassette found under a motel sink
- a haunted backyard party after everyone good has gone home
- sun-bleached melodies with ugly roadside jokes underneath
- trash-can melancholy, weird creatures, bad signs, false refuges, and hungover menace

Gentrification should not become polished modern rock, metalcore, country, glossy synthwave, or clean pop. It should stay loose, murky, funny, sad, and a little wrong in the teeth.

## Working Format Lock

`Tin Grin` established the preferred working format for Gentrification creature tracks.

Use:

```text
short lines
ugly-funny images
direct slang hook
simple repeated chorus
underproduced cassette Beach Goth
haunted beach / spooky carnival atmosphere
roadside trash humor
sadness hidden under a danceable stumble
no lyric notes about instruments or vocal style
no real band names
no citations inside lyrics
```

Avoid:

```text
long lore exposition
section directions inside lyrics unless needed
lyrics that describe the production
lyrics that over-explain the creature
polished wording
large paragraphs
```

## Lyric Language

Good Gentrification creature lyrics should use:

- creature slang as chorus fuel
- grimy everyday details
- cheap motels, bins, blacktop, runoff, broken signs, bad lights
- humor that smells like wet cardboard
- one or two vivid names repeated hard
- direct lines that Suno can chant

Useful examples:

```text
Tin grin
By the bins again
Little skull-face
Counting sins again
```

```text
Wreck Rex
On the canyon wall
Looks like junk
Till the junk starts to crawl
```

```text
Cloud spine
On the black wet stone
Cloud spine
Don’t cross alone
```
