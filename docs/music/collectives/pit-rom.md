## Core Purpose

`Pit ROM` is the actual 16-bit BGM artist for `Red Warrant` and related Devilment Studio cartridge-action projects.

The project goal is practical: generate killer 16-bit video game BGM first. Game lore and band lore exist to sharpen track prompts.

## Role

`Pit ROM` translates heavy-band aggression into playable background music.

They are not simply a chiptune metal act. They are a game-score machine that understands rooms, enemies, stage pacing, boss pressure, and feedback safety.

## Game-Audio Rules

Pit ROM tracks should be aggressive but playable.

Core rule:

```text
If it sounds like gameplay information, keep it out of the BGM.
If it sounds like violence in the room, it can stay.
```

Avoid in BGM prompts:

```text
UI chirps
menu beeps
coin sounds
power-up sounds
item sounds
warning tones
alarms
sirens
lasers
realistic gunshots
impact sound effects
voice samples
spoken words
vocals
choir
realistic drums
modern hi-fi polish
```

## Relationship to Writ Hammer

`Writ Hammer` supplies the full-band source mythology.

`Pit ROM` supplies the playable cartridge translation.

Pipeline:

```text
Writ Hammer writes the riot.
Pit ROM loads it into the cartridge.
Devilment Studio turns it into stages.
```

Translation examples:

```text
hardcore shout hook -> FM lead slogan motif
thrash riff -> square-wave combat bass
pit breakdown -> enemy-wave section
sub-bass wobble -> low controlled chip pressure
political lyric -> stage title / boss concept
```
