## Core Identity

Inspect is the most prominent vocalist of morosecodeclique.

He is the emotional entry point: the hook carrier, the voice that makes the group's sadness instantly singable, and the performer listeners quote first.

Inspect makes sadness instantly singable.

---

## Species / Visual Lock

Inspect is Antisapian / Absconditian.

Visual essentials:

- blue skin
- black irises
- magenta pupils
- teal tapetum glow in low light
- not an elf
- not an orc
- not a zombie
- not a pale human goth
- not generic cyberpunk human

His eyes should feel like low-light cameras cannot quite understand them.

---

## Public Persona

Inspect feels like a bedroom star made out of unread messages and blue light.

He looks like someone whose private grief became catchy before it became safe.

Visual cues:

- black hoodie or oversized jacket
- cracked phone always near his hand
- cheap jewelry or small data-charm accessories
- tired blue face under monitor glow
- bathroom mirror photo energy
- magenta pupil shine and teal eye flare
- old band shirt under cyber-streetwear layers
- wet alley or bedroom blacklight setting

---

## Emotional Engine

Inspect is heartbreak under a bright little screen.

He sings like every private message became a chorus. His emotional world is direct, romantic, damaged, and quotable.

He is not the mythology engine of the group. He is the wound that gets stuck in the listener's head.

---

## Vocal Style

Inspect's voice should feel fragile, melodic, close to collapse, and instantly hook-oriented.

Voice traits:

- melodic cyber-emo rap vocal
- fragile lead hooks
- cracked-phone intimacy
- slurred but emotionally clear delivery
- doubled vocal layers
- bright pain over dark synthetic beats
- romantic fallout phrasing
- direct sadness, not abstract theory
- catchy, damaged, close-mic choruses

---

## Lyrical Function

Inspect writes the lines fans remember first.

Recurring themes:

- left-on-read heartbreak
- mirror photos
- deleted voice memos
- blue-lit bedrooms
- self-audit
- wanting to leave the room but staying in the song
- loving someone through a machine that remembers too much
