## Core Identity

Shame is the human anchor of Dead Mile Cipher.

He is not the flashiest member and does not rap like a technical exhibitionist. He sounds like a person trying to tell the truth before the room changes shape.

His job in the collective is to translate Meridian Prime horror into ordinary pain: rent, work, guilt, dead friends, motels, bad coffee, busted teeth, unpaid bills, cheap rooms, and the shame that sticks under the nails.

He is called Shame, but he may be the most morally intact member of the group.

---

## Public Persona

Exhausted working-class poet. Plain hoodie, old work boots, thrift-store notebook, motel-key necklace, tired eyes, weathered hands.

He feels less like a performer and more like someone in a diner booth at 2:17 a.m., talking through coffee grit and unpaid grief.

---

## Private Wound

Shame survived something others did not.

He suspects survival was not noble. It was procedural. Someone signed. Someone ran. Someone stayed quiet. Someone got processed out of the story.

He is not proud of living, but he has made testimony out of it.

---

## Voice Lock

Shame's voice is raspy, rhythmic, and deeply expressive.

It carries a distinct warm Midwestern human quality that prioritizes emotional clarity over technical vocal perfection. He should sound lived-in, conversational, wounded, direct, and trustworthy.

### Core Vocal Attributes

**Gravelly Texture**  
His voice has a natural smoky grit. The texture gives his delivery a weathered everyman quality. It should suggest sleep debt, old walls, winter air, bad coffee, and years of saying "I'm fine" incorrectly.

**Conversational Cadence**  
He blurs the line between rapping and spoken word. He leans on natural speech inflections, pauses, sighs, muttered asides, and intimate timing. He should often sound like he is telling the listener something he did not plan to admit.

**Elastic Dynamics**  
He can drop into a soft muffled near-whisper for shame, memory, apology, or dread, then snap into a full-throated yell when anger, passion, or desperation breaks through. The yell should feel earned, not hyped.

## Crew Relationships

### Shame and Blue Tapetum

He trusts her precision but envies her ability to disappear. She trusts his pain but not his openness.

### Shame and Burn Notice

He distrusts Burn Notice because Burn speaks fluent systems-language. Burn's documents are useful, but Shame hears the old machine in every sentence.

### Shame and Discontent One

He depends on Discontent One to make sense of the route, but sometimes hates how beautiful Discontent makes suffering sound. Discontent One sees Shame as the crew's emotional proof-text.
