## Group Evolution

Dead Mile Cypher did not begin as a finished four-member collective. It mutated through stages.

### S.A.D.

**Lineup:** Shame and Discontent One  
**Full phrase:** Shame and Discontent

S.A.D. was the earliest collaboration between Shame and Discontent One. It should feel like a cassette-only project built from grief, field recordings, motel-room notebooks, route hum, bad rooms, and survival notes.

S.A.D. made grief tapes.

### Down Pour

**Lineup:** Shame, Discontent One, Burn Notice

Down Pour began when Shame and Discontent One met Burn Notice. Burn added live beat impact, corporate-language satire, hard rhythmic pressure, and the sense that the music was becoming evidence.

Down Pour made evidence sound heavy.

### Dead Mile Cypher

**Lineup:** Shame, Discontent One, Burn Notice, Blue Tapetum

Dead Mile Cypher began when the group met Blue Tapetum. Blue completed the group by adding lead turntablism, philosophical rapid-fire lyricism, perception critique, and turntable response as a core musical voice.

Dead Mile Cypher made the evidence contagious.

### Arrestthemall Records

After becoming Dead Mile Cypher, the group founded its own label: `Arrestthemall Records`.

Arrestthemall Records should feel like a DIY label, a graffiti tag, a threat, a joke, and a mission statement. It is not about claiming individual innocence. It is about naming the whole guilty architecture.

Possible label thesis:

```text
If the whole machine is guilty, arrest the architecture.
```

Arrestthemall Records made it impossible to deny who heard it.

## Venetian Mask / Urban Clothes Visual Identity

Each member wears a different Venetian mask and urban clothing style during performances.

The masks should not look like clean ballroom cosplay. They should feel stolen, painted, tagged, cracked, taped, altered, and dragged through graffiti tunnels, pirate-radio rooms, and bad venues.

The masks function as disguise, argument, and visual thesis: identity, surveillance, personhood, role, performance, and being misread.

### Shame Mask Direction

Cracked white or off-white mask, work-stained, maybe a half-mask. Handwritten tally marks, rent numbers, motel-room marker, or small confession fragments. Warmest and least theatrical.

### Discontent One Mask Direction

Saurian-adapted Venetian mask, not human-shaped. It must fit fully saurian facial anatomy. Asymmetrical, route-map fragments, pressure marks, signal scratches, field-tape symbols, and Aeonolacertian mark logic.

### Burn Notice Mask Direction

Glossy corporate-white or black mask with barcode stickers, fake safety seals, redaction blocks, claim-language fragments, stamped warnings, and scuffed gold trim.

### Blue Tapetum Mask Direction

Dark blue-black mask with magenta and teal accents, reflective eye cuts, lens motifs, needle-thin scratch marks, mirror-shard suggestions, and low-light / UV-readable symbols.

## Tagger Backgrounds

Each member comes from a tagger background and has a distinct tagging style that mirrors their music.

### Shame Tagging Style

Blunt, human, handwritten tags. Big sad block letters, motel marker, bathroom-stall truth, simple phrases that hurt. Shame's tags read like confessions.

### Discontent One Tagging Style

Route glyphs, saurian tool-mark patterns, impossible map lines, pressure symbols, layered field-note diagrams. This should not read as human graffiti. It should feel Aeonolacertian and saurian-first.

### Burn Notice Tagging Style

Stencil work, fake notices, redacted posters, invoice-style paste-ups, counterfeit public warnings, altered safety signage, and official-looking satire. Burn tags like a forged municipal department.

### Blue Tapetum Tagging Style

Razor-clean micro-tags, mirror marks, surveillance blind-spot symbols, scratch-like lines, UV / low-light paint, coded turntable spirals, and marks that only fully appear under certain light.
