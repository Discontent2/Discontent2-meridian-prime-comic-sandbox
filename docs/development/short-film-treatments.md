# Meridian Prime Short-Film Treatments

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Format Prototype / Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon short-film development.

## Short-Film Strategy

A Meridian Prime short should sell the world in one pressure event.

Best length:

- 8 to 15 minutes for proof-of-concept
- 20 to 30 minutes for festival / prestige short

Best structure:

1. Start with work.
2. Let wrongness enter through procedure.
3. Force one irreversible field choice.
4. End with residue.

---

## Short Film 1: Circle Camp

**Runtime:** 18 to 25 minutes  
**Tone:** survival thriller  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

During a crystal storm, a Traverse crew enters Circle Camp outside The Claw and discovers that the fuel cache they need to survive has been logged full but is functionally unusable.

### Characters

- **Mara Venn:** Traverse Lead, calm until arithmetic breaks.
- **Joss Calder:** Dieselwright mechanic, believes machines lie less than forms.
- **Tavi Rook:** logistics specialist, youngest person willing to say the plan fails.
- **Orra Senn:** Mountaineer, old enough to hate storms personally.

### Beat Treatment

1. Dawn departure rhythm: cold steel, engine checks, crew humor.
2. Storm warning: nacreous dark clouds on the horizon.
3. The Claw is closed by rule. No argument.
4. Circle Camp begins: convoy carves slow loops outside the crevasse field.
5. First refuel reveals cache inconsistency.
6. Tavi recalculates survival time and finds a missing day.
7. Prime Ops says the cache was serviced.
8. Joss physically checks the fuel and finds contamination / unusable supply.
9. Crew debates risk: keep circling, abandon cargo, attempt dangerous transfer.
10. A tractor stalls in the night circle.
11. Orra calls the decision: survival over mission.
12. They abandon nonessential cargo and cannibalize heat from one Mod.
13. Morning dig-out.
14. They leave a marker warning the next crew.
15. Final image: Prime Ops report labels it minor fuel irregularity. The warning marker remains.

### Production Value

Can be made with limited cast, vehicle interiors, sound design, storm exterior plates, and one strong wide shot of circling tracks.

## Short Film 2: Flags Missing

**Runtime:** 12 to 18 minutes  
**Tone:** procedural suspense  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

A Mountaineer halts a Traverse on The Path after noticing that black flags are missing from the next marker interval, despite Prime Ops confirming the route as safe.

### Characters

- **Sela Marr:** Mountaineer, experienced, unpopular with schedule people.
- **Dane Kol:** Lead, under pressure to keep moving.
- **Pip Arren:** flagman of the day, green but observant.
- **Iven Lusk:** comms operator trying to keep Prime Ops calm.

### Beat Treatment

1. Entering crevasse field: flags on the proper side.
2. Pip notices an interval that feels empty.
3. Sela calls: stop ops, stop ops, stop ops.
4. Lead demands confirmation.
5. Prime Ops says no flag outage is recorded.
6. Sela sends Pip by Track Bike only as far as safe procedure allows.
7. GPR gives a partial contradiction.
8. A red rotating marker blinks where none should be visible.
9. Dane must choose between the official path and Sela's judgment.
10. They inch backward and discover a false flag trail starting beyond the missing interval.
11. The crew survives by not trusting the clean answer.
12. Closing shot: Prime Ops labels the incident Special Flagging Operations.

### Production Value

Strong sound design, cab tension, exterior flags in whiteout, and a terrifying lack of visible monster.

## Short Film 3: Winter-Over Prints More

**Runtime:** 10 to 15 minutes  
**Tone:** dark workplace comedy with danger  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

A Prime Ops supervisor orders all unofficial Winter-Over stickers removed from equipment, accidentally erasing the only warning on a misnumbered Tool.

### Characters

- **Nix Fallow:** Winter-Over maintenance worker, sticker artist, tired philosopher of bad equipment.
- **Cal Breen:** Prime Ops supervisor, clean-wall absolutist.
- **Moss Iver:** mechanic, knows every Tool by scar.
- **Luna Pell:** bar worker who prints better warning labels than Prime Ops.

### Beat Treatment

1. Montage of stickers across Prime Ops.
2. Cal orders removal before Mainbody inspection.
3. Nix protests that some stickers are warnings.
4. Cal removes one from a Tool labeled with a joke.
5. Moss notices the Tool number does not match its weld scar.
6. The official inventory says everything is fine.
7. Nix finds old maintenance photos proving the Tool is misidentified.
8. A Traverse departure clock counts down.
9. Cal resists admitting an unofficial sticker beat the system.
10. Luna prints a new sticker: THIS TOOL LIES.
11. The correct Tool is swapped just before departure.
12. Final shot: Prime Ops wall, now sticker-free. A tiny new sticker appears under the camera.

### Production Value

Mostly interior, character-driven, funny until the equipment stakes become real.

## Short-Film Guardrail

Shorts should not explain the Meridian Prime mythology. They should make the audience ask what kind of world produces these procedures, jokes, machines, and lies.
