# Meridian Prime Development Department Room Rules

**Sandbox Status:** Development Department / Side Story Sandbox  
**Repository:** `Discontent2/Discontent2-meridian-prime-comic-sandbox`  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon unless deliberately promoted through the Candidate Canon process.

## Operating Principle

The main Meridian Prime repository is the canon library. This repository is the quarantined ideation lab.

Canon may flow into sandbox concepts as reference, flavor, rules, terminology, and constraint. Sandbox inventions do not flow back into main canon automatically.

## Repository Rules

### Main Canon Repository

`Discontent2/meridian-prime`

Treatment:

- Read-only reference.
- Do not create, update, overwrite, or delete files.
- Do not patch, refactor, reorganize, or repair canon files from this room.
- Treat it as locked glass: look through it, do not touch it.

### Development Sandbox Repository

`Discontent2/Discontent2-meridian-prime-comic-sandbox`

Treatment:

- This is the only repository this room may write to.
- Development-room material should live under `docs/development/` unless another sandbox folder is specifically requested.
- Sandbox documents must be clearly labeled as development, side-story, format prototype, what-if, or candidate canon.

## Quarantine Rule

Nothing created in this room becomes main canon automatically.

A side-story idea may be exciting, useful, commercial, emotional, or beautiful without being canon. This room exists to take risks safely.

## Required Labels

Use these labels clearly in sandbox files.

### Canon Reference

Directly drawn from the main canon repository. Preserve when used as reference.

### Development Concept

Free ideation. Not canon.

### Side Story Sandbox

A non-canon story concept set in or inspired by Meridian Prime.

### What-If Continuity

A deliberate alternate continuity, contradiction, or experimental version.

### Format Prototype

A film, TV, game, comic, audio, prose, or hybrid structure test.

### Promo Exaggeration

A heightened image, premise, scene, or framing intended for marketing, pitch, hype, cover art, trailer logic, or adaptation compression.

### Candidate Canon

A sandbox invention that may be worth reviewing for promotion into main canon. Use rarely.

### Rejected / Do Not Promote

A useful experiment that should remain outside main canon.

- Tenet Nocsid III
- Rob Holliday
- Horus Skelton
- Lindy Nocsid
- Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289

These may appear as background rumor, distant institutional pressure, public-facing references, or deliberately marked What-If material, but they should not be solved or redefined here.

## Protected Mystery Rule

Do not accidentally define or solve:

- the full Core mystery
- gateway mechanics
- Aeonos / Àæonos cosmology
- Rob's complete poisoning chain
- the assassin inside NCI
- Horus's entire plan
- The House Ten Built's full secrets
- the ultimate nature of gods or cosmic forces
- Book One's reveal order

Side stories may brush against mystery. They should not swallow the main saga's engine.

## Candidate Canon Process

1. Invent freely in the sandbox.
2. Mark something **Candidate Canon** only if it genuinely deserves review.
3. Add it to `docs/development/side-story-candidate-canon-log.md`.
4. Bring it back to the main Meridian Prime canon/story room for review.
5. Only after explicit approval may it be added to `Discontent2/meridian-prime`.

## Development Ethos

Be bold. Be commercial. Be cinematic. Let the sandbox wear goggles and steal a snowcat.

But keep the canon vault locked.
