# Canon Weight Ladder

> Sandbox workflow guide for judging how much canon pressure a new idea carries before review.

**Sandbox Status:** Workflow Guide  
**Canon Status:** Not canon. This file organizes sandbox review behavior only.  
**Authoritative Promotion Rules:** `Discontent2/meridian-prime:docs/development/sandbox-promotion-rules.md`

## Purpose

Use this ladder before marking any sandbox idea as Candidate Canon.

A good sandbox idea can still be too large, too early, or too close to protected mystery material. Canon weight helps decide how carefully an idea must be reviewed.

## Canon Weight Tiers

### 1. Planetary Law

A rule that affects the whole setting.

Examples:

- physics
- cosmology
- global geology
- planetary seasons
- universal ecology rules
- gateway logic

Review posture: highest caution. Do not promote from a side-story file alone.

### 2. Major Civilization Space

A major people-place system.

Examples:

- a civilization homeland
- a city-state with unique authority
- a large sovereign region
- a major cultural sphere

Review posture: requires source-of-truth, route, jurisdiction, and protected mystery checks.

### 3. Region / Route System

A large geography or travel network.

Examples:

- basin routes
- seasonal roads
- sea approaches
- trade circuits
- hidden route systems

Review posture: requires seasonal access and route-adjacency review.

### 4. Settlement

A city, town, camp, station, port, base, or hidden headquarters.

Review posture: define location, function, authority, economy, and route role.

### 5. Faction

A political, religious, criminal, civic, corporate, family, or social group.

Review posture: define goals, limits, relationships, and what authority it actually controls.

### 6. Institution

A formal or semi-formal system.

Examples:

- courts
- guilds
- agencies
- contractors
- research offices
- gate authorities

Review posture: define jurisdiction, procedures, and failure modes.

### 7. Local Custom

A ritual, saying, practice, taboo, festival, greeting, or field habit.

Review posture: usually safe if local and clearly labeled.

### 8. Organism

A creature, plant, fungus, food species, parasite, or ecological force.

Review posture: define seasonal behavior, route effect, settlement effect, and cultural reading.

### 9. Substance

A fictional fuel, medicine, toxin, intoxicant, food product, material, or contraband.

Review posture: keep fictional and story-facing. Do not include real-world recipes, dosages, extraction methods, or technical procedures.

### 10. Market Item

A traded object, sticker, charm, tool, garment, meal, souvenir, repair part, or black-market good.

Review posture: usually sandbox texture unless it becomes plot-relevant.

### 11. Side-Story Flavor

A line, gag, visual detail, minor prop, local rumor, or adaptation texture.

Review posture: default sandbox. Promote only if repeated use proves it belongs in canon.

## Required Questions Before Candidate Canon

Before promoting any idea into Candidate Canon staging, answer:

1. What canon weight does this idea carry?
2. Which main canon files does it depend on?
3. Does it touch protected mysteries?
4. Does it change route logic, jurisdiction, or seasonal access?
5. Does it strengthen existing canon without replacing it?
6. Is it better as sandbox texture?

## Core Rule

The larger the canon weight, the slower the promotion path.

The sandbox should remain wild. Promotion should be disciplined.
