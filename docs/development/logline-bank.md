# Meridian Prime Logline Bank

**Sandbox Status:** Development Department / Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon logline bank. Promote nothing automatically.

## Purpose

A living bank of compact Meridian Prime expansion hooks for later development into comics, shorts, game missions, prose, audio drama, or TV.

## Survival / Traverse Loglines

1. **Circle Camp** - A crystal storm traps a Traverse outside The Claw, and the crew's survival plan fails when the fuel cache data proves cleaner than the fuel.
2. **Flags Missing** - A Mountaineer halts a Traverse after black flags vanish from The Path, but Prime Ops insists the route is still proven.
3. **The Fuel Cache at Mile 300** - A safe-zone cache is sealed, logged, and unusable, forcing a crew to choose what survives the route home.
4. **Magic Carpet** - A mechanic must drag a dead tractor home across hostile terrain while everyone else argues over what they can abandon.
5. **The Land Grew** - A MITE crew wakes to find the route's pressure ridges have grown overnight, swallowing their flagged way back.
6. **Stop Ops** - A junior crew member calls the emergency halt phrase without permission and accidentally saves a Traverse from a hidden Path failure.
7. **Last Season's Route** - A crew follows a previous season's route to save time and discovers why old safe paths are never safe by inheritance.
8. **The Wrong Side Rule** - A tired flagman notices black flags on the wrong side and has minutes to prove the entire convoy is traveling a false Path.
9. **Soft Brake** - A defect-named MITE tractor becomes the only machine that can hold a recovery line during a crevasse rescue.
10. **No Reverse** - A hated old tractor with a humiliating defect-name becomes essential when a route can only be survived forward.

## Mystery / Horror Loglines

11. **NC-S+285** - A Named Crack bears the name of a worker whose official record says he never died.
12. **The Marker That Came Back** - A special route marker removed by Prime Ops keeps reappearing at the same mile after every storm.
13. **Dead Channel Breathing** - An infrastructure monitor hears life on a channel that route records classify as dismantled decades ago.
14. **The Empty Mod** - A recovered Mod returns from a failed route with heat, food, and bedding intact, but no crew and no exterior tracks.
15. **Blue Flags at Night** - A WWISSaRD route glows blue after dark, even where no flags were planted.
16. **The Fourth Bladder** - A fuel cache lists three bladders, but the fourth one is older, unnumbered, and warm.
17. **Red Light on the Fork** - A solar marker flashes a route warning in a pattern no current Prime Ops system uses.
18. **The Mile That Moved** - Every instrument agrees the crew has advanced one mile. Every flag says they have returned to the same marker.
19. **The Polite Report** - A Prime Ops clerk edits a fatal incident into a weather delay and then starts receiving corrected drafts from nowhere.
20. **The Old Handshake** - A ghost handshake repeats a crew member's name before that person joins the mission.

## Workplace / Culture Loglines

21. **Winter-Over Prints More** - A sticker war in Prime Ops turns into a safety crisis when one unofficial joke saves a crew.
22. **Great Mouthfeel** - A bar-bathroom phrase becomes a Ghostwaver shibboleth after Prime Ops tries to remove it from every wall.
23. **The Numbering Job** - Winter-Over workers argue over tool numbering until they discover two Tools with the same number and different histories.
24. **Defect-Name Day** - A new MITE tractor earns its insulting name during a terrible shift and refuses to die afterward.
25. **Printer Jam Tragedy** - A dispatcher delays a dangerous packet by pretending the printer failed, buying a crew enough time to catch the lie.
26. **The Company Potluck** - A minor NCI gathering turns into a pressure cooker when a contractor, a Prime Ops auditor, and a Ghostwaver all claim the same old route rumor.
27. **The Blue Flag Bar Tab** - WWISSaRD scientists try to expense a survival celebration, revealing a route discrepancy in the receipt chain.
28. **If a Transport Arrives** - A Winter-Over joke sticker becomes a grim instruction during an evacuation nobody wants to authorize.
29. **The Fuel Clerk** - A Prime Ops fuel clerk discovers that one cache receives refills on paper but not in the world.
30. **The Unofficial Manual** - Mechanics circulate a forbidden repair booklet because official procedure keeps killing the same part.

## Species / Culture Loglines

31. **Pressure-Runner** - A Velocisapien courier carries route-memory through a pressure corridor before the official record can erase it.
32. **Wire Access** - A Lividisapien salvage worker reads an old route system that higher-tier tech cannot interpret.
33. **The Black-Glass Witness** - A Nullsapien observer records a Traverse incident perfectly and refuses to explain why the official file is wrong.
34. **Boiler-Ska Courier** - A Saurasapien pressure-runner and Meridian flagman must outrun a Growth Event with opposite ideas of what memory means.
35. **Vault Mesh Missing** - A Vaultsapien archivist finds a survivor ledger entry for someone still alive on Meridian Prime.
36. **Veil on The Path** - A Veilsapien scout uses AR overlays to identify missing flags, but the overlay shows a route that should not exist.
37. **Amaranth Simulation** - An Amaranthsapien researcher simulates a crystal storm and predicts a voice inside it.
38. **Foundry Heart** - An Ankylosapien repair worker holds a failing pressure gate while a Meridian crew debates whether to leave the station behind.
39. **Civic Mourning Chain** - A Cerasapien guardian brings mourning law to a Named Crack that World Works refuses to acknowledge.
40. **Doom at the Gate** - A Tyrannosapien commander refuses a corporate evacuation order to protect a mixed crew trapped at an old pressure facility.

## Game-Forward Loglines

41. **Red Flags Route** - Players manage a MITE route team laying red flags across unstable terrain while supplies, daylight, and patience run out.
42. **Cache Audit** - A logistics survival mission where every fuel number is suspect and every mile costs heat.
43. **Track Bike Duty** - A fast, risky side mission to replace missing route markers before a convoy reaches the danger zone.
44. **Break Free** - A morning-after-Circle-Camp mission where the crew must dig out, repair, refuel, and decide whether to continue.
45. **The Tool Run** - Players recover a missing Tool component from a half-buried equipment yard during a Growth Event.
46. **Dead Channel Debug** - A signal puzzle mission built around old infrastructure, ghost handshakes, and corrupted comms.
47. **Black Flags Left** - A route-navigation mission where flag-side doctrine is the core mechanic.
48. **Storm Hold Seven Days** - Resource-management survival across a ten-year storm.
49. **The Wrong Manifest** - Cargo puzzle thriller: the manifest says the right gear is aboard, but the Mod disagrees.
50. **Named Crack Memorial** - A tabletop investigation mission where route memory, grief, and hazard mapping collide.

## Bank Rule

Every logline must eventually answer:

- What is the job?
- What makes the job wrong?
- What does the crew know that official records do not?
