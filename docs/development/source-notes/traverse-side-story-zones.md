# Traverse Side-Story Zones

**Sandbox Status:** Development Department / Source Notes  
**Label:** Development Concept using Canon Reference  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon expansion map for side-story development.

## Purpose

This file identifies the strongest Traverse-adjacent zones for non-canon Meridian Prime side stories.

The goal is to find story engines that are compelling without forcing Tenet, Rob, Horus, or MITE II to carry every narrative.

## Zone 1: Circle Camp / Storm Hold

**Core Engine:** A Traverse cannot enter a crevasse field during a crystal storm and must survive outside it.

Story pressure:

- fuel consumption
- crew exhaustion
- buried equipment
- repeating circular motion
- discipline under boredom and fear
- bad math becoming horror
- official terms versus crew terms

Best formats:

- short film
- survival prose
- comic one-shot
- tactical game mission

## Zone 2: Fuel Cache Failure

**Core Engine:** A safe zone becomes unsafe because the fuel cache is missing, empty, buried, contaminated, mislabeled, or politically inconvenient.

Story pressure:

- abandonment decisions
- load redistribution
- damaged trust in Prime Ops
- resource thriller math
- mystery of who signed off on the cache

Best formats:

- film thriller
- game mission
- TV episode
- prose novelette

## Zone 3: Black-Flag Path Doctrine

**Core Engine:** The Path is only safe because crews maintain it. A missing or wrong-side black flag threatens the whole mission.

Story pressure:

- Mountaineer authority
- stop ops calls
- wrong-side doctrine
- missing markers
- official denial
- crew fear hidden under procedure

Best formats:

- TV bottle episode
- audio drama
- suspense comic
- tabletop scenario

## Zone 4: Named Cracks

**Core Engine:** Traverse Ops remembers the dead through unofficial hazard names. World Works records the hazard but softens or hides the death.

Story pressure:

- memorial culture
- erased fatalities
- families paid off
- recurring cracks
- field folklore
- crew truth vs official map

Best formats:

- horror comic
- anthology prose
- audio drama
- mystery episode

## Zone 5: WWISSaRD Research Routes

**Core Engine:** Science Traverses go where extraction crews avoid, ignore, or consider too strange.

Story pressure:

- overbuilt official acronym culture
- field crews mocking science while depending on it
- strange data
- ethically dubious experiments
- ancient signals misread as study noise

Best formats:

- TV anthology
- weird-science comic
- audio drama
- investigation game mission

## Zone 6: Winter-Over Culture

**Core Engine:** The off-season crews maintain the systems everyone else survives by, while inventing unofficial culture in isolation.

Story pressure:

- stickers
- equipment numbering
- loneliness
- dark jokes
- maintenance mistakes
- grudges inside the machine room
- low-status people knowing high-value truths

Best formats:

- dark comedy
- workplace bottle episode
- animated short
- short prose cycle

## Zone 7: Magic Carpet Recovery

**Core Engine:** A dead machine must be dragged home or abandoned.

Story pressure:

- the mechanic cannot fix it
- tool attachment problems
- tow risk
- what gets left behind
- machine superstition
- crew emotional dependence on equipment

Best formats:

- game level
- comic one-shot
- prose survival story

## Zone 8: Ghostwaver Signal Culture

**Core Engine:** Unofficial broadcasters, signal scavengers, and rumor networks hear what official systems deny.

Story pressure:

- pirate radio
- ghost handshakes
- old infrastructure
- signal collage
- official silencing
- rumors that may not be true but still save lives

Best formats:

- audio drama
- comic
- TV episode
- interactive ARG-style pitch

## Zone 9: Mixed-Species Field Crews

**Core Engine:** Meridian Prime conditions require practical expertise across human, Saurasapien, and Antisapien cultures.

Story pressure:

- skill respect vs political discomfort
- different sensory truths
- signal truth vs machine truth vs terrain truth
- cultural misunderstanding under field danger
- shared survival creating temporary family

Best formats:

- ensemble TV
- comic limited series
- tabletop scenario
- tactical RPG party structure

## Zone 10: Isolated Stations and Failed Routes

**Core Engine:** Places at the edge of the map preserve contradictions.

Story pressure:

- old equipment
- degraded dieselpunk infrastructure
- quiet coverups
- route memory
- local myths
- people who stayed too long

Best formats:

- prose novella
- horror comic
- audio drama
- exploration game mission

## Development Rule

Every side-story zone should answer three questions:

1. What does the official report say?
2. What did the crew actually experience?
3. What survives as rumor, scar, sticker, signal, crack name, or route habit?
