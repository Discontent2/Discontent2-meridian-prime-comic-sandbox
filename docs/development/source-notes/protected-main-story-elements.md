# Protected Main-Story Elements

**Sandbox Status:** Development Department / Source Notes  
**Label:** Canon Reference  
**Canon Boundary:** This file protects main-story gravity wells from accidental sandbox promotion or contradiction.

## Purpose

This file identifies elements that should remain controlled by the main Meridian Prime canon/story process.

The Development Department may reference these elements as distant background, rumor, shadow, paperwork echo, or What-If Continuity, but should not define, resolve, contradict, or center them unless explicitly requested.

## Primary Gravity Wells

### Tenet Nocsid III

Protected because he is the primary protagonist of the main Traverse / hidden-route story.

Avoid using him as the hero of side stories unless specifically asked.

### Rob Holliday

Protected because his death is a route, not a simple backstory event.

Avoid solving, compressing, or simplifying:

- his poisoning
- his final choices
- his relationship with Horus
- his relationship with Tenet I
- his final move before death

### Horus Skelton

Protected because he is not a generic villain. His role is tied to old friendship, resentment, World Works authority, the Core, and inherited Nocsid history.

Avoid using him as a simple corporate bad guy of the week.

### Lindy Nocsid

Protected because she is tightly tied to NCI, Tenet, Book One's early wrongness, and family pressure.

Use only with care.

### Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289

Protected because it is Tenet's signature machine and part of his visual and emotional identity.

Do not rename it, reassign it, destroy it, or turn it into generic MITE gear.

### MITE II

Protected because it is the main novella's route engine.

Do not create alternate MITE II missions unless labeled What-If Continuity.

### The House Ten Built

Protected because it is a secret-bearing legacy site tied to Grandfather Ten, Rob, Horus, Tenet, and the old rupture.

Do not define its full secrets in sandbox continuity.

### Grandfather Ten's Pistol

Protected because it is not merely a weapon. It is an inheritance object, trust marker, key, test, and route-authority symbol.

Do not use it as a random prop.

### Rob's Death Chain

Protected because its structure matters to the main saga.

Do not reveal or redefine:

- the assassin inside NCI
- Chrono-Floquet Distillate mechanics
- Rob's complete investigation
- Horus's exact access chain
- the final confrontation details beyond canon reference

### Hidden Formation Near the Claw

Protected because it is a Book One reveal engine.

Sandbox stories may use other anomalies, other failed routes, other local myths, or false rumors.

### Core / Gateway / Aeonos / Àæonos Truth

Protected because it is saga-scale mystery.

Sandbox concepts may gesture at:

- old infrastructure
- ghost signals
- ruin marks
- mistranslated names
- conflicting field myths

They should not solve the ultimate cosmology.

## Forbidden Accidental Moves

Do not make a new side character secretly responsible for:

- Rob's poisoning
- the MITE II packet
- Horus's plan
- the hidden formation
- The House Ten Built
- the Core
- the gateway

Do not make every small incident a direct piece of the main conspiracy.

A local coverup can matter without secretly being the whole saga.

## Safe Background Usage

Allowed background uses:

- a crew complains about Horus's office
- a Prime Ops clerk references NCI as hard to manage
- a Winter-Over sticker jokes about director-level paperwork
- a Ghostwaver rumor mentions an old Nocsid machine
- a Named Crack story contradicts World Works records
- a side crew hears a ghost handshake but never learns its ultimate source

## Development Rule

When in doubt, invent sideways rather than upward.

Sideways invention creates new local pressure.

Upward invention risks hijacking main canon.
