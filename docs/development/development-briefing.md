# Development Department Briefing

**Sandbox Status:** Development Department / Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon briefing based on canon references from `Discontent2/meridian-prime`.

## Executive Read

Meridian Prime is built for expansion because the main story is intentionally narrow while the world around it is wide, procedural, dangerous, culturally textured, and visually distinct.

The main Book One engine belongs to Tenet, MITE II, Rob's death-route, Horus's over-clean approval chain, the hidden formation, and the first route-to-truth discovery. The Development Department should not compete with that engine. It should prove that the world can sustain other stories.

Best expansion principle:

**Do not make every side story secretly central. Make side stories locally life-or-death.**

## What Works Commercially

Meridian Prime side stories can sell multiple flavors:

- industrial survival thriller
- cold-weather procedural mystery
- science-route horror
- workplace dark comedy
- cosmic rumor without cosmic exposition
- mixed-species field-team drama
- anthology tragedy
- 16-bit route-management survival
- comic-book visual spectacle
- audio-drama signal suspense

## The Franchise Spine Outside Tenet

The strongest repeatable engine is Traverse Ops.

A Traverse gives every story:

- a vehicle train
- a mission objective
- a route
- fuel pressure
- field roles
- command hierarchy
- terrain danger
- communication limits
- official paperwork
- crew truth
- old infrastructure
- possible coverup pressure
- a reason characters cannot simply leave

## Safe Expansion Tone

The sandbox should preserve these tonal ingredients:

- field competence first
- grief or fear leaking through work, not speeches
- bureaucracy that is chilling because it is plausible
- machines with personality through defects and repairs
- humor that comes from people doing hard jobs
- beauty under lethal conditions
- local mysteries that may remain partly unresolved

## Avoiding Main-Story Contamination

The Development Department should not solve:

- Rob's poisoning chain
- Horus's full plan
- the assassin inside NCI
- The House Ten Built
- the Core
- gateway mechanics
- Book One's hidden formation

Side stories should use smaller mysteries:

- Why is this fuel cache empty?
- Who moved these black flags?
- Why does this old relay answer only during storms?
- Why does a Named Crack remember a person erased from records?
- Why did Prime Ops classify a survival mistake as a successful procedure?
- Why is a WWISSaRD team studying something extraction crews were told to ignore?

## Development Direction

Priority should go to concepts that are:

1. Legible without explaining the entire saga.
2. Visual enough for comics or screen.
3. Mechanical enough for games.
4. Small enough not to rewrite canon.
5. Strange enough to feel unmistakably Meridian Prime.

## Best First Slate

Recommended first slate focus:

1. `Circle Camp` - survival thriller.
2. `Flags Missing` - black-flag procedural suspense.
3. `NC-S+285` - Named Crack memorial mystery.
4. `The Fuel Cache at Mile 300` - resource disaster thriller.
5. `Winter-Over Prints More` - workplace dark comedy with consequences.
6. `Ghostwaver Relay` - audio/signal mystery.
7. `Magic Carpet` - recovery mission and machine-culture piece.
8. `Blue Flags` - WWISSaRD science horror.
9. `Pressure-Runner` - Saurasapien courier action.
10. `Wire Access` - Antisapien signal salvage cyber-thriller.

## Department Workflow

1. Build logline bank.
2. Sort concepts by format.
3. Expand top pitches into one-page treatments.
4. Mark any useful inventions as Candidate Canon only when rare and justified.
5. Track rejected or non-promotable ideas rather than deleting them.
6. Keep main canon clean until explicit promotion.

## Department Motto

**The side route does not need to reveal the Core. It only needs to make someone survive the mile marker.**
