# Meridian Prime Anthology Concepts

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Development Concept / Format Prototype  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon anthology development.

## Anthology Thesis

A Meridian Prime anthology should not explain the whole universe. It should make each episode feel like a field report someone tried to bury.

Core format:

- one local incident
- one crew, station, route, signal, or artifact
- one official explanation
- one field truth
- one residue: rumor, sticker, crack name, signal, repair habit, or scar

## Anthology Model A: Field Report Horror

Each episode begins with an official incident classification and ends with what the crew actually remembers.

### Episode Shape

1. Cold open: field problem.
2. Official framing: sanitized report language.
3. Crew reality: procedure under stress.
4. Contradiction: the report cannot contain what happened.
5. Residue: the event survives unofficially.

### Best Episodes

- `NC-S+285`
- `Dead Channel Breathing`
- `Blue Flags at Night`
- `The Empty Mod`
- `The Mile That Moved`

## Anthology Model B: Workplace Pressure Comedy

The world is lethal, but the people maintaining it are exhausted, funny, petty, and competent.

### Episode Shape

1. Mundane maintenance problem.
2. Ridiculous bureaucratic obstacle.
3. Crew workaround.
4. Workaround exposes real danger.
5. Joke becomes survival tool.

### Best Episodes

- `Winter-Over Prints More`
- `The Numbering Job`
- `Defect-Name Day`
- `Printer Jam Tragedy`
- `The Unofficial Manual`

## Anthology Model C: Survival Route Thriller

Every episode follows a crew through a specific operational crisis.

### Episode Shape

1. Mission objective.
2. First practical wrongness.
3. Resource or route pressure.
4. Procedure fails.
5. Judgment decides who survives.

### Best Episodes

- `Circle Camp`
- `Flags Missing`
- `The Fuel Cache at Mile 300`
- `Magic Carpet`
- `The Wrong Side Rule`

## Anthology Model D: Signal and Memory

Built for audio drama or moody prestige TV. The stories center on Ghostwavers, Antisapiens, old infrastructure, and forbidden memory.

### Episode Shape

1. A signal appears where none should exist.
2. A person with unofficial access hears it.
3. Official channels deny it.
4. The signal asks a cost.
5. The truth survives as broadcast, rumor, or corrupted archive.

### Best Episodes

- `Ghostwaver Relay`
- `Wire Access`
- `The Old Handshake`
- `Dead Channel Debug`
- `Vault Mesh Missing`

## Anthology Model E: Culture Contact Field Stories

Mixed-species field crews face local incidents that force different ways of reading truth into collision.

### Episode Shape

1. Practical mission.
2. Cultural mismatch.
3. Terrain, signal, or machine contradiction.
4. Mutual reliance under hazard.
5. Respect earned, not preached.

### Best Episodes

- `Pressure-Runner`
- `Veil on The Path`
- `Foundry Heart`
- `Civic Mourning Chain`
- `Boiler-Ska Courier`

## Season One Candidate Order

1. `Circle Camp` - survival hook.
2. `Winter-Over Prints More` - tonal breadth.
3. `Flags Missing` - procedural suspense.
4. `Ghostwaver Relay` - signal culture.
5. `NC-S+285` - memory horror.
6. `Magic Carpet` - machine culture.
7. `Blue Flags` - science weirdness.
8. `Pressure-Runner` - kinetic species-culture story.

## Recurring Devices

- Official report excerpts.
- Crew slang titles.
- Route maps that disagree with memory.
- Stickers as chorus.
- Ghostwaver snippets.
- Prime Ops edits.
- Hazard names.
- Equipment defect-names.
- Closing image of what remains after the paperwork.

## Anthology Guardrail

No episode should reveal the full Core, solve Rob, define Horus's whole plan, or make its protagonist secretly responsible for the main plot.

The anthology should make the world feel deeper by letting some truths remain local, partial, and alive.
