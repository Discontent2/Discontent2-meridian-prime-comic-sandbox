# Meridian Prime Audio Drama Concepts

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Format Prototype / Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon audio development.

## Audio Direction

Meridian Prime is strong for audio because field life depends on comms, dead channels, signal ghosts, dispatch voices, old infrastructure, storm noise, engine complaints, and the difference between official speech and crew speech.

Core audio identity:

- radio discipline
- static as texture
- machinery as percussion
- storm pressure
- signal pings
- half-heard field jokes
- official messages that sound too clean
- Ghostwaver broadcasts that sound too alive

## Audio Concept 1: Ghostwaver Relay

**Format:** 6 x 20-minute limited audio series  
**Tone:** signal noir / conspiracy-adjacent mystery  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

A Ghostwaver host catches a ghost handshake from a route that officially has no infrastructure, then becomes the target of three departments that all want the signal buried for different reasons.

### Audio Devices

- pirate broadcast intro
- call-in rumors
- jamming tones
- old route pings
- Lividisapien wire-access diagnostics
- Prime Ops compliance warnings
- signal loops that change slightly per episode

### Episode Spine

1. The joke broadcast.
2. The handshake repeats.
3. The route worker recognizes the rhythm.
4. Prime Ops jams the show.
5. The signal gets distributed as a street beat.
6. The truth survives as audio culture, not report data.

## Audio Concept 2: Flags Missing

**Format:** 1 x 45-minute audio special  
**Tone:** real-time procedural suspense  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

A Traverse crew on The Path stops after black flags vanish, and the entire story unfolds through cab comms, Track Bike check-ins, Prime Ops calls, and storm noise.

### Audio Devices

- repeated stop ops call
- mounting silence after each marker number
- GPR sweep tones
- flagman breathing on open mic
- Prime Ops official calm
- Lead's clipped commands

### Ending Sound

A missing flag pole tapping against the side of the Track Bike where no one put it.

## Audio Concept 3: The Fuel Clerk

**Format:** 4 x 25-minute limited series  
**Tone:** bureaucratic thriller  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

A Prime Ops fuel clerk discovers that one safe-zone cache is being refilled on paper but not in the field, then has to decide whether to break procedure before the next storm window.

### Audio Devices

- office hum
- printer sounds
- inventory calls
- fuel pump recordings
- old voice mails
- increasingly tense dispatch logs

### Why Audio Works

The horror is in the paperwork. Every number sounds ordinary until the listener understands what it costs.

## Audio Concept 4: NC-S+285

**Format:** 3 x 30-minute investigative audio drama  
**Tone:** memorial mystery  
**Label:** Side Story Sandbox

### Logline

A young flagman records oral histories about a Named Crack and discovers that every person who remembers the death tells the story differently, while official records deny it happened at all.

### Audio Devices

- interviews
- old crew tapes
- hazard map dictation
- route wind
- crack groans
- family messages
- edited official transcript fragments

### Ending Sound

The crew saying the Named Crack's name before crossing.

## Audio Concept 5: Dead Channel Debug

**Format:** interactive-style audio episode / game companion  
**Tone:** technical suspense  
**Label:** Format Prototype

### Logline

An infrastructure monitor must decide which of three signal feeds to trust: official comms, old infrastructure, or a corrupted ghost channel that keeps warning the crew in broken field slang.

### Audio Devices

- three distinct channel treatments
- signal-band perception shifts
- old terminal boot tones
- distorted route warnings
- crew arguing over whether a dead channel can be useful

## Recurring Audio Motifs

- Official channels are clean, clipped, and over-compressed.
- Field channels are noisy, warm, practical, and alive.
- Ghostwaver channels are layered, rhythmic, and unstable.
- Antisapien signal perception can be represented with harmonic undertones, directionality, or pulses.
- Crystal storms should sound beautiful and hostile, not generic wind.

## Audio Guardrail

Audio dramas may imply larger mystery through signal behavior, but they should not define the Core, gateway mechanics, or main Book One reveals.
