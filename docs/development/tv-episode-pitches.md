# Meridian Prime TV Episode Pitches

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Format Prototype / Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon TV development.

## TV Direction

Meridian Prime works best on television as a procedural survival anthology with workplace texture and slow-burn cosmic pressure.

The show should feel like:

- field work under impossible conditions
- official reports versus lived truth
- humor before terror
- machinery as character
- strange beauty that can kill you

## Series Format Option

**Title:** `Meridian Prime: On Traverse`  
**Format:** 8 x 45-minute anthology episodes  
**Season Engine:** Each episode follows one route incident. Episodes share institutions, terms, visual language, and rumors, but do not require the same protagonist.

## Episode 1: Circle Camp

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** survival thriller

### Logline

A MITE crew trapped outside The Claw during a crystal storm enters Circle Camp, only to discover the safe-zone fuel cache cannot support the survival plan Prime Ops approved.

### Core Conflict

Procedure says they survive by circling, refueling, sleeping, digging out, and repeating. The field says the numbers are wrong.

### Key Characters

- exhausted Traverse Lead
- Dieselwright mechanic
- anxious logistics specialist
- older Mountaineer who hates hope when math is bad
- green flagman trying not to become a cautionary sticker

### Ending Residue

A new unofficial sticker appears: **CIRCLE CAMP IS NOT RETREAT. CHECK THE CACHE.**

## Episode 2: Winter-Over Prints More

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** workplace dark comedy

### Logline

A sticker war between Winter-Over crews and Prime Ops turns into a safety crisis when the only accurate warning on a Tool is unofficial, ugly, and impossible to approve.

### Core Conflict

Prime Ops wants clean equipment. Winter-Over wants surviving crews.

### Key Characters

- sticker artist with maintenance access
- humorless Prime Ops supervisor
- bar worker who knows too much
- mechanic who treats every Tool like a suspect

### Ending Residue

The sticker is removed. Ten more appear by morning.

---

## Episode 3: Flags Missing

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** procedural suspense

### Logline

A Mountaineer calls stop ops after black flags vanish along The Path, trapping a convoy between official certainty and the empty snow-crystal field ahead.

### Core Conflict

If the Mountaineer is wrong, the mission fails publicly. If Prime Ops is wrong, everyone dies quietly.

### Key Characters

- Mountaineer with a history of one bad call
- Lead under schedule pressure
- flagman of the day
- infrastructure monitor reading a faint old ping

### Ending Residue

The missing flags are found in a place no wind should have put them.

---

## Episode 4: Ghostwaver Relay

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** signal mystery

### Logline

A Ghostwaver pirate broadcaster jokes about a ghost handshake from a dead route, then realizes World Works heard it too.

### Core Conflict

The signal is probably nothing. Everyone in power behaves like it is not nothing.

### Key Characters

- Ghostwaver host
- Lividisapien wire-access tech
- Prime Ops comms compliance officer
- retired route worker who recognizes the pulse

### Ending Residue

The broadcast is jammed, but the rhythm becomes a street chant.

---

## Episode 5: NC-S+285

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** memorial horror / mystery

### Logline

A new flagman learns that a Named Crack is named after a worker who officially survived the route, retired, and signed forms afterward.

### Core Conflict

The map remembers a death the archive refuses.

### Key Characters

- young flagman
- old Scarwalker
- Prime Ops records clerk
- family member of the supposedly living worker

### Ending Residue

The crack keeps the name. The official file gains a blank page.

---

## Episode 6: Magic Carpet

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** machine survival drama

### Logline

After a tractor dies On Traverse, a mechanic must rig the Magic Carpet recovery system while the crew argues over what must be sacrificed to bring everyone home.

### Core Conflict

A machine failure becomes a moral inventory.

### Key Characters

- field mechanic
- HEO who loves the dead tractor
- logistics specialist with ruthless cargo math
- rookie who keeps apologizing to the machine

### Ending Residue

The tractor is renamed worse than before, which means the crew loved it.

---

## Episode 7: Blue Flags

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** weird science field horror

### Logline

A WWISSaRD team follows a blue-flag route to a research site whose mile markers change only in official reports.

### Core Conflict

Science wants more data. Traverse wants the route to stop lying.

### Key Characters

- WWISSaRD principal investigator
- skeptical Traverse Lead
- Antisapien infrastructure monitor
- quiet technician who notices the same marker twice

### Ending Residue

The report classifies the event as instrumentation drift. The crew refuses to use that route name again.

---

## Episode 8: Pressure-Runner

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Tone:** action / culture contact

### Logline

A Velocisapien courier carries a local route-memory token through a pressure corridor while Meridian officials and Antisapien watchers both claim authority over the message.

### Core Conflict

Who owns field memory: the institution, the signal, or the runner who survives carrying it?

### Key Characters

- Velocisapien pressure-runner
- Meridian checkpoint officer
- Veilsapien observer
- old route drummer who knows the token's original rhythm

### Ending Residue

The message arrives, but not in the language anyone expected.

## TV Guardrail

Every episode may deepen the world. No episode should become the secret missing chapter of Book One unless deliberately relabeled as What-If Continuity.
