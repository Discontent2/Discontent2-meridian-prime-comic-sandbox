# Meridian Prime Side-Story Slate

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon development slate. Nothing here is main canon unless promoted through review.

## Slate Purpose

This slate proves Meridian Prime can support multimedia expansion beyond the main Tenet / MITE II novella.

Development target:

**Stories where the world is big, the danger is local, and the main canon remains protected.**

## Ranking Criteria

Pitches are ranked by:

1. Format flexibility.
2. Immediate hook clarity.
3. Low risk to protected canon.
4. Strong Meridian Prime identity.
5. Visual / playable / cinematic potential.

---

## 1. Circle Camp

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox / Format Prototype  
**Primary Formats:** short film, comic one-shot, game mission, prose survival story  
**Potential:** Very High

### Logline

When a crystal storm traps a Traverse outside The Claw, the crew enters Circle Camp, but their survival math collapses after the fuel cache reads full on paper and wrong in the field.

### Why It Works

Circle Camp is a clean survival engine: cannot go forward, cannot go back, cannot stop moving too long. The entire premise can be understood in one image: a convoy driving circles through a glittering white hell while fuel gauges fall.

### Sandbox Freedom

Invent a new MITE or mixed Traverse crew. Keep the storm local. Do not connect the cache failure to Rob, Horus, or MITE II.

---

## 2. Flags Missing

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox / Format Prototype  
**Primary Formats:** TV episode, audio drama, comic one-shot  
**Potential:** Very High

### Logline

A Mountaineer calls stop ops after black flags vanish from The Path, forcing a crew to decide whether to trust Prime Ops confirmation or the empty field in front of them.

### Why It Works

The stakes are procedural and terrifying. Nobody needs lore exposition to understand that missing black flags in a crevasse field are bad news.

### Sandbox Freedom

Invent a Path segment, a nervous Lead, and a Mountaineer with enough authority to stop everything.

---

## 3. NC-S+285

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Primary Formats:** horror comic, prose novelette, audio drama  
**Potential:** High

### Logline

A new flagman discovers a Named Crack named after a dead worker whose official record says he lived, retired, and never crossed that route.

### Why It Works

Named Cracks turn hazard maps into memorial folklore. This can be spooky without requiring literal ghosts.

### Sandbox Freedom

The mystery may resolve as coverup, misremembered route culture, identity swap, or unresolved field myth.

---

## 4. The Fuel Cache at Mile 300

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Primary Formats:** film thriller, game mission, TV episode  
**Potential:** High

### Logline

A Traverse arrives at a safe-zone fuel cache during a storm window and finds the cache intact, sealed, documented, and unusable.

### Why It Works

Resource math creates immediate pressure. The enemy is not a monster. It is a number that will not stretch.

### Sandbox Freedom

Possible causes include contamination, bad logging, corporate triage, theft, Growth Event damage, or an old storage lie.

---

## 5. Winter-Over Prints More

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox / Format Prototype  
**Primary Formats:** animated short, workplace comedy, prose cycle  
**Potential:** High

### Logline

A Winter-Over sticker war becomes a safety crisis when one unofficial joke sticker preserves the only true warning on a sabotaged Tool.

### Why It Works

It gives Meridian Prime humor teeth. Low-status maintenance culture gets to save the day by being impossible to fully control.

### Sandbox Freedom

Invent sticker slogans, maintenance grudges, and a local Prime Ops supervisor who badly underestimates printer access.

---

## 6. Ghostwaver Relay

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Primary Formats:** audio drama, comic, TV episode  
**Potential:** High

### Logline

A Ghostwaver pirate broadcast picks up a ghost handshake from a route with no listed infrastructure, and three departments arrive to make the joke disappear.

### Why It Works

Audio-first potential is excellent: static, coded jokes, forbidden memory, official pressure, and old signals breathing through dead channels.

### Sandbox Freedom

Do not reveal the ultimate source of the signal. Let the story be about who tries to silence it.

---

## 7. Magic Carpet

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox / Game Mission Prototype  
**Primary Formats:** 16-bit game level, comic one-shot, prose survival story  
**Potential:** Medium-High

### Logline

When a tractor dies On Traverse, a mechanic must rig the Magic Carpet recovery system while the crew decides what cargo, comfort, and pride must be abandoned.

### Why It Works

It turns repair culture into drama. A machine failure becomes an ethical problem.

### Sandbox Freedom

Invent the dead tractor, its defect-name, and the mechanic culture around it.

---

## 8. Blue Flags

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Primary Formats:** WWISSaRD anthology episode, weird-science comic, prose mystery  
**Potential:** Medium-High

### Logline

A WWISSaRD team follows blue flags toward a research site whose mile markers keep changing in the report but not in the field.

### Why It Works

It lets science be useful, ridiculous, and frightening at once.

### Sandbox Freedom

Invent the research team, site, and local phenomenon. Do not solve Core mechanics.

---

## 9. Pressure-Runner

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Primary Formats:** action comic, animated short, game side mission  
**Potential:** Medium

### Logline

A Velocisapien courier carries route-memory through a pressure corridor while Meridian officials and Antisapien signal watchers both claim the message belongs to them.

### Why It Works

It showcases Saurasapien pressure-rhythm culture and gives the IP a kinetic street-level story.

### Sandbox Freedom

Invent local courier politics and a message whose importance is local rather than saga-ending.

---

## 10. Wire Access

**Label:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Primary Formats:** cyber-thriller comic, game infiltration mission, audio drama  
**Potential:** Medium

### Logline

A Lividisapien signal salvage worker finds an old route system that only low-tier wire access can read, making them the least authorized person in possession of the truest data.

### Why It Works

It turns technological hierarchy upside down. The person with the least official access becomes the only one who can hear the old machine correctly.

### Sandbox Freedom

Keep the truth local. Avoid making the system a direct Core gateway explanation.

---

## Slate Strategy

Develop the top three first:

1. `Circle Camp`
2. `Flags Missing`
3. `NC-S+285`

Then build format-specific versions for game, comic, audio, and TV.
