# Meridian Prime Comic One-Shot Pitches

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Format Prototype / Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon comic development.

## Comic Direction

Meridian Prime comics should sell silhouette, texture, danger, and weird beauty:

- tractors as moving cathedrals of ugly survival
- black flags against crystal storm light
- patched machinery with personality
- Saurasapien pressure-rhythm bodies and fashion
- Antisapien signal glow and bio-cabling
- Prime Ops clean paperwork contrasted with dirty field truth

## One-Shot 1: Circle Camp

**Issue Length:** 24 pages  
**Tone:** survival thriller  
**Visual Hook:** A convoy driving endless circles under nacreous storm clouds.

### Logline

A MITE crew enters Circle Camp outside The Claw, but the safe-zone fuel cache fails before the storm does.

### Page Spine

1. Cold open: storm wall on horizon.
2. Lead refuses to enter The Claw.
3. Circle Camp begins.
4. Fuel cache inspection.
5. The numbers do not match.
6. Crew conflict over rationing.
7. Tractor damage overnight.
8. Emergency redistribution.
9. Final dig-out and departure.
10. Closing sticker / field residue.

### Best Splash Image

Five-mile circles carved into glittering white terrain, visible from above like a survival sigil.

## Visual Guardrails

- Do not over-polish MITE equipment.
- Do not make Saurasapiens generic dinosaur people.
- Do not make Antisapiens elves, zombies, or ordinary cyberpunk humans.
- Keep field gear practical.
- Let beauty sharpen danger.
