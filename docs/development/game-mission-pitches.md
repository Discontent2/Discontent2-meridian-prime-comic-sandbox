# Meridian Prime Game Mission Pitches

**Sandbox Status:** Side Story Sandbox  
**Label:** Format Prototype / Development Concept  
**Canon Boundary:** Non-canon game development.

## Game Direction

Meridian Prime is naturally gameable because Traverse Ops already has:

- route objectives
- fuel limits
- crew roles
- GPR / GOS navigation
- flags and markers
- vehicle trains
- field repairs
- storm rules
- cargo triage
- official reports
- hidden discrepancies

Best game style:

**16-bit tactical survival / route-management RPG with mission-based episodes.**

## Mission 1: Circle Camp

**Primary Loop:** survive a crystal storm outside The Claw.

### Objectives

1. Enter Circle Camp before storm lockout.
2. Maintain circular movement long enough to avoid burial.
3. Refuel from cache.
4. Diagnose cache discrepancy.
5. Decide whether to ration, abandon cargo, or risk a field repair.
6. Break free in the morning.

### Failure States

- fuel runs out
- crew fatigue collapses
- tractor immobilized
- storm buries Mod train
- unsafe attempt to cross The Claw

### Unique Mechanic

Circle-driving rhythm: movement consumes fuel but prevents burial. Stopping preserves fuel but increases dig-out and damage risk.

---

## Mission 2: Flags Missing

**Primary Loop:** navigate a compromised Path.

### Objectives

1. Investigate missing black flags.
2. Halt convoy with stop ops.
3. Deploy Track Bike for local inspection.
4. Compare GPR, old marker data, and visual flags.
5. Reconstruct safe passage.
6. File a report or hide the contradiction.

### Failure States

- wrong side of black flags
- Track Bike loss
- crevasse incident
- Prime Ops confidence penalty
- crew trust collapse

### Unique Mechanic

Flag-side doctrine puzzle: the player must determine whether the convoy is on the correct Path based on direction, flag side, and marker sequence.

---

## Mission 3: Cache Audit

**Primary Loop:** solve a fuel mystery before the weather closes.

### Objectives

1. Arrive at safe-zone cache.
2. Inspect bladders / tanks.
3. Compare manifest to actual fuel.
4. Identify contamination, mislabeling, theft, Growth Event damage, or deliberate false logging.
5. Recalculate route survival.
6. Choose cargo abandonment and crew redistribution.

### Failure States

- insufficient fuel
- wrong cargo abandoned
- crew member stranded
- contaminated fuel damages tractor
- official report buries the lesson

### Unique Mechanic

Manifest truth test: paperwork, instruments, and physical inspection can disagree.

---

## Mission 4: Magic Carpet Recovery

**Primary Loop:** recover dead equipment.

### Objectives

1. Diagnose tractor failure.
2. Determine whether field repair is possible.
3. Prepare Magic Carpet.
4. Attach Tool.
5. Redistribute load.
6. Tow broken tractor to safe zone or mark for future recovery.

### Failure States

- Tool failure
- tow line break
- loss of critical cargo
- mechanic injury
- abandoned equipment unrecoverable

### Unique Mechanic

Cargo sacrifice board: every item abandoned affects later mission capability or crew morale.

---

## Mission 5: Track Bike Duty

**Primary Loop:** fast local route inspection.

### Objectives

1. Launch Track Bike from convoy.
2. Replace missing flags.
3. Verify special marker status.
4. Return before storm visibility drops.
5. Report discrepancy.

### Failure States

- battery depletion
- lost bike
- missing marker chain
- wrong flag color
- exposure injury

### Unique Mechanic

Local mobility versus vulnerability: Track Bikes move quickly but cannot survive the field like tractors.

---

## Mission 6: Dead Channel Debug

**Primary Loop:** signal investigation.

### Objectives

1. Detect ghost handshake.
2. Assign infrastructure monitor.
3. Trace dead channel.
4. Patch old interface.
5. Decode enough to identify a route hazard.
6. Decide whether to broadcast, report, or conceal.

### Failure States

- corrupted data
- signal overload
- Prime Ops comms lockout
- false route marker accepted
- old system shuts down

### Unique Mechanic

Signal trust meter: official systems, old infrastructure, and crew intuition produce competing truth streams.

---

## Mission 7: The Land Grew

**Primary Loop:** reroute after a Growth Event.

### Objectives

1. Wake after overnight crystal growth.
2. Locate buried flags.
3. Use GPR to re-evaluate path.
4. Clear pressure ridges or reroute.
5. Preserve enough equipment to continue.

### Failure States

- lost route
- blade damage
- buried Mod
- wrong pressure ridge estimate
- route closed too late

### Unique Mechanic

Terrain mutability: the map updates only after player verifies the field.

---

## Mission 8: Named Crack Memorial

**Primary Loop:** tabletop-style investigation mission.

### Objectives

1. Research a Named Crack.
2. Interview crew veterans.
3. Compare official hazard log to field memory.
4. Discover why the name matters.
5. Decide whether to preserve, report, rename, or erase.

### Failure States

- family harmed by disclosure
- useful hazard memory erased
- official retaliation
- wrong person memorialized

### Unique Mechanic

Memory evidence: oral history, unofficial maps, and route behavior are evidence types.

## Campaign Structure Option

A 12-mission campaign could follow a new MITE crew through one season, with side missions from Winter-Over, WWISSaRD, Ghostwaver, and Antisapien signal perspectives.

## Game Guardrail

Do not turn campaign progression into solving the Core. Let the campaign be about keeping people alive in a world where the official map is always one lie behind the land.
