# Project Green Glove: The Worldskin Prototype

**Status:** Sandbox Concept / Candidate Canon Seed / Non-Canon / Requires Canon Review Before Promotion  
**Department:** Meridian Prime World Building / World Works Development  
**Faction:** World Works Corp  
**Concept Type:** Solarpunk Planetary Conjugate Contact-Membrane Program

**Canon Risk:** High  
**Candidate Canon Status:** Hold in sandbox  

## Core Identity

Project Green Glove, formally called the **Worldskin Prototype**, is a World Works Corp black-development program: a solarpunk planetary Conjugate interface system designed in anticipation of finding the elusive Dry Gate.

It is not a wearable suit for a person. It is a **matter-world lander / field base** that unfolds on the Aeonos side of a suspected Dry Gate contact zone, then deploys a non-self-replicating, garden-like interface membrane over a limited contact patch.

World Works describes it as ethical observation and sustainable first-contact readiness.

The uglier translation:

```text
World Works built a greenhouse for an impossible doorway and called the lock a flower.
```

## Stable Concept

```text
Project Green Glove is a solarpunk planetary contact membrane: a sealed, non-self-replicating World Works interface wrap deployed over a limited àæonos-adjacent ecosystem so researchers can observe, translate, sample, and model its natural behaviors before future operations begin.
```

In crude but useful visual shorthand, it is a **planetary glovebox / contact condom / field-ecology wrap** for a dangerous anti-world interface. It is not meant to envelope all of àæonos in its current prototype stage. It is designed to cover a chamber, basin, grove, mineral vent, reef, ruin-pocket, wetland, fungus field, or other small ecosystem-scale sample patch.

World Works dreams of scaling it.

That dream is the problem.

## What It Is

The Worldskin Prototype has three layers.

### 1. The Lander / Base

A beautiful solarpunk World Works field station deployed on Aeonos / Meridian Prime near a suspected Dry Gate site.

Visual language:

```text
white ceramic ribs
amber solar glass membranes
sunleaf solar arrays
rootlike anchor legs
living moss filtration walls
algae-lit coolant channels
seed-pod sensor capsules
pearlescent contact tanks
waterless fountain-like cooling towers
soft green interior glow
sustainable research-retreat aesthetics
```

It looks like a garden-lab, ecological retreat, or humane first-contact station.

It is actually a pre-extraction platform.

### 2. The Contact Cradle

A sealed central chamber inside the base built around a suspected boundary event.

The Contact Cradle contains:

```text
amber glass pressure walls
solar membrane screens
ceramic restraint ribs
mirrored sensor pools
seed-pod samplers
rootlike stabilizer anchors
soft garden structures that are actually clamps
isolation curtains
field ecology observation decks
corporate safety glass everywhere
```

It is not a room for a person. It is a room for an impossible edge.

### 3. The Worldskin Membrane

The actual planetary Conjugate suit.

This is a deployable interface layer, not a self-replicating nanobot swarm.

Visual / functional language:

```text
solar-thread mesh
pearl ceramic nodes
photosynthetic panels
rootlike sensory tendrils
non-replicating seed pods
contact-safe petals that open and close
sampling blisters
signal veins
soft green glow that becomes increasingly wrong
translucent membrane tension
wrap-like field ecology envelope
```

The membrane attempts to create a local contact-safe “skin” over a small patch of àæonos-adjacent contact space.

## What It Is Not

Project Green Glove is not:

```text
not a self-replicating nanobot cloud
not gray goo
not a ship that physically lands on àæonos
not a normal mining craft
not a wearable person-suit
not a confirmed working Dry Gate technology
not proof of what the Dry Gate really is
not proof of what àæonos ecosystems are truly like
```

World Works has a model.

The model may be beautiful.

The model may also be wrong in every way that matters.

## Core Horror

On paper:

```text
ethical pre-operation ecological observation
```

In practice:

```text
a beautiful solarpunk contact membrane that can study an àæonos-side ecosystem without immediate annihilation
```

In truth:

```text
a prototype that teaches World Works how to convert a living place into a managed asset
```

The horror is not that the system instantly destroys the sample.

The horror is that it preserves the sample while making the sample capturable.

Core line:

```text
World Works did not build the membrane to kill the ecosystem. They built it to learn where the ecosystem keeps its handles.
```

## Risk Stack

The Worldskin Prototype is dangerous because:

```text
the membrane may alter the behavior it claims to observe
the ecosystem may adapt faster than World Works can model it
the membrane may mistake response for consent
the membrane may mistake silence for permission
the membrane may preserve life while making escape impossible
World Works may learn the wrong lesson from contaminated observations
the sampled zone may treat observers as part of its weather, memory, food chain, or immune response
the prototype may become a false sanctuary
```

Best thematic failure:

```text
It can create an interface, but it cannot recognize refusal.
```

Another:

```text
It was built to touch a planet without learning how to be touched back.
```
