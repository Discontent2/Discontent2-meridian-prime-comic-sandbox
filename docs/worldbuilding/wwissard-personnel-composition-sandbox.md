## Purpose

This file clarifies visual and story assumptions around WWISSaRD personnel, especially in scenes involving Ildi Vey's Conjugate Chimera creation-horror backstory.

## Core Clarification

There is no mandate that WWISSaRD scientists, technicians, observers, handlers, contractors, or support staff must all be Meridian Prime humans.

Because WWISSaRD originates from a Meridian Prime human faction / institutional network, the majority of its personnel will naturally be human. That majority should shape the institution's culture, language, paperwork, authority posture, funding channels, and default assumptions.

However, WWISSaRD can plausibly include non-human personnel when the story calls for it.

## Personnel Composition Guidance

### Default Majority

Most WWISSaRD staff should be Meridian Prime humans because of the institution's origin, recruitment base, political structures, and factional channels.

This is the default visual expectation in ordinary WWISSaRD labs, offices, field teams, and bureaucratic settings.

### Non-Human Presence Is Allowed

WWISSaRD may also include:

- Antisapien scientists, technicians, interpreters, consultants, or coerced specialists;
- Aeonolacertian researchers, observers, technical consultants, defectors, prisoners, exchange personnel, or constrained collaborators;
- other species or faction-linked specialists where their biology, perception, technology access, or cultural knowledge is useful;
- contractors, subcontractors, captured experts, black-budget consultants, or deniable personnel from outside the core human faction.

Non-human WWISSaRD personnel should not imply that the entire institution is non-human or evenly multi-species. Their presence should usually indicate specialized need, political compromise, coercion, collaboration, curiosity, opportunism, or institutional reach.

## Ildi Backstory Application

In Ildi Vey's creation-horror backstory, WWISSaRD / WWISSaRD-adjacent facilities may include mostly human staff, but it is valid for the scene to include non-human scientists or observers if they serve the story.

Possible reasons non-human staff may appear in Ildi's Bi-Phase Integration context:

- an Antisapien technician understands signal-band biology better than human staff;
- an Aeonolacertian observer can perceive Conjugate Chimera scar biofluorescence without blacklight;
- a non-human consultant is present because human instrumentation cannot interpret the phenomenon cleanly;
- a specialist is coerced, compromised, or working under institutional pressure;
- WWISSaRD is using external expertise while preserving human institutional control.

## Visual Development Note

Do not visually imply that WWISSaRD is exclusively staffed by reptilian, Antisapien, or other non-human personnel unless a specific story scene requires an unusual unit.

Also do not visually imply that WWISSaRD is human-only.

The strongest default image is:

> A human-majority Meridian Prime institution with enough non-human specialists, contractors, and observers to make its complicity feel wider, stranger, and more politically uncomfortable.

## Horror Tone Use

Mixed personnel can make WWISSaRD horror more unsettling when used carefully.

The horror is not that non-human people are present.

The horror is that many kinds of people can learn to speak institutional language when power, funding, fear, curiosity, or survival pressure teaches them how.

Good use:

- a human administrator controls the file while a non-human specialist understands the biological danger;
- an Aeonolacertian consultant sees scar-light before the humans can instrument it;
- an Antisapien technician looks away because they understand too much;
- a mixed staff lets the scene imply distributed complicity without making any species inherently villainous.

Bad use:

- making all scientists non-human by default;
- making non-human staff visually monstrous to signal evil;
- implying WWISSaRD's crimes are caused by species identity rather than institutional power;
- erasing the human-majority origin of the faction.

## Final Principle

**WWISSaRD is human-majority by origin and institutional gravity, but not human-only by rule. Its labs can include non-human specialists when the story needs expertise, coercion, ambiguity, or wider complicity.**
