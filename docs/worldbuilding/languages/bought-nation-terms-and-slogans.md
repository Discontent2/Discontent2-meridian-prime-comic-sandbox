## “Bought, Not Bot”

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

The near-misreading of **Bought Nation** as **Bot Nation** should be used carefully.

### Bot Nation

Outside slur or lazy mispronunciation.

Implications:

- corporate-programmed
- disposable labor
- contract drones
- work-unit people
- settlement machinery
- not fully political subjects

### Bought Nation Response

**Bought, not bot. Bought means somebody paid. Somebody owes.**

This slogan turns insult into debt logic.

It says:

- World Works paid for the settlement compact.
- Earth failed to remember its people.
- someone owes recognition, land, and dignity.
- bought status is evidence of obligation, not lack of humanity.

## Prime Ops Language

**Political Development**

Prime Ops tends to speak operationally rather than ideologically.

Likely terms:

- Settlement Base
- compact districts
- route families
- settlement crews
- highland labor pool
- evacuation population
- snowfield support households
- descent support families

Prime Ops can sound dehumanizing without intending cruelty.

Possible Prime Ops phrase:

**Settlement Base must remain supplied before the wet lock.**

Meaning:

People may freeze or starve if the route fails.
