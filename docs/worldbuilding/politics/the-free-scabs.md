## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file develops **The Free Scabs**, a roaming expat / outlaw compact operating in the scabland basin territories around the Dry Circuit.

They are not a generic bandit gang.

They are what every Meridian Prime civilization throws away, organized enough to throw something back.

---

## Core Concept

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Free Scabs are a wasteland / scabland basin-based outlaw compact made of defectors, fugitives, ruined contractors, rejected gate-runners, Hydropolis exiles, Craton oath-breakers, Bought Nation runaways, World Works deserters, and Traverse ghosts.

They roam the exposed basin when the water is low and vanish when the seasons turn.

They raid, trade, smuggle, rescue, sabotage, guide, blackmail, and disappear.

They are dangerous because they are partly right.

---

## Name Meaning

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

**The Free Scabs** carries two meanings at once.

### Scabland Meaning

They come from scabland terrain:

- flood-scoured basin scars
- cracked seasonal crust
- exposed ancient seabeds
- hydrocrystal scabfields
- dry river wounds
- old flood channels
- places where water carved the world down to bone

### Labor Meaning

**Scab** is also a labor insult, especially in a world shaped by The Bought Nation, World Works, Prime Ops, NCI, contractor houses, lease labor, and route work.

The name can mean strikebreaker, labor traitor, or worker who left collective struggle.

The Free Scabs reclaim the accusation.

Possible saying:

**Better a scab than a wound that never closes.**

## Internal Factions

### 1. The Compact Teeth

**Political Development**

The political core of The Free Scab Compact.

They want the Free Scabs to become an outlaw polity with codes, territory, and recognition.

### 2. The Dust Tithe

**Development Apocrypha**

Raiders who believe every convoy owes the basin payment.

They are most likely to turn the Free Scabs into pure banditry.

### 3. The Dead Channel Choir

**Contradictory Account / Development Apocrypha**

Hydropolis exiles who navigate by old signals and sell forbidden route intelligence.

They hear maps where others hear static.

### 4. The Gate-Broken

**Religious Tradition / Political Development**

Craton exiles who enforce harsh internal law and protect headquarters thresholds.

Their threshold discipline keeps the Free Scabs from dissolving into chaos.

### 5. The Rust Clerks

**Corporate Distortion / Development Apocrypha**

World Works deserters, burned analysts, and stolen-ledger specialists.

They forge permits, route seals, ownership documents, manifests, and corporate ghosts.

### 6. The Soft Hands

**Contradictory Account / Mythic Development**

A secret humanitarian wing that rescues abandoned workers, lost children, stranded migrants, and people left behind by official route closures.

Some deny they exist.

Some insist the Soft Hands are the only reason the Free Scabs have a soul left.

## Relationship to the Dry Circuit

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Free Scabs are the unofficial fifth hazard of the Dry Circuit.

The official hazards are terrain, signal, permit, season, and threshold.

The Free Scabs are what happens when all of those abandon people long enough for them to organize.

Possible route status label:

**Free Scab Activity: Mobile / Unconfirmed / Assume Witnessed**

---

## Relationship to The Bought Nation

**Political Development**

The Bought Nation has a painful relationship with The Free Scabs.

Some see them as traitors who abandoned collective struggle.

Some see them as what happens when the Compact fails its own children.

Some Reclaimers secretly coordinate with them.

Some Reclaimers hate them because Scab raids make Bought Nation autonomy look criminal.

The Free Scabs mock Bought Nation restraint but still recruit from its wounds.

## Relationship to Hydropolis

**Political Development / Scientific Hypothesis**

Hydropolis considers The Free Scabs a signal and identity problem.

They carry:

- false records
- broken civic identities
- stolen seals
- unregistered movement histories
- dead-channel traffic
- exiled signal expertise

Hydropolis may want certain Free Scab signal exiles returned.

It may also secretly use them when official witness routes cannot go where they need to go.

---

## Relationship to the Craton

**Religious Tradition / Political Development**

The Craton may see The Free Scabs as guestless movement: people without lawful threshold, oath, gate, or proper crossing.

Craton exiles within the Free Scabs create special tension.

The Gate-Broken may preserve fragments of Craton discipline while violating the very gate-law that gave those disciplines meaning.

---

## Relationship to World Works and Prime Ops

**Corporate Distortion / Political Development**

World Works publicly treats The Free Scabs as criminal interference.

Privately, certain departments may use them for deniable movement, stolen cargo recovery, controlled leaks, or anti-Reclaimer pressure.

Prime Ops treats them as route threats but has likely bought their help in emergencies.

This should create moral dirt under every clean uniform.
