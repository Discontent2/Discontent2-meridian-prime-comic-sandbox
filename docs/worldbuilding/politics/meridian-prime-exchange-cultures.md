## Core Principle

**Every civilization pays with the thing it trusts most.**

| Civilization / Space | Primary Exchange Form | Core Trust |
|---|---|---|
| **Prime Ops / Human Escarpment Logistics** | **Scrip / Fiat Issue** | decree, logistics authority, depot backing |
| **Anticlinal Craton / Aeonolacertian Sovereignty** | **Jewels / Pressure Value** | pressure, time, sacred weight, geological memory |
| **Hydropolis / Antisapien Civic-Signal State** | **Lumen / Civic Lattice Exchange** | witnessed origin, signal trust, civic continuity |
| **The Bought Nation** | **Receipts** | proof of labor, survival, injury, settlement, and obligation |

Keeper line:

**Prime Ops trusts decree. The Craton trusts pressure. Hydropolis trusts verification. The Bought Nation trusts proof. The unofficial markets trust weight.**

# The Meridian Exchange Standard

Because Meridian Prime's major societies use radically different exchange systems, cross-border trade requires neutral conversion customs.

## Bar-Weight

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A physical exchange standard used between settlements, convoys, ports, border markets, and unofficial exchanges.

The Bar-Weight consists of standardized, notched rods made from dense industrial alloy. Each rod is marked with visible weight cuts, authority stamps, route-origin marks, and inspection scars. In frontier exchange, smaller segments may be separated along pre-notched divisions.

Use:

- inter-settlement trade
- convoy settlement
- port exchange
- emergency barter between groups that distrust each other's domestic money
- neutral-value transactions where scrip, jewels, Lumen, or receipts are contested

Cultural reading:

- Prime Ops treats Bar-Weight as a regulated conversion object.
- The Craton treats Bar-Weight as crude but honest.
- Hydropolis treats Bar-Weight as origin-poor and therefore limited.
- The Bought Nation treats Bar-Weight as useful but politically mute.
- Unofficial markets like Bar-Weight because it does not ask questions.

Best line:

**A Bar-Weight does not care who issued the promise. It only asks what the promise weighs.**

## Telex

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A trilateral ledger standard used for large cross-civilization settlements without moving heavy physical goods each time.

It is not trusted equally by all parties.

Use:

- fuel-for-food accounting
- Hydropolis port exchange
- Craton jewel equivalence records
- Prime Ops convoy settlement
- Bought Nation receipt recognition disputes
- high-value route obligations

Cultural reading:

- Prime Ops sees Telex as administrative necessity.
- Hydropolis sees Telex as incomplete unless witnessed through the Civic Lattice.
- The Craton sees Telex as thin unless backed by proper offering or jewel relation.
- The Bought Nation sees Telex as dangerous unless receipts are attached.
- Unofficial markets treat Telex as a last resort.

Best line:

**Telex records what moved. It does not always record what was owed.**

# 1. Prime Ops Scrip / Fiat Issue

**Political Development / Candidate Canon Seed**

Prime Ops uses fiat because its economy is built on logistics control, depot access, route authorization, payroll, ration pricing, fuel distribution, and institutional backing.

## Fuel-Scrip

Primary Prime Ops exchange form.

Fuel-Scrip began as rationing paper for diesel, fuel access, and depot-controlled necessities. Over time, it became the standard human escarpment money-form because every road, machine, camp, and checkpoint eventually needs fuel.

Associated forms:

- **Fuel-Scrip** — standard Prime Ops fiat issue
- **Depot Notes** — local depot issue
- **Rations** — working-class slang for low-value paper money
- **Route Tender** — formal inter-depot payment term
- **Gears / Sprockets** — stamped steel pieces used for small industrial purchases and worker transport
- **Bullions** — serialized lead-jacketed industrial reserve blocks used for major procurement
- **Emergency Issue Slips** — temporary paper authorizations during route crisis

Prime Ops view:

**This has value because the operating authority recognizes it.**

Best line:

**Prime Ops scrip does not ask whether you believe in it. It asks whether you need fuel.**

# 2. Anticlinal Craton Jewels / Pressure Value

**Religious Tradition / Candidate Canon Seed**

The Craton uses jewels because value is understood through pressure, endurance, relation, lineage, and lawful placement.

Craton jewels are not simply decorative wealth. Their value depends on formation, provenance, pressure history, and social relation.

## Pressure Gems

Primary Craton exchange form.

Associated forms:

- **Pressure Gems** — standard high-trust jewel value
- **Geode Pearls** — interior-grown value objects
- **Gate-Jewels** — used in threshold exchange
- **Weight-Stones** — heavier value stones used in formal settlement
- **Threshold Facets** — guest-right exchange pieces
- **Red Glass Facets** — possibly tied to Red Glass Geodeum traditions
- **Ancestral Chips** — inherited fragments with lineage significance
- **Gauge-Plates** — heavy etched plates used for high-value Craton industrial exchange
- **Pressure-Chits** — stamped access pieces for pressure-vault work, heat, or controlled machinery access
- **Calors** — theoretical energy-value unit used by Craton engineers
- **Slag-Bits** — low-value foundry fragments accepted in outer workshops

Craton view:

**This has value because it endured pressure correctly and remains within lawful relation.**

Best line:

**A Craton jewel is not valuable because it shines. It is valuable because it endured pressure correctly.**

# 3. Hydropolis Lumen / Civic Lattice Exchange

**Scientific Hypothesis / Political Development**

Hydropolis uses signal-verified civic exchange because its civic life depends on identity, origin record, witnessed transfer, and continuity.

This should not feel like ordinary human finance. It is a Hydropolis civic-signal value system.

## Lumen

Primary Hydropolis exchange unit.

A Lumen represents verified access priority inside the city's optical and signal infrastructure. It is less like a physical object and more like witnessed civic permission measured through the Lattice.

Associated forms:

- **Lumen** — primary Hydropolis unit
- **Civic Lattice** — the verified exchange network
- **Signal Scrip** — low-value civic issue
- **Verified Drops** — small authenticated payments
- **Mirror Marks** — cross-basin exchange marks
- **Origin Marks** — transaction-origin tags
- **Black-Glass Ledgers** — deep civic record systems
- **Wet Keys** — seasonal access and exchange credentials
- **Shards** — encrypted physical data-chips used when Lattice access is interrupted or intentionally avoided
- **Flux** — volatile value class adjusted by real-time resource, signal, and civic load conditions
- **Nodes** — large-scale wealth clusters tied to civic institutions, families, towers, or regulated syndicates

Hydropolis view:

**This has value because its origin and transfer were witnessed, verified, and preserved.**

Best line:

**In Hydropolis, money does not ask how much you have. It asks who witnessed it becoming yours.**

# 4. The Bought Nation Receipts

**Political Development / Candidate Canon Seed**

The Bought Nation uses receipts because its economy grows out of labor, injury, survival, erased promises, and proof of settlement.

Receipts are not merely paperwork. They are survival evidence.

## Echo Receipts

Primary Bought Nation exchange form.

Associated forms:

- **Echo Receipts** — proof that erased labor still speaks
- **Work Receipts** — labor proof
- **Bread Slips** — ration and food entitlement proof
- **Injury Receipts** — workplace harm records
- **Birth Ledgers** — belonging and settlement proof
- **Route Loss Claims** — route death or loss records
- **Meal Stamps** — low-value food exchange
- **Settlement Proofs** — land and habitation evidence
- **First Receipt Relics** — ceremonial proof objects tied to founding memory
- **Black Ledger Marks** — unofficial receipt annotations used by Reclaimers and hidden clerks

Bought Nation view:

**This has value because someone owes us for what happened.**

Best line:

**The Bought Nation pays with proof because proof is the only homeland Earth left them.**

# 5. Unofficial Market Bar-Weight / Hard Gears

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

Unofficial markets favor physical exchange because it is portable, immediate, and less dependent on institutions.

## Deadweight Bars

Primary large-value unofficial exchange form.

Associated forms:

- **Deadweight Bars** — heavy Bar-Weight segments used in high-risk exchange
- **Hard Gears** — durable stamped metal pieces used in smaller market trade
- **Slough Rounds** — regional stamped metal pieces used in the Eschar Slough
- **Cut-Gears** — divided metal pieces used for fractional payment
- **Storm Silver** — wet-season trade metal
- **Bone-Marked Gears** — metal pieces with local reputation marks
- **No-Face Gears** — anonymous market pieces used where names are dangerous
- **Red Table Bars** — medical-market Bar-Weight segments accepted around the Red Table

Unofficial market view:

**No archive. No promise. Just weight.**

Free Scab line:

**Scrip is worth what the checkpoint remembers. A Bar-Weight is worth what your hand can close around.**

Best line:

**Hard metal is what people use when they do not trust scrip, signal, jewel, receipt, oath, or god.**

# Secondary Exchange Distrust

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Each civilization may accept other forms of exchange, but with suspicion.

| Exchange Form | Who Distrusts It Most | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Fuel-Scrip** | Free Scabs, Craton, Reclaimers | imposed value, vulnerable to institutional refusal |
| **Pressure Gems** | Prime Ops, World Works | difficult to standardize, context-heavy, culturally loaded |
| **Lumen** | Bought Nation, Free Scabs | requires infrastructure, identity trust, and verified origin |
| **Echo Receipts** | World Works, Prime Ops bureaucracy | politically explosive proof of obligation |
| **Bar-Weight / Hard Gears** | Hydropolis, Prime Ops | physical and origin-light |
| **Telex entries** | Free Scabs, Reclaimers, Craton traditionalists | too dependent on who controls the record |
