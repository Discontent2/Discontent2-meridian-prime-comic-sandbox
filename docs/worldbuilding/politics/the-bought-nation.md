## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document develops **The Bought Nation**, a rejected / forgotten migrant human polity on the Meridian Prime Escarpment Continent.

It gives the human Meridian settlement sphere a deeper political wound:

- Earth forgot them.
- World Works counted them.
- Prime Ops kept them alive.
- NCI and other contractors kept the work moving.
- Their own people remembered themselves into nationhood.

---

## Core Concept

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Bought Nation is not a fully sovereign state.

It is a migrant settlement polity whose people were brought to the Meridian Prime Escarpment under settlement contracts, survival promises, labor obligations, debt instruments, and World Works ownership clauses.

They were not dramatically exiled by Earth.

They were forgotten by Earth.

Not hated enough to remember.

Not valued enough to retrieve.

Filed outward, shipped onward, contractually transformed into a settlement labor base, and gradually erased from the larger human story.

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Bought Nation is the forgotten migrant people of the Escarpment, purchased into survival by World Works, maintained by Prime Ops, worked by NCI and contractor houses, and still trying to become a nation no ledger can own.

## Origin Wound: Earth Forgot Them

**Mythic Development / Political Development**

The Bought Nation's origin should not be a simple exile story.

It is more devastating if the founding wound is abandonment disguised as opportunity.

Possible historical sequence:

1. Migrant populations are recruited, relocated, displaced, or economically trapped into settlement programs.
2. Earth-side oversight fades.
3. World Works absorbs settlement logistics into corporate management.
4. Original migration promises become charter language, then ledger language, then archive fragments.
5. Children born on the Escarpment inherit obligations they did not sign.
6. The people realize no outside polity is coming to remember them properly.
7. They begin calling themselves **The Bought Nation**.

Core line:

**Earth forgot us. World Works counted us. We remembered ourselves.**

## The “Bought / Bot” Tension

**Development Apocrypha**

**Bought Nation** can be cruelly misconstrued as **Bot Nation** by outsiders.

This works best as a slur or careless mispronunciation, not the self-name.

Outsiders may use **Bot Nation** to imply Compact people are:

- corporate-programmed
- disposable labor
- settlement machinery
- work-unit people
- contract drones
- not fully political subjects

The Bought Nation may answer:

**Bought, not bot. Bought means somebody paid. Somebody owes.**

This distinction should matter emotionally and politically.

## The Legal Trap

**Corporate Distortion / Political Development**

The Bought Nation should not be written as simple slavery.

That would flatten the horror.

The stronger model is legal entanglement:

- inherited lease dependency
- contract-bound housing
- corporate fuel access
- restricted movement through safety policy
- employment tied to survival infrastructure
- debt instruments framed as opportunity
- corporate-controlled external recognition
- settlement councils permitted but limited
- citizenship without independent gate control

World Works can say:

**No one is owned. Every obligation is contractual.**

The Bought Nation hears:

**A cage with receipts is still a cage.**

## Social Classes and Identity Groups

### 1. Charter Families

**Political Development**

Descendants of first migrant settlers.

They keep old documents proving promises World Works never fully honored.

They may preserve settlement charters like scripture.

### 2. Route Families

**Development Apocrypha**

Families tied to Traverse work, NCI, repair yards, descent labor, and Dry Circuit survival.

They are respected because everyone knows the settlements die without them.

### 3. Leaseborn

**Corporate Distortion / Political Development**

People born into World Works housing, debt systems, or employment-dependent infrastructure.

They are technically citizens of the Compact but practically inherit obligations.

### 4. Free Yarders

**Development Apocrypha**

Independent mechanics, haulers, cooks, medics, salvage crews, black-market suppliers, and informal route workers who live in the gaps.

Prime Ops distrusts them.

NCI likely knows where to find them.

### 5. Paper Citizens

**Corporate Distortion**

People legally counted as Compact members but physically trapped in remote camps, snow stations, work blocks, or lease settlements.

They exist more clearly in ledgers than in politics.

### 6. Reclaimers

**Political Development**

A political faction that wants true land ownership, treaty recognition, independent route authority, and the end of corporate settlement dependency.

World Works calls them destabilizers.

Prime Ops may call them a route-security concern.

NCI families may be divided over them.

## Relationships to Major Powers

### World Works Corp

**Corporate Distortion / Political Development**

World Works owns or controls the settlement framework.

It may celebrate Bought Nation culture publicly while preventing true sovereignty privately.

Official phrase:

**Meridian Human Settlement Compact.**

Translation:

**A nation leased back to itself one generation at a time.**

### Prime Ops

**Political Development**

Prime Ops is not purely villainous.

Prime Ops personnel may genuinely keep Compact people alive by running emergency convoys, maintaining snowfield supply systems, repairing roads, and organizing evacuations.

But Prime Ops also enforces the infrastructure of dependency.

Best tension:

**Prime Ops saves the Compact every season and cages it every day.**

### NCI and Contractor Houses

**Development Apocrypha**

NCI is crucial because it gives the Bought Nation human texture.

NCI may be one of the few contractor houses trusted by Route Families because its workers live among them, marry into them, repair their homes, and know the old lies.

NCI is not outside the system.

It profits from contracts too.

But it also preserves field memory World Works cannot fully buy.

Possible NCI saying:

**A contract does not know who carried the wrench.**

### Prime Plateau / The National Park

**Political Development**

The Prime Plateau becomes a major pressure point for the Bought Nation.

The Bought Nation needs access because the plateau is:

- descent hinge
- work zone
- refuge
- cloudforest resource
- repair corridor
- local home
- cultural borderland
- place where local knowledge beats corporate maps

World Works calls it protected.

Bought Nation people may hear:

**protected from us.**

### Hydropolis

**Contradictory Account / Political Development**

Hydropolis may see Bought Nation identity as legally injured because corporate contracts interfere with personhood, movement, records, and recognition.

Possible Hydropolis term:

**The Leased People.**

Hydropolis sympathy may not equal open support.

It may recognize the wound while refusing to let corporate identity contamination enter Hydropolis systems unchecked.

### Anticlinal Craton

**Religious Tradition / Contradictory Account**

The Craton may see the Bought Nation as a people without a true gate.

Possible Craton term:

**The Nation Without Its Own Gate.**

This is harsh but not necessarily contemptuous.

To the Craton, sovereignty means controlling thresholds.

If World Works owns the gates, roads, fuel, charters, and permissions, the Bought Nation's nationhood is wounded but not meaningless.
