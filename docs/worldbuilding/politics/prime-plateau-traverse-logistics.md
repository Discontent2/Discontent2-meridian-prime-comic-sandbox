## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file develops the Prime Plateau as the operational hinge between Prime Ops and the Dry Circuit.

It focuses on logistics, route control, convoy staging, seasonal shutdowns, and political disputes.

The Prime Plateau is not just geography.

It is where every descent becomes a decision.

---

## Core Logistics Principle

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Prime Ops can launch a convoy.

The Prime Plateau decides whether that convoy should continue.

The canyon system enforces the answer.

---

## Operational Role

**Development Apocrypha**

The Prime Plateau functions as:

- mid-elevation staging shelf
- brake and engine cooldown zone
- refuel and coolant depot
- cargo transfer yard
- descent-rated vehicle checkpoint
- end-of-shift camp cluster
- storm shelter zone
- rescue staging area
- route-status decision point
- first major seasonal warning platform
- jurisdictional choke point between high escarpment and lower basin

It gives Traverse logistics a necessary intermediate layer between Prime Ops and the Mirror Basin.

---

## Convoy Procedure

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A standard dry-season convoy descending toward the Dry Circuit may follow this rhythm:

1. Launch from Prime Ops.
2. Enter the Great Cut or major canyon descent system.
3. Reach the Prime Plateau.
4. Perform brake, engine, track, and cargo checks.
5. Confirm weather, GPR, Hydropolis signal status, and lower route conditions.
6. Split, delay, refit, or proceed.
7. Pass through lower descent gates.
8. Enter Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit lowlands.

This makes the descent a multi-stage operation rather than a single route segment.

- convoy readiness

**Development Apocrypha**

### Local Plateau Settlements

**Political Development**

The Prime Plateau may include settlements that are neither fully Prime Ops nor fully World Works controlled.

Priority:

- water rights
- fuel-cache control
- storm shelter rights
- descent tolls or service fees
- employment from convoys

### Hydropolis Witnesses

**Political Development**

Hydropolis may want monitoring rights on the plateau because seasonal route decisions affect signal safety and Dry Circuit access.

Priority:

- signal behavior before descent
- hidden survey detection
- cargo identity consistency
- Dry Season Witnessing
- early warning of Dry Port paranoia

Possible tension:

Prime Ops may see this as interference before the convoy has even reached Hydropolis jurisdiction.

### Craton Observers

**Religious Tradition / Political Development**

Priority:

- pressure behavior
- machine weight
- sacred geology
- forbidden mapping
- guest-right preparation

Possible tension:

World Works may claim the plateau is human logistics territory while the Craton reads part of the canyon as pressure-relevant land.

## Route Conflicts

**Political Development**

The Prime Plateau creates several recurring conflicts:

1. **Launch vs. Continue**
   Prime Ops may launch a convoy, but plateau crews may recommend stopping.

2. **Paper Clearance vs. Field Clearance**
   World Works documentation may say the route is viable while local field crews say the Cut is not taking weight.

3. **Cargo Classification vs. Descent Reality**
   Cargo legal enough for the plateau may be unsafe for the lower canyon.

4. **Hydropolis Signal Concern vs. Prime Ops Jurisdiction**
   Hydropolis witnesses may flag cargo before it reaches Hydropolis territory.

5. **Craton Pressure Concern vs. Human Route Need**
   Craton observers may object to machine weight or survey behavior long before the convoy reaches the Maw.

6. **Local Survival Rights vs. Corporate Control**
   Plateau settlements may resist being absorbed into World Works operational geometry.

## Story Hooks

### 1. The Plateau Says No

**Political Development**

A convoy has Prime Ops clearance, but plateau mechanics and local route-watch crews refuse to certify the lower descent.

The clock is running.

### 2. The Clean Survey Annex

**Corporate Distortion / Suppressed Archive**

World Works opens a spotless plateau survey annex under the language of safety stabilization.

NCI mechanics notice its antennas point at the wrong part of the canyon.

### 3. Rob's Old Brake Marks

**Development Apocrypha**

An old descent record or machine scar connected to Rob / Tenet route history survives on the plateau.

It does not solve Rob's death, but it can make the route feel emotionally haunted.

### 4. The Hydropolis Witness Before the Basin

**Political Development**

A Hydropolis witness flags a signal problem on the plateau, before the convoy has entered Hydropolis-controlled space.

Prime Ops argues jurisdiction.

The signal keeps changing.

### 5. The Craton Observer at the Shelf

**Religious Tradition / Contradictory Account**

A Craton observer appears on the plateau because a cargo's weight, vibration, or route angle is disturbing pressure markers far below.

No Meridian map shows such a connection.

The Craton map does.
