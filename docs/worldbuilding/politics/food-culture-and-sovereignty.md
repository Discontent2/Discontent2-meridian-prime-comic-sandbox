## Core Principle

**Candidate Canon Seed**

On Meridian Prime, food is never just food.

It is proof that something survived the last season and intends to charge rent.

A crop can be:

- ration
- medicine
- tax base
- treaty gift
- contraband
- poison
- festival food
- sacred threshold object
- survivor proof
- evidence of land claim

## Food and The Bought Nation

**Political Development**

The Bought Nation treats food as proof of labor, settlement, and owed belonging.

Core political claim:

**If our dead are in the road and our crops are in the soil, who says this land is leased?**

Important foods:

- **Cloudwood Chestnuts** — staple flour and Charterhold ration base.
- **Mossroot Tubers** — survival starch and emergency plateau food.
- **Dust Lentils** — highland protein ration.
- **Riverbed Squash** — drawdown crop from seasonal silt.
- **Fog Pear Cider** — comfort drink and political gathering staple.
- **Channel Lentils** — recovery crop after wet retreat.

### Cultural Uses

Bought Nation food culture may emphasize:

- ration cakes
- chestnut flour breads
- preserved fog fruit
- descent-stew served before dangerous routes
- flood-retreat harvest feasts
- Reclaimer meals built from locally claimed crops
- memorial meals for workers lost on routes

### Political Uses

Food disputes can include:

- World Works claiming cloudforest harvest rights
- Prime Ops controlling ration storage
- local councils arguing over harvest during route closures
- Reclaimers using crop fields as evidence of national claim
- Ledger Falls logging threatening Cloudwood Chestnut groves
- Charterhold archive meals reenacting first migrant survival

Possible saying:

**The charter promised land. The chestnuts proved we stayed.**

## Food and Hydropolis

**Political Development / Scientific Hypothesis**

Hydropolis treats food through civic ecology, signal law, verified harvest, and record continuity.

Important foods:

- **Mirror Lentils** — common protein staple.
- **Signal Kelp Ribbons** — edible only after clearance.
- **Moor-Nuts** — festival food.
- **Glasswake Plums** — lawful export and diplomatic gift.
- **Moor-Lily Fruit** — regulated civic crop.
- **Canal Greens** — verified water crop.

### Cultural Uses

Hydropolis food culture may emphasize:

- verified harvest lines
- civic crop registries
- signal-cleared kelp
- festival Moor-Nuts
- tower-shadow fruit markets
- protected nurseries near food sources
- food as identity-safe matter

### Political Uses

Food disputes can include:

- unverified food entering Hydropolis channels
- black-market Signal Nuts or Drift Beans
- Archive Mold contamination of food storage
- Free Scab piracy of wet-season food barges
- World Works mislabeling civic food zones as removable bio-obstruction

Hydropolis principle:

**Food without verified origin is a record risk.**

Possible civic phrase:

**Not weed. Witness. Not fruit. Record.**

## Food and The Free Scabs

**Development Apocrypha / Political Development**

The Free Scabs treat food as survival, threat, contraband, leverage, and dark hospitality.

Important foods:

- **Pirate Beans** — hardy smuggler crop.
- **Scab Yams** — dry-season survival starch.
- **Blackwater Figs** — fermented into Free Scab grog.
- **Blackwake Nuts** — mangrove staple.
- **Reef Lentils** — wet-season channel food.
- **False Harbor Fruit** — toxic warning / lure marker.
- **Bride Lantern Seeds** — disturbing folklore object tied to Corpse Lantern Brides.

### Cultural Uses

Free Scab food culture may emphasize:

- ugly foods outsiders refuse
- shipboard stews
- stolen spice
- fermented blackwater fruit
- ration barter in The Throat
- food as password
- poisoned lookalikes as intimidation
- Soft Hands rescue meals, if they exist

### Political Uses

Food disputes can include:

- stealing wet-season barges
- smuggling Signal Kelp Ribbons
- growing Pirate Beans in hidden Slough plots
- using False Harbor Fruit as a warning or trap marker
- hostage meals that blur hospitality and threat
- Free Scabs trading safe food knowledge for passage

Free Scab line:

**Eat ugly. Live long. Trust no glowing fruit.**

## Toxic Lookalikes as Cultural Danger

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Toxic lookalikes should be major story tools because Meridian Prime landscapes change by season.

Examples:

- Lanternberries vs Bridefruit
- Fog Almonds vs Green Hell Widow-Nuts
- Dust Melons vs False Watermelons
- safe Signal Kelp Ribbons vs Latch Kelp Shoots
- Blackwater Figs vs False Harbor Fruit
- Channelnuts vs Bonewhite Nuts

Cultural role:

- children learn lookalike lessons early
- guides test apprentices on lookalike knowledge
- outsiders die from confident ignorance
- World Works labels may be technically correct and practically insufficient
- Free Scabs weaponize misidentification

Possible saying:

**The planet does not punish hunger. It punishes certainty.**
