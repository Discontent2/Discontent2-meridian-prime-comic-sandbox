## Purpose

Develop a safe fictional physics language for Conjugate Contact Frames / Mirror-Shells.

This file does not replace canon.

It exists to clarify how Contact Frames can feel technologically rich while preserving the locked rule:

**A Contact Frame does not make antimatter safe. It prevents contact.**

## Core Model

Contact Frames are not suits.

They are mobile non-contact environments that allow true Àæonos-origin visitors to operate in matter-side environments through mediated systems.

A Contact Frame is closer to:

- quarantine room
- pressure lock
- diplomatic vessel
- sensor body
- containment shell
- hazard-rated prosthetic city
- walking embassy
- sacred guest architecture

Core line:

**A Contact Frame is a body-shaped border treaty.**

## Frame Layer Model

A Contact Frame may be described as a layered system:

### 1. Visitor Interior

The sealed conjugate-origin occupancy environment.

Never casually shown or opened.

Story function:

The impossible guest remains physically separated from matter reality.

### 2. Null-Contact Layer

The primary non-contact barrier.

Candidate canon vocabulary:

**null-contact layer**

Function:

Prevents matter / conjugate-life contact.

Failure mode:

Immediate catastrophic breach risk.

### 3. Topological Isolation Lattice

Fictional boundary-control system that maintains shell integrity as a boundary condition rather than ordinary armor strength.

Candidate canon vocabulary:

**topological isolation**

Function:

Keeps the contact boundary coherent under motion, load, signal translation, docking, and environmental stress.

Failure mode:

Boundary inversion, edge leak, false enclosure, split shell.

### 4. Translation Membrane

Sensor, signal, pressure, and motor translation layer.

Function:

Lets the visitor perceive, communicate with, and manipulate matter-side environments without physical contact.

Failure mode:

Identity lag, gesture delay, voice phase error, false touch, sensory burn.

### 5. Matter-Side Actuator Body

The visible Frame body that interacts with the world.

Function:

Walks, speaks, touches tools, docks, signs agreements, and performs lawful guest activity.

Failure mode:

Actuator lock, etiquette freeze, motor echo, breach posture.

### 6. Civic / Legal Shell

Hydropolis cultural, legal, and diplomatic protocols surrounding the Frame.

Function:

Declares the contained visitor a guest, person, envoy, patient, pilgrim, criminal, witness, or prohibited arrival.

Failure mode:

Personhood dispute, quarantine seizure, diplomatic rupture, frame arrest.

## Candidate Frame Failure Taxonomy

### Boundary Integrity Loss

The Contact Frame can no longer prove its boundary is coherent.

Hydropolis response:

Immediate Frame hush, dock lockdown, witness-silence, breach bells.

### Edge Leak

A localized failure in the boundary lattice.

Not a full breach yet.

Useful story symptom:

Nearby signals shear, instruments disagree, skin prickles, black glass sings wrong.

### Shell Inversion

The Frame begins translating exterior and interior boundary rules incorrectly.

Danger:

The Frame may act calm while its containment model is reversing.

### False Enclosure

The Frame reports sealed status while a secondary boundary has already failed.

World Works danger:

Corporate systems may hide this status to avoid liability.

### Split Shell

The Frame maintains multiple incompatible boundary states.

Possible Chimera-adjacent implication:

Do not reveal casually. Protected mystery risk.

### Dead Shell

The occupant is unreachable or gone, but the Frame still behaves according to stored protocol.

Horror use:

A polite empty body requesting dock clearance.

## Docking Protocol Vocabulary

Candidate terms:

- **Frame hush**: public quiet / signal suppression during high-risk docking, breach review, or visitor transfer.
- **Docking topology**: certified boundary shape required for safe port entry.
- **Boundary proof**: legal and technical verification of intact separation.
- **Shell witness**: person or institution authorized to verify a Frame's status.
- **Cold courtesy**: etiquette around not crowding, touching, warming, or startling a newly docked Frame.
- **Mirror-Shell berth**: sealed dock cell for Frame arrival.
- **Frame scar code**: visible or encoded marking showing past breach, repair, or containment trauma.
- **Guest quarantine**: not punishment; the period during which boundary stability is proven.

## World Works Misread

World Works wants to reduce Contact Frame logic to assets:

- containment unit
- visitor interface platform
- shell mobility system
- docking property
- conjugate access vehicle
- Frame-compatible site infrastructure

World Works error:

It treats the Frame as a machine to own, while Hydropolis treats it as a boundary-personhood event.

Core contrast:

**World Works wants the shell. Hydropolis governs the crossing.**

## Visual Development Notes

Contact Frames should not look like generic spacesuits.

Visual motifs:

- inner absence suggested, not shown
- black-glass sensory panels
- cold halos near joints
- floating boundary seams
- non-touch hand gestures
- mirrored seals
- legal glyph tags
- hush bells / hush lights
- breach-scar inlays
- docking clamps shaped like ceremonial hands that never close

Frame movement should feel mediated:

- slight delay before touch
- precise etiquette pauses
- sensor head turns before body turns
- gestures too careful to be casual
- entire public rooms quieting when a Frame enters
