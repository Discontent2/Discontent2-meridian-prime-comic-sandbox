## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines the **Meridian / human Traverse Ops map-language** for the Dry Circuit.

This is the map a Prime Ops planner, NCI field lead, Traverse dispatcher, or World Works logistics officer would understand first.

It is practical, satellite-based, route-centered, and operational.

It asks:

**Can the convoy survive the route and arrive on schedule?**

---

## Core Interpretation

**Development Apocrypha**

The Meridian map treats the Dry Circuit as a logistics problem.

It prioritizes:

- terrain
- convoy stages
- fuel
- camps
- route support
- hazard entrances
- major turns
- border checkpoints
- GPR results
- satellite imaging
- LiDAR surface shape
- FLIR heat stress
- cargo and machine accountability

It does not ignore politics or sacred geography, but it translates them into operational risk.

Hydropolis becomes a signal-control jurisdiction.

The Craton becomes a threshold authority.

The Dry Circuit becomes a supported or unsupported route.

## Visual Style

**Development Apocrypha**

The Meridian Dry Circuit map should look like a high-altitude satellite logistics overlay from a dangerous frontier operation.

Visual traits:

- overhead satellite imagery
- muted dry-season colors
- pale crystal desert flats
- black-glass basin streaks
- cracked hydrocrystal beds
- dry canal scars
- jagged shard fields
- industrial yards near Prime Ops
- clean tactical route overlays
- waypoint dots with codes
- hazard zones shaded or outlined
- end-of-shift camps marked clearly
- support nodes marked with practical icons
- border checkpoints marked as route controls

This is not a mythic parchment map.

This is not primarily a signal interpretation map.

This is a map for machines, crews, fuel, and timing.

## Core Route Shape

**Development Apocrypha**

The Dry Circuit forms a large operational triangle:

1. **Prime Ops to Hydropolis**
2. **Hydropolis to the Anticlinal Craton / World Maw**
3. **Anticlinal Craton to Prime Ops**

Map geometry is symbolic but should feel operationally plausible.

The route is not exact canon geography yet.

Do not lock exact mileage, coordinates, or final terrain placement.

## Waypoint Code System

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Use simple operational codes:

| Prefix | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **L** | major location |
| **C** | end-of-shift camp |
| **T** | major turn |
| **H** | hazard area entrance |
| **R** | relay or route support point |
| **G** | gate or border control point |

Example:

- `L-00 Prime Ops`
- `C-01 First Shift Camp`
- `H-02 Mirror Glare Entrance`
- `T-03 Maw Approach Turn`
- `G-04 Gate Release Point`

## What the Meridian Map Misreads

**Contradictory Account**

The Meridian map is useful but culturally blunt.

It may misread:

- Hydropolis silence as dead zone rather than legal control
- Craton quiet as lack of infrastructure rather than sovereign refusal
- sacred thresholds as access restrictions
- signal contamination as equipment error only
- Dry Port paranoia as survey rumor rather than geopolitical fear
- No-Map Hospitality as inefficient rather than deliberate sovereignty

The Meridian map knows where the convoy can go.

It does not always know what the convoy has entered.
