## Long Seasonal Cycle on Maps

**Candidate Canon Seed / Calendar Development Needed**

Official Meridian Prime years are not established yet.

Working Earth-duration approximation:

| Season / State | Approximate Earth Duration | Map Consequence |
|---|---:|---|
| Dry Season | 2 Earth years | Dry Circuit opens, basin floor exposed, Hydropolis grounded, Craton mountain-like |
| Spring / Cataract Season | 1 Earth year | cataracts return, routes break, rivers awaken, descent becomes dangerous |
| Wet Season | 2 Earth years | lower routes drown, Hydropolis rises, Craton becomes island-like, escarpment snowfield |
| Fall / Drawdown Season | 1 Earth year | waters retreat, roads re-emerge, maps are unstable and politically contested |

**Important Calendar Note:**

The user specified wet and dry seasons last approximately two Earth years each, spring and fall approximately one Earth year each, and that a full Meridian Prime year is approximately four Earth years.

This creates a useful unresolved calendar issue.

Do not silently resolve it.

Future calendar design should decide whether:

- spring / fall overlap with wet / dry transitions
- the hydrological cycle differs from the civil year
- Earth-year estimates are rough field shorthand
- different civilizations count seasons differently

**Candidate Canon Seed**

## Seasonal Transformation of Major Landmarks

### Prime Ops

**Development Apocrypha**

Dry season:

- launch point for descent and Dry Circuit logistics
- high-desert industrial hub
- staging lanes active

Wet season:

- snowfield logistics hub
- lower routes inaccessible
- internal highland survival network becomes more important

Cartographic shift:

Prime Ops goes from gateway to the Dry Circuit to snowfield command / isolation hub.

### Hydropolis

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry season:

- city sits on basin floor
- dry gates active
- underground city and dry canals exposed
- road access possible

Wet season:

- city floats / rises with basin water
- main city moored to four 2,000-foot skyscraper elevator towers
- water access dominates
- underground city still connected by tower elevators

Cartographic shift:

Hydropolis moves vertically rather than simply relocating horizontally.

### Anticlinal Craton / World Maw

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry season:

- geode mountain
- land approach possible
- Maw Gate Apron accessible by route

Wet season:

- geode island
- land approaches drowned
- water / cliff / dock protocols become more important

Cartographic shift:

Craton maps must show both mountain logic and island logic.

### Rivers / Dry Circuit Corridors

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

Dry season:

- riverbeds become roads
- some rivers go underground
- Traverse follows these corridors

Spring:

- cataracts signal the return of water
- routes fracture
- underground channels awaken

Monsoon / Haboob:

- inland seas form
- dust, flood, static, and visibility collapse interact

Wet season:

- full oceanic basin state
- lower roads drowned

Fall:

- water retreats
- routes reappear but are unstable

Cartographic shift:

A river corridor may be labeled as road, cataract, inland sea, ocean route, or mud belt depending on season.

## Same Place, Different Maps

**Contradictory Account**

The same place should not always have one name, one boundary, or one legal status.

Example: a lower basin dry flat near the Shard margin.

| Map Tradition | Label |
|---|---|
| Meridian / Prime Ops | H-06 Shard Margin Sinkfields |
| Hydropolis / Antisapien | Unauthorized Echo Attachment Zone |
| Craton / Aeonolacertian | The Dry Sea That Still Swallows |
| World Works | Seasonal Feasibility Corridor 7-B |
| Lividisapien Revisionist | Why Is This Relay Still Talking? |

This kind of layered labeling makes Meridian Prime feel ancient, bureaucratic, haunted, and alive.
