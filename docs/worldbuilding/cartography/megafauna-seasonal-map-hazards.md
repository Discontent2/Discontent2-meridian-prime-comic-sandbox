## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines how Meridian Prime megafauna should appear on maps and route charts.

Megafauna should not be treated as random monster icons.

They are seasonal terrain actors whose movements can close routes, predict floods, reveal wrecks, hide pirate lanes, and turn good maps into lies.

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime megafauna mark should answer three questions:

1. **Where does the animal make travel dangerous?**
2. **How does the danger change by season?**
3. **What does the animal reveal that ordinary terrain maps miss?**

Core line:

**Some hazards move because they are alive. Some animals matter because they move the hazard.**

## Seasonal Marking Rules

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry-season maps should emphasize:

- exposed road conflicts
- dry riverbed dormancy hazards
- herd corridors across roads
- animals mistaken for terrain
- active predation near camps, cisterns, repair stops, and idling machines
- buried megafauna under crust

Best map note:

**Dry does not mean empty. It means some things are waiting.**

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Cataract-season maps should emphasize:

- animals moving ahead of rising water
- spawning emergence
- evacuation conflicts with wildlife corridors
- cliff refuge zones
- under-road collapse
- sudden channel activation
- routes blocked by migrating herds

Best map note:

**When the water wakes, the animals remember first.**

### Haboon Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Haboon-season maps should emphasize:

- visibility failure plus animal movement
- false terrain under dust / floodsheet conditions
- predators using storm noise
- livestock-scale or megafauna panic routes
- pirate use of wildlife territories
- signal and GPR confusion around buried or drifting animals

Best map note:

**The storm hides teeth as well as roads.**

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season maps should emphasize:

- aquatic megafauna routes
- islanding / rafting / buoyant dormancy
- maritime hazards
- tower-associated animals near Hydropolis
- Craton wet-dock fauna hazards
- submerged Dry Circuit predator zones
- protected nurseries and no-anchor zones

Best map note:

**When the roads drown, the fauna writes the water map.**

## Cultural Map Biases

### Meridian / Prime Ops

Likely treatment:

- route closures
- hazard bands
- repair-stop danger
- herd avoidance
- vehicle damage risk

Prime Ops maps may flatten animals into operational obstacles.

### World Works

Likely treatment:

- asset interference
- wildlife management zones
- mitigation corridors
- survey obstruction
- liability avoidance

World Works maps may sound so dead they make living teeth look like line items.

### NCI / Traverse Crews

Likely treatment:

- field warnings
- old hand-map names
- camp danger stories
- machine damage memories
- practical animal lore

NCI maps may note:

**Paper says passable. Condors say otherwise.**

### Hydropolis

Likely treatment:

- protected nurseries
- signal / animal interaction
- wet-season drift paths
- civic exclusion zones
- resonance conflicts near towers

Hydropolis may treat some fauna as civic seasonal participants.

### Craton

Likely treatment:

- sacred pressure corridors
- elder weight
- threshold fauna
- warning behaviors
- no-map or no-drone zones

Craton maps may not separate animal movement from land memory.

### Free Scabs

Likely treatment:

- usable predator territories
- smuggling cover
- false-island warnings
- natural ambush walls
- wet-season pirate lanes shaped by fauna

Free Scab maps may mark animals as accomplices, tollkeepers, or traps.

## Protected Mystery Guardrails

**Protected Mystery Guardrail**

Megafauna maps may deepen ecology, route danger, sacred geography, and seasonal logistics.

They must not casually reveal:

- final Craton sacred interior geography
- Thunder Egg / Stone Heart truth
- full Core mystery
- Dry Port truth
- gateway mechanics
- Rob's full death-chain implications
- Horus Skelton's full plan

Some species may be mythologized or misinterpreted.

Do not make every myth literally true.
