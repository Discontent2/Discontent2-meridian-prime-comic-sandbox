## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document defines how the revised Meridian Prime port system should appear on maps and route diagrams.

Do not map every port as a fixed harbor symbol.

Meridian Prime ports may be:

- mobile platform clusters
- seasonal elevation camps
- floating civic terminal layers
- guarded geological access points
- wet-season basalt quays
- closed off-season threshold zones

---

## Core Cartographic Principle

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime port map should answer five questions:

1. **What season is this port able to receive?**
2. **What kind of arrival does it accept: wheels, hulls, signal, pressure, or proof?**
3. **Who decides whether arrival counts?**
4. **What must be inspected before movement continues?**
5. **What part of the port moves, closes, rises, or opens with the season?**

Core line:

**A Meridian Prime port is not a dot. It is a seasonal verb.**

## Seasonal Map Treatment

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry-season maps should emphasize:

- Prime-Ops Port platform routes
- low or mid elevation strategic camps
- Hydropolis Terminal basin-floor access
- Craton Bore Holes / Pressure Throats
- Dry Circuit convoy approach lanes
- dry cargo transfer yards
- crawler repair lanes
- port month holding areas

Map note:

**Dry season receives wheels, crawlers, and dust-thin patience.**

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Cataract-season maps should emphasize:

- platform fallback camps
- closing approaches
- unstable river and canyon transitions
- Hydropolis vertical adjustment
- Craton closure signals
- Blackstep Quay not yet reliably open
- route withdrawal corridors

Map note:

**Cataract season turns approach roads into questions.**

### Haboon Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Haboon-season maps should emphasize:

- strategic elevation camp movement
- storm shelves
- platform sheltering zones
- false port signals
- emergency holding zones
- Craton off-season closure
- smuggling pressure around closed ports

Map note:

**Haboon weather makes every port rumor dangerous.**

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season maps should emphasize:

- Prime-Ops highwater or snowline platform camps
- Hydropolis floating terminal layer
- Blackstep Quay open status
- waterborne approach lanes
- basalt shelf docking zones
- cargo lifts and barge queues
- closed dry access points
- off-season closure zones if transition is active

Map note:

**Wet season receives hulls, lifts, and whatever survived becoming buoyant.**

## Prime-Ops Port Map Treatment

**Political Development / Candidate Canon Seed**

Map Prime-Ops Port as a mobile system, not a fixed point.

Show:

- platform cluster positions
- platform migration paths
- strategic elevation camps
- convergence yards
- fuel and repair zones
- NCI contractor bays
- launch flats
- storm fallback shelves
- return quarantine yards
- route authority lanes

Avoid:

- fixed dry-port symbol
- implying Prime-Ops Port is the protected-mystery Dry Port
- treating the port as one immovable facility

Suggested map label:

**Prime-Ops Port: current platform convergence.**

Best map note:

**The port moved. The paperwork says it was always here.**

## Craton Bore Access / Pressure Throat Map Treatment

**Religious Tradition / Candidate Canon Seed**

Map Craton dry-season access carefully.

Outsider maps may label these features as:

**Craton Bore Holes.**

Craton-facing maps may use:

**Pressure Throats.**

Show:

- dry-season access shafts
- controlled approach fields
- guest-right inspection rings
- cargo lowering / lifting zones
- Pressure Gem exchange chambers, if public enough
- no-map sacred interiors blurred or omitted
- defensive chokepoints without tactical detail

Avoid:

- exact sacred interior routes
- implying public access
- treating bore access as ordinary industrial freight shafts

Suggested map label:

**Pressure Throat: dry-season Craton access, permission required.**

Best map note:

**The map shows the mouth, not what the mountain says after swallowing.**

## Off-Season Closure Map Treatment

**Religious Tradition / Candidate Canon Seed**

The Craton closes Blackstep Quay during unstable transition seasons.

Map this as:

- closure bands
- unstable approach fields
- closed wet-gate symbols
- no-docking warnings
- Craton refusal marks
- hazard overlays for half-exposed basalt and unstable water
- smuggling pressure zones outside official closure

Suggested map label:

**Off-Season Closure: Craton contact refused.**

Best map note:

**Closed does not mean empty. Closed means the Craton is no longer answering.**

## Port-Month Map Layer

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Because the Dry Circuit run includes approximately one Earth month at each major port of call, maps should show port-month zones where crews, cargo, and paperwork accumulate.

Port-month zones may include:

- repair fields
- cargo stacks
- crew leave districts
- medical lanes
- inspection corridors
- exchange counters
- quarantine yards
- missing-person boards
- black market bleed-over
- local ritual waiting areas

Map note:

**A port month is where movement becomes paperwork, rumor, appetite, and repair.**

## Faction Map Biases

### Prime Ops / Traverse-Ops

**Political Development**

Emphasize:

- platform positions
- route authority
- fuel and repair logistics
- strategic elevation camps
- hazard fallback sites
- convoy timing

Prime Ops maps want the port to look controlled even when it is crawling away from the weather.

### Hydropolis

**Political Development / Scientific Hypothesis**

Emphasize:

- verified arrival layer
- Civic Lattice boundary
- terminal elevation
- origin and signal clearance
- restricted lower-city access

Hydropolis maps distinguish physical contact from civic arrival.

### Craton

**Religious Tradition**

Emphasize:

- permission
- threshold
- Pressure Throat status
- Blackstep Quay seasonal opening
- off-season refusal
- sacred omission

Craton maps may not show everything outsiders think a port map should show.

### Free Scabs / Unofficial Markets

**Development Apocrypha**

Emphasize:

- platform migration rumors
- false port signals
- smuggling opportunities during closure
- port-month black market bleed-over
- forgotten approach roads
- unofficial waiting grounds

Free Scab maps love the places official maps call temporary.

### Bought Nation / Reclaimers

**Political Development**

Emphasize:

- port labor camps
- receipt counters
- missing-person boards
- cargo delay claims
- Prime-Ops platform worker grievances
- Hydropolis admission disputes

Bought Nation maps remember who waited, who worked, and who did not return.
