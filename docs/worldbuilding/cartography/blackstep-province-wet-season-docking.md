## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document gives future maps and visual development a practical guide for showing the Blackstep Province across seasonal states, especially wet-season docking around the Anticlinal Craton.

The goal is to prevent the Craton from feeling like a geode mountain that simply becomes an island without shoreline logic.

The Blackstep Province makes the Craton's wet-season access harsh, sacred, volcanic, and politically controlled.

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season Craton maps should show three nested systems:

1. **Geode Mountain / Craton Body**
2. **Blackstep Province / Basalt Apron**
3. **Wet-Season Water Boundary / Docking Reefs**

The Craton does not become easier to reach when the basin floods.

It changes how outsiders are refused.

## Docking Protocol Concepts

**Development Apocrypha / Do Not Promote Yet**

Possible wet-season docking stages:

1. **Outer Waiting Water** - ships wait beyond sacred basalt shelves.
2. **Signal / Silence Check** - Hydropolis or Craton-controlled communication constraints apply.
3. **Weight Declaration** - cargo, passengers, engine vibration, and hull pressure are declared.
4. **Anchor Refusal Zone** - ships cannot anchor in certain waters.
5. **Gate Drum / Pressure Warning** - Craton authority signals if approach may continue.
6. **Permitted Landing Terrace** - only approved docks can be used.
7. **Guest Transfer** - passengers become guests under threshold law.
8. **Cargo Separation** - cargo may not inherit guest-status.

Do not canonize exact ritual sequence yet.

## Relationship to Eschar Slough and Free Scab Piracy

**Development Apocrypha**

The Eschar Slough and Blackstep Province should create wet-season maritime tension.

The Eschar Slough gives the Free Scabs pirate geography.

The Blackstep Province gives the Craton sacred wet gates.

Between them lies a dangerous wet-season traffic problem:

- legitimate Craton-bound vessels
- Hydropolis navigation concerns
- Prime Ops emergency craft
- World Works survey boats
- Free Scab pirates
- sacred no-anchor waters
- false beacon hazards

This creates a natural maritime conflict zone without making the Free Scabs secretly control the Craton's docks.
