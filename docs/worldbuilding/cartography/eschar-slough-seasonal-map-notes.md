## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document gives future mapmakers a compact treatment guide for The Eschar Slough.

The Slough must not be mapped as one static location.

It is a seasonal transformation field and the homeland/base-system of The Free Scabs.

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Eschar Slough is not simply an archipelago.

It is scabland when dry, locking channels when cataract waters return, storm maze during haboon conditions, and pirate archipelago in wet season.

A correct map should show the same ridges changing meaning across seasons.

## Seasonal Map States

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Map label:

**Eschar Slough: Exposed Scabland / Free Scab Activity Active**

Show:

- cracked flats
- salt scars
- black crust
- hydrocrystal scabfields
- exposed island ridges that do not yet read as islands
- dry channels
- hidden cisterns
- buried boats
- vehicle approaches
- possible approaches to The Scab Cathedral

Free Scab mode:

- banditry
- salvage
- convoy raids
- route intelligence
- cistern control

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Map label:

**Eschar Slough: Locking Channels / Access Closing**

Show:

- rising water lines
- cut-off ridges
- evacuation routes
- routes becoming channels
- flood gates without gates
- early boat deployment
- road-to-water transition hazards

Free Scab mode:

- evacuation rackets
- rescue or ransom decisions
- boat launching
- flood salvage
- approach denial

**Candidate Canon Seed**

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

## Relationship to The Scab Cathedral

**Protected Mystery Guardrail / Development Apocrypha**

Do not map The Scab Cathedral as a fixed, obvious city.

Instead show:

- multiple false candidate sites
- access windows
- route convergence hints
- dead-channel references
- seasonal harbor rumors
- Blackwake Harbor redactions
- dry approach marks through The Bone Channels

A map may suggest:

**The Cathedral hears from here when the water is low.**

It should not casually say:

**The Scab Cathedral is here.**
