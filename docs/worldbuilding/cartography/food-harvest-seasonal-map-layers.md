## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines how Meridian Prime food systems should appear on maps.

Food maps should not look like gentle agricultural charts.

They should show:

- harvest windows
- toxic lookalike zones
- civic verification areas
- food sovereignty claims
- contraband corridors
- medical harvest sites
- ration routes
- seasonal crop emergence
- poisonous bloom warnings
- disputed corporate harvest blocks

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime food map should answer four questions:

1. **What can be eaten here?**
2. **What nearby thing looks edible but kills, sickens, confuses, or compromises identity?**
3. **Who controls the harvest?**
4. **What season makes the map lie?**

Core line:

**A food map is a survival document with politics hiding in the pantry.**

---

## Map Layer Families

**Development Apocrypha / Do Not Promote Yet**

| Layer | Use |
|---|---|
| **Nutritional Harvest Zone** | safe or culturally known food sources |
| **Toxic Lookalike Zone** | dangerous foods that resemble staples |
| **Medical Harvest Zone** | fictional medicine or salve resource areas |
| **Recreational / Restricted Food Zone** | intoxicants, ordeal foods, banned signal-foods |
| **Ration Route** | routes moving staple foods between settlements |
| **Food Sovereignty Claim** | areas where food production proves political belonging |
| **Corporate Harvest Block** | World Works-managed food or bioresource zones |
| **Civic Verified Food Zone** | Hydropolis-cleared crops and water foods |
| **Under-Mapped Sacred Food Zone** | Craton food systems that outsiders should not map fully |
| **Contraband Food Route** | Free Scab or black-market food movement |

## Seasonal Map Treatment

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry-season food maps emphasize:

- stored foods
- root crops
- dry-channel crops
- highland rations
- hidden water fruits
- dormant toxic lookalikes
- food transport by convoy
- dry harvest from exposed basin and plateau

Map note:

**Dry season shows what stores, roots, and survives in dust.**

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Cataract-season food maps emphasize:

- first wet growth
- dangerous lookalike emergence
- canyon spray crops
- routes becoming food-isolated
- harvest before flooding cuts access
- fungal or bloom contamination starting early

Map note:

**The first water wakes the wrong plants too.**

### Haboon Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Haboon-season food maps emphasize:

- ration stress
- toxic dust on crops
- storm damage to harvest stores
- emergency food theft
- Free Scab food raids
- bloom confusion in low visibility
- unsafe field identification

Map note:

**During haboon, hunger and bad visibility become the same predator.**

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season food maps emphasize:

- aquatic crops
- barge food routes
- floating harvest fields
- Hydropolis verified foods
- Free Scab piracy of food barges
- Craton wet-isolation stores
- Blackstep dock hospitality foods
- submerged toxic lookalikes

Map note:

**When the roads drown, food travels by water and rumor.**

- chestnut groves
- ration routes
- worker harvest zones
- promised vs leased fields
- Reclaimer food sovereignty markings

**The field is evidence. The harvest is testimony.**

### Prime Ops Maps

**Development Apocrypha**

Emphasize:

- rations
- verified safe harvest
- toxic exclusion
- convoy food storage
- emergency reserves
- settlement food continuity

Prime Ops may miss cultural meaning but catch famine risk.

**Corporate Distortion**

Emphasize:

- bioresource yield
- harvest blocks
- workforce nutrition assets
- food continuity products
- toxicity mitigation zones
- liability areas

World Works map principle:

**If it feeds people, count it before they do.**
