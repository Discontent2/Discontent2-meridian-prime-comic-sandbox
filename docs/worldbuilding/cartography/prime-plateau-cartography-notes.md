## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document defines how the Prime Plateau should appear on future maps of Meridian Prime.

The Prime Plateau is the major mid-elevation cartographic hinge between Prime Ops, the Escarpment descent, and the lower Dry Circuit.

Future maps should avoid treating the 8,000-foot descent as a single uninterrupted route line from Prime Ops to the Mirror Basin.

The descent should be staged.

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Dry Circuit begins at Prime Ops, but operationally begins at the Prime Plateau.

A correct Dry Circuit map should show:

```text
Prime Ops
  ↓
The Great Cut / major canyon system
  ↓
The Prime Plateau / mid-elevation staging shelf
  ↓
lower descent gates and canyon roads
  ↓
Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit lowlands
```

## Required Map Elements

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Future maps involving the Escarpment descent should include some or all of these elements:

- Prime Ops highland node
- major canyon system, working name: **The Great Cut**
- Prime Plateau mid-elevation shelf
- upper descent gate
- lower descent gate
- switchback routes
- plateau camps
- plateau fuel / repair nodes
- cargo transfer yards
- brake-cooling zones
- canyon hazard entrances
- cataract routes
- haboon dust / flood funnel zones
- wet-season snowline or closure boundaries
- route decision point between descent options

## Seasonal Map States

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Map label:

**Prime Plateau: Active Mid-Elevation Staging Shelf**

Visual emphasis:

- clear plateau roads
- active camps
- open descent gates
- route staging yards
- fuel and repair icons
- dry canyon floors

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Map label:

**Prime Plateau: Cataract Watch / Route Breakage Likely**

Visual emphasis:

- active waterfalls
- flooded canyon routes
- evacuation arrows
- route washout marks
- warning towers
- unstable descent markers

### Haboon Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Map label:

**Prime Plateau: Storm Hold / Descent Not Advised**

Visual emphasis:

- dust funnels
- flash-flood chutes
- storm shelter icons
- zero-visibility zones
- signal degradation marks
- convoy hold points

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Map label:

**Prime Plateau: Snowline Support / Lower Routes Closed**

Visual emphasis:

- snowfield edge
- iced descent gates
- upper plateau support lines
- lower route closure marks
- icefall / cataract warning marks

## How Each Map Tradition Treats the Prime Plateau

### Meridian / Human Traverse Maps

**Development Apocrypha**

Meridian maps treat the plateau as:

- staging shelf
- decision point
- route support zone
- brake and engine management boundary
- cargo transfer point
- emergency refuge

Meridian label tendency:

**Prime Plateau Operations Shelf**

### World Works Maps

**Corporate Distortion**

World Works maps treat the plateau as:

- infrastructure corridor
- survey access hinge
- lease opportunity
- route control bottleneck
- feasibility and logistics platform

World Works label tendency:

**Mid-Elevation Operational Corridor / Plateau Access Block**

### NCI / Field Contractor Maps

**Development Apocrypha**

NCI and old field maps treat the plateau as:

- old repair lore
- trusted camps
- bad turns
- brake-death markers
- storm shelter memory
- Rob / Tenet route history candidate

NCI label tendency:

**The Shelf** or **The Glass Bench**

### Hydropolis / Antisapien Signal Maps

**Development Apocrypha**

Hydropolis maps treat the plateau as:

- route behavior shift
- signal degradation shelf
- signal monitoring / witness point
- early-warning zone for cataract return
- possible place where official route data starts lying

Hydropolis label tendency:

**Escarpment Signal Transition Shelf**

### Aeonolacertian / Craton Pressure Maps

**Religious Tradition / Development Apocrypha**

Craton maps treat the plateau as:

- weight-gathering shelf
- first major humility test before lower crossings
- place where machines become dangerous if not checked
- canyon-mouth pressure wound system

Craton label tendency:

**The Road's Held Breath**

## Future Visual Development Requirements

**Development Apocrypha**

Future map images should:

- scale the Prime Plateau as a major landform, not a tiny waypoint
- keep Prime Ops smaller than the escarpment but visibly connected to the plateau
- show the canyon system as a branching network, not one road line
- place several end-of-shift camps on or near the plateau
- show seasonal water / snow / storm behavior in the canyon system
- mark the plateau as the final major decision zone before committing to the lower Dry Circuit
