## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document establishes a shared cartographic framework for Meridian Prime maps.

It is not one map.

It is the atlas grammar that lets many maps feel like they belong to the same world.

The guide should support:

- prose reference maps
- comic maps
- in-world documents
- graphic novel inserts
- tabletop game handouts
- 16-bit game overworld maps
- tactical UI overlays
- field reports
- propaganda maps
- sacred maps
- redacted corporate maps

## Core Principle

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime map is never neutral.

Every map answers three questions:

1. **What can be crossed?**
2. **What can be trusted?**
3. **What is the map forbidden to admit?**

## Three Primary Map Minds

**Development Apocrypha**

Meridian Prime cartography should preserve three distinct map minds.

| Map Mind | Primary Question | Primary Truth | Primary Blind Spot |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Meridian / Human** | Can the route be supported? | Terrain, fuel, machines, camps, timing. | Sacred meaning and signal intent. |
| **Antisapien / Hydropolis** | What is the route saying or hiding? | Signal, identity, contradiction, echo, civic permission. | Embodied pressure law and honest machine noise. |
| **Aeonolacertian / Craton** | What is being pressed, crossed, or dishonored? | Threshold, guest-right, pressure, silence, memory, wound. | Logistical urgency and signal-law nuance. |

All future maps should know which map mind produced them.

A map without a point of view should be treated as suspicious.

## Shared Base Map Rules

**Development Apocrypha**

All Meridian Prime maps should share a few base conventions unless an in-world mapmaker intentionally breaks them.

### 1. Major Civilization Spaces

The three major civilization spaces should remain visually distinct:

- human crystal desert escarpment settlement sphere
- Anticlinal Craton / World Maw
- Hydropolis / Aquapolis / the Drowned Circuit in the Mirror Basin

### 2. Routes Are Seasonal

Routes are never permanently safe by default.

A route can be:

- proposed
- unproven
- proven for the current season
- last season's route
- unsupported
- closed
- redacted
- forbidden
- sacredly withheld
- signal-contaminated
- no-map by treaty or ritual

### 3. Hazards Have Entrances

Major hazards should have entrance markers, not just area labels.

A route crew needs to know when the operating condition changes.

### 4. Camps Are Survival Intervals

End-of-shift camps are not towns.

They are planned survival intervals with fuel, shelter, beacon logic, or ritual permission.

### 5. Unknown Does Not Mean Empty

Blank space may mean:

- unknown
- forbidden
- redacted
- sacredly withheld
- signal-dark
- no-map by hospitality
- politically disputed
- not yet surveyed
- surveyed but hidden

Use different marks for different kinds of blankness.

## Coordinate and Waypoint Logic

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Use practical prefixes for operational maps:

| Prefix | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **L** | major location |
| **C** | end-of-shift camp |
| **T** | major turn |
| **H** | hazard entrance |
| **R** | relay or route support point |
| **G** | gate or border control point |
| **X** | redacted, unverified, protected mystery, or forbidden marker |
| **W** | witness point or disputed evidence point |
| **S** | signal point, relay anomaly, or Hydropolis signal-law marker |
| **P** | pressure marker, threshold zone, or Craton permission point |

Examples:

- `L-00 Prime Ops`
- `C-04 Dry Canal Camp`
- `H-06 Shard Margin Sinkfields`
- `G-03 Maw Gate Apron`
- `X-02 Unverified Contact Geometry`
- `W-01 Dry Season Witnessing Site`

### Numbering Rule

**Development Apocrypha**

Numbers may reset by route leg, map sheet, or operation.

Do not canonize a universal global coordinate system yet.

## Shared Symbol Families

**Development Apocrypha**

All map symbols should belong to one of these families:

1. **Route Symbols** - roads, trails, proven routes, unproven routes, cut routes, closed routes.
2. **Survival Symbols** - camps, fuel, shelter, water, recovery sled, repair node.
3. **Hazard Symbols** - crust collapse, glare, sinkfield, heat, fauna, dead channel.
4. **Jurisdiction Symbols** - Prime Ops, Hydropolis, Craton, World Works, NCI, witness authority.
5. **Ritual Symbols** - Idle at the Maw, Signal Surrender, No-Map Hospitality, Three-Way Permit.
6. **Signal Symbols** - RF field, dead channel, signal contamination, identity seal, contradiction mark.
7. **Pressure Symbols** - threshold, guest-status, weight path, wound mark, silence field.
8. **Mystery Symbols** - rumored, redacted, protected, unverified, contradictory, suppressed archive.

Detailed symbol entries belong in `map-symbol-dictionary.md`.

## Seasonal Map Variants

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The same territory should map differently depending on season.

### Dry Season Maps

Emphasize:

- exposed flats
- dry canals
- route access
- heat-bloom
- crust collapse
- dead channels
- Dry Circuit traffic
- Hydropolis dry gates
- Craton approach roads

### Wet Season Maps

Emphasize:

- navigable water
- barges
- submerged infrastructure
- closed dry roads
- canal authority
- Hydropolis wet-body configuration
- island logic around the Craton
- flood, fog, and reflection hazards

### Transition Season Maps

Emphasize:

- closing pressure
- unreliable routes
- shifting permissions
- emergency returns
- late convoys
- contested weather windows

### Growth Event Maps

Emphasize:

- newly changed terrain
- route contradiction
- pressure ridges
- rollers
- unexplained formations
- official map failure

### Chrono-Loom Visibility Maps

Emphasize:

- time-window overlays
- old horizon appearances
- possible memory geography
- forbidden or disputed interpretation

## Redaction and Protected Mystery Rules

**Development Apocrypha**

Maps should preserve mystery without becoming visually empty.

Use different redaction styles for different kinds of concealment:

| Redaction Type | Visual Treatment | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate redaction | black bars, gray boxes, clean missing data | World Works or official concealment |
| Hydropolis sealed record | geometric seal, signal lock, black-glass ring | identity, signal, or visitation record sealed |
| Craton no-map zone | intentional blank hollow, crossed compass, pressure boundary | route may not be recorded |
| Traverse unknown | dotted route, question marker, GPR gap | not yet proven or not supported |
| Mythic uncertainty | layered ghost line, half-symbol, contradictory label | legend or disputed tradition |
| Protected mystery | X marker, partial label, warning frame | do not confirm final truth |

### Core Guardrail

A map may point toward mystery.

A map should not casually solve protected mystery.

**Development Apocrypha**

Example:

## Cartographic Bias Rules

**Development Apocrypha**

Every map should distort something.

### Meridian Map Bias

Shows:

- supportability
- fuel
- camps
- route stages
- terrain hazard
- machine risk

Hides or flattens:

- sacred refusal
- identity harm
- signal contradiction
- nonhuman jurisdiction nuance

### Antisapien Map Bias

Shows:

- signal behavior
- identity
- contradiction
- old infrastructure echoes
- false silence
- device conduct

Hides or flattens:

- pressure law
- guest-status
- human machine necessity
- sacred non-recording

### Aeonolacertian Map Bias

Shows:

- threshold
- pressure
- guest-right
- wound memory
- machine insult
- silence

Hides or flattens:

- logistics timing
- signal law
- ordinary survey needs
- desperate survival motive

### World Works Map Bias

Shows:

- asset access
- legal corridors
- route feasibility
- logistics optimization
- survey priority

Hides or flattens:

- sacred sovereignty
- Hydropolis refusal
- Craton memory
- political hunger
- Dry Port motive
