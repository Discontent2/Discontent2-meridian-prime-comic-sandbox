## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines layered naming for the Prime Plateau after the discovery that the plateau is best treated as a temperate cloud rainforest and possibly an old protected reserve / national park.

The goal is to let different maps and groups name the same place differently without collapsing the contradiction.

## Use of “Green Hell”

**Development Apocrypha**

**Green Hell** should be used as Traverse / NCI / mechanic slang, not as the official name.

It works because it sounds like something a driver, mechanic, or route lead would say after dealing with:

- fog
- wet brakes
- moss-slick switchbacks
- root-buckled roads
- fungal corrosion
- RF-deadening vegetation
- coolant contamination
- canopy collapse
- engine-warmth fauna
- roads that look softer than they are

Example field usage:

**Welcome to the Green Hell. Do not trust anything soft.**

### Guardrail

Do not make **Green Hell** the formal atlas name.

It is powerful because it belongs to crews who have suffered there.

## Use of “The National Park”

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

**The National Park** is excellent as an ironic field name and possibly a surviving legal misnomer.

Because the human Escarpment sphere is a patchwork rather than a clean nation-state, the name raises useful questions:

- Whose nation?
- Whose park?
- Who declared it protected?
- Who maintains it?
- Who profits from calling it protected?
- Who gets moved out when protection becomes management?

### Best Interpretation

The Prime Plateau may have once been declared a protected reserve by early Meridian settlers because it was the most accessible temperate cloud rainforest near Prime Ops.

Later, World Works and Prime Ops reframed that protection into managed access, route stabilization, ecological control, and infrastructure corridors.

### Field Use

A Traverse crew may say:

**We are descending through the National Park.**

Meaning:

**We are entering moss, fog, rootbreak, fungal corrosion, switchback ghosts, and a canyon system that wants to fold us into weather.**

## Map Label Principles

**Candidate Canon Seed**

### 1. Formal Maps Should Layer Names

Example:

**Prime Plateau / Prime Plateau Conservation Zone**

or:

**Prime Plateau, called “The National Park” by Prime Ops field crews**

### 2. Crew Maps Should Use Green Hell Sparingly but Strongly

The slang should appear in handwritten notes, route warnings, NCI maps, and old crew jokes.

Example:

**GREEN HELL STARTS HERE**

### 3. Public Maps Should Sound Gentler Than the Place Is

Visitor-facing or official names should feel misleadingly calm.

Example:

**Prime Plateau Conservation Zone — Controlled Access Scenic Corridor**

### 4. World Works Maps Should Hide Desire Behind Care

Example:

**Mid-Elevation Ecological Stabilization Reserve**

Translation:

**We want to control this chokepoint.**

### 5. Local Names Should Resist Reduction

Local plateau names should make clear the area is not only a road.

Example:

**The Green Shelf keeps us before it carries you.**
