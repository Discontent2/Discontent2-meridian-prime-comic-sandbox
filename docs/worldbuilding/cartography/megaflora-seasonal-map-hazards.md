## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines how Meridian Prime mega-flora should appear on maps and route charts.

Mega-flora should not be treated as generic tree icons.

They are:

- seasonal landmarks
- hidden-water indicators
- false ground
- route barriers
- sacred groves
- pirate concealment
- docking hazards
- logging conflict zones
- signal interference fields
- food sovereignty systems

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime mega-flora mark should answer three questions:

1. **How does this flora alter movement or visibility?**
2. **How does that alteration change by season?**
3. **Who treats the flora as hazard, resource, refuge, or sacred boundary?**

Core line:

**Plants do not merely occupy the map. They edit it.**

## Seasonal Marking Rules

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry-season maps should emphasize:

- false ground
- buried water memory
- puncture hazards
- hidden root collapse
- dormant civic or sacred flora fields
- logging or harvesting corridors
- dry-route visibility blocked by root, vine, or canopy systems

Best map note:

**Dry does not mean barren. It means some things are waiting underground.**

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Cataract-season maps should emphasize:

- flora swelling before full floods arrive
- canyon curtain growth
- flood-predictor plants
- route-to-water transition vegetation
- seed or spore release windows
- rapidly changing ledge or shoreline vegetation

Best map note:

**The plants know the water is coming before the maps do.**

### Haboon Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Haboon-season maps should emphasize:

- low visibility concealment growth
- root and reed traps under storm conditions
- false-surface blooms
- uprooted or floating mats
- entanglement zones
- pirate and predator use of flora cover

Best map note:

**Storms hide roads. Flora decides what the storm hides them in.**

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season maps should emphasize:

- floating fields
- channel forests
- mooring or docking entanglement
- sacred no-anchor flora
- protected shallows
- pirate concealment channels
- aquatic bloom obstructions
- terrace and ridge refuges defined by plant systems

Best map note:

**When the roads drown, the flora writes the shoreline.**

## Cultural Map Biases

### Meridian / Prime Ops

Likely treatment:

- route obstruction
- false ground warning
- logging resource
- camp viability
- docking obstruction
- signal-loss vegetation

Prime Ops maps tend to treat flora as operations problems.

### World Works

Likely treatment:

- harvestable biological asset
- mitigation obstacle
- managed bio-material zone
- liability cluster
- survey inconvenience

World Works language will make a sacred grove sound like a procurement memo.

### NCI / Traverse Crews

Likely treatment:

- field warnings
- old route names
- do-not-park-here annotations
- water knowledge
- practical shelter or salvage notes

NCI map note example:

**If the willows are bone-white, the road is lying.**

### Hydropolis

Likely treatment:

- signal ecology
- protected bloom fields
- civic water markers
- canal law restrictions
- witness-flora zones

Hydropolis is likely to treat some flora as part utility, part citizen.

### Craton

Likely treatment:

- sacred growth
- pressure-listening flora
- protected terraces
- no-map cultivation
- refusal landscapes

Craton maps may blur or omit flora that outsiders should not approach.

### Free Scabs

Likely treatment:

- concealment growth
- engine-fouling blooms
- pirate channels
- cache roots
- natural fortifications

Free Scab maps likely treat flora as accomplice.

## Protected Mystery Guardrails

**Protected Mystery Guardrail**

Mega-flora maps may deepen ecology, route logic, sacred geography, piracy concealment, and wet-season border systems.

They must not casually reveal:

- final Craton sacred interior geography
- Thunder Egg / Stone Heart truth
- full Core mystery
- Dry Port truth
- gateway mechanics
- Rob's full death-chain implications
- Horus Skelton's full plan

Do not make every sacred plant literally magical.

Do not make every scientific interpretation correct.
