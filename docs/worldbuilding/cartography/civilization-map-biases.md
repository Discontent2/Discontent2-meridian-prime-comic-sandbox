## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines the major **map biases** of Meridian Prime.

No Meridian Prime map shows the world as it is.

Every map shows what its maker believes can kill, save, shame, expose, conceal, or be owned.

These biases should inform future:

- atlas maps
- route maps
- faction maps
- comic inserts
- game UI maps
- tabletop handouts
- field reports
- propaganda documents
- sacred diagrams
- leaked records

---

## Core Principle

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A map is not only a picture of territory.

It is an argument about what matters.

The cartographer always tells you what they fear by what they choose to measure.

## 1. Meridian / Human Traverse Maps

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

### Primary Bias

Operational survival.

Meridian maps care about:

- route viability
- fuel
- end-of-shift camps
- machine strain
- recovery options
- cargo weight
- weather windows
- hazard entrances
- GPR confidence
- Prime Ops support range

They mark a route as good if a convoy can survive it.

### They Misread

- sacred boundaries as access complications
- signal-law as communications protocol
- Craton silence as lack of infrastructure
- Hydropolis refusal as bureaucracy
- old ruins as route hazards or survey targets

### Map Motto

**If the tractor can cross it, the map thinks it understands it.**

### Best Use

Use for Traverse planning, MITE / SpITE operations, Prime Ops logistics rooms, NCI route briefings, and scenes where practical survival collides with deeper meaning.

---

## 2. World Works Corporate Maps

**Corporate Distortion**

### Primary Bias

Access and control.

World Works maps care about:

- assets
- leases
- jurisdiction gaps
- extraction corridors
- survey zones
- deniable routes
- underutilized territory
- disputed authority
- concealed project overlays
- route feasibility language

They mark a place as useful if it can be accessed, exploited, studied, classified, or made legally ambiguous.

### They Misread

- refusal as inefficiency
- sacred geography as stakeholder complexity
- Hydropolis authority as access friction
- Craton sovereignty as legacy restriction
- mystery as opportunity

### Map Motto

**Nothing is forbidden. Some things are merely awaiting authorization.**

### Best Use

Use for sanitized briefings, internal black maps, corporate strategy, Dry Port suspicion, over-clean mission packets, and scenes where language hides hunger.

---

## 3. NCI / Field Contractor Maps

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

### Primary Bias

Lived route memory.

NCI maps care about:

- old machine scars
- trusted camps
- bad turns
- unofficial bypasses
- where someone almost died
- bridges that groan wrong
- dead tractors used as landmarks
- Rob / Ten / old crew annotations
- paper says safe, field says no notes

They mark a route as known only if someone they trust survived it and remembered why.

### They Misread

- formal authority as less valuable than field memory
- new official maps as suspicious by default
- clean corporate data as potentially dishonest
- mythic warnings as maybe practical warnings in costume

### Map Motto

**The official map tells you where to go. The old map tells you where not to die.**

### Best Use

Use for NCI interiors, Red Umbrielor route history, Rob / Ten legacy clues, The House Ten Built materials, and field-operator contrast against official maps.

---

## 4. Hydropolis / Antisapien Signal Maps

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

### Primary Bias

Signal behavior and identity.

Hydropolis maps care about:

- signal seals
- dead channels
- identity confidence
- RF shadows
- old relay behavior
- contradiction marks
- unauthorized echo attachments
- Contact Frame-adjacent alarms
- civic wrappers
- false silence

They mark a place as dangerous when signal behavior disagrees with record, identity, or permission.

### They Misread

- honest machine noise as suspicious clutter
- Craton silence as concealment
- human improvisation as sloppy identity behavior
- pressure ritual as data refusal
- ordinary maintenance as potential tampering

### Map Motto

**The road is not where the ground lies. The road is where the signal confesses.**

### Best Use

Use for Hydropolis civic maps, Ildi-adjacent signal analysis, dry-season signal hazards, identity disputes, and old-route infrastructure mysteries.

---

## 5. Lividisapien Revisionist Maps

**Contradictory Account / Suppressed Archive**

### Primary Bias

Contradiction.

Lividisapien maps care about:

- mismatched records
- erased route histories
- ghost signals
- public/private map differences
- contradictions between permits and terrain
- false corporate labels
- places where official silence contains traffic
- archive scars
- leaked witness marks

They mark a place as important when two authorities disagree about whether it exists.

### They Misread

- coherence as suspicious
- stable records as possibly sanitized
- ritual secrecy as cover-up
- ordinary error as deliberate suppression
- myth as hidden evidence

### Map Motto

**Where the map disagrees with itself, start digging.**

### Best Use

Use for leaks, graffiti maps, counter-system truth hunts, Livid Æ Revisionists, Dry Port paranoia, and places where official certainty smells too clean.

---

## 6. Vaultsapien Archive Maps

**Suppressed Archive / Religious Tradition**

### Primary Bias

Continuity and verification.

Vaultsapien maps care about:

- record chains
- identity routes
- survivor ledgers
- authenticated witnesses
- who crossed where and under what name
- sealed testimony
- lineage of permits
- archival confidence
- corrections to false history

They mark a place as real when its record survives challenge.

### They Misread

- oral memory as unstable unless witnessed
- Lividisapien leaks as reckless
- human field notes as emotionally useful but procedurally weak
- Craton no-map zones as archival wounds
- unrecorded survival as troubling

### Map Motto

**A road without a witness is only a rumor with footprints.**

### Best Use

Use for official Hydropolis archives, identity disputes, survivor records, sealed witness maps, and historical continuity conflicts.

---

## 7. Null Court Governance Maps

**Religious Tradition / Political Development**

### Primary Bias

Authority, control, and silence.

Null Court maps care about:

- signal hierarchy
- permission fields
- sealed corridors
- civic quiet zones
- forbidden broadcast zones
- high-order AI / governance overlays
- predictive risk
- population movement
- controlled contact
- politically necessary omissions

They mark a place as safe when it can be governed without unnecessary noise.

### They Misread

- disorder as threat
- Livid contradiction as social hazard
- human field improvisation as unmanaged risk
- Craton refusal as rival sovereignty
- mystery as something best sealed until useful

### Map Motto

**A map should not show everything. It should show what must be obeyed.**

### Best Use

Use for Hydropolis governance, quiet fields, sealed contact routes, high-order civic control, and politically restrained map documents.

---

## 8. Aeonolacertian / Craton Pressure Maps

**Religious Tradition / Candidate Canon Seed**

### Primary Bias

Threshold, weight, and guest-right.

Craton maps care about:

- pressure
- gates
- wounds
- guest-status
- silence
- vibration
- weight paths
- no-map hospitality
- sacred geology
- ancestral memory
- machine insult
- what land can bear

They mark a place as dangerous when something crosses without proper relationship to the threshold.

### They Misread

- human urgency as disrespect
- Hydropolis signal law as evasive echo
- World Works documentation as hunger in costume
- cargo as less important than the behavior of those carrying it
- survey work as theft unless ritually constrained

### Map Motto

**Crossing is not movement. Crossing is agreement.**

### Best Use

Use for Craton gate authority, sacred geography, pressure rites, threshold disputes, guest-status, and Aeonolacertian visual map culture.

---

## 9. Aeonolacertian Warrior / Gate-Guard Maps

**Political Development / Religious Tradition**

### Primary Bias

Defense and permitted force.

These maps are more tactical than sacred pressure maps.

They care about:

- gate approaches
- kill-zones, though likely not labeled that crudely
- drone denial
- machine weight
- convoy posture
- weaponized survey behavior
- breach scars
- where outsiders can be stopped without entering sacred interior
- which guests become teeth

### They Misread

- nervous outsiders as potential aggression
- Hydropolis observation as surveillance
- Meridian recovery operations as tactical probing
- route deviation as hostile approach

### Map Motto

**A guest has hands. An invader has teeth. Learn the difference before the gate opens.**

### Best Use

Use for Craton defense maps, gate staging, tension at the Maw, and situations where one outsider's behavior endangers an entire convoy.

---

## 10. Hydropolis Civic Tourist / Public Maps

**Corporate Distortion / Religious Tradition**

### Primary Bias

Controlled welcome.

These are the maps outsiders are allowed to see.

They care about:

- legal entry routes
- approved districts
- dry / wet transit
- signal surrender points
- public archive halls
- safe markets
- restricted zones disguised as maintenance areas
- civic beauty
- visitor compliance

They misread deliberately.

### They Hide

- Contact Frame infrastructure
- internal faction borders
- sealed archives
- Livid contradiction zones
- Null Court quiet fields
- true signal topology

### Map Motto

**Welcome. Do not confuse welcome with access.**

### Best Use

Use for in-world visitor pamphlets, public-facing Hydropolis documents, controlled exposition, and visual irony against restricted maps.

---

## 11. Traverse Crew Hand Maps

**Contradictory Account / Development Apocrypha**

### Primary Bias

Superstition plus hard-earned practicality.

Traverse crew hand maps care about:

- where the road feels wrong
- camp gossip
- machine failures
- old deaths
- crews that vanished
- routes that talk back
- nicknames
- shortcut rumors
- forbidden jokes
- what the official map forgot

They mark danger with folk logic, but the folk logic often has teeth.

### They Misread

- technical causes as personality
- political decisions as curses
- signal anomalies as ghosts
- corporate lies as weather
- weather as personal insult

### Map Motto

**Machines get insulted. Cracks get respected. Roads get remembered.**

### Best Use

Use for crew culture, field humor, route superstition, old tractor lore, and emotionally grounded map fragments.

---

## 12. Dry Port Rumor Maps

**Suppressed Archive / Do Not Promote**

### Primary Bias

Obsession.

These maps may be made by World Works analysts, Hydropolis witnesses, smugglers, Lividisapien leakers, or paranoid Traverse veterans.

They care about:

- unexplained geometry
- missing route data
- impossible signals
- concealed surveys
- old docking shapes
- false negatives
- overclean manifests
- buried infrastructure
- no-fly gaps
- places where everyone insists there is nothing

They misread almost everything because they are built from fear.

Sometimes fear points its dirty little finger at the right door.

### Map Motto

**No proof. Too much pattern. Keep watching.**

### Best Use

Use only when preserving uncertainty around the Dry Port, second-port paranoia, World Works motive, Hydropolis fear, or Livid contradiction culture.

### Guardrail

Do not let Dry Port rumor maps confirm:

- the Dry Port exists
- the Claw-region hidden formation is the Dry Port
- World Works knows the final truth
- Hydropolis has complete records
- the Craton understands the final contact-port mystery
