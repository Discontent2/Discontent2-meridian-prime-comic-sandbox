## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This file defines how Meridian Prime mushrooms should appear on maps, field guides, route notes, and civilization-biased cartography.

Mushrooms should not be mapped only as food.

They should also mark:

- buried water
- unsafe shelter
- toxic lookalikes
- fungal contamination
- death-sites
- route-softening ground
- verified civic ecology
- Craton under-mapped food systems
- Free Scab warnings and jokes with teeth

---

## Core Cartographic Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime mushroom map mark should answer four questions:

1. **Is this cap food, warning, poison, taboo, medicine, or bait?**
2. **What does it resemble that could fool an outsider?**
3. **What hidden condition caused it to fruit here?**
4. **Which season makes the mistake worse?**

Core line:

**A mushroom mark is a note from the underground, and the underground is not always friendly.**

## Seasonal Marking Rules

### Dry Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry-season maps should emphasize:

- dormant caps
- hidden water clues
- edible emergency mushrooms
- poisonous lookalikes in dust or fog
- shelter contamination markers
- caps marking carcasses, wrecks, or predator beds

Map note:

**Dry caps often mark what the surface is hiding.**

### Cataract Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Cataract-season maps should emphasize:

- seep-wall mushrooms
- mist-spread spores
- canyon respirator zones
- flood-return puffball timing
- false water-sound and vertigo hazards

Map note:

**When water wakes the walls, spores travel faster than warnings.**

### Haboon Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Haboon-season maps should emphasize:

- spore dust mixed with storm dust
- false light and false distance
- toxic lookalike confusion in low visibility
- exhausted travelers misreading rest-zone mushrooms
- Free Scab use of false cap markers

Map note:

**In haboon weather, a bad mushroom does not need poison. It only needs you to guess.**

### Wet Season

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season maps should emphasize:

- canal clearance
- false harbor caps
- tower food verification
- under-mapped Craton cavern fungi
- mushroom blooms in wet shelters
- floodbank drowsiness hazards
- dock rot markers
- death-site lights and glowing caps

Map note:

**When the roads drown, mushrooms mark which ground remembers being land.**

## Key Map Warnings by Region

### Prime Plateau / Green Hell

**Development Apocrypha**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Cloud Morels and Lantern Shelves are valuable; Bridecaps and Bunk Rot Buttons punish confidence.**

Map warnings:

- verify glowing shelf fungi with local guide knowledge
- Bunk Rot Buttons mark unsafe shelters
- Cloud Morel zones may be politically contested harvest sites
- Bridecaps should be mapped as false-light toxicity risk

### Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit

**Development Apocrypha**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Saltcaps and Channel Puffballs feed convoys; Mirage Puffballs and Mouthbed Caps distort the route.**

Map warnings:

- Mouthbed Caps can signal Gulper-associated terrain
- Mirage Puffballs can create false horizons
- Channel Puffballs become spore hazards if too mature

### Hydropolis Zone

**Political Development / Scientific Hypothesis**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Tower Gills and Mirror Caps are food only if verified; Archive Buttons are record-risk, not merely poison.**

Map warnings:

- use civic clearance marks
- distinguish foundation ecology from archive contamination
- Drift Gills should be restricted / nightlife-risk markers

### Eschar Slough

**Development Apocrypha**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Blackcap Oysters and Scab Puffballs sustain Free Scabs; Corpse Lantern Buttons and False Harbor Caps mean the lights are lying.**

Map warnings:

- Corpse Lantern Buttons mark death-sites and possible Bride-field risk
- False Harbor Caps mark false light and toxic lure hazard
- Free Scab maps may intentionally obscure which caps are warning and which are bait

### Anticlinal Craton

**Religious Tradition**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Geode Milk Caps and Pressure Truffles are food only within permission. Gateblind Caps and Black Tongue Shelves may be real, taboo, exaggerated, or all three.**

Map warnings:

- under-map sacred mushroom locations
- use blurred or threshold-marked zones
- do not treat Craton mushrooms as public forage

### Blackstep Province

**Development Apocrypha**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Basalt Brackets and Spray Shelf Mushrooms feed dock crews; Anchor Rot Caps and Drakebait Buttons mark failing moorings or hungry company.**

Map warnings:

- Anchor Rot Caps mark mooring decay
- Drakebait Buttons attract predators
- edible Basalt Brackets harden into false shelf hazards as they age

### Lower Basin River Corridors

**Development Apocrypha**

Most important mushroom map conflict:

**Willow Veil Caps and Floodcap Buttons help camps survive; Drowncaps and Veil Mold Bells make rising water look restful.**

Map warnings:

- Drowncap zones are not safe rest zones
- Veil Mold Bells indicate road-softening and hidden water
- Floodcap Buttons mark timing before boat obstruction growth

## Protected Mystery Guardrails

**Protected Mystery Guardrail**

Mushroom maps may deepen food culture, ecology, route clues, Hydropolis civic law, Craton guest law, and Free Scab survival practice.

They must not casually reveal:

- Dry Port truth
- Core mystery
- gateway mechanics
- Thunder Egg / Stone Heart truth
- exact Craton sacred interior geography
- Rob's full death-chain implications
- Horus Skelton's full plan

Mushroom marks can signal hidden conditions without solving protected mysteries.
