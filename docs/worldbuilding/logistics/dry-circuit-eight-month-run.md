## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document establishes the current working logistics rhythm for a standard Dry Circuit run.

The goal is to define how long the route takes, why travelers arrive desperate, and why ports, cargo transfer, rest, inspection, trade, repair, and leave time matter.

---

## Core Duration

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A standard Dry Circuit run takes approximately:

**8 Earth months total.**

This includes:

- travel time
- hazard delay
- cargo transfer
- restock
- port inspection
- crew leave
- repair
- medical clearance
- cargo reconciliation
- route reauthorization

Best line:

**The Dry Circuit is long enough that cargo becomes old news, rumors become religion, and every port receives strangers wearing familiar faces.**

---

## Basic Run Model

**Candidate Canon Seed**

| Phase | Duration | Location / Function |
|---|---:|---|
| Launch / staging | folded into port cycle | **Prime-Ops Port** platform convergence and Traverse-Ops preparation |
| Leg 1 | about 2 Earth months | Prime Plateau / Great Cut / basin descent toward Hydropolis |
| Port of call 1 | about 1 Earth month | **Hydropolis Terminal** cargo, verification, rest, medical clearance, leave |
| Leg 2 | about 1 Earth month | Hydropolis toward Craton access / Blackstep approach depending season |
| Port of call 2 | about 1 Earth month | Craton dry access through bore systems or wet access through Blackstep Quay |
| Leg 3 | about 2 Earth months | return route toward Prime-Ops side |
| Return port cycle | about 1 Earth month | **Prime-Ops Port** recovery, debrief, repair, cargo reconciliation, crew rotation |

Total:

**8 Earth months.**

## Motion Time vs Port Time

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A standard run includes roughly:

- **5 Earth months of motion / hazard exposure**
- **3 Earth months of port time**

Port time is not leisure.

A port month includes:

- cargo transfer
- supply restock
- leave time
- crew replacement
- medical triage
- vehicle repair
- fungal / spore decontamination
- cargo disputes
- exchange conversion
- inspection rituals
- contraband pressure
- casualty accounting
- missing-person notices

Best line:

**A Dry Circuit port does not rest a convoy. It decides which parts of the convoy are still allowed to continue.**

---

## Dry Season Capacity

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Because the dry season lasts approximately **2 Earth years**, a standard 8-month Dry Circuit run occupies about one-third of a dry season.

Implication:

- most crews can safely complete **two full circuits** per dry season
- a third circuit is possible only under desperate, elite, corporate, or legendary conditions
- missing a port window can destroy an entire seasonal trade plan
- late convoys risk haboon/cataract transition hazards

Best line:

**Eight months is not a commute. It is a seasonal oath with tires.**

## Distance Treatment

**Development Apocrypha / Do Not Promote Yet**

Do not lock exact distance yet.

A useful rough working range for the route-distance equivalent is:

**3,500 to 5,500 km.**

This is not straight-line distance.

It includes:

- descent switchbacks
- canyon detours
- dry river roads
- hazard bypasses
- scouting delays
- repair stops
- convoy crawl zones
- false flats
- animal avoidance
- spore or fungal stops
- port approach queues
- Craton / Hydropolis access restrictions

Best principle:

**The Dry Circuit is measured less in miles than in things that make miles stop counting.**

## Traveler Desperation Curve

### After Leg 1: Arrival at Hydropolis Terminal

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Crews may arrive with:

- altitude and descent fatigue
- brake damage
- spore cough
- cracked ration discipline
- early route injuries
- water hunger
- signal hunger
- medical urgency
- need for verified food and shelter

### After Leg 2: Arrival at Craton Access / Blackstep Approach

**Candidate Canon Seed**

This leg is shorter, but politically sharper.

Crews may arrive with:

- valuable Hydropolis cargo
- smuggling temptation
- Craton protocol anxiety
- trade language stress
- port access uncertainty
- weather-window pressure

### After Leg 3: Return Toward Prime-Ops Port

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Crews may return with:

- exhausted mechanics
- damaged platforms or crawlers
- altered manifests
- accumulated receipts
- missing people
- unofficial goods hidden in repairs
- unresolved port disputes
- moral fatigue

Best line:

**No one arrives at a port as the same legal person who departed the last one.**

## Revised Port Names in the Run

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Use the following current port names:

- **Prime-Ops Port** — mobile Traverse-Ops platform system, not a fixed dry port
- **Hydropolis Terminal** — terminal layer within the floating / vertical city
- **Craton Bore Holes** — outsider term for dry-season Craton access points
- **Pressure Throats** — proposed Craton term for sacred / formal bore access
- **Blackstep Quay** — wet-season Craton port and basalt threshold dock system

Avoid using **Prime-Ops Dry Port** as the current name.

Avoid confusing Prime-Ops Port with the protected-mystery **Dry Port**.
