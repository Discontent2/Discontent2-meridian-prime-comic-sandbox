## Core Identity

Craton mech culture grows from heavy labor, public witness, and Geodeum spectacle.

Craton mechs are not primarily sleek military machines. They are usually modified construction, quarry, loading, lifting, hauling, excavation, and industrial support machines that have been ritualized, ornamented, and adapted for Mech Geodeum use.

Craton society may use steampunk visual language without being technologically primitive. Brass, pressure gauges, piston banks, blackened iron, rivets, chain drums, vents, analog displays, ceremonial plates, and visible linkages are cultural style, maintenance philosophy, and public legibility.

Core lines:

```text
Craton mech culture begins with labor and ends in witness.
A Craton mech is never allowed to forget what it was built to carry.
The machine is not only a tool. In the Geodeum, it becomes testimony with joints.
```

## Cultural Principle

Craton mech culture treats machines as public extensions of burden, status, labor history, crew reputation, and Stonebound identity.

A Mech Geodeum contest may also be:

```text
crew against crew
work lineage against work lineage
Crown claim against Crown claim
old quarry record against public spectacle
faction pride against social embarrassment
debt history against witnessed consequence
operator reputation against inherited machine memory
```

## Mech Culture Roles

Operators are the public face of Mech Geodeum machines. They may be Stonebound, hired champions, claim-bearers, debt representatives, ceremonial proxies, or skilled industrial operators pulled into spectacle.

Rig crews may include:

```text
rigwrights
pressure keepers
chain tenders
plate fitters
stone-dust cleaners
balance callers
seal readers
chime keepers
ring patchers
cradle fitters
```

These are cultural / story roles, not real-world engineering instructions.

A machine's lineage can matter as much as its operator. A mech may carry the reputation of a work yard, quarry house, rig crew, family workshop, Stonebound circle, Crown sponsor, or civic faction.

## Mech Types

```text
Lift-Rig: heavy loader or lifting frame adapted for arena use.
Quarry Walker: stone-yard or quarry machine adapted for terrain and impact endurance.
Gate Setter: barrier or gate-handling machine tied to threshold contests.
Haul Frame: cargo or ore-hauling platform adapted for endurance and mass.
Brace Engine: stabilizing or shoring machine adapted to resist collapse or impact.
Scaffolder: scaffold, bridge, or height-work machine adapted for vertical arena movement.
```

Stonejaw's Lift Rig is a Lift-Rig.

## Mech Geodeum Culture

A Mech Geodeum is not merely a metal fight pit. It is a public arena where industrial machines carry social meaning.

A Mech Geodeum may stage Crown contests, machine inheritance disputes, crew honor trials, public repair rites, debt contests, operator accusation scenes, proxy faction contests, passage-right machine ordeals, spectacle matches, and ritualized machine retirement.

## Machine Inheritance

Craton mech culture treats inherited machines as dangerous public objects.

When an operator takes on a famous rig, they may inherit old debts, crowd expectations, unfinished claims, crew loyalty, machine-name pressure, prior operator reputation, sponsor obligations, public suspicion, and ritual burden.

Stonejaw's Lift Rig is the established example: Dragulies acquired it from the previous Crown holder named Stonejaw. This addendum does not define how or why that transfer happened.

## Visual Language

Craton mechs should generally feel:

```text
heavy
legible
worked-on
ritualized
industrial
brass-and-iron
stone-dusted
pressure-driven
publicly repaired
beautiful through burden
```

Avoid defaulting to sleek military robots, smooth futuristic mecha, clean power armor, human-shaped armor suits, floating magic machines, frictionless sci-fi gloss, modern gun platforms, or exact copies of existing franchises.
