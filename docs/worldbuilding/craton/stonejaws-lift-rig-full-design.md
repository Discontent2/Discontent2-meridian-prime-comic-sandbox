## Core Identity

**Stonejaw's Lift Rig** is Dragulies's famous loader-type Geodeum mech: a modified heavy labor frame with Craton steampunk industrial styling, inherited from the previous Crown holder, Stonejaw.

It is not a sleek military machine.

It is a construction-born arena relic, a public inheritance, and a machine with work still trapped in its bones.

Core lines:

```text
Stonejaw's Lift Rig is the burden with arms.
It was built to lift stone. The Geodeum taught it to lift verdicts.
```


Visual read from a distance:

```text
A hulking brass-and-iron loader mech with raised industrial arms, a deep operator cradle, a counterweighted back, and the posture of a work machine forced into ceremony.
```

## Loader-Frame Anatomy

The torso is a reinforced loader body with riveted plates, a pressure-core housing, old repair seams, a central load spine, brace rings around the operator cradle, and Crown markings over older stone-yard scars.

The arms are oversized lifting assemblies with pressure pistons, stone clamps or fork plates, ceremonial edge guards, impact scars, chain housings, pressure cables, and old lifting marks still visible beneath Geodeum paint.

The legs are wide and braced, with heavy piston thighs, stabilizer feet, stone-grip cleats, ankle braces made from quarry hardware, and impact-dented shin shields.

The back carries the counterweight, pressure tanks, vent stacks, coiled chain drums, old quarry balance plates, and arena heraldry bolted over earlier work markings.

Stonejaw's Lift Rig does not need a humanoid head. Prefer a raised cage-and-lamp sensor crown, brass lamp array, protective brow plate, pressure-gauge face cluster, or iron grille. Do not make it a human knight helmet.

## Cockpit / Operator Fit For A Tyranosapien

The operator station is an **operator cradle**, not a human seat.

It must account for:

```text
large saurian torso
forward-leaning stance
tail accommodation or rear tail channel
wide hip and thigh clearance
non-human shoulder mechanics
shorter but powerful Tyranosapien arms
clawed hands
large clawed feet
neck and snout clearance
restraint systems that do not assume human anatomy
```

Cradle features:

```text
standing / braced semi-standing operator posture
chest brace and shoulder harness fitted to Tyranosapien anatomy
tail channel or rear counterbalance slot
claw-grip control handles
forearm pressure levers sized for strong shorter arms
jaw-clear viewing gap or angled viewport
snout-safe breathing clearance
heavy foot braces for clawed feet
side-entry hatch wide enough for saurian body shape
```

Controls are story-facing only: brass pressure levers, claw-grip toggles, forearm yokes, foot brace plates, load-balance gauges, chain-tension bells, witness chime pull, and Crown seal lock. Do not define real engineering.

## Arena Modifications

Arena modifications preserve labor-machine ancestry:

```text
reinforced loader arms
arena plating over quarry frame
ceremonial Crown Stonebound sigils
stone-scored shoulder guards
removable ring plates from earlier matches
pressure-release vent flares for crowd visibility
impact bells that ring when the frame takes major force
protective operator cage
clamp guards
stabilizer cleats for Geodeum stone floors
old work serial marks deliberately left visible
```

If it threatens, it threatens by weight, pressure, pinning, lifting, blocking, shoving, forcing space, and controlling the ring. Avoid default guns, missiles, or sleek military parts.

## Steampunk Industrial Styling

Stonejaw's Lift Rig should emphasize:

```text
brass pressure gauges
blackened iron frame
riveted plates
visible piston banks
steam / pressure vents
chain drums
analog dials
hand-stamped plates
exposed linkages
grease-darkened joints
stone dust
ceremonial metalwork
work scars left intact
```

The rig can be highly capable, but it should never look frictionless, glossy, or magically clean.
