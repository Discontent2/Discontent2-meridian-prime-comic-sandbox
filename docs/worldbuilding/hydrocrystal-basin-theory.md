## Hydrocrystal Basin Model

A hydrocrystal basin is a region where water, crystal growth, pressure, charge, fracture, and wave propagation interact strongly enough to affect routes, signals, settlement, and belief.

Possible features:

- hidden water under crystal crust
- pressure-charged strata
- seasonal conductivity shifts
- signal shadows
- resonance corridors
- false-silent zones
- mapped roads that change state after stress events
- geode-vault growth pockets
- band-gap terrain where signals fail or bend
- pressure lines interpreted as sacred corridors

Canon-safe phrasing:

**Some deep-pressure crystal regions may behave like hydrocrystal basins or immature geode-vault systems. This remains disputed, local, and politically sensitive.**

## Seasonal Phase-Change Cartography

Meridian Prime routes may behave like phase systems.

A route can change from:

- road to river
- crust to sinkfield
- channel to reef
- bridge to food source
- dock to cliff
- dry basin to signal mirror
- dead channel to live handshake

Percolation analogy:

Connectivity matters. Roads do not merely open or close; they form clusters, break clusters, and reconnect at different thresholds.

Canon-safe principle:

**Wet season does not erase the map. It changes which connections percolate.**

## Growth Events

Growth Events can be interpreted through pressure accumulation, fracture propagation, crystal growth, and phase-transition analogies.

Safe model:

- strain accumulates invisibly
- seasonal water pressure changes the substrate
- crystals grow or fracture along prepared lines
- small triggers can cause large changes if the region is near criticality
- official maps fail because the terrain crossed a threshold

Do not make Growth Events fully solved.

Possible line:
