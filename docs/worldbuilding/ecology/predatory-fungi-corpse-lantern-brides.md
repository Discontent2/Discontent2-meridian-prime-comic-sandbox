## Parent Lineage

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Corpse Lantern Brides are a dangerous variant complex of the already-proposed **Corpse Lanterns** of the Eschar Slough.

Parent form:

- **Corpse Lanterns**: tall, pale fungal fruiting bodies that bloom from carcasses, drowned wrecks, old mass-death sites, and flood-buried refuse.

Variant form:

- **Corpse Lantern Brides**: deceptive lantern colonies that mimic safe camp lights, boat lamps, wedding lamps, mourning candles, or harbor signals in order to draw travelers toward death-sites, predator water, wreck traps, or spore basins.

---

## Core Concept

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Corpse Lantern Brides are pale predatory fungal colonies that grow from death-sites and then imitate human-scale safety signals.

They do not chase prey.

They make danger look inhabited.

Dry season:

- they resemble bone-white stalks, old camp lamps, or dull altar candles near wrecks and buried bodies.

Wet season:

- they glow brighter over shallow blackwater, drowned roads, corpse pockets, and wreck fields.

Best summary:

**Corpse Lantern Brides do not lure people with beauty. They lure people with the promise that someone else survived.**

## Name Meaning

**Development Apocrypha**

The word **Bride** may come from several contradictory traditions.

### 1. Free Scab Usage

**Contradictory Account**

Free Scabs may call them Brides because their lights gather around death like wedding lamps around a doorway.

In this reading, the Slough is marrying the dead back into itself.

### 2. Bought Nation / Route Family Usage

**Contradictory Account**

Route families may say Brides were named after old mourning rites where lanterns were lit for the unreturned.

Corpse Lantern Brides mock that gesture by lighting themselves.

### 3. World Works Classification

**Corporate Distortion**

World Works may avoid the folk name entirely.

Possible term:

**Phototactic Mortality-Site Fungal Cluster.**

Translation:

**A light that grows where people died and may help more people die there.**

### 4. Hydropolis Signal Reading

**Scientific Hypothesis / Political Development**

Hydropolis may classify them as false-signal ecology, especially in wet-season travel lanes.

Possible term:

**Bioluminal False-Harbor Bloom.**

## Core Behavior

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Corpse Lantern Brides exploit four instincts:

1. Move toward light in darkness.
2. Trust repeated light patterns as habitation.
3. Read lamp-height as human or vessel presence.
4. Assume an existing light means survivable ground or water.

This makes them especially dangerous in:

- storm haze
- haboon dusk
- blackwater channels
- flooded road ghosts
- wreck fields
- refugee movement
- desperate rescue searches
- Free Scab pirate territory

---

## Dry-Season Form

**Candidate Canon Seed**

In dry season, Corpse Lantern Brides appear as:

- pale bone-colored stalks
- dull capped fruiting bodies
- lamp-shaped fungal nodes
- low clusters around buried wreckage
- faintly glowing rings near old camps
- chalky deposits around carcass pockets
- brittle stalks that sound like glass when cracked

Dry-season hazards:

- mark death-sites but may also create new ones
- lure travelers toward unstable crust
- hide sinkholes, Black Spore Tar, or old wreck pits
- attract scavengers and Blackwake Crocs near remaining water
- create false camp impressions in low visibility

---

## Wet-Season Form

**Candidate Canon Seed**

In wet season, Corpse Lantern Brides become much more dangerous.

They may appear as:

- glowing lamps over shallow blackwater
- false boat lights
- pale wedding-procession lines across channels
- low ghost-harbor constellations
- floating lantern caps attached to submerged mycelium
- luminous stalks growing from wrecked hulls and drowned cargo

Wet-season hazards:

- lure boats into wreck fields
- mimic safe harbor lights
- conceal submerged corpses and debris
- attract Blackwake Crocs and scavenger fauna
- lead desperate swimmers into spore basins
- create false approach lines toward pirate ambushes

Free Scab warning:

**If the lamp has no smoke, count your dead before docking.**

## Seasonal Behavior

### Dry Season: Bone Lamps

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Dry-season Brides are dimmer and more skeletal.

They are often mistaken for old route markers, abandoned camp lamps, chalk fungus, or mineral deposits.

Map label:

**Corpse Bride Field: Dry / Death-Site Marker / False Camp Risk**

### Cataract Season: Waking Lanterns

**Development Apocrypha**

As water returns, dormant colonies brighten.

They may begin glowing before the surrounding channel is fully wet, warning that subsurface water or corpse-rich sediment is near.

Map label:

**Corpse Bride Field: Waking / Rising Water Near Death-Sediment**

### Haboon Season: False Rescue Lights

**Candidate Canon Seed**

During haboon conditions, dust, rain, floodsheet glare, and low visibility make Corpse Lantern Brides especially dangerous.

They can resemble rescue lamps, camp fires, vehicle signals, or hand lanterns.

Map label:

**Corpse Bride Field: False Rescue Light Risk**

### Wet Season: False Harbor Brides

**Candidate Canon Seed**

During wet season, Bride colonies glow strongly around wreck fields and shallow blackwater.

They are most dangerous to boats, swimmers, stranded refugees, and Free Scab targets.

Map label:

**Corpse Bride Field: False Harbor / Predator Attraction / Do Not Approach**

## Variants

### 1. Camp Brides

**Development Apocrypha**

Dry-season clusters that mimic the height and spacing of human camp lanterns.

Primary hazard:

- lure travelers toward abandoned camps, wreck pits, or soft scabland.

Map note:

**Multiple lights do not mean multiple survivors.**

### 2. Harbor Brides

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season clusters that mimic safe harbor lights or docking lamps.

Primary hazard:

- lure small craft into shallow wreck fields, Blackwake Croc channels, or pirate-controlled waters.

Map note:

**Harbor light without signal confirmation is not harbor light.**

### 3. Procession Brides

**Mythic Development / Contradictory Account**

Rare lines of Bride lights that appear to move across water or along old roadbeds.

Scientific possibility:

- sequential fruiting bodies brightening in waves due to moisture, pressure, or spore release.

Mythic interpretation:

- the dead walking their own route home.

Map note:

Do not confirm whether Procession Brides actually move.

### 4. Widow Brides

**Development Apocrypha**

Dim isolated Bride colonies that grow from single deaths rather than mass wreckage.

Primary hazard:

- mark lone casualties, missing travelers, or hidden predator kills.

Possible use:

- forensic clue or emotional story marker.

### 5. Blackwake Brides

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Bride colonies growing with Black Spore Tar or Eschar Bloom.

Primary hazard:

- lights hover above sticky slicks, toxic surface blooms, or engine-fouling blackwater.

Free Scab use:

- may be weaponized as ambush markers by those who know how not to follow them.

### 6. Chapel Brides

**Contradictory Account / Do Not Promote Yet**

A rumored variant growing inside old wrecked shelters, shrine-like ruins, or enclosed cargo hulls.

Primary hazard:

- creates the impression of a lit interior or occupied refuge.

Guardrail:

Do not canonize until story need justifies it.

## Ecology and Feeding

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

Corpse Lantern Brides may feed on:

- carcass nutrients
- drowned organic matter
- dead algae mats
- wreck sludge
- protein-rich sediment
- rotting cargo
- drowned timber
- old bone fields
- Black Spore Tar substrates
- mineral-rich flood deposits

Predatory strategy:

They do not directly consume prey immediately.

They lure prey into terrain where injury, drowning, predators, or exhaustion provide future nutrients.

This makes them a **delayed predatory fungus** or **trap-field fungus**, not a biting organism.
