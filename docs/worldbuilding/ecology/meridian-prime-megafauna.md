## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document establishes the first working set of Meridian Prime megafauna by major traversable region.

Because Meridian Prime's routes, basins, plateaus, and borders transform seasonally, each megafauna entry must answer the planet's central ecological question:

**What does this creature do when the roads drown?**

# 1. High Escarpment Continent

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: high-elevation crystal desert.

Wet season: snowfield / ice-locked highland.

## 1A. Glassback Mammoths

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive, shaggy, low-slung herbivores with translucent mineral plates along the spine and shoulders.

They graze on crystal lichens, wind-buried scrub, salt-mineral outcrops, and snowline growth.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- move in slow herds across crystal desert heights
- block highland convoy roads
- shatter fragile crystal crust with herd weight
- follow warm engine exhaust or salt spills
- can mistake fuel depots and coolant stations for mineral lick sites

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- grow dense insulating underfur
- migrate to wind-scoured ridgelines where snow is thinner
- use tusks / plated heads to break ice crust for lichen access
- enter semi-torpor during long whiteouts
- shelter calves in leeward crystal hollows

### Map Hazard Role

Herd corridors become seasonal road closures.

A Glassback migration band may force Prime Ops to reroute highland traffic even before the Dry Circuit descent begins.

### Cultural Interpretation

Meridian crews may treat them as stubborn road hazards.

Bought Nation route families may read their movement as snowfield weather prophecy.

World Works may classify them as **large ungulate obstruction events** and thereby earn everyone's hatred.

---

## 1B. Rime Condors

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Huge high-altitude scavenger-predators with reflective wing membranes, frost-resistant lungs, and cliff rookeries above the Great Cut.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- follow convoys expecting breakdowns
- circle repair stops and accident sites
- attack exposed crew during long stops
- drop stones, bones, or scavenged fragments onto smaller machines
- arrive at wreck sites before rescue teams do

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- ride snowstorm thermals
- retreat to cliff-cave rookeries
- feed on snowfield die-offs
- store carcasses in ice ledges
- huddle in communal frost roosts during multi-day storms

### Map Hazard Role

Condor circling patterns can reveal wrecks, weak ice, hidden dead, or predator concentration zones.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops hates them because they advertise failure.

Traverse crews say:

**If the rimes are waiting, the road already knows.**

# 2. The Great Cut / Cataract Ladder

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: canyon descent system.

Cataract season: roaring waterfall chain.

Wet season: icefall, chute, or sealed descent hazard.

## 2A. Cut Striders

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Tall, many-jointed canyon megafauna with hooked feet adapted to basalt, crystal, cliff walls, and switchback shelves.

Their silhouettes resemble walking scaffolds in fog.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- cross switchbacks at dangerous times
- kick loose crystalfall or rockfall
- use road cuts as migration shelves
- spook crews and descent animals
- graze mineral moss from canyon walls

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- climb above active cataracts
- shelter in high canyon caves
- feed on spray-fed lichens
- use cliffwall nesting ledges above floodline
- young cling to mineral nests or vertical crèches

### Map Hazard Role

Strider crossings mark cliff instability zones.

A map may show **strider ledges** as both animal paths and rockfall warning bands.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops sees them as route obstructions.

Local plateau guides may treat them as living weather vanes for canyon stability.

---

## 2B. Cataract Eels

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

Long armored amphibious megafauna that live in underground rivers during dry season and emerge when cataracts awaken.

Their existence may be better documented by damage patterns than direct sightings.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- rarely seen above ground
- tunnel through wet under-roads and subsurface channels
- create underground vibration mistaken for machinery
- may be drawn to coolant spills, warm drainage, or floodgate seepage
- can collapse apparently dry route segments from beneath

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- ride flood channels down the Great Cut
- spawn in temporary cataract pools
- hunt in high-energy runoff channels
- retreat underground or into deep flow pockets as waters stabilize

### Map Hazard Role

A dry road above eel tunnels may not be structurally dry.

Hydrology maps should mark eel-channel suspicion as collapse risk.

### Cultural Interpretation

World Works may deny direct evidence until a road disappears.

NCI mechanics call them **the Cut's wiring with teeth**.

# 3. Prime Plateau / The National Park / Green Hell

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: temperate cloud rainforest staging shelf.

Wet season: snowline cloudforest / lower routes closed.

## 3A. Cloudwood Grazer-Titans

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Huge browsing animals that move through the cloudforest canopy and understory, feeding on hanging lichens, moss mats, soft crystal-bark growth, and fog-fed canopy fruit.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- knock canopy limbs onto roads
- block fog routes
- trigger root slides
- attract predators near convoy camps
- create broad animal tracks that may look like useful alternate roads

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- climb or migrate to upper fog ridges
- enter slow metabolic **mist sleep** during cold wet periods
- feed on snow-bent canopy growth
- shelter young inside hollow crystalwood trunks or root caverns

### Map Hazard Role

Their trails become alternate paths, but only locals know which are safe.

A Titan trail may bypass a washed-out road or lead straight into root collapse.

### Cultural Interpretation

Local plateau people may treat them as forest road-makers.

Prime Ops treats them as canopy-load hazards.

World Works may try to model them and fail because they do not file movement plans.

---

## 3B. Fogback Ambushers

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Large, low, soft-footed predators with heat-sensing facial pits and mosslike dorsal camouflage.

They hunt in fog belts and near engine-warmth zones.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- stalk repair crews
- hide near idling vehicles
- follow oil, coolant, blood, and warm metal scent
- use foghorns, machinery, and cataract roar to mask approach
- occupy camps that look operationally excellent on paper

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- retreat into warm root-caves
- hunt snow-stalled smaller fauna
- den near geothermal vents, decomposing vegetation pockets, or old coolant dumps
- become less mobile but more territorial

### Map Hazard Role

Fogback territories make certain camps unusable despite good infrastructure.

### Cultural Interpretation

Traverse crews treat them as proof that the Green Hell listens.

Local saying:

**If the fog has a back, it already saw yours.**

# 4. Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit Lowlands

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: exposed basin floor, dry river roads, paleochannels.

Wet season: inland sea / oceanic basin.

## 4A. Basin Rollers

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Enormous armored, low-profile grazers or scavengers that curl, brace, or hunker against haboon winds and move across dry basin flats like living boulders.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- crush route markers
- collapse thin hydrocrystal crust
- wander onto Dry Circuit tracks
- resemble terrain on bad scans
- may shelter in dry washes during heat extremes

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- migrate to exposed ridges and shallow islands
- enter buoyant dormancy in mineral-mud wallows
- some float like armored rafts, drifting until they ground again
- feed on flood-bloom mats in shallow water

### Map Hazard Role

A boulder field may become mobile overnight.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops marks them as moving terrain hazards.

Free Scabs sometimes use Roller drift as cover for smuggling.

---

## 4B. Paleochannel Gulpers

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive amphibious ambush-feeders that bury themselves in dry riverbeds during dry season, sealed in mineral mucus until moisture returns.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- lie dormant under dry river roads
- create soft sink zones
- wake early due to convoy vibration, coolant spills, or underground seepage
- breathing vents may be mistaken for rock cracks
- jaws can open under thin crust without warning

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- become fully aquatic during basin flooding
- hunt in submerged Dry Circuit channels
- spawn in inland sea shallows
- retreat into mud chambers during drawdown

### Map Hazard Role

Some dry river roads are sleeping mouths.

Route maps should mark Gulper beds as both collapse and predation hazards.

### Cultural Interpretation

NCI old hands say:

**Never trust a riverbed that forgot how to echo.**

# 5. Hydropolis / Mirror Basin Urban-Wet Zone

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: basin-floor vertical city and dry canals.

Wet season: moored floating city, tower traffic, water lanes.

## 5A. Mooring Leviathans

**Candidate Canon Seed / Development Apocrypha**

Huge slow aquatic animals that arrive with wet-season waters and navigate by low-frequency vibration around Hydropolis' mooring towers.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- absent from exposed city routes
- represented by dried skeletons, old scars, civic warning markers, and tower-foundation damage records
- may persist as eggs, larvae, or deep-water juveniles in unmapped basin depressions if later approved

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- fully aquatic
- circle deep tower foundations
- feed on basin bloom organisms
- use Hydropolis mooring hum, tower vibrations, or city shadow as orientation markers
- may mistake engine resonance for mating or territorial signals

### Map Hazard Role

Hydropolis must route vessels around leviathan drift paths.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis may treat them less as animals and more as seasonal civic conditions.

Civic phrase:

**The towers rise, and the old bodies return.**

---

## 5B. Glasswake Rays

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Broad, kite-shaped megafauna with translucent mineral cartilage that skim wet-season shallows and submerged streets.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- adults absent from dry streets
- eggs or mineralized nursery plates remain in dry canal beds
- disturbing nursery plates may trigger legal, ecological, or civic penalties
- dry canal crews mark dormant nurseries before road use

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- emerge with floodwaters
- migrate through Mirror Basin shallows
- use Hydropolis' shadow, signal hum, or tower reflections as orientation markers
- feed on suspended bloom organisms and small channel fauna

### Map Hazard Role

Ray nurseries become protected navigation exclusions and dry-season road restrictions.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis likely treats them as protected seasonal citizens of water, not pests.

World Works calls them **mobility interference fauna** and should be booed by every sensible person in the room.

# 6. The Eschar Slough

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: exposed scabland, dead flood terrain, Free Scab bandit country.

Wet season: pirate archipelago / blackwater channels.

## 6A. Blackwake Crocs

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Huge armored semi-aquatic predators with dark mineral plating and pressure-sensitive jaw ridges.

They haunt blackwater channels during wet season and mud cracks during dry season.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- hide in cracked slough pits
- guard muddy cisterns
- ambush animals and people near hidden water
- may be mistaken for basalt debris or wreck fragments

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- dominate blackwater channels
- follow pirate traffic for scraps
- nest in floating reed-crystal mats
- overturn small craft
- occupy channels Free Scabs either avoid or weaponize

### Map Hazard Role

Free Scab pirate lanes avoid some croc territories and weaponize others.

### Cultural Interpretation

Free Scabs respect them as border guards that cannot be bribed.

Prime Ops treats them as proof the Slough is not worth entering.

---

## 6B. Slough Humpbacks

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive amphibious filter-feeders or mud-grazers that look like low islands when resting.

Their backs collect reeds, salt crust, wreck debris, and mineral growth.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- mistaken for terrain
- can rise under vehicles
- create false islands and false roads
- may conceal old wrecks, nests, or Free Scab myths

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- become fully buoyant
- drift through blackwater channels
- feed on flood blooms
- ground themselves on high ridges during drawdown
- become living hazards to navigation

### Map Hazard Role

Some islands in the Eschar Slough should be marked as unconfirmed.

### Cultural Interpretation

Free Scabs may exaggerate them deliberately so outsiders fear every island.

Or worse: the exaggerations are understatements.

# 7. Anticlinal Craton Interior / Geode Mountain

**Region Status:** RELIGIOUS TRADITION / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: geode mountain stronghold.

Wet season: geode island stronghold.

## 7A. Thunderback Behemoths

**Religious Tradition / Candidate Canon Seed**

Huge Craton-native animals with resonant body cavities, heavy skull crests, and pressure-sensitive footpads.

They move slowly but can produce ground-shaking calls.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- move along sacred pressure corridors
- block routes through weight, not aggression
- trigger rockfalls if startled
- produce calls outsiders may mistake for geological events
- graze mineral lichens, pressure-warmed fungi, or geode-edge growth

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- climb to geode terraces above floodline
- shelter in pressure-warm caverns
- enter low-movement resonance torpor
- rely on stored fat and mineral-rich lichens
- young may be hidden in guarded resonance hollows

### Map Hazard Role

Their migration routes are sacred pressure corridors, not just animal paths.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton interpretation:

They are elder weight.

Outsider mistake:

Treating them as livestock-scale wildlife instead of moving sacred geography.

---

## 7B. Geode Crown Raptors

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Large pack-hunting saurian aerial or gliding predators adapted to crystal cliffs, geode caverns, and high basalt updrafts.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- nest near gates and geode ledges
- attack drones, climbers, exposed scouts, and careless survey teams
- may be tolerated by Craton guards as natural perimeter defense
- use reflective mineral crests for pack signaling

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- retreat to high cliff nests
- hunt wet-season dock approaches
- scavenge shipwrecks along Blackstep cliffs
- ride storm updrafts around basalt shelves
- prey on smaller wet-season birds, fish, and landing carrion

### Map Hazard Role

Raptor nesting zones become no-drone and no-climb areas.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton gate-guards may read raptor agitation as warning.

World Works may classify them as a drone-loss problem, which is how one tells when a soul has become a spreadsheet.

# 8. Blackstep Province / Craton Basalt Apron

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: exposed basalt terraces and threshold roads.

Wet season: docking reefs and sacred wet gates.

## 8A. Basalt Tuskers

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive cliff-footed herbivore / omnivore megafauna with tusks adapted to scraping mineral algae, basalt lichen, reef growth, and step-shelf vegetation.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- occupy basalt terraces
- damage dry docking roads
- charge at certain engine vibrations
- scrape pressure markers if desperate or stressed
- leave tusk scars that can be mistaken for tool marks

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- retreat to high basalt shelves
- swim short channels between steps
- feed on wet basalt algae and reef mats
- raise young on inaccessible ledges
- use no-anchor zones as accidental refuges

### Map Hazard Role

Tusker ledges may close otherwise usable wet gates.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton observers may treat Tusker behavior as pressure feedback.

Prime Ops sees dock closure.

The Craton sees warning in weight.

---

## 8B. Reef-Hammer Drakes

**Development Apocrypha / Candidate Canon Seed**

Large semi-aquatic reptilian or saurian predators with hammerlike skulls used to crack shell-creatures, reef armor, and sometimes small hulls.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- dormant or sluggish in basalt caves
- guard shaded pools
- attack crews near wet-dock repairs
- occupy lava tubes that crews mistake for useful storage

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- become active marine predators
- patrol basalt reefs
- follow ships toward docking terraces
- nest in flooded lava tubes
- exploit waves and basalt teeth to trap prey

### Map Hazard Role

Wet-season docking charts must mark drake reefs.

### Cultural Interpretation

Free Scabs may use drake territory as a natural ambush wall.

Craton maps may treat them as part of the dock's refusal rather than an external hazard.

# 9. Lower Basin River Corridors / Dry River Roads

**Region Status:** DEVELOPMENT APOCRYPHA / CANDIDATE CANON SEED

Dry season: dry river roads, paleochannels, underground water traces.

Wet season: channels, inland seas, submerged route ghosts.

## 9A. Shardhorn Waders

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Tall, heavy wetland grazers with blade-like mineral horns and long legs.

They appear in great numbers during transition seasons.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- rare or scattered
- skeleton trails mark old water paths
- desperate individuals may follow convoys for water
- horn-scrapes on crystal reeds or dry banks indicate future channel routes

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- migrate with rising channels
- browse submerged crystal reeds
- form defensive circles in shallow water
- use horn fans to break surface crust or floating mineral mats

### Map Hazard Role

Their movement predicts which dry roads will become channels first.

### Cultural Interpretation

Route crews may watch them as living flood gauges.

Hydropolis may track them as wetland-cycle indicators.

---

## 9B. Mudvault Tortoises

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Gigantic shelled basin creatures that seal themselves under mud / crystal crust during dry season.

### Dry-Season Behavior

- shells create false stable ground
- vehicles can crack their vaults and trigger sudden movement
- locals may use shell hum to detect underground water
- their buried forms distort GPR readings

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- emerge and swim slowly through inland seas
- feed on aquatic vegetation and mineral bloom
- lay eggs in high drawdown berms
- return to mud vaults as waters retreat

### Map Hazard Role

Mudvault fields are both water indicators and collapse hazards.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops dislikes them because they make scans argue with themselves.

Bought Nation route families may treat old shell fields as inherited water knowledge.

## Cross-Region Ecological Notes

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

The same seasonal flood cycle that transforms territory also transforms animal behavior.

Potential ecological patterns:

- highland megafauna use ridgelines, snow hollows, and torpor
- canyon megafauna use vertical escape and underground channels
- cloudforest megafauna use fog, root systems, and canopy shelter
- basin megafauna use dormancy, buoyancy, islanding, or amphibious phases
- Hydropolis-associated megafauna use mooring towers, signal hum, and basin bloom cycles
- Eschar Slough megafauna become part of pirate navigation and concealment
- Craton megafauna are interpreted through pressure, threshold, and sacred weight
- Blackstep megafauna turn docking into a living hazard system
