**Development Apocrypha**

This document introduces the fictional black market fauna-based pharmacopoeia of Meridian Prime.

It is the dirty mirror of the legal food, medicine, mushroom, and ritual substance layers.

This is not monster-part potion logic.

It is:

- desperation medicine
- poached sacred bodies
- counterfeit cure culture
- battlefield triage
- pirate stimulants
- illicit sensory enhancers
- civic-origin violations
- corporate deniable research
- labor medicine arriving too late through the wrong door

# Substance Catalogue

## 1. Glassback Plate Dust

**Source:** Glassback Mammoth mineral plates, usually shed, scavenged, or illegally carved.

**Claimed Use:** bone-setting aid, frost resilience, endurance support.

**Likely Truth:** useful mostly as mineral supplement, ritual comfort, or local expert treatment. Black market claims are often inflated.

**Market:** Bought Nation highland camps, Prime Ops injury crews, World Works ration fraud.

**Legal Status:** restricted harvest. Poaching Glassbacks is heavily punished by route families.

**Story Use:** a convoy medic must decide whether to use illegal Plate Dust from a dead calf.

Moral note:

Shed plate is one thing. Carved plate is another. Calf plate is a wound with paperwork still trying to catch up.

---

## 2. Rime Condor Lung-Salts

**Source:** Rime Condor respiratory tissues or nest salts, often taken from rookeries.

**Claimed Use:** altitude breathing, cold-air stamina, panic control.

**Likely Truth:** dangerous and often counterfeit. Legitimate nest-mineral versions may help with fictional altitude sickness, but organ-derived versions are black-market quackery.

**Market:** high escarpment smugglers, illegal climbers, desperate descent teams.

**Legal Status:** rookery violation. Prime Ops bans it. Route families curse it.

Best line:

**Condors find the dead. They should not be made to sell breath to the living.**

---

## 3. Cut Strider Tendon Thread

**Source:** shed or poached Cut Strider tendon fibers.

**Claimed Use:** surgical stitching, brace cord, joint stabilization.

**Likely Truth:** excellent fictional medical suture material when shed-certified and cleaned through legitimate channels. Black market versions cause infection, rejection, or canyon fever.

**Market:** canyon medics, smugglers, NCI emergency kits.

**Legal Status:** legal if shed-certified; illegal if poached.

**Story Use:** a black market field surgeon saves someone with illegal Strider Thread, then gets hunted by procurement auditors. The clipboard grows teeth.

---

## 4. Cataract Eel Shock-Gel

**Source:** mucus or gel from Cataract Eels, usually gathered after flood events or from poached specimens.

**Claimed Use:** nerve pain relief, muscle reactivation, shock recovery.

**Likely Truth:** unpredictable fictional neuromuscular stimulant/depressant depending on condition, season, and handling.

**Market:** Great Cut, Dry Circuit trauma crews, outlaw fighters.

**Legal Status:** route-prohibited before descent. Dangerous around machinery.

**Story Use:** a mechanic uses it to keep hands working during an emergency repair, then loses fine motor control at the worst possible moment.

---

## 5. Fogback Pit-Oil

**Source:** facial pit secretions or glandular oils from Fogback Ambushers.

**Claimed Use:** heat-sensing enhancement, stealth, predator masking, fever detection.

**Likely Truth:** mostly illegal scent / thermal masking used by poachers, Free Scab scouts, and foolish outsiders.

**Market:** Prime Plateau, Green Hell smugglers, Free Scab guides.

**Legal Status:** banned in Charterhold and Ledger Falls because it attracts or confuses predators.

Best description:

Not a drug. A predator contract written on your skin.

---

## 6. Basin Roller Mud-Fat

**Source:** mineralized fat and mud from Basin Roller dormancy wallows.

**Claimed Use:** joint balm, burn protection, haboon skin sealant.

**Likely Truth:** some legitimate use as protective grease, but black market batches are often contaminated with Saltmold, spore residue, or carcass rot.

**Market:** Dry Circuit convoys, Free Yarders, lowland camps.

**Legal Status:** tolerated if wallow-harvested; illegal if animal-harvested.

**Story Use:** a ration depot sells contaminated Mud-Fat and a whole crew develops skin rot before a haboon run.

---

## 7. Paleochannel Gulper Bile

**Source:** Gulper digestive compounds, almost always poached or scavenged.

**Claimed Use:** corrosive wound cleaning, poison antidote, truth purge.

**Likely Truth:** extremely dangerous fictional corrosive medical fraud. Mostly criminal intimidation material.

**Market:** Free Scab Dust Tithe, black clinics, World Works deniable labs.

**Legal Status:** contraband everywhere sane.

Name stack:

- Prime Ops: **Prohibited Caustic Faunal Derivative**
- Free Scabs: **Mouth-Gold**
- NCI: **No. Absolutely not. Put that jar back.**

---

## 8. Glasswake Ray Cartilage Jelly

**Source:** shed cartilage, nursery plates, or illegally harvested Glasswake Rays.

**Claimed Use:** burn treatment, signal-sensitivity calming, joint repair.

**Likely Truth:** legitimate Hydropolis medicine may use verified shed material. Black market versions are civic crime, ecological harm, and possible identity-contamination risk.

**Market:** Hydropolis underground clinics, World Works biomedical smugglers.

**Legal Status:** Hydropolis verified-origin only.

Hydropolis principle:

**A medicine without origin is an injury with better manners.**

---

## 9. Mooring Leviathan Resonance Oil

**Source:** oil, wax, or tissue allegedly from Mooring Leviathans.

**Claimed Use:** calming panic, treating vibration sickness, hearing the towers.

**Likely Truth:** most is fake. Real material, if it exists in circulation, would be wildly illegal and politically explosive.

**Market:** Hydropolis cultish underground, wealthy collectors, corporate labs.

**Legal Status:** sacred / civic / ecological catastrophe.

**Story Use:** someone sells fake Leviathan oil. Then one vial turns out real.

---

## 10. Blackwake Croc Jaw-Salts

**Source:** mineral deposits from Blackwake Croc jaw ridges or nesting sites.

**Claimed Use:** pain dulling, courage, bite-strength rituals, pirate initiation.

**Likely Truth:** mild stimulant or pain-numbing folklore at best; often mixed with worse substances.

**Market:** Eschar Slough, Free Scab raiders, pirate crews.

**Legal Status:** illegal in Prime Ops territory; tolerated in the Slough if not stolen from another crew's marked channel.

Free Scab saying:

**Do not buy courage from something that eats boats.**

---

## 11. Slough Humpback Filter-Milk

**Source:** filtered secretions or bloom-rich residues from Slough Humpbacks.

**Claimed Use:** detox, hunger suppression, fever treatment.

**Likely Truth:** possibly useful in small fictional medical contexts if verified, but black market versions are usually swamp fraud, bloom contamination, or slow poison.

**Market:** Free Scab black apothecaries, desperate wet-season refugees.

**Legal Status:** uncertain because some authorities deny Slough Humpbacks are reliably real.

**Story Use:** a fake cure becomes valuable because belief in it reveals who survived the Slough.

---

## 12. Thunderback Resonance Marrow

**Source:** Thunderback Behemoth bone or marrow, almost always taboo-poached.

**Claimed Use:** strength, pressure endurance, sacred hearing, warrior recovery.

**Likely Truth:** deeply illegal, spiritually obscene to the Craton, medically misunderstood by outsiders.

**Market:** World Works black research, elite collectors, anti-Craton smugglers.

**Legal Status:** absolute Craton taboo.

Craton response:

Not a fine. Not a warning. A threshold closes forever.

Best line:

**Some contraband is not illegal because it is dangerous. It is dangerous because it was stolen from a sacred animal and then named medicine.**

---

## 13. Geode Crown Raptor Crest-Powder

**Source:** shed crest flakes or poached raptor crests.

**Claimed Use:** focus, predator vision, drone-pilot reflexes, intimidation rites.

**Likely Truth:** mostly stimulant fraud, sometimes toxic mineral contamination.

**Market:** drone crews, scouts, mercenaries, Free Scab duelists.

**Legal Status:** Craton no-drone / no-climb zone violation if sourced from nests.

**Story Use:** counterfeit Crest-Powder causes hallucinated movement, making a scout shoot at shadows.

---

## 14. Basalt Tusker Ledge-Grease

**Source:** naturally rubbed oils and minerals from Tusker ledges, or illegal tissue rendering.

**Claimed Use:** dockhand joint balm, hull-rub protection, pressure bruise treatment.

**Likely Truth:** legitimate ledge-gathered form may be useful; animal-rendered black market form is illegal and hated by dock crews.

**Market:** Blackstep dock workers, smugglers, wet-gate crews.

**Legal Status:** permitted only if non-poached and locally controlled.

Blackstep dock saying:

**If the ledge gave it, use it. If the knife took it, sink it.**

---

## 15. Reef-Hammer Drake Tooth Oil

**Source:** tooth pulp, jaw glands, or fake substitutes.

**Claimed Use:** painkiller, fighting drug, hull-breach courage.

**Likely Truth:** dangerous depressant / stimulant folklore, widely adulterated.

**Market:** pirates, dock gangs, reckless convoy guards.

**Legal Status:** contraband near Blackstep and Hydropolis lanes.

**Story Use:** someone high on Drake Tooth Oil thinks he can swim a black reef. The reef writes the obituary.

---

## 16. Shardhorn Wader Horn-Shavings

**Source:** naturally shed horn fragments or poached horn.

**Claimed Use:** fertility, flood-sense, blood strengthening, water prediction.

**Likely Truth:** folk medicine, mostly symbolic. Some shed horn may be used in legal flood rites.

**Market:** river camps, Bought Nation route families, counterfeit sellers.

**Legal Status:** legal if shed-collected; poaching treated as flood-season sabotage.

**Story Use:** low-stakes folk medicine becomes high-stakes when drought makes people desperate.

---

## 17. Mudvault Shell-Lacquer

**Source:** shed shell flakes, old empty shells, or illegal live shell damage.

**Claimed Use:** waterproofing wounds, sealing gear, preserving medicines.

**Likely Truth:** legitimate material culture, but black market versions cause infections if used medically.

**Market:** lower river corridors, convoy crews, Free Scab boatmen.

**Legal Status:** legal as craft material, restricted as medical product.

**Story Use:** a fake Shell-Lacquer seal ruins an entire medicine cache.

# Black Market Product Classes

## A. Desperation Medicine

**Development Apocrypha**

Used when official care is absent.

Examples:

- Glassback Plate Dust
- Cut Strider Tendon Thread
- Basin Roller Mud-Fat
- Glasswake Ray Cartilage Jelly
- Mudvault Shell-Lacquer

Tone:

Sad, practical, morally gray.

## B. Route Performance Drugs

**Development Apocrypha**

Used to keep crews moving beyond safe limits.

Examples:

- Rime Condor Lung-Salts
- Cataract Eel Shock-Gel
- Geode Crown Raptor Crest-Powder
- adulterated Shardwake products

Tone:

Labor exploitation wearing a stimulant mask.

## C. Pirate and Raider Pharmacopoeia

**Development Apocrypha**

Used by Free Scabs, Dust Tithe, smugglers, wet-season raiders, and dock gangs.

Examples:

- Blackwake Croc Jaw-Salts
- Reef-Hammer Drake Tooth Oil
- Slough Humpback Filter-Milk
- Paleochannel Gulper Bile / Mouth-Gold

Tone:

Half medicine, half threat, half lie. The Slough does accounting with knives.

## D. Sacred Body Contraband

**Religious Tradition / Do Not Promote**

The darkest category.

Examples:

- Thunderback Resonance Marrow
- Geode Crown Raptor nest material
- unauthorized Craton beast products
- pressure-corridor fauna derivatives

Tone:

Not illegal drug. Desecration disguised as therapy.

## E. Civic-Origin Violations

**Political Development / Scientific Hypothesis**

Hydropolis-specific or Hydropolis-adjacent.

Examples:

- Glasswake Ray Cartilage Jelly
- Mooring Leviathan Resonance Oil
- tower-associated fauna products
- unverified wet-zone animal derivatives

Tone:

Contamination, identity risk, ecological crime.
