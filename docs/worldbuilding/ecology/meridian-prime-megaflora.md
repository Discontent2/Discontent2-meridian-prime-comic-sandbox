## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document establishes the first working set of Meridian Prime mega-flora by major traversable region.

If megafauna answers:

**What moves when the roads drown?**

Then mega-flora answers:

**What remains rooted when the world changes shape around it?**

---

## Core Mega-Flora Design Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime mega-flora entry is not complete until it documents:

| Required Detail | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Dry-season form | How crews, routes, settlements, or scavengers encounter it when land is exposed |
| Wet-season survival strategy | How it survives flooding, snow burial, drowning, or seasonal transformation |
| Travel / settlement effect | How it changes movement, docking, logging, signal flow, shelter, or hazard |
| Cultural interpretation | Who claims, fears, harvests, protects, or worships it |

Core line:

**When the roads drown, the animals move. The plants decide what remains.**

# 1. High Escarpment Continent

Dry season: high-elevation crystal desert.

Wet season: snowfield / ice-locked highland.

## 1A. Shardroot Spires

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Giant crystalline root-columns that rise from mineral-rich hardpan. From a distance they resemble dead glass trees, though most living tissue lies underground.

### Dry-Season Form

- act as windbreaks in the crystal desert
- create hidden root hollows under route crust
- attract Glassback Mammoths to mineral moisture pockets
- serve as route landmarks and false shelter points

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- retract exposed living membranes beneath ice and snow
- outer spires become snowbreaks and navigation markers
- inner sap slows into antifreeze resin
- deep roots retain pressure and water memory below the freeze line

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Useful landmark, hidden collapse risk.

### Cultural Interpretation

Bought Nation route families may mark old Shardroot Spires as **water memory**.

---

## 1B. Rimebloom Mats

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive lichen-like colonies spread across high ridges and snow shelves, dull and mineral-gray in dry periods but faintly heat-active under snow.

### Dry-Season Form

- appear brittle and dead
- may shatter under heavy engines
- often mistaken for harmless crust
- can foul traction when powdered

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- activate beneath snow cover
- produce localized thermal pockets
- feed Glassback Mammoths and smaller snowfield fauna
- preserve living tissue through dense insulated layers

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Snowfield survival zone and vehicle sink hazard.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops may misread them as thermal anomalies.

Old route crews read them as signs that snow is thinner than it looks.

# 2. Great Cut / Cataract Ladder

Dry season: canyon descent.

Cataract season: roaring water ladder.

Wet season: chute / icefall / sealed hazard.

## 2A. Cataract Beards

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive hanging root-vine systems growing from canyon walls, nourished by mist, mineral spray, and seepage.

### Dry-Season Form

- dry into rope-like tangles
- hide side-canyons and blind ledges
- snag equipment and vehicles
- used illegally as descent or climbing aids by smugglers

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- swell with water and become living curtains
- stabilize themselves through wall-clasping root fans
- preserve seeds in mineral pods
- in cold periods freeze into root-ice sheets without fully dying

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Marks future waterfall paths before water returns.

### Cultural Interpretation

Locals may read Beard thickness as an omen of how violent cataract season will be.

---

## 2B. Echo Fern Towers

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Tall fernlike canyon plants with hollow mineral stems that resonate under wind, flood pressure, and canyon acoustics.

### Dry-Season Form

- emit tones in canyon winds
- reveal airflow and pressure shifts
- interfere with acoustic sensing
- cluster in side pockets and ledge basins

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- bend flat under torrent force
- rise again as waters recede
- release spores during cataract thunder
- root tightly into wet mineral cracks

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Natural early-warning system, if locals know how to listen.

### Cultural Interpretation

NCI saying:

**If the ferns stop singing, get out of the Cut.**

# 3. Prime Plateau / The National Park / Green Hell

Dry season: temperate cloud rainforest staging shelf.

Wet season: snowline cloudforest / lower routes closed.

## 3A. Cloudwood Giants

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Enormous crystal-veined cloudforest trees that harvest fog through canopy webs and root into deep moisture systems.

### Dry-Season Form

- define canopy roads and shadow corridors
- buckle pavement with root pressure
- drop limbs onto Traverse routes
- form the valuable timber source behind Ledger Falls

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- hold snow on layered canopy shelves
- channel meltwater into root cisterns
- harden bark into frost-resistant outer armor
- protect understory moisture refuges

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Canopy density, fog catchment, road damage, logging conflict.

### Cultural Interpretation

To Ledger Falls they are cargo.

To Reclaimers they are a political wound.

To the Green Hell they are a cathedral roof.

---

## 3B. Mossvaults

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive sponge-like moss colonies deep enough to swallow people, animals, or machines.

### Dry-Season Form

- resemble stable green ground
- conceal wrecks, road edges, and root pits
- store emergency water
- collapse under uneven weight

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- swell into floating or semi-floating bog shelves
- insulate roots and burrows
- preserve warm underlayers
- become favored Fogback Ambusher territory

### Travel / Settlement Effect

False ground, water reserve, predator habitat.

### Cultural Interpretation

Local saying:

**The Green Hell has floors that drink.**

# 4. Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit Lowlands

Dry season: exposed basin floor, paleochannels.

Wet season: inland sea / oceanic basin.

## 4A. Saltwake Reeds

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Giant mineral reeds that persist as dormant root systems beneath dry basin crust and erupt upward when water returns.

### Dry-Season Form

- buried as reed-stubs under dry channels
- puncture tires, boots, and soft vehicle skins
- mark hidden water tables
- collect salt and dust into misleading low ridges

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- rise into tall reed forests
- create navigation mazes
- shelter nurseries and wetland fauna
- anchor silt and flood bloom drift

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Dry hazard becomes wet maze.

### Cultural Interpretation

Dry-season crews curse them.

Wet-season navigators fear them more.

---

## 4B. Bloom-Sargassum

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Floating mega-algal mats appearing during wet season in warm nutrient-rich basin water.

### Dry-Season Form

- survives as desiccated black sheets in low depressions
- can foul filters when powdered
- looks like stain, ash, or dead bloom memory

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- regrows into floating fields
- traps debris, carcasses, and small craft
- feeds Mooring Leviathans and Slough Humpbacks
- shifts with currents and thermal gradients

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Wet-season navigation obstruction and food-web engine.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis may regulate it as if it were semi-civic seasonal infrastructure.

# 5. Hydropolis / Mirror Basin Urban-Wet Zone

Dry season: basin-floor city and dry canals.

Wet season: moored vertical city.

## 5A. Signal Kelp

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Semi-aquatic mega-flora growing around submerged signal infrastructure, mooring towers, and canal routes.

### Dry-Season Form

- persists as black-veined root plates in dry canals
- marked, protected, or restricted by Hydropolis
- may accumulate signal residue in civic myth or sensor practice

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- grows long translucent ribbons
- attenuates, redirects, or scatters signal fields
- creates sheltered channels for smaller life
- thickens around tower shadows and stable hum zones

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Signal interference plus ecological protection zone.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis civic view:

**Not weed. Witness.**

---

## 5B. Moor-Lilies

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Huge floating bloom structures that anchor to submerged architecture and tower-shadow current lines.

### Dry-Season Form

- survive as sealed seed-discs in dry basins and canal beds
- disturbing dormant discs may be a civic offense
- often mapped before road reuse

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- unfold into floating platforms
- support micro-ecosystems
- cluster where depth, stillness, and shadow align
- mark old streets, hidden shallows, or dangerous depths

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Wet-season shallow markers and protected civic flora.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis may treat them as public ecological markers rather than mere plants.

# 6. The Eschar Slough

Dry season: scabland, dead flood terrain.

Wet season: pirate archipelago / blackwater channels.

## 6A. Blackwake Mangroves

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Twisted mineral-root mega-flora with black bark and hooked roots adapted to both drought exposure and drowning.

### Dry-Season Form

- appear dead or half-burned
- roots form ambush mazes
- conceal hidden water and Free Scab caches
- cut boots, tires, and exposed skin

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- roots become boat traps and hidden channels
- stabilize pirate coves and blackwater margins
- host Blackwake Crocs and other dangerous life
- grow denser in nutrient-rich sheltered channels

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Natural fortification and smuggler concealment.

### Cultural Interpretation

Free Scab view:

**Not forest. Fortress.**

---

## 6B. Eschar Bloom

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A black-red seasonal bloom spreading over Slough waters like oil, though fully living.

### Dry-Season Form

- persists as flaky black crust on scabland flats
- masks unstable ground
- powders into staining residue when crushed

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- blooms across blackwater channels
- obscures depth changes and debris
- clogs engines and fouls filters
- conceals movement from distant visual reading and some scans

### Travel / Settlement Effect

False water surface and visibility distortion.

### Cultural Interpretation

Best line:

**The Slough does not hide its pirates. It grows over them.**

# 7. Anticlinal Craton Interior / Geode Mountain

Dry season: geode mountain.

Wet season: geode island.

## 7A. Pressure Cedars

**Religious Tradition / Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive slow-growing Craton trees with compressed trunks and root systems responsive to pressure shifts.

### Dry-Season Form

- stabilize geode slopes
- mark sacred pressure corridors
- bark cracking may warn of geological stress
- shade pressure-warm hollows and migration paths

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- seal bark against saturation
- contract roots into fissures and stone seams
- collect fog and rain for high-terrace ecosystems
- preserve deep-root tension even in saturated ground

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Sacred slope stabilizer and pressure-warning flora.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton interpretation:

Not trees, but old listeners.

---

## 7B. Geode Orchards

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Crystal-fruited mega-flora cultivated or protected in geode terraces and pressure-warm hollows.

### Dry-Season Form

- produce ritualized food stores
- feed Craton communities and some megafauna
- reflect mineral light in striking ways
- may indicate hidden terraces or tended space

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- retreat into enclosed geode garden systems
- rely on sheltered pollination webs
- outer groves become inaccessible to outsiders
- fruit cycles may synchronize with pressure-warm interior conditions

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Food sovereignty and sacred interior indicator.

### Cultural Interpretation

Should be under-mapped due to Craton no-map logic.

# 8. Blackstep Province / Craton Basalt Apron

Dry season: exposed basalt terraces.

Wet season: docking reefs and sacred wet gates.

## 8A. Basalt Mats

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Hard dark reeflike plant colonies spread over lava benches and basalt shelves.

### Dry-Season Form

- appear as black crust
- conceal cracks and lava-tube openings
- become dangerously slick when damp
- collect dust that softens visual contrast

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- become living reef surfaces
- feed Basalt Tuskers
- mineral edges harden under tide and spray
- cling to steep stone where waves break hardest

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Dock hazard, reef ecology, Tusker feeding ground.

### Cultural Interpretation

To outsiders they look like dead black growth.

To the Craton they may be part of the dock's living refusal.

---

## 8B. Anchor Thorn Kelp

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Wet-season giant kelp-like flora anchored to basalt shelves with thorny holdfasts.

### Dry-Season Form

- dormant holdfasts remain as black hooks in terrace cracks
- dangerous to feet, tires, ropes, and repair crews
- often missed until too late

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- grows into thick kelp barriers
- tangles anchors and hull edges
- hides Reef-Hammer Drakes
- strengthens around forbidden docks and slow water

### Travel / Settlement Effect

No-anchor zones become literally entangling.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton phrase:

**The dock refuses twice: stone first, root second.**

---

# 9. Lower Basin River Corridors / Dry River Roads

Dry season: roads.

Wet season: river / inland-sea channels.

## 9A. Channelbone Willows

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Pale skeletal trees growing along underground water lines and dry paleochannels.

### Dry-Season Form

- leafless and white
- mark buried water
- roots undermine roads
- branches catch route flags, bones, and flood debris

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- leaf suddenly into silver-green canopy
- bend with floodwater
- root strongly into saturated banks
- shelter waders, smaller fauna, and small craft eddies

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Water predictor and road-undermining hazard.

### Cultural Interpretation

Route crews may read them as living flood memory.

---

## 9B. Floodwake Lotus

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Huge aquatic plants whose seeds lie dormant in dry mud for years until floodwater returns.

### Dry-Season Form

- seed pods hard as stones
- damage tires if exposed
- mark old flood basins
- often overlooked until a route is built over them

### Wet-Season Survival Strategy

- unfold into large floating leaves
- create dense lotus fields
- attract megafauna and smaller wetland life
- spread rapidly in sheltered flood basins

### Travel / Settlement Effect

Wet-season passage obstruction and wildlife congregation zone.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis may read bloom spread as a seasonal basin health index.

## Cross-Region Ecological Notes

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

Potential broad ecological patterns:

- highland mega-flora stores moisture and resists snow burial
- canyon mega-flora turns wind, mist, and sound into warning systems
- cloudforest mega-flora becomes both resource and trap
- basin mega-flora alternates between drought-memory and flood-expansion
- Hydropolis-associated flora interferes with signal, law, and navigation
- Eschar Slough flora behaves like concealment, fortification, and piracy ecology
- Craton flora is inseparable from sacred geography
- Blackstep flora makes docking a living hazard system
