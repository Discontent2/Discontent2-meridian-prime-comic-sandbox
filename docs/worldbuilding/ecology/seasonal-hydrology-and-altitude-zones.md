## Meridian Prime Year Length

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A full Meridian Prime seasonal cycle currently lasts approximately **four Earth years**.

Official Meridian Prime calendar units, year names, month systems, and local timekeeping remain undeveloped.

Working approximation:

| Season | Approximate Earth Duration | Core State |
|---|---:|---|
| **Dry Season** | 2 Earth years | exposed basin roads, high desert escarpment, grounded Hydropolis, Craton as geode mountain |
| **Spring / Cataract Season** | 1 Earth year | meltwater, cataracts, return of rivers, route instability |
| **Wet Season** | 2 Earth years | escarpment snowfield, Hydropolis afloat / moored, Craton as geode island, basin ocean state |
| **Fall / Drawdown Season** | 1 Earth year | waters retreat, inland seas contract, routes re-emerge, dangerous transition |

**Important Note:** The user specified that wet and dry seasons last approximately two Earth years each, while spring and fall last approximately one Earth year each.

This makes a full seasonal cycle approximately **six Earth years** if all four durations are added literally.

However, the user also specified that a full Meridian Prime year is approximately **four Earth years**.

**Contradiction Opportunity / Calendar Development Needed:**

This apparent mismatch should be preserved for now as a calendar-design issue rather than silently corrected.

Possible future resolutions:

1. **Earth-year shorthand is approximate and overlapping.** Spring and fall may overlap the transition edges of wet/dry seasons rather than adding fully on top.
2. **Meridian Prime civil year differs from full hydrological cycle.** A civil year may be about four Earth years, while a full hydrological mega-cycle may be longer.
3. **Official Meridian Prime year is not yet established.** Current Earth-year references are field estimates, not final canon astronomy.
4. **Different civilizations count the year differently.** Humans, Hydropolis, and Craton traditions may disagree on seasonal boundaries.

Do not finalize official Meridian Prime calendar mechanics yet.

## Primary Altitude Zones

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

Meridian Prime's major seasonal geography depends on elevation.

### 1. High Escarpment Continent

Human Meridian settlement sphere.

Core traits:

- high-elevation crystal desert ecology
- dry-season high desert
- wet-season snowfield
- patchwork human settlement and logistics jurisdictions
- Prime Ops service areas
- World Works lease zones
- NCI and private contractor routes
- fuel-cache communities
- old Traverse settlements
- abandoned stations

Key principle:

**The escarpment is not ruled. It is maintained badly enough to survive.**

### 2. Escarpment Descent Zone

The Traverse must descend approximately **8,000 feet** during dry season to escape the escarpment and reach the lower Dry Circuit.

Core traits:

- steep grade routes
- switchbacks
- descent stations
- brake-heating zones
- old lift roads
- winch anchor points
- rockfall / crystalfall corridors
- snowmelt cataract danger in spring
- closure risk when wet-season snowfields return

Key principle:

**The Dry Circuit begins with descent.**

### 3. Basin Floor / Dry Circuit Lowlands

Dry-season route space connecting the escarpment, Hydropolis, and Craton approaches.

Core traits:

- dry river corridors
- underground river channels
- basin flats
- hydrocrystal crust
- black-glass substrate near Hydropolis
- Shard Sea margins
- sinkfields
- dead-channel belts
- dry camps

Key principle:

**In dry season, the rivers become roads.**

### 4. Hydropolis / Mirror Basin Vertical City Zone

Antisapien / Hydropolis territory.

Core traits:

- vertical migration city
- dry-season basin-floor city
- wet-season moored floating city
- underground city connected by four skyscraper elevator / mooring towers
- civic signal fields
- wet/dry access systems
- black-glass substrate
- dry canal / wet canal transformation

Key principle:

**Hydropolis does not flee the flood. It rides it.**

### 5. Anticlinal Craton Mid-Elevation Zone

Aeonolacertian / Craton territory.

Core traits:

- mid-elevation geode landform
- dry-season geode mountain
- wet-season geode island
- gates and pressure markers
- Shard Sea / wet-season water relationship
- sovereign seasonal access systems
- sacred pressure territory

Key principle:

**The Craton is mountain when the world withdraws and island when the world remembers water.**

## Anticlinal Craton Seasonal Form

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Anticlinal Craton is a mid-elevation geode landform whose seasonal relationship to surrounding water changes dramatically.

### Dry Season Form

During dry season, the Craton reads as a **geode mountain**.

Dry-season features:

- land approaches open
- gates become reachable by dry routes
- pressure-marker avenues visible
- Shard margins exposed
- sacred land corridors accessible under permission
- Dry Circuit traffic possible near its threshold

### Wet Season Form

During wet season, the Craton reads as a **geode island**.

Wet-season features:

- lower routes drowned
- water approaches dominate
- docks, cliffs, or seasonal landings may become important
- Craton sovereignty becomes more visibly island-like
- outsider approach changes from road protocol to water protocol

### Transition Seasons

Spring and fall create dangerous ambiguity.

Spring brings water back through cataracts and underground river systems.

Fall withdraws water but leaves unstable flats, mud, salt crust, hidden channels, and broken routes.

## Rivers, Underground Channels, and Seasonal Water States

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

The Dry Circuit follows river systems that sometimes go underground during dry season.

These same corridors transform across the seasonal cycle.

### Dry Season

Rivers are mostly absent, underground, reduced, or intermittent.

The exposed riverbeds become natural roads and Traverse corridors.

### Spring / Cataract Season

Cataracts signal spring.

Water returns with force through:

- waterfalls
- escarpment melt routes
- underground river eruptions
- reopened channels
- gorge floods
- basin reactivation

Cataracts are the warning bells that the Dry Circuit is ending.

### Monsoon / Haboob Season

During monsoon / haboob conditions, river corridors become inland seas, storm basins, or dust-water chaos fields.

This season may overlap with spring / fall transition behavior depending on final calendar development.

Features:

- haboob walls
- monsoon flood sheets
- inland seas
- sudden channels
- visibility collapse
- lightning / static storms
- mud and hydrocrystal destabilization

### Wet Season

During wet season, the basin reaches oceanic conditions.

Lower dry routes are drowned.

Hydropolis rises.

The Craton becomes island-like.

The high escarpment becomes snowfield.

### Fall / Drawdown Season

Water retreats.

Routes begin to re-emerge, but remain unstable.

Features:

- half-drowned camps
- exposed mud
- salt crust
- wreckage and debris lines
- returning riverbed roads
- newly changed terrain
- disputed first access to reopened routes

## Dry Circuit Seasonal Function

**Candidate Canon Seed**

The Dry Circuit is only fully traversable during dry season.

Its existence depends on:

- exposed river corridors
- basin floor access
- stable descent from the escarpment
- Hydropolis dry-season form
- Craton dry-season geode mountain form
- Shard margin exposure
- camps and fuel caches being usable

The route dies seasonally.

It does not vanish from memory.

It becomes submerged, snow-locked, or politically transformed.

Key principle:

**The Dry Circuit is a road only when the world allows roadhood.**
