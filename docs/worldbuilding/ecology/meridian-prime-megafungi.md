## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document establishes the first working set of Meridian Prime mega-fungi by major traversable region.

If megafauna asks:

**What moves when the roads drown?**

And mega-flora asks:

**What remains rooted when the world changes shape?**

Then mega-fungi asks:

**What feeds on the old world and decides what becomes useful again?**

Mega-fungi should carry the ecology of rot, salvage, medicine, contamination, memory, spores, archive danger, signal weirdness, and the terrible generosity of decay.

---

## Core Mega-Fungi Design Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime mega-fungi entry is not complete until it documents:

| Required Detail | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Dry-season form | Dormant crust, spore dust, buried mycelium, mineral shell, wreck rot, or hidden vein |
| Wet-season bloom | How it erupts, spreads, floats, infects, glows, filters, or becomes useful |
| Route / settlement effect | How it damages roads, machines, bodies, signals, food stores, maps, archives, or structures |
| Cultural interpretation | Who harvests, fears, weaponizes, worships, regulates, or denies it |

Core line:

**Mega-fungi are the treaty between dead things and the next season.**

# 1. High Escarpment Continent

Dry season: high-elevation crystal desert.

Wet season: snowfield / ice-locked highland.

## 1A. Glassrot Veins

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Subsurface fungal networks that digest fractured crystal, dead lichen mats, buried carcasses, and old route materials.

They appear as faint milky threads in cracked crystal and dry hardpan.

### Dry-Season Form

- live below route crust
- weaken crystal roads from underneath
- bloom around old fuel spills and dead Glassback Mammoths
- give off a faint mineral-sweet smell before crust failure
- may appear as pale veins in fractured road crystal

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- enter cold-slow metabolism under snow
- bloom around thaw pockets and carcass caches
- produce antifreeze spores in rime-coated crystal hollows
- continue digesting buried organic matter beneath snowpack

### Route / Settlement Effect

Road collapse warning and carcass-cycle marker.

Glassrot can make apparently stable highland crystal fail under convoy weight.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops calls it contamination.

Route families may say:

**The road is digesting lies.**

---

## 1B. Rimecap Colonies

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Large frost-domed fungi that bloom under snowfields, often over hidden thermal pockets or buried organic matter.

### Dry-Season Form

- survive as hard mineral buttons in high desert gravel
- almost invisible except as pale circular patches
- can be mistaken for pebbles, shell fragments, or old mineral pellets

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- swell under snow into warm fungal domes
- create hollow snow traps
- feed small fauna and attract Rime Condors
- preserve spores in insulating frost chambers

### Route / Settlement Effect

Snow shelter for fauna, collapse hazard for crews.

### Cultural Interpretation

Highland guides may treat Rimecap domes as both gift and trap.

Prime Ops maps may reduce them to **subsurface thermal-fungal voids**, which sounds useful until someone falls through one.

# 2. The Great Cut / Cataract Ladder

Dry season: canyon descent.

Cataract season: roaring water ladder.

Wet season: chute / icefall / sealed hazard.

## 2A. Sporefall Curtains

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Fungal sheets growing behind Cataract Beards, seep lines, and shadowed canyon walls.

During cataract season, they release spores through waterfall mist.

### Dry-Season Form

- appear as dry gray sheets on canyon walls
- crumble into spore dust when disturbed
- irritate lungs and filters
- colonize old shelter fabric, rope, and brake dust

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- become slick, faintly glowing curtains behind waterfalls
- release spores in mist clouds
- colonize wet brake housings, ropes, tents, and shelter seams
- spread through waterfall spray without needing open soil

### Route / Settlement Effect

Respirator zone and equipment-fouling hazard.

### Cultural Interpretation

NCI saying:

**If the falls are breathing, mask before you pray.**

---

## 2B. Echo Mold

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A resonant fungus growing inside Echo Fern Towers, canyon acoustic hollows, horn towers, abandoned warning stations, and sensor housings.

### Dry-Season Form

- creates false echoes
- confuses acoustic scans
- grows in old warning horns and canyon listening equipment
- makes some side-canyons seem larger, closer, or emptier than they are

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- expands through flooded canyon air pockets
- amplifies or dampens natural warning tones
- can make canyon warnings unreliable during flood rise
- spreads through damp acoustic cavities and hollow stems

### Route / Settlement Effect

Signal / acoustic distortion hazard in the Great Cut.

### Cultural Interpretation

Plateau guides may distinguish true fern-song from mold-song.

World Works may treat both as sensor noise, a mistake with teeth.

# 3. Prime Plateau / The National Park / Green Hell

Dry season: temperate cloud rainforest staging shelf.

Wet season: snowline cloudforest / lower routes closed.

## 3A. Route Rot

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A broad family of mega-fungal mats that digest old roadbeds, timber braces, rubber, insulation, warning paint, and improperly sealed machines.

### Dry-Season Form

- creeps under moss and road edges
- softens old bridge supports
- eats abandoned signage and warning paint
- smells like wet pennies and old bread
- colonizes depots that are sealed badly but called safe anyway

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- thickens under snow-insulated canopy
- spreads into parked vehicles, sealed depots, and forgotten shelters
- grows luminous fruiting shelves on dead Cloudwood Giants
- uses meltwater and fog drip to cross otherwise dry barriers

### Route / Settlement Effect

Infrastructure decay, bridge failure, camp abandonment, vehicle contamination.

World Works term:

**Biological Infrastructure Degradation Event.**

NCI term:

**The road getting hungry.**

### Cultural Interpretation

NCI mechanics know which rot means repair, which rot means collapse, and which rot means the bridge already died weeks ago but remained polite.

---

## 3B. Lantern Mycelia

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Giant glowing mycelial webs under cloudforest floors and inside hollow trunks.

Locals use some Lantern Mycelia as fog-navigation markers.

### Dry-Season Form

- faint bioluminescent threads appear during heavy fog
- mark underground moisture and root cavities
- grow through hollow Cloudwood and Mossvault edges
- can lure travelers off road if misread

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- brighten under snow-dark canopy
- guide some fauna to warm root caves
- grow into glowing shelf clusters around old wrecks and deadfalls
- preserve mycelial heat inside insulated root systems

### Route / Settlement Effect

Navigation aid, false trail, sacred / local knowledge layer.

### Cultural Interpretation

Local saying:

**Follow the lanterns only if someone living taught you which ones lie.**

# 4. Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit Lowlands

Dry season: exposed basin floor, dry river roads, paleochannels.

Wet season: inland sea / oceanic basin.

## 4A. Saltmold Bloom

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A mineral-tolerant fungal or fungal-algal hybrid that survives as white crust in dry basin flats and blooms into slick mats during rising water.

### Dry-Season Form

- forms pale crust mistaken for salt
- clogs filters when pulverized
- marks old floodwater residue
- can make dry flats smell faintly briny after heat pulses

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- becomes slippery floating mats
- fouls boat intakes
- feeds basin detritus cycles
- can hide Paleochannel Gulper vents
- spreads fastest in shallow mineral-rich floodwater

### Route / Settlement Effect

False salt crust, wet-season engine hazard, bloom-cycle indicator.

### Cultural Interpretation

Prime Ops hates it for filters.

Hydropolis may read it as part of basin recovery ecology rather than mere contamination.

---

## 4B. Mouthbed Spores

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Fungal colonies that grow around dormant Paleochannel Gulpers, buried carcasses, and seep-fed dead zones in dry riverbeds.

### Dry-Season Form

- create faint ring patterns around sleeping predators
- produce odorless spores that irritate eyes
- soften dry roadbeds
- form chalky spore halos around breathing vents and old carcass pockets

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- burst upward as dark spore plumes when channels fill
- attract scavengers and fish
- reveal predator beds too late for careless travelers
- spread across submerged riverbed sediment

### Route / Settlement Effect

Indirect Gulper warning, sink risk, respiratory hazard.

### Cultural Interpretation

NCI old hands may read Mouthbed rings as warnings.

World Works survey crews may read them as mineral staining and learn humility the hard way.

# 5. Hydropolis / Mirror Basin Urban-Wet Zone

Dry season: basin-floor city and dry canals.

Wet season: moored vertical city, tower traffic, water lanes.

## 5A. Archive Mold

**Candidate Canon Seed / Corporate Distortion Risk**

A controversial fungus that grows in old Hydropolis signal chambers, submerged archives, canal walls, memory-storage ruins, and damp black-glass conduits.

### Dry-Season Form

- appears as black-violet veining in dry conduits
- may preserve trace chemical or electrical residues
- is heavily regulated by Hydropolis
- blooms around old record systems and sealed civic chambers

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- expands in submerged chambers
- interferes with signal integrity
- may preserve, distort, or mimic record-memory in civic myth
- releases spores through maintenance shafts and flooded conduits

### Route / Settlement Effect

Restricted signal-archive contamination zone.

May require quarantine, witness protocol, and record-integrity checks.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis civic view:

Dangerous witness, not mere mold.

World Works view:

Removable contamination.

That phrase is a small match in a dry archive.

---

## 5B. Tower Gill Fungus

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Massive gill-like fungal shelves that grow on submerged tower foundations, old lower-city supports, and shadowed mooring structures.

### Dry-Season Form

- dormant plates cling to dry concrete, black glass, and tower bases
- brittle but protected by Hydropolis maintenance law
- used as indicators of past water levels

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- unfold into filtering gills
- clean, alter, or chemically buffer water around mooring towers
- attract Glasswake Rays and smaller fauna
- interfere with maintenance drones and hull approaches

### Route / Settlement Effect

Tower maintenance zone, civic ecology marker, protected habitat.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis may treat Tower Gill as part of the city's wet-season breathing system.

Prime Ops may call it maintenance obstruction and be wrong in a very expensive way.

# 6. The Eschar Slough

Dry season: scabland, dead flood terrain, Free Scab bandit country.

Wet season: pirate archipelago / blackwater channels.

## 6A. Black Spore Tar

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A tar-like fungal mat that grows over dead flood channels, wreckage, and old organic sludge.

It looks like oil, burned sludge, or a shadow that got tired.

### Dry-Season Form

- forms sticky black crust
- preserves footprints and vehicle tracks for days
- traps boots and small tires
- hides Free Scab caches
- stains tools, skin, hulls, and route markers

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- loosens into black slicks
- fouls hulls and engines
- obscures depth
- helps conceal pirate movement
- spreads along slow blackwater channels

### Route / Settlement Effect

Track memory, engine hazard, concealment field.

### Cultural Interpretation

Free Scab use:

**Ambush ink.**

Prime Ops marks it as a contamination hazard and misses that it also remembers every footprint.

---

## 6B. Corpse Lanterns

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Tall, pale fungal fruiting bodies that bloom from carcasses, drowned wrecks, old mass-death sites, and flood-buried refuse.

### Dry-Season Form

- hard bone-colored stalks mark buried dead
- used as grim navigation markers
- grow around wreck pockets and old kill sites
- may be harvested for medicinal or poisonous compounds, if later approved

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- glow over shallow water
- attract scavengers, Blackwake Crocs, and desperate travelers
- mark old battle or wreck sites
- release spores from lantern sacs during warm wet nights

### Route / Settlement Effect

Death-site landmark, predator lure, false safe harbor marker.

### Cultural Interpretation

Free Scab proverb:

**Never anchor where the dead are lighting lamps.**

# 7. Anticlinal Craton Interior / Geode Mountain

Dry season: geode mountain stronghold.

Wet season: geode island stronghold.

## 7A. Pressure Mycelium

**Religious Tradition / Scientific Hypothesis**

Deep fungal webs growing through cracks, geode hollows, pressure-warm stone, and root systems of sacred Craton flora.

Aeonolacertians may treat these networks as living listeners.

### Dry-Season Form

- stabilizes or reveals microfractures
- grows near Pressure Cedars
- fruiting bodies pulse under weight shifts
- may mark sacred pressure corridors without being publicly mapped

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- contracts deeper into warm fissures
- blooms in protected geode chambers
- responds to saturation pressure in sacred corridors
- may alter growth patterns after heavy wet-season loading

### Route / Settlement Effect

Do not fully map.

Pressure-sensitive sacred ecology.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton phrase:

**Stone remembers slowly. Fungus answers first.**

---

## 7B. Geode Milk Fungus

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Pale nutrient-rich fungal shelves cultivated, protected, or semi-cultivated in pressure-warm caverns and geode orchards.

### Dry-Season Form

- harvested as emergency food or medicine
- fed to young, elders, or convalescents
- grows on mineralized deadwood and old root systems
- forms pale shelves in warm enclosed Craton hollows

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- thrives in enclosed humid geode gardens
- sealed away from outsiders
- responds well to controlled warm moisture
- may be part of Craton food sovereignty during wet isolation

### Route / Settlement Effect

Craton interior food system and under-mapped survival resource.

### Cultural Interpretation

Outsiders may call it food.

Craton tradition may call that description incomplete, rude, and possibly hungry in the wrong way.

# 8. Blackstep Province / Craton Basalt Apron

Dry season: exposed basalt terraces and threshold roads.

Wet season: docking reefs and sacred wet gates.

## 8A. Basalt Bracket Fungus

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Hard shelf-like fungus growing on basalt cliffs and lava benches, nearly stone-black until wet.

### Dry-Season Form

- looks like layered rock
- conceals cracks, nests, and lava-tube mouths
- can break under weight despite looking solid
- may be scraped by Basalt Tuskers

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- swells into slick black shelves
- feeds Basalt Tuskers
- creates slippery dock hazards
- grows fastest in spray zones around wet gates

### Route / Settlement Effect

False basalt shelf, dock-edge hazard, wet-gate maintenance problem.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton maps may treat it as part of the dock's refusal rather than an external hazard.

---

## 8B. Anchor Rot

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A marine-adapted fungal growth that colonizes ropes, anchor chains, hull scars, abandoned mooring gear, and forbidden docking equipment.

### Dry-Season Form

- survives in dormant hooklike crusts on old mooring gear
- damages stored ropes and landing equipment
- remains in basalt cracks where water once held an anchor line

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- spreads through wet ropes, anchors, hull seams, and illegal dock lashings
- weakens improper moorings
- thrives in no-anchor zones where abandoned gear accumulates
- releases spores when chains grind across basalt

### Route / Settlement Effect

Mooring failure and illegal anchorage warning.

### Cultural Interpretation

Craton interpretation:

The dock punishes improper touch.

World Works interpretation:

Replace the rope.

The gap between those two interpretations can sink a boat.

# 9. Lower Basin River Corridors / Dry River Roads

Dry season: paleochannels and dry roads.

Wet season: rivers and inland seas.

## 9A. Channel Veil Mold

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A fine gray-green fungal veil that grows over dry riverbeds where underground water is close.

### Dry-Season Form

- marks buried moisture
- makes roadbeds slick after night fog
- softens crust under repeated convoy weight
- appears as a faint veil over otherwise ordinary dust

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- becomes submerged filament clouds
- feeds small aquatic life
- signals early channel return
- spreads along newly active streambeds

### Route / Settlement Effect

Hidden water and route-softening marker.

### Cultural Interpretation

Bought Nation route families may treat it as a living underline beneath old river roads.

Prime Ops may mark it as traction risk.

Both are right, which is annoying and useful.

---

## 9B. Floodcap Towers

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Large mushroom towers that remain dormant as hard knobs in dry mud, then grow rapidly when floodwater returns.

### Dry-Season Form

- break axles if struck
- indicate old flood basins
- resemble stones, buried shells, or old route debris
- may be mapped only after someone hits one

### Wet-Season Bloom / Survival Strategy

- rise above shallow water
- provide bird and small-fauna perches
- release spores during first flood winds
- clog narrow boat routes
- decay into rich basin sediment during drawdown

### Route / Settlement Effect

Flood-timing marker and boat obstruction.

### Cultural Interpretation

Hydropolis may read Floodcap emergence as a basin timing index.

Free Scabs may use them as temporary blind corners in shallow water.

## Cross-Region Ecological Notes

**Scientific Hypothesis / Development Apocrypha**

Mega-fungi adds the decay and recovery layer to Meridian Prime ecology.

Potential broad patterns:

- highland fungi digest crystal, carcasses, and cold-buried lichen mats
- canyon fungi turn mist and acoustic spaces into spore corridors
- cloudforest fungi threaten infrastructure while guiding locals through fog
- basin fungi make dry roads softer than they look and wet channels stranger than charts admit
- Hydropolis fungi are entangled with archive, signal, and civic witness logic
- Eschar Slough fungi preserve tracks, hide pirates, and turn death into landmarks
- Craton fungi are often interpreted through pressure, memory, food sovereignty, and sacred stone
- Blackstep fungi turn docks, ropes, basalt shelves, and wet gates into living refusal systems
