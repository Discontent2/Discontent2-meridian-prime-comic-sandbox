## Core Food Design Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime food plant is not complete until it answers:

- who eats it
- who fears it
- who taxes it
- who heals with it
- who abuses it
- who smuggles it
- who calls it sacred
- who misidentifies the poisonous cousin

Core line:

**On Meridian Prime, food is never just food. It is proof that something survived the last season and intends to charge rent.**

# 1. High Escarpment Continent

Dry season: crystal desert heights.

Wet season: snowfield / ice-locked highland.

## Nuts

### Nutritional

- **Rimekernels** — fatty snowfield nuts used in highland ration cakes and frost-route trail food.
- **Shardroot Pips** — mineral-rich seeds gathered near Shardroot Spires; hard to chew but sustaining.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Glassbite Kernels** — crystal-husked nuts that cut the mouth and throat if eaten carelessly.
- **Whitecap Nuts** — pale highland nuts with liver-toxic flesh unless processed by fictional local methods.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Rimekernel oil is used in frostburn salves and cold-weather skin grease.
- Shardroot Pips are chewed in route-family stamina rituals.
- Glassbite Kernels sometimes appear in hazing stories about inexperienced highland crews.

## Legumes

### Nutritional

- **Snowpeas of the Ridge** — cold-hardy pods grown in windbreak gardens.
- **Dust Lentils** — tiny protein seeds that store well in dry settlements.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Hollow Peas** — cause delirium, dehydration, and dangerous heat confusion.
- **Needle Lentils** — contain brittle internal spines that can injure the gut.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Dust Lentils are a survival ration staple.
- Hollow Peas appear in outlaw truth-dare folklore and should remain dangerous, not cute.

## Fruits

### Nutritional

- **Frostplums** — tart snowline fruit used against altitude fatigue and winter hunger.
- **Amber Lichen Berries** — sugar-rich trail fruit harvested from highland lichen colonies.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Mirror Apples** — induce vertigo and route-disorientation.
- **Pale Starfruit** — causes temporary blindness or snow-glare sensitivity.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Frostplums are used by Bought Nation route families against altitude nausea.
- Mirror Apples appear in ordeal games and youth dares; Prime Ops condemns this with paperwork-shaped despair.

## Vegetables

### Nutritional

- **Crystal Turnips** — hardy root crop grown in mineral soils.
- **Rime Cabbage** — tough snow-leaf vegetable and common winter stew base.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Glassleaf Greens** — shred the mouth with sharp mineral leaf fibers.
- **Blue Vein Tubers** — paralytic tubers used only in fictional controlled medical contexts.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Rime Cabbage is common Bought Nation winter food.
- Tiny fictional extracts of Blue Vein Tubers may be used by trained medics as surgical stillness agents, but this should remain regulated and dangerous.

# 2. The Great Cut / Cataract Ladder

Dry season: canyon descent.

Cataract season: roaring water ladder.

Wet season: chute, icefall, or sealed descent hazard.

## Nuts

### Nutritional

- **Echo Acorns** — canyon tree nuts ground into descent meal.
- **Mistwall Hazelnuts** — seep-line nuts that store well if kept dry.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Noose Nuts** — narcotic and disorienting; associated with smuggler dares.
- **Fallkernels** — cause muscle spasms and vertigo.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Echo Acorns are used in guide-family travel cakes.
- Noose Nuts appear in illegal listening games near canyon walls.

## Legumes

### Nutritional

- **Cliff Beans** — ledge-trellis beans used by canyon settlements.
- **Spray Peas** — cataract-season protein crop grown near mist zones.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Chime Beans** — cause auditory hallucinations and false water-sound perception.
- **Red Seep Peas** — blood-thinning toxin; dangerous before descent injuries.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Spray Peas are valued by canyon guides.
- Chime Beans fuel dangerous listening rites and should remain contested.

## Fruits

### Nutritional

- **Cut Figs** — mineral-sweet fruit given before long descents.
- **Mist Grapes** — spray-fed fruit fermented into weak route wine.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Dropfruit** — induces fainting or sudden weakness.
- **Spore Pears** — carry fungal irritants that damage lungs and filters.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Cut Figs are descent food.
- Mist Grape wine is a canyon guide social drink and trade good.

## Vegetables

### Nutritional

- **Ledge Onions** — hardy bulbs used in cold canyon broth.
- **Spray Fern Hearts** — edible fern shoots and guide-family prestige food.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Hookroot** — tightens the throat and should be feared.
- **Bitter Curtain Greens** — cause severe cramps and dehydration.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Ledge Onion broth is route comfort food.
- Spray Fern Hearts may be served after successful descent certification.

# 3. Prime Plateau / The National Park / Green Hell

Dry season: temperate cloud rainforest staging shelf.

Wet season: snowline cloudforest / lower routes closed.

## Nuts

### Nutritional

- **Cloudwood Chestnuts** — major plateau staple; ground into flour in Charterhold and Ledger Falls.
- **Fog Almonds** — oily portable nuts used by NCI crews and cloudforest guides.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Green Hell Widow-Nuts** — slow fever toxin; often confused with Fog Almonds by outsiders.
- **Mossblind Nuts** — cause temporary vision distortion and fog misreading.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Cloudwood Chestnut flour is a Bought Nation staple.
- Fog Almond oil is used for waterproofing skin, leather, and tool wraps.
- Mossblind Nuts appear in illegal vision games and old route punishments.

## Legumes

### Nutritional

- **Vine Beans** — canopy-grown protein crop.
- **Green Shelf Peas** — local staple grown in fog gardens and platform plots.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Bellpod Beans** — soporific beans used in criminal sleep-poison lore.
- **Rotvine Peas** — stomach toxin associated with Route Rot contamination.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Vine Beans are common NCI yard food.
- Bellpod Beans may be used as fictional sedatives in tightly controlled medical contexts but are more often feared as sabotage food.

## Fruits

### Nutritional

- **Fog Pears** — water-heavy fruit used in Charterhold ciders and travel stores.
- **Lanternberries** — mild-glowing edible berries used as local trail markers.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Bridefruit** — mimics Lanternberries but causes confusion and false-light attraction.
- **Glass-Sour Apples** — highly acidic fruit that damages teeth and storage containers.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Lanternberries are used for low-light trail marking by locals.
- Fog Pear cider is a Charterhold comfort drink.
- Bridefruit may be connected in folklore to Corpse Lantern Brides but should not be confirmed as the same organism.

## Vegetables

### Nutritional

- **Mossroot Tubers** — emergency starch from cloudforest soils.
- **Cloudfern Hearts** — tender greens harvested by local experts.

### Poisonous / Dangerous

- **Pitmoss Sprouts** — associated with Bellmoss-like digestive bogs.
- **Greenbite Shoots** — venomous sap causes swelling and fever.

### Medical / Recreational Notes

- Mossroot is survival food.
- Cloudfern Hearts are served at plateau weddings, Reclaimer gatherings, and fog-shelter feasts.
