## Purpose

**Development Apocrypha**

This document fills the gap left by the first food-web pass: everyday and culturally harvested mushrooms.

**Mega-fungi** are ecological forces.

**Mushrooms** are the fruiting bodies, caps, shelves, puffballs, nodules, stalks, and truffles that people actually pick, cook, fear, trade, regulate, ban, ritualize, or misidentify.

---

## Core Mushroom Design Rule

**Candidate Canon Seed**

A Meridian Prime mushroom is not complete until it answers:

- who can safely identify it
- what it resembles
- what season makes the mistake deadly
- who eats it
- who forbids it
- who harvests it for medicine
- who abuses it recreationally or ritually
- what map clue it gives to terrain, water, rot, death, or contamination

Core line:

**On Meridian Prime, mushrooms are the fruit of hidden decisions. By the time a cap appears, the ground has been planning for weeks.**

# 1. High Escarpment Continent

Dry season: crystal desert heights.

Wet season: snowfield / ice-locked highland.

## Nutritional Mushrooms

### Rime Buttons

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Small cold-hardy button mushrooms that form beneath snow crust and along the edges of Rimecap Colonies.

Uses:

- snowfield survival food
- dried into highland ration mixes
- eaten by route crews during long whiteout waits

Map clue:

- indicates shallow snow warmth, buried organic matter, or old mammoth routes.

Cultural note:

Bought Nation highland families may call them **winter buttons** and teach children to identify them before they are old enough to handle fuel tools.

### Glasscup Mushrooms

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Mineral-rich cup fungi that grow near Shardroot Spires and fractured crystal hollows.

Uses:

- dried into a route-salt or mineral seasoning
- added to bitter highland stews
- used as a trail electrolyte food in fictional form

Map clue:

- marks water memory near Shardroot systems.

## Poisonous / Dangerous Mushrooms

### White Sleep Caps

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Pale caps that induce hypothermia-like drowsiness and dangerous calm.

Hazard:

- victims sit, rest, and do not wake before the cold takes them.

Seasonal danger:

- worst during wet-season snowfield conditions.

Local warning:

**Never trust a mushroom that makes the snow feel kind.**

### Shardgill Splinters

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Thin mineral-gilled mushrooms whose internal ridges fracture into cutting splinters.

Hazard:

- mouth and gut injury if eaten.
- can contaminate stored mushroom baskets.

Map clue:

- often grow in Glassrot-weakened crystal.

Map clue:

## Poisonous / Dangerous Mushrooms

### Sporelung Fans

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Fan-shaped fungi that release respiratory irritant spores when touched or when cataract mist hits them.

Hazard:

- lung irritation
- filter fouling
- panic coughing near ledges

Map clue:

- respirator zone.

### Dropstep Bells

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Bell-shaped mushrooms that cause vertigo and fainting when eaten or when spores concentrate in still ledges.

Hazard:

Cultural note:

# 3. Prime Plateau / The National Park / Green Hell

Dry season: temperate cloud rainforest staging shelf.

Wet season: snowline cloudforest / lower routes closed.

## Nutritional Mushrooms

### Cloud Morels

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Prized fog-season mushrooms growing around Cloudwood Giants, Mossvault edges, and old root hollows.

Uses:

- Charterhold delicacy
- Ledger Falls harvest good
- Reclaimer feast food
- high-value barter at NCI yards

Map clue:

- indicates healthy cloudforest soil and stable root moisture.

Cultural note:

Cloud Morels are one of the strongest candidates for a signature Bought Nation plateau food.

### Lantern Shelves

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Edible glowing shelf fungi when verified by local knowledge.

Uses:

- guide food
- emergency light-food in fog shelters
- minor festival ingredient in plateau communities

Map clue:

- may mark safe moisture corridors or old deadfall.

Warning:

- easily confused with poisonous Bridecaps.

## Poisonous / Dangerous Mushrooms

### Bridecaps

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Toxic lookalikes of Lantern Shelves, linked by folklore to false lights, Corpse Lantern Brides, and the Green Hell's appetite for certainty.

Hazard:

- confusion
- false-light attraction
- route disorientation

Seasonal danger:

- worst in fog and wet canopy gloom.

Local warning:

**Not every lantern wants you home.**

### Bunk Rot Buttons

**Candidate Canon Seed**

Small mushrooms growing in warm abandoned shelters, sleeping bags, vehicle seats, and badly sealed depots.

Hazard:

- indicate Warmth-Hunger Mold or Route Rot contamination.
- do not necessarily poison immediately, but mark unsafe shelter.

NCI rule:

**Buttons in the bunk mean nobody sleeps.**

## Cultural Mushroom Notes

### Bought Nation

**Political Development**

Mushrooms are poor food with good memory.

Route families know which caps mean water, rot, shelter, death, or safe fog routes.

Charterhold likely licenses mushroom sellers through experience, kin reputation, or route apprenticeship rather than pure paperwork.

### Hydropolis

**Political Development / Scientific Hypothesis**

Mushrooms are legal only if their growth history is verified.

A mushroom from the wrong canal is not only unsafe. It may be a record problem.

### Craton

**Religious Tradition**

Mushrooms are not casual food.

Cavern mushrooms may be part of guest law, healing, elder care, pressure rites, and wet-season food sovereignty.

### Free Scabs

**Development Apocrypha**

Mushrooms are survival, poison, warning signs, and jokes with teeth.

Free Scab saying:

**Never eat what glows unless someone uglier than you ate it first.**

### World Works

**Corporate Distortion**

Likely term:

**Fungal Consumable Risk Class.**

Translation:

**A mushroom they do not understand but intend to label anyway.**
