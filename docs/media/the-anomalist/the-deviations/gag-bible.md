# The Deviations Gag Bible

**Status:** Sandbox Media Artifact / Non-Canon / Requires Review Before Promotion  
**Project:** Meridian Prime  
**Newspaper:** The Anomalist  
**Funnies Section:** The Deviations

## Core Motto

The universe is indifferent, but it did remember to draw a tiny speech balloon.

## Core Concept

*The Anomalist* is a normal newspaper inside an abnormal world.

*The Deviations* is its funnies section: a single-panel gag cartoon feature that turns massive Meridian Prime pressures into tiny disposable jokes.

The newspaper should feel ordinary. The reality around it is not.

The strip may feel like something a tired cartoonist drew at 3:17 a.m. after seeing a dinosaur in the parking lot and deciding not to ask questions.

## Canon Status

By default, every Deviations gag is:

```text
Sandbox Media Artifact / Non-Canon / Requires Review Before Promotion
```

Individual gag panels are not canon unless explicitly promoted.

The Deviations may be emotionally true and factually unreliable.

## What The Deviations Does

The strip creates small cartoon pressure valves for a vast strange universe. It may reflect:

- public anxiety
- route pressure
- World Works corporate care
- Hydropolis bureaucracy
- scientific arrogance
- misclassification
- old machines waking up
- false sanctuaries
- Antisapian signal weirdness
- Aeonolacertian everyday normalization
- microscopic dread
- cosmic indifference

It must never become a lore-answer machine.

## Protected Mystery Guardrail

Do not define, reveal, overwrite, or solve:

- the Core
- Dry Port truth
- Dry Gate truth
- gateway mechanics
- Contact Frame mechanics
- protected route topology
- Ildi’s restricted truth
- Conjugate Chimera truth

- the NCI assassin
- Book One answers

Allowed approaches:

- rumor
- false record
- bad cartoon interpretation
- public misunderstanding
- unverified civic panic
- bureaucratic euphemism
- small surreal echo

Forbidden approaches:

- confirmed answer
- true mechanism
- actual location
- final explanation
- canon revelation

## Humor Tone

The default humor is gentle weirdness first.

Depending on the gag, it may become:

- deadpan
- slapstick
- darkly absurd
- surreal
- quietly cosmic
- confusing in a funny way

The characters are naive, innocent, disposable little panic-puppets. They can be transformed, ignored by the universe, eaten in a bloodless cartoon way, or dropped into existential disaster, but the cruelty should stay surreal and cosmic rather than nasty.

## Stock Character Masks

The same name can appear in different bodies depending on the joke. Continuity does not matter. The name is a mask, not a biography.

### Ed

**Mask:** The Useful Idiot  
**Function:** Ed helps, but help is usually the problem.  
**Can Appear As:** man, worm, dinosaur, robot, ghost, amoeba, office appliance.  
**Typical Expression:** blank confidence or delayed panic.  
**Typical Joke Role:** causes the disaster while thinking he solved it.

Example line:

```text
Good news. I taught it manners.
```

### Glenda

**Mask:** The Calm Witness  
**Function:** Glenda observes the impossible and reacts as if it is mildly inconvenient.  
**Can Appear As:** woman, bird, alien, cave fungus, lab assistant, moon creature.  
**Typical Expression:** deadpan neutrality.  
**Typical Joke Role:** says the simple sentence that makes the horrible thing funny.

Example line:

```text
I liked it better before it understood furniture.
```

### Tommy

**Mask:** The Innocent Panic-Puppet  
**Function:** Tommy is not necessarily a child. Tommy is the small, naive, doomed-feeling creature who asks the wrong question.  
**Can Appear As:** boyish figure, tiny dinosaur, larva, nervous robot, junior caveman, microbe.  
**Typical Expression:** wide-eyed concern.  
**Typical Joke Role:** realizes the setup too late.

Example line:

```text
Is the microscope supposed to be looking back?
```

### Dr. Bounds

**Mask:** The Expert Who May Be an Idiot  
**Function:** Science person. Explains things with authority, sometimes correctly, sometimes catastrophically.  
**Can Appear As:** scientist, alien researcher, prehistoric doctor, lab-coated amoeba, cave professor.  
**Typical Expression:** professional blankness during chaos.  
**Typical Joke Role:** delivers confident nonsense.

Example line:

```text
Technically, it’s only a mistake if we survive the peer review.
```

### Mr. Schmidt

**Mask:** The Authority Figure Who Is Also an Idiot  
**Function:** Boss, mayor, captain, judge, principal, commander, official inspector.  
**Can Appear As:** human, dinosaur administrator, alien bureaucrat, cave elder, robot foreman.  
**Typical Expression:** stern stupidity.  
**Typical Joke Role:** enforces rules that make no sense in an impossible situation.

Example line:

```text
I don’t care if it’s sentient. It didn’t file a permit.
```

### Guy / The Whatever-It-Is

**Mask:** The Thing That Should Not Talk  
**Function:** The wild-card. A loaf of bread, amoeba, moon, fossil, squid, rock, puddle, office machine, or mystery object casually speaks.  
**Naming Rule:** If it is vaguely male-coded or generic, call it Guy. If it is truly strange, name it by object or organism.  
**Typical Expression:** neutral animal/object face.  
**Typical Joke Role:** says the punchline with no right to be speaking.

Example line:

```text
I have been elected by the soup.
```

## Main Settings

The first six recurring gag arenas are:

- Lab
- Outer Space
- Prehistoric World
- Microscopic World
- Alien Planet
- Cave

Reality follows loose logic. A panel can reset everything.

Humans, animals, microbes, rocks, machines, fossils, and cosmic phenomena can talk when the joke requires it.

## Visual Rules

### Format

Panels may be:

- square
- vertical rectangle
- occasionally horizontal if the gag needs breathing room

Every panel should have a heavy black border.

### Linework

The style should be:

- clean-crude
- controlled simple lines
- heavy black outlines
- no fine cross-hatching
- no realism flexing
- direct, functional, joke-first drawing

### Characters

Default body language:

- boxy potato bodies
- short legs
- thick necks
- slight slouch
- messy hair when applicable
- simple hands or mitten-like shapes

Eyes:

- wide white circles
- tiny pupils
- blank stare
- panic stare
- delayed-realization stare

Mouths:

- tiny deadpan line most of the time
- huge screaming hole only when the joke needs panic

Animals:

- upright on two legs
- slightly human hips and shoulders
- neutral animal face
- not overly expressive
- blankness is the joke

### Background Rules

Keep backgrounds minimal.

Allowed background levels:

- horizon line only
- one prop only
- simple room

Common props:

- microscope
- palm tree
- cave rock
- spaceship window
- alien plant
- lab table
- fossil
- ray gun
- tiny flag
- suspicious puddle
- clipboard
- permit form
- route sign

### Color Rules

Weekday panels are mostly black and white.

Sunday panels may use soft muted watercolor washes:

- browns
- muted greens
- soft blues
- dull yellows
- gray-purple shadows

Sci-fi exceptions are allowed for glowing slime, lasers, alien radiation, weird portal light, or ominous microscope glow.

## Joke Rules

Most gags should happen:

- just before disaster
- one second after disaster
- during realization

The punchline can come from:

- dialogue
- caption
- visual reveal
- expression
- scientific label
- object speaking
- calm reaction to impossible danger

Preferred joke style:

```text
one sentence that makes the image funny
```

The reader should stare for three seconds, then realize the trapdoor has opened beneath them.

# The Deviations Panel 1: Nervous Sample

**Status:** Sandbox Media Artifact / Non-Canon / Requires Review  
**Newspaper:** The Anomalist  
**Funnies Section:** The Deviations  

## Setting

Lab

## Cast Mask

Dr. Bounds and Tommy

## Moment

One second before disaster

## Visual Setup

A simple lab room. One microscope on a table. Tommy, drawn as a tiny boxy potato-bodied lab assistant, leans toward the microscope. Dr. Bounds stands beside him with a clipboard. Inside the microscope lens, a huge eye is visible staring back.

## Caption or Dialogue

Tommy: “Dr. Bounds, is the sample supposed to be nervous?”

## Punchline Logic

The microscopic thing is aware, afraid, and somehow large enough emotionally to make the scientists look like the experiment.

## Meridian Prime Pressure Echo

Echoes scientific overconfidence, microscopic dread, and the Meridian Prime habit of turning observation into accidental contact.

## Protected Mystery Check
