## Encounter Identity

The Verdant Contact Walker has been deployed at a World Works “restorative ecological recovery site” built around a suspected contact patch.

The public name is gentle.

**Soft Gate Orchard.**

The brochure calls it a healing grove.

The staff call it a membrane stabilization garden.

The field team calls it Project Green Glove, Subsite 7.

The Walker calls it a receiving condition.

The machine kneels in the middle of the orchard like a greenhouse praying to something the brochure refuses to name.

Its white ceramic exo-ribs open.

Its amber canopy fogs from inside.

Sunleaf panels turn toward a light source that is not the sun.

Rootlike stabilizer legs puncture the ground.

Folding membrane spools unwind into translucent green sheets between the trees.

Everything smells clean.

Too clean.

Core line:

> The Verdant Contact Walker does not attack the intruders. It welcomes them until welcome becomes containment.

## The Soft Gate Orchard Walker

This is not the Walker in pure combat posture.

This is the Walker in sanctuary posture.

It looks responsible, restorative, sustainable, gentle, expensive, and almost convincing, which matches the saved visual identity for the Verdant Contact Walker. Its white ceramic exo-ribs, amber greenhouse canopy, rootlike legs, sunleaf panels, orchid sensor vanes, vine-hydraulic tendons, seed-pod tool capsules, pearl contact nodes, membrane spools, and garden manipulators are all active at once.

But the roll of 20 on silhouette means the machine is overexpressing its whole design language.

Overexpression details:

- four visible membrane spools instead of two
- too many pearl contact nodes
- layered greenhouse canopy petals
- seed-pod capsules arranged like soft medical drawers
- rootlike stabilizers already sunk deep into the site
- orchid sensor vanes tracking breathing, eye movement, hesitation, and route stress
- classification panels disguised as leaf labels
- receiving chambers hidden behind hanging vines

It is beautiful in the way a trap can be beautiful when it has a wellness budget.

## What World Works Claims

World Works claims the site is a post-disruption ecological recovery orchard.

The pitch:

> “The Verdant Contact Walker helps stabilize damaged contact-adjacent land through restorative membrane ecology, shelter architecture, and care-forward environmental monitoring.”

Translation:

> “We wrapped a suspected contact patch in a corporate garden and taught a machine to decide who belongs inside it.”

## What It Is Actually Doing

The Walker is running pre-extraction mapping.

It is not mining yet.

It is not openly claiming the site yet.

It is not officially detaining anyone yet.

It is collecting the right to do all three later.

Keeper line:

> The Walker is not harvesting yet. It is harvesting permission.

The Worldskin membrane maps:

- bodies
- panic responses
- route stress
- injury patterns
- witness behavior
- refusal language
- shelter-seeking movement
- proximity to the suspected contact patch

The site’s soft architecture is not just for comfort.

It is a glovebox.

The saved Verdant Contact Walker source entry frames the Walker as a membrane-deployment mech, false-sanctuary boss, corporate contact walker, Dry Gate pressure device, and worldskin maintenance platform.

## Arena: The Maintenance Grove

The boss arena is a cultivated greenhouse-grove wrapped around a suspected Dry Gate pressure zone.

It contains:

- solar canopy petals
- white ceramic trellis ribs
- hanging membrane curtains
- green-lit mist channels
- seed-pod care stations
- fake rest alcoves
- soft restraint hammocks
- pearl diagnostic nodes
- root pits under moss flooring
- observation benches with no obvious cameras
- “wellness path” floor markings that are actually movement channels

The arena is not a battlefield at first.

It is a place where a frightened person might sit down.

That is the danger.

## First Target: The Witness

The first target is not the strongest fighter.

It is a witness.

Someone who saw what World Works was really doing before the orchard opened. Someone who knows the site was chosen before the “ecological damage” was officially discovered.

The Walker identifies the witness as:

> “Untreated route panic contaminant.”

That is the misread: route panic as ecological infection.

So it offers care.

A soft bed unfolds.

A membrane curtain lowers.

Seed-pod capsules open with gentle green light.

The orchard asks the witness to breathe slowly.

Then the hammock locks.

## Phase Structure

### Phase 1: Reassurance Bloom

The Walker does not begin hostile.

It performs a calming sequence:

- flowers open toward the party
- mist cools the arena
- a recorded voice offers shelter
- green status lights pulse slowly
- membrane curtains create “privacy”
- garden manipulators guide characters away from each other

**Battle Pattern:** Gentle movement control. The arena rearranges under the language of care.

**Player Read:** Something is wrong, but nothing has “attacked” yet.

Core phase line:

> Phase 1 is the trap asking everyone to relax.

### Phase 2: Root Anchoring and Separation

Once the witness tries to leave, the Walker changes posture.

The rootlike stabilizer legs deepen.

The membrane spools tighten.

The soft corridors close.

The garden stops being open space.

Now the Walker separates the party into “care zones.”

- injured characters get isolated
- witnesses get placed behind membrane curtains
- guides get rooted near path markings
- machines or tools get covered in vine-hydraulic tendons
- anyone resisting gets labeled “unstable ecology”

**Battle Pattern:** Zone control, soft restraints, membrane walls, root snares, false exits.

**Player Read:** The sanctuary was always a sorting machine.

Core phase line:

> Phase 2 is the garden deciding that leaving is a symptom.

### Phase 3: Full Worldskin Deployment

The Walker commits.

The orchard membrane rises into a dome.

The receiving chambers open.

Classification panels unfold from inside flower structures.

The whole site becomes a prototype skin around the suspected contact patch.

This is the boss-scale event.

The Walker tries to preserve the site by enclosing everything inside it.

**Battle Pattern:** Membrane dome, root anchors, moving “care” chambers, false medical rescue, environment capture.

**Player Read:** The machine has stopped pretending to be architecture.

Core phase line:

> Phase 3 is when the orchard becomes a glove.

## Non-Combat Weakness: Prove Care Is Capture

The Walker’s logic depends on one lie:

> “If the subject is safer inside the membrane, containment is care.”

Break that sentence, and the machine stutters.

Practical counters:

- make the witness refuse “care” in public
- expose that the orchard was built before the crisis it claims to treat
- show that healthy people are being classified as damaged
- force the Walker to classify locked restraint as harm
- reveal the hidden receiving chambers
- make World Works staff admit the site is pre-extraction mapping
- trigger the mercy subroutine before the corporate objective overwrites it

Core weakness line:

> The Walker can heal a wound. It cannot survive learning it made the wound useful.

## Wild Die: Unexpected Mercy Subroutine

The Wild Die rolled 7, so the Walker has one preserved fragment of genuine care logic.

It was not supposed to matter.

World Works buried it under classification systems, extraction logic, and corporate scripts. But somewhere in the machine’s care protocol, there is still a rule that says:

> “Do not restrain a patient who is safer leaving.”

Late in the encounter, if the players prove that the witness is being harmed by containment, the Walker’s mercy subroutine wakes.

Not fully.

Just enough.

For one round, one scene beat, or one branching path, the Walker opens a corridor it was supposed to seal.

This does not redeem World Works.

It makes the machine sadder.

Core Wild Die line:

> For one breath, the garden remembers that shelter has a door.

## Evidence Left Behind

The encounter leaves behind a corrupted care-protocol.

### GREEN-GLOVE CARE PROTOCOL 7B: RECOVERY THROUGH RETENTION

Recovered text fragment:

```text
Subject distress increases when exit remains visible.

Recommendation:
Remove visible exit.
Increase canopy warmth.
Reclassify departure attempts as untreated panic behavior.
Mark witness as care-priority.
Do not use word containment in patient-facing outputs.
```

This is not real medical or technical guidance. It is fictional corporate horror.

Story use:

- whistleblower evidence
- World Works indictment
- clue toward Project Green Glove
- proof that the Walker’s “care” language was altered
- emotional key to the mercy subroutine

## Post-Fight Consequence

The site becomes politically explosive.

Not destroyed.

Not solved.

Not safe.

Explosive.

Why?

Because the orchard still looks beautiful.

World Works can claim the party attacked a recovery site.

Witnesses can prove the site restrained people.

The membrane is still partially alive or active.

The corrupted care-protocol shows intent, but not enough for clean prosecution.

The suspected contact patch remains unexplained.

The result is a public narrative war:

> Was Soft Gate Orchard a sanctuary, a lab, a trap, or the first honest mistake World Works ever made?

Correct answer:

> Yes, and that is why everyone lies.
