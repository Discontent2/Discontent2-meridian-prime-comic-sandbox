## Core Concept

The Low Road Roadhouse is not officially a roadhouse.

Officially, it is a wet-season dock relay on the NCI / Prime Ops edge, marked seasonal-closed on clean maps and quietly used by anyone too desperate, too broke, too late, or too guilty to take the official route.

Unofficially, it is a roadside bar, pirate radio booth, black-market map counter, salvage porch, wet dock, illegal clinic, and argument room stacked under one rusted roof.

During wet season, the low road drowns. The old roadside structure rises on chained pontoons, dock planks, salvage drums, and spite. Skiffs tie up where trucks used to park. The front room serves bad ration liquor and worse advice. The back room broadcasts illegal route warnings through a pirate signal known as **Low Road Radio**.

**Core Line:** The farther the road sinks, the louder the radio gets.

## Out-of-Town Social Function

The Roadhouse is several rooms at once:

- **Pirate signal relay:** illegal wet-season warnings, route gossip, missing crew notices, coded requests.
- **Black-market map counter:** unofficial low-road routes, flooded spur corrections, bad-map trades.
- **Wet-season dock:** skiff ties, hidden cargo, last-minute crossings.
- **Medical salvage corner:** route medicine, bandages, clinic batteries, unfiled injury notes.
- **Faction negotiation room:** Traverse crews, route planners, NCI-adjacent operators, Prime Ops inspectors, and black-market intermediaries.
- **Bad decision location:** the kind of place where a person can sell a true story for a false map and still think they won.

**Core Line:** The Low Road Roadhouse is what happens when survival refuses zoning.

## Local Rule / Taboo

**Taboo:** Do not ask who filed the first report after sundown.

- **Practical reason:** people get drunk, specific, and armed with memory.
- **Political reason:** too many jurisdictions can be embarrassed by the answer.
- **Radio reason:** after sundown, Low Road Radio sometimes rebroadcasts old voices from the first week after the fire.
- **Fearful reason:** after sundown, the avoided doorway sweats salt.
- **Useful reason:** the taboo prevents the Roadhouse from becoming a courtroom every night.

The taboo is meant to be breakable in a story.

## First Scene Seed

The protagonists arrive by wet-season skiff, expecting a dock and finding a roadhouse floating crooked over red water.

Inside, Low Road Radio is live.

A black-market mapmaker folds a chart the moment they enter. Oren Pell asks whether they need food, fuel, medicine, a clean route, or a lie with better boots.

Then the broadcast cuts through the room:

"Low road advisory. The closure report was filed before the flood."

Every conversation dies.

Behind the avoided doorway, something heavy slides against wood.

Oren reaches for the salt tin where he keeps the duplicate stamp and says:
