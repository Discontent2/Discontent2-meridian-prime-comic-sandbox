Sarrik’s lived name is clean enough for local records, but **“Mastbite”** came from a storm-shift incident near a weather mast where they survived exposure, bad water, and a map dispute that should have killed three crews. The nickname sounds like bravado, but it is really an old accusation wearing a grin with sharp teeth.

### Core Concept

Sarrik “Mastbite” Venn is a Velocisapien route guide who specializes in **wet-season channel crossings near weather masts and signal towers**. They are not officially in charge of the region, but everyone who survives there eventually learns to ask what Sarrik thinks before trusting a road, a mast light, or a clean-looking map.

They sell black-market route knowledge, but not because they are casually criminal. They believe official maps get people killed, especially during wet-season shifts when a marked corridor can become a drowning machine by morning.

### First Impression

A lean Velocisapien in patched route gear, standing too still under a weather mast while rain hisses off metal struts. A quiet medical patch sits high along their ribs, half-hidden under practical wraps. Their eyes follow water movement more than people.

They look like someone who already knows which direction the ground will betray you from.

### Visible Trait / Silhouette

**Quiet medical patch.**  
They never mention it. Anyone who asks gets a flat stare and a change of subject sharp enough to cut rope.

### Personality Pressure

**Fear of ownership.**  
Sarrik hates contracts, permanent service, marked debts, official assignments, and anything that turns survival knowledge into somebody else’s property. They will guide a crew through impossible water, but they will not be “retained,” “claimed,” “sponsored,” or “secured.”

### Useful Skill

**Navigates wet-season channels.**  
Sarrik can read temporary water paths, mast-light distortion, mud-shear, insect movement, floating debris patterns, and the difference between a ford and a trap wearing a ford’s hat.

### Problem

**Belongs to two conflicting worlds.**  
They function as a local authority around the weather mast corridor, but their real knowledge comes through black-market mapmaker networks. Official people need them. Official people also want them contained.

### Secret or Withheld Truth

**Hidden survivor guilt.**  
Sarrik once chose which crew to lead through a storm corridor and which crew to leave waiting. The ones who waited did not all survive. Sarrik still believes the choice was tactically correct. That does not mean they have forgiven themselves.

### How They Help

Sarrik can:

- guide a crew through flooded or shifting terrain
- identify when an official route map is seasonally lethal
- interpret weather mast behavior without overexplaining the signal tech
- warn when a route has been deliberately made unsafe
- get the crew past local suspicion through grudging authority

### How They Complicate

Sarrik refuses ownership so aggressively that they may abandon a deal the moment someone phrases help like possession. They also have enemies among official route authorities, rival map sellers, and survivors who believe Sarrik chose wrong during the old storm incident.

They are useful. They are not obedient.

### Voice Sample

> “That is not a road. That is water practicing fraud. Step there and I’ll charge your ghost double.”
