## Core Identity

The Blue-Still Offering Chamber is a moss-covered stone room hidden by access that changes with the hour. Sometimes it is found behind a shadowed wall of hanging moss. Sometimes beneath a tilted slab. Sometimes not at all.

Inside, vapor rises from narrow cracks in the floor and gathers beneath old agate-streaked stones, turning the chamber blue, wet, and strangely kind. Small white flowers grow in pockets where no soil should hold them. Tree frogs sit on mossy ledges like tiny green priests, listening to thunder far outside.

## Short Principle

A chamber where panic arrives late and leaves early.

## Keeper Line

The Blue-Still Offering Chamber does not answer the road. It gives the Traverse team one night where the road does not ask.

## Visual Scene

The chamber is low, round, and old, its walls padded in moss thick enough to swallow echoes. Agate bands curl through the stone like quiet weather trapped underground. A vapor plume breathes from a crack near the center, silvering the air without choking it. The light is bioluminescent, not bright, not dramatic, just enough to make every damp surface glow with a soft blue pulse.

Near one wall, someone has left a small offering: three dry matches wrapped in waxed cloth, placed beside a smooth stone. No altar. No demand. Just the careful kindness of a previous traveler who wanted the next stranger to have fire.

A harmless set of soft footprints crosses the moss near the far wall. They are not fresh. They are not threatening. But they imply this place has been found before, and left intact.

The footprints do not lead out. They fade near the offering, as if whoever left them chose to stop running here.

## Flora

Small white flowers grow from mossy seams in the wall and around the vapor crack. Their petals close when voices rise and reopen when the room settles. Moss covers nearly every hard edge, making the chamber feel less carved than cushioned.

## Limited Fauna

Tree frogs perch in the moss, blinking slowly. They do not flee from the Traverse team unless someone moves too quickly. One frog sits near the old machine light, its throat pulsing in time with the ambient hum.

If the player waits on the save screen, one of the tree frogs slowly blinks in rhythm with the machine light.

## Mineral / Terrain Features

Agates ripple through the walls in bands of blue, gray, milk-white, and amber. Some bands form partial circles, like old fingerprints pressed into stone. The floor slopes gently toward the vapor plume, but there is enough dry moss near the edges to sleep.

## Natural Soundscape

Distant thunder rolls behind layers of stone. The vapor plume whispers. Tree frogs chirp once, then wait a long time before answering each other. The old machine light emits a faint, patient hum, less like machinery and more like an object relieved it still has one gentle job left.

## Rest Function

This chamber serves as a dream sequence entry and save-screen refuge. The Traverse team can sleep, repair gear, dry clothing near the warm floor cracks, and enter a safe symbolic dream without receiving protected answers. The dream may process fear, grief, or trust, but it does not reveal hidden canon machinery.

## Save Screen Presentation

The old machine light acts as the save point. It glows softly from a moss-covered panel on the back wall, half-swallowed by flowers. When activated, the UI appears as faint agate rings spreading outward from the machine light. The save confirmation sound is a low bell tone followed by the distant thunder folding itself away.

**Save Screen Composition:** Two figures rest in blue vapor light: a Glassphase Antisapian sits near the misted wall, their refractive skin splitting the old machine glow, while a tired human cleans dirt from their hands beside a small circle of repaired gear.

## Companion Moment

The two members sit apart, but not alone. Their gear lies between them in a loose repair circle: straps, buckles, a torn sleeve, a field kit, one dry match packet that neither of them wants to use yet.

The human scrubs dirt from their hands with a damp cloth, slower than necessary. The Glassphase Antisapian watches light move across the agate wall instead of looking into the vapor reflection. After a long quiet, the human nudges a broken clasp across the moss.

“Your hands are steadier than mine.”

The Antisapian fixes it without comment, then leaves it closer than before.

## Hidden Detail

Soft footprints cross the far moss. They belong to no visible creature in the chamber and are too old to imply danger. The frogs do not avoid them. The flowers grow over some of them, as if the room has been slowly tucking the evidence into bed.

## Soft Tension

Someone has been here recently enough to leave the dry matches protected from damp, but not recently enough to threaten the team’s rest. The sanctuary feels shared, not occupied. Borrowed, but kindly.

## Emotional Aftertaste

This exact arrangement matters later: the broken clasp, the unused dry matches, the two figures sitting apart but passing tools between them. The team leaves with no grand revelation, only a small emotional breadcrumb: trust sometimes begins as repaired gear.
