## Core Identity

The Pearlreed Candle Shrine is an abandoned route shrine hidden behind a curtain of reeds deep inside a dry canyon. At deep night, the reeds part just enough to reveal a small candlelit chamber built around a stone fountain that still runs with clear water. Pearlescent deposits cover the shrine walls and fountain lip, turning every candle flame into a soft shell-glow. Desert succulents grow from cracks in the shrine floor, fat with stored rain, while tiny pale crustaceans drift through the fountain basin like harmless thoughts.

## Short Principle

A forgotten shrine where silence learns to heal.

## Visual Scene

Behind the reed curtain, the canyon seems to fold inward. The shrine is small, circular, and half-open to the night above. Its roof collapsed long ago, leaving a ragged star-window over the central fountain. Candle lanterns hang from old hooks in the wall, each one protected from wind by cloudy glass. No one knows who keeps the candles dry.

The fountain is shallow and round, carved from warm stone crusted in pearlescent mineral. Its water spills from a narrow spout shaped like a closed eye, then gathers in a basin full of tiny crustaceans no larger than fingernails. The creatures move slowly, almost ceremonially, and accept crumbs without swarming.

A dry map has been tucked under a flat rock near the fountain. The map is old, but not ancient. Folded carefully. Protected from spray. No one opens it during the rest.

## Flora

Desert succulents grow in small clusters along the shrine wall and around fallen stone. Some have pale waxy leaves, others carry bead-sized flower buds that only open at night. The reeds outside form a living veil, hiding the entrance and giving the shrine a hush like folded cloth.

## Limited Fauna

Tiny crustaceans live in the fountain basin. They are translucent cream-white, with pearl-like shells and slow delicate legs. They respond to movement in the water but show no fear. If offered a crumb, they gather carefully, each taking only a little.

## Mineral / Terrain Features

Pearlescent deposits coat the fountain lip, wall niches, fallen tiles, and old shrine floor. The mineral catches candlelight in pink, cream, blue, and faint green. Some deposits have grown over old route carvings, softening anything that might have once looked official.

## Natural Soundscape

Warm wind hums through canyon holes beyond the reeds. The fountain trickles in patient loops. Candle flames tick softly behind glass. The tiny crustaceans make almost no sound, except for the faintest shell-click when one climbs over another beneath the water.

## Rest Function

This shrine serves as a safe dream-sequence entry and save-screen rest. The Traverse team can refill water, sleep near the fountain, clean small wounds, repair clothes, and drift into symbolic dreams shaped by silence, candlelight, and water. The dreams may soften fear or help companions process grief, but they reveal no protected answers.

## Save Screen Presentation

The save screen uses a crystal glow UI motif. Pearlescent mineral rings around the fountain become faint menu borders, while candle lanterns form the save icons. When the player saves, the fountain water brightens for one breath, and the tiny crustaceans pause as if listening.

**Save Screen Composition:** Three figures rest in a candlelit shrine behind reeds: a solid blue-skinned Glassphase Antisapian listens to the ambient fountain music, a Velocisapien repairs torn clothing with quick clawed hands, and a Tyrannosapien holds a candle lantern like it weighs more than light.

## Companion Moment

One companion hums with the shrine’s music without meaning to. The note is almost swallowed by the fountain, but the others hear it. No one asks them to stop.

The Velocisapien, perched with their tail curled close around a pack, works at a torn sleeve with needle-fast precision. The Glassphase Antisapian sits near the fountain, solid blue skin catching candlelight in subtle refractive bands, listening as though the music is a kind of weather. The Tyrannosapien rests near the widest wall, careful not to crowd the others, one large clawed hand wrapped around the candle lantern.

After a while, the Antisapian drops a crumb into the fountain. The tiny crustaceans gather. The Tyrannosapien watches them with an expression almost too gentle for their heavy jaw.

Then someone tells a harmless story about a pack strap, a stolen fruit, and a bird that judged everyone involved.

For once, the laughter does not hurt.

## Hidden Detail

A dry map is tucked under a flat rock near the fountain. Its edges are weighted with pearl-stone chips. Someone wanted it found, but not tonight.

## Soft Tension

A sealed note remains unread beside the map. It is wrapped in waxed cloth and tied with thread. The team notices it, acknowledges it, and leaves it alone. The sanctuary keeps its peace because no one forces the mystery to speak.

## Emotional Aftertaste

The road feels possible again. Not easy. Not kind. But possible.

When the team leaves, one candle is still burning behind the reed curtain. No one lit it. No one puts it out.
