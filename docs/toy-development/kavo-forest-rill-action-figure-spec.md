# Kavo "Forest" Rill Action Figure Spec

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Status:** First-pass action figure specification  
**Scale Target:** 5-inch retro collector prototype  
**Safety Positioning:** Adult collectible prototype. Not for children. Not safety-certified.

---

## Figure Objective

Create a physical, 3D-printable Kavo "Forest" Rill action figure that reads clearly as:

- Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid
- Mountaineer / route guide / wilderness EMT / GPR path-reader
- terrain truth specialist
- pressure-route street culture professional
- relaxed, funny, dangerous, useful

The figure should be toyetic without becoming goofy. It should feel like it could stand on a collector shelf beside other Meridian Prime field crew figures and still dominate its little patch of plastic weather.

---

## Format

**Recommended scale:** 5 inches tall, excluding tail extension.

**Toy category:** Retro collector action figure prototype.

**Audience:** Adult collectors, sculpt review, internal development, not mass-market child toy.

**Pose language:** Relaxed route-readiness.

**Silhouette anchors:**

- saurian head and snout
- red sparse proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades or route goggles
- angled brow
- light route harness crossing torso
- visible pressure-route badge cluster
- wrapped claws
- long expressive tail
- wide four-toed feet

---

## Proportion Direction

**Toy Adaptation Choice:** Chunky retro action proportions with practical print thickness.

Suggested proportions:

- Head: slightly oversized for expression and shelf read.
- Torso: compact, humanoid, athletic, not superhero-perfect.
- Arms: long enough to imply agile route work, thick enough for pegs.
- Hands: four fingers, clawed, grip-ready.
- Legs: powerful but not monster-heavy.
- Feet: broad four-toed stance for balance.
- Tail: long, sturdy, slightly curved, expressive.

Avoid hyper-real dinosaur anatomy. He is a person with saurian morphology, clothing, tools, and social presence.

---

## Head Sculpt Requirements

The head is the figure's brand stamp.

Required elements:

- saurian snout
- expressive brow ridge
- slight sly mouth shape
- almost vestigial lips, not mammalian lips
- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- readable cheek and jaw structure
- sockets or sculpt support for shades / goggles

Expression target:

> Calm enough to make you think he is joking, sharp enough to make you wonder whether you are the joke.

---

## Body Sculpt Requirements

Required elements:

- upright mostly humanoid saurian body plan
- scaled skin texture on visible areas
- light route harness over torso
- messenger / field strap logic
- subtle checker trim on one or two readable zones
- pressure-route badge cluster
- practical clothing sections that feel worn, not fashionable only
- claw-wraps
- wide stance-compatible feet

Suggested clothing / gear layout:

- cropped dark field jacket or vest shape, sculpted open enough to show harness
- harness crossing chest and waist
- small EMT pouch on hip or back belt
- badge cluster on upper harness strap
- checker trim on cuff, strap tab, or jacket edge
- wrapped wrists / claws

---

## Tail Spec

Tail is essential.

The tail must communicate:

- balance
- attitude
- emotional control
- combat posture
- route-readiness

Recommended first prototype:

- keyed removable tail or swivel tail
- thick base peg
- gentle curve for shelf silhouette
- tip should not be needle-thin
- tail should help figure stand in a tripod stance

Optional later version:

- ball-jointed tail for expressiveness, only after static or swivel-tail prototype succeeds.

---

## Accessory Integration

Hands should be able to hold:

- route probe
- black flag marker
- GPR / route-reader device

Harness or belt should support visual storage for:

- EMT pouch
- brass whistle / route token
- badge cluster

Removable shades / goggles are desirable but optional. If too fragile, create:

1. sculpted-shades head
2. route-goggles head
3. plain-face concept head only for sculpt review

---

## Color Direction Preview

Primary palette:

- scaled skin: muted teal, olive, amber, or route-dust green pending final approval
- proto-quill mohawk: sparse red
- shades: black frames, dark lenses
- harness: dark field leather / rubberized black / worn brown
- trim: subtle black-white checker accents
- metal: brass / copper / worn steel
- badges: small contrasting color hits
- wraps: off-white, route-dust tan, or dark worn cloth

This palette should feel pressure-route, not superhero-bright.

---

## Shelf Read Checklist

At three feet away, the viewer should see:

- saurian head
- red quill crest
- black shades or route goggles
- harness
- badges
- tail
- wide clawed feet
- relaxed danger

At one foot away, the viewer should discover:

- checker trim
- pressure-route badge details
- claw wraps
- EMT / Mountaineer gear
- scale texture
- route-reader accessory

---

## Do Not Overdesign

The first prototype should not carry every idea at once. Kavo does not need to be a walking hardware drawer.

Maximum recommended visible accessory load on figure body:

- harness
- badge cluster
- EMT pouch
- route token / whistle
- goggles or shades

Loose accessories can carry the rest.
