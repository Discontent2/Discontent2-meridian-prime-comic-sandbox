# Kavo "Forest" Rill Articulation Plan

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Status:** First-pass articulation and parts-breakdown plan  
**Figure Scale:** 5-inch retro collector prototype

---

## Design Principle

Start simple. A durable prototype is better than a fragile joint festival.

Kavo's articulation should support relaxed route-readiness, tool-holding, and tail-based attitude. It does not need extreme martial-arts posing. He is not a ninja. He is a Mountaineer who reads the route before the route bites.

---

## Recommended First Prototype Articulation

Target: 7 to 9 articulation points.

| Body Area | Recommended Joint | Priority | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Neck | Swivel or simple ball | High | Needed for sly side-eye and route-reading posture. |
| Shoulders | Swivel or simple ball | High | Must hold route probe or black flag marker. |
| Wrists | Swivel | Medium | Useful for accessory posing. Keep pegs thick. |
| Hips | Simple T-joint or swivel hips | High | Classic retro stance, stable, easier to print. |
| Tail | Keyed removable or swivel | High | Essential for balance and personality. |
| Waist | Optional swivel | Low | Add only if torso / harness tolerances allow. |
| Elbows | Optional hinge or cut joint | Low | Nice later, not required for first prototype. |
| Knees | Optional hinge or cut joint | Low | May weaken legs; test later. |

---

## Prototype Engineering Choice

First prototype recommendation:

- neck swivel
- two shoulder swivels
- two wrist swivels
- two hip swivels or T-joint hips
- keyed removable tail or swivel tail

Approximate total:

- 8 points if both wrists and tail move
- 7 points if tail is keyed removable but not poseable

---

## Tail Engineering

The tail is not decoration.

Tail options:

### Option A: Static Tail

**Pros:** easiest print, strongest, cleanest sculpt.  
**Cons:** less toyetic, lower expressive value.

### Option B: Keyed Removable Tail

**Pros:** easier packing, easier painting, strong alignment, usable for balance.  
**Cons:** not poseable unless designed as a swivel.

### Option C: Swivel Tail

**Pros:** retro action figure feel, expressive, useful for balance tweaks.  
**Cons:** needs socket tolerance testing.

### Option D: Ball-Jointed Tail

**Pros:** most expressive.  
**Cons:** highest failure risk, possible looseness, fragile socket at this scale.

**Recommended first prototype:** keyed removable tail with a round enough socket to support later swivel testing.

## Joint Tolerance Test Pieces

Before printing the full figure, create small joint test coupons:

- neck peg and socket
- shoulder peg and socket
- wrist peg and socket
- hip peg and socket
- tail peg and socket

Recommended tolerance test range for resin prototypes:

- tight fit: 0.10 mm clearance
- medium fit: 0.15 mm clearance
- loose fit: 0.20 mm clearance

Actual values depend on printer, resin, cure time, shrinkage, and sanding.

---

## Pose Goals

The first prototype should support:

- neutral standing pose
- relaxed lean with tail support
- head turned slightly as if reading terrain
- one hand holding route probe
- one hand holding GPR / route-reader device
- black flag marker held or displayed nearby

Do not require:

- kneeling
- sprinting
- two-handed weapon grip
- acrobatic balance poses
- high kicks

---

## Sculpt Notes for Articulation

Avoid fragile areas:

- thin quills
- thin claws
- tiny free-floating straps
- glasses arms thinner than print-safe scale
- badge details that become flakes
- hollow sockets too close to figure edges

Hide or justify joint seams through:

- harness straps
- clothing cuffs
- scale banding
- wrist wraps
- belt / hip gear
- natural tail base folds

---

## Future Upgrade Pass

After the durable prototype works, consider:

- alternate head with route goggles
- elbow cuts
- knee cuts
- waist swivel
- ball-tail variant
- alternate gripping hands
- display base shaped like black-flagged Path terrain

Do not add these until the first prototype proves the silhouette, balance, and print tolerances.
