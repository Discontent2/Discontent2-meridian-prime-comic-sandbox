## Recommended Printing Method

**First prototype:** resin 3D print for detail and character sculpt evaluation.

**Possible later durability test:** FDM or tough resin for larger parts, especially tail and accessories.

Recommended resin type:

- tough or ABS-like resin for joints and tail
- standard detail resin only for non-load-bearing review parts

Avoid brittle decorative resins for pegs, claws, glasses, tail sockets, and accessory grips.

## Safety Positioning

This prototype is:

- adult collectible prototype
- not intended for children
- not safety-certified
- not for sale until safety, material, labeling, and compliance questions are reviewed by qualified professionals

3D-print safety:

- use ventilation
- wear gloves when handling uncured resin
- wear eye protection when needed
- avoid skin contact with uncured resin
- cure parts properly
- control sanding dust
- wet-sand where appropriate
- wear respiratory protection when sanding cured resin
- avoid sharp points and brittle tips
- do not let children handle uncured resin or fragile test parts

## Print Orientation Notes

General orientation suggestions:

- Head: angle backward slightly so snout and brow details are supported cleanly.
- Torso: angle 30 to 45 degrees to reduce flat suction areas.
- Arms / legs: angle along the longest axis to preserve surface detail.
- Tail: angle diagonally; support underside and socket carefully.
- Accessories: print in multiples because tiny tools love vanishing into the carpet goblin dimension.

Avoid placing supports on:

- face
- shades lenses
- badge faces
- checker trim
- hand grip interiors
- visible tail top line

---

## Joint Test Plan

Before full print, create joint test coupons for:

- neck peg / socket
- shoulder peg / socket
- wrist peg / socket
- hip peg / socket
- tail peg / socket
- hand grip around route probe

Recommended tolerance test range:

- 0.10 mm clearance
- 0.15 mm clearance
- 0.20 mm clearance

Record printer, resin, layer height, exposure, wash time, cure time, and final fit result.

---

## Assembly Guidance

Suggested assembly order:

1. Clean and cure all parts fully.
2. Test fit joints before sanding detail areas.
3. Sand pegs gradually, never force brittle joints.
4. Test tail fit and standing balance before painting.
5. Prime parts separately.
6. Paint head, body, limbs, and accessories.
7. Seal paint.
8. Final assemble.
9. Re-test balance with accessories in hand.

---

## Balance Strategy

Kavo should stand with three-point logic:

- wide four-toed feet
- slightly bent relaxed knees
- tail touching or nearly touching the ground behind him

Tail should be shaped to help, not fight, the figure's center of gravity.

---

## Failure Risks

Watch for:

- tail too heavy pulling figure backward
- feet too narrow
- shades too fragile
- quills too thin
- claws too sharp or brittle
- wrist pegs too small
- route flag pole snapping
- route probe warping
- paint scraping at joints

---

## First Test Print Goals

The first test print succeeds if:

- silhouette reads as Kavo / Forest
- figure stands without a display base
- tail supports balance
- head expression works at shelf distance
- hands hold at least one accessory
- no required canon marker is missing
- no part is dangerously sharp or absurdly fragile
