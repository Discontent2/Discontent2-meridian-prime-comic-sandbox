## Recommended Printing Method

**First prototype:** resin 3D print for sculpt detail, anatomy review, fit testing, garment readability, watch scale, and paint evaluation.

**Possible later durability test:** tough resin, ABS-like resin, or hybrid larger-part strategy for load-bearing parts such as the tail, pelvis, hands, watch-bearing wrist, and accessories.

Recommended resin type:

- Tough or ABS-like resin for joints, pegs, sockets, hands, tail, boots, wrist watch area, and accessories.
- High-detail resin only for non-load-bearing visual review parts.
- Avoid brittle decorative resins for pegs, claws, shades, watch buttons, tail sockets, and accessory grips.

Recommended first print layer height:

- 0.03 mm to 0.05 mm for head, hands, face, watch, badges, and accessory masters.
- 0.05 mm is acceptable for blockout, large torso, limbs, boots, tail, jacket, and pants tests.

Use actual printer, resin, exposure, and cure settings from physical test results, not assumptions.

## Resin and Shop Safety

- Work with ventilation.
- Wear nitrile gloves when handling uncured resin, contaminated tools, supports, or wash containers.
- Wear eye protection when pouring resin, removing supports, clipping brittle parts, or handling IPA / wash fluid.
- Avoid all skin contact with uncured resin.
- Follow resin manufacturer safety data and disposal instructions.
- Keep uncured resin and contaminated paper towels away from children, pets, food areas, and household surfaces.
- Cure parts fully before sanding or heavy handling.
- Control sanding dust.
- Wet-sand where appropriate.
- Wear respiratory protection when sanding cured resin.
- Do not blow resin dust into the room.
- Blunt sharp claws, quills, tail tips, route probe tips, flag markers, boot claws, and watch corners.
- Do not let children handle uncured resin, fragile test prints, sharp failed prints, or loose prototype parts.

## Support Strategy

### General Support Rules

- Use heavier supports on hidden structural areas.
- Use lighter supports near visible detail, then inspect for warping.
- Avoid support contact on face, shades lenses, watch screen, badge faces, checker trim, hand grip interiors, visible tail top line, and optional handheld route-reader screen.
- Add extra support to heavy tail base and underside curve to prevent sagging.
- Print delicate accessories in multiples.
- Keep tiny accessory handles slightly oversized for sanding and fit tuning.
- Keep watch buttons, strap texture, cargo pocket flaps, and jacket cuffs robust rather than tiny.
- Do not chase perfect miniaturization if a thicker part gives a better adult prototype.

### Part-Specific Notes

**Head:** support from rear skull, lower jaw underside, and neck underside. Keep proto-quills sturdy enough to survive support removal.

**Jacket / torso:** support hidden underside, side seams, and back surfaces. Avoid lapel faces, collar texture, checker trim, and badge faces.

**Hands / watch:** support glove backs, cuff underside, and watch strap underside. Avoid palm grip grooves, claws, fingertips, watch screen, watch buttons, and visible scaled wrist.

**Cargo pants / boots:** support from soles and inner leg. Tread can accept cleanup scars better than front toe claw or cargo pocket faces.

**Tail:** support from underside and lower curve. Preserve keyed socket dimensions. Add redundant supports at base.

**Accessories:** print route probe, black flag marker, token, clip, goggles, and optional handheld route-reader in duplicate or triplicate.

## Hollowing and Drainage

Default recommendation for Prototype A:

- Do not hollow small parts, hands, watch wrist sample, accessories, heads, or joints.
- Consider hollowing only large torso pieces if resin weight, suction, or cure concerns require it.
- If hollowing torso parts, use proper drain holes in hidden areas and fully cure interiors.
- Do not hollow tail unless it becomes too heavy and an engineer reviews wall thickness.
- Never leave uncured resin trapped inside a sealed part.

## Wash, Cure, Sanding, and Primer Sequence

1. Remove build plate with gloves and eye protection.
2. Let excess resin drip according to shop practice.
3. Wash parts using the resin manufacturer's recommended method.
4. Use a separate cleaner pass for detailed parts if needed.
5. Dry parts fully before curing.
6. Remove supports while parts are slightly warm / manageable if that is safe for the resin and detail.
7. Cure parts fully according to resin guidance.
8. Inspect for uncured pockets, cracks, warping, tackiness, or trapped resin.
9. Wet-sand support marks and peg surfaces as needed.
10. Test-fit raw joints before primer.
11. Sand pegs gradually. Never force brittle joints.
12. Prime parts with thin coats.
13. Re-test joints after primer.
14. Paint with Prototype A guide.
15. Seal paint with finish appropriate to each area.
16. Final assemble only after paint and clear coat are fully cured.
17. Re-test balance with tail and accessories.

Sanding notes:

- Wet-sand cured resin when possible.
- Wear respiratory protection for any dry sanding.
- Keep sanding dust contained.
- Prioritize fit surfaces: neck peg, wrists, shoulder pegs, hips, tail key, accessory handles.
- Preserve face, watch screen, checker trim, scale texture, badge shapes, cargo pockets, jacket collar, and route-reader screen details.

Primer notes:

- Use thin primer coats.
- Do not flood sockets, pegs, grip channels, watch details, or tail key.
- Prime test coupons before final joint decisions.
- Record whether primer changes fit.

## Assembly Guidance

1. Clean and cure all parts fully.
2. Inspect parts under strong light.
3. Test fit joints before sanding detail areas.
4. Sand pegs gradually.
5. Test left wrist watch scale against hand / glove / cuff read.
6. Test jacket / harness shoulder clearance.
7. Test pants / hip movement clearance.
8. Test tail fit and standing balance before painting.
9. Test accessory handles in hand coupons.
10. Prime parts separately.
11. Re-test joints after primer.
12. Paint head, body, garments, limbs, tail, watch, and accessories.
13. Seal paint.
14. Final assemble.
15. Re-test balance with accessories in hand.
16. Photograph front, side, back, watch close-up, garment close-up, and shelf-read angles.
17. Log failures and revisions.

Do not glue movable joints unless the prototype is intentionally being converted into a static paint master.

## Balance Strategy

Kavo should stand using three-point logic:

- wide four-toed field boots
- stable hip stance
- tail touching or nearly touching the ground behind him

Balance test positions:

1. Neutral stand, no accessories.
2. Neutral stand with wrist watch only.
3. Neutral stand with route probe / staff in hand.
4. Neutral stand with black Path flag marker.
5. Tail installed, no support base.
6. Tail installed and lightly rotated if swivel passes.
7. Harness and pack attached if separate.

Failure signs:

- tail pulls figure backward
- boots are too narrow
- hips are too loose
- tail socket droops
- watch overweights / distorts left wrist silhouette
- hands cannot hold accessories without tipping the figure
- field pack makes figure back-heavy

## Failure Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Watch too large | Resize to compact wrist scale, use KFR-COUPON-004, compare against hand / glove silhouette. |
| Watch reads as wrist warmer | Keep distinct hard case, screen, side buttons, and strap. Preserve black fingerless glove. |
| Human skin contamination | Enforce Route-Dust Olive Teal scaled skin on all exposed Forest parts. Reject human-skin figure references. |
| Jacket blocks shoulders | Trim jacket bulk, test shoulder coupon with harness and sleeve. |
| Pants pockets block hips | Flatten or shift pocket relief, test hip coupon. |
| Tail too heavy and pulls figure backward | Thicken boot stance, adjust tail angle, lighten tail only if safely hollowed, use tail as third contact point. |
| Tail socket cracks | Increase wall thickness, use keyed peg with generous fillets, test coupon before full print. |
| Feet too narrow | Widen boot soles and keep four-toe stance flat. |
| Shades too fragile | Thicken frames, avoid separate thin arms for first print. |
| Proto-quills too thin | Shorten and thicken quills; avoid needle points. |
| Claws too sharp / brittle | Blunt and thicken claws. |
| Wrist pegs too small | Increase peg diameter and hide seam with glove cuff / watch placement. |
| Route flag pole snaps | Increase diameter, use tough resin, print duplicates. |
| Route probe warps | Print diagonally with adequate supports; test thicker shaft. |
| Paint scrapes at joints | Use coupon testing, sand pegs, avoid heavy primer in sockets. |
| Badge details flake | Simplify relief and avoid tiny raised slivers. |
| Accessory handles do not fit hands | Use KFR-COUPON-008 before final accessory print. |
| Field pack makes figure back-heavy | Use lightweight pack, sculpted-on pack, or omit pack from first balance test. |
