# Kavo "Forest" Rill Packaging Concept

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Status:** First-pass cardback / bio-file concept  
**Product Positioning:** Adult collectible prototype. Not for children. Not final retail packaging.

---

## Packaging Goal

Create retro cardback energy without copying protected trade dress or another toyline's identity.

The package should feel like a Meridian Prime field-file artifact that got turned into a collector card: bold, strange, procedural, and toyetic.

---

## Legal / Originality Guardrail

Do not use:

- turtle shells
- sewer language
- pizza language
- ooze / mutagen language
- ninja vocabulary
- four-color turtle-team structure
- direct parody of protected cardback layouts
- copied logo shapes or trade dress

Borrow only broad toy-line grammar:

- strong character name
- bold portrait art
- visible accessories
- bio-file text
- action callouts
- collectible figure energy

---

## Back Card Bio-File Draft

**Name:** Kavo Rill  
**Crew Name:** Forest Rill  
**Species:** Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid  
**Role:** Mountaineer / route guide / wilderness EMT / GPR path-reader  
**Traverse Function:** Terrain Truth  
**Known For:** Seeing the dangerous path before the paperwork admits it is dangerous.

Bio copy:

Kavo Rill moves through Traverse work with the calm of someone who has already heard the terrain lie. The crew calls him Forest because he can see the forest through the trees, which is funny on a world where forests are rare and bad route jokes survive longer than good equipment. As MITE II's Mountaineer and GPR path-reader, Forest reads crevasse fields, pressure ridges, black-flagged Paths, and bad mission timing with the same streetwise patience. Quiet until funny, funny until dangerous, dangerous until useful, he is the one you want watching the route when the official map starts sweating.

## Safety / Prototype Labeling

Packaging should state:

- Adult collectible prototype.
- Not intended for children.
- Contains small parts.
- Not safety-certified.
- Prototype materials and paints are for display only unless reviewed for handling safety.

Commercial release would require qualified review of toy safety, age grading, small parts, sharp points, paint / material safety, labeling, and testing requirements.
