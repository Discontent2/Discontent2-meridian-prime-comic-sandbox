## Canon Reference

Kavo Rill is his real name.

Forest Rill is his human / crew name.

He is a Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid.

He is a Mountaineer, route guide, wilderness EMT, and GPR path-reader.

He represents terrain truth among the first three MITE II crew anchors:

1. Rudy Calder = machine truth
2. Kavo "Forest" Rill = terrain truth
3. Ildi Vey = signal truth

Kavo should feel:

- relaxed menace
- precise
- streetwise
- impossible to rush
- quiet until funny
- funny until dangerous
- dangerous until useful

His first toy read should be: this character knows the safe path before the map admits the path is dangerous.

The prototype must preserve:

- mostly humanoid saurian body plan
- universal tail
- four-fingered clawed hands
- four-toed clawed feet
- expressive saurian face
- saurian head / snout / brow structure
- scaled skin
- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades when not using route goggles
- route goggles as alternate look
- subtle checker trim
- light route harness
- pressure-route badges
- claw-wraps
- relaxed posture that can become dangerous instantly

---

## Velocisapien / Boiler-Ska Design Grammar

Velocisapien design logic should communicate 2-tone ska / boiler-ska / pressure-route street style through:

- posture
- silhouette
- route harness
- messenger strap logic
- badge placement
- checker trim
- shades / goggles
- confident lean
- underclass route-culture detail
- motion language
- practical field tools

Do not translate this through literal music props, guitars, microphones, concert staging, parody band outfits, or modern stagewear.

---

## Toy Adaptation Choice

Recommended figure format:

- 5-inch retro collector action figure scale
- adult collectible / prototype positioning
- chunky but not goofy proportions
- strong silhouette
- tail engineered for balance and attitude
- toyetic expression: sly, calm, dangerous
- pose: relaxed route-readiness, not mid-sprint
- hands sculpted to hold route tools
- feet wide enough to stand without a base
- accessories large enough to print and handle

The figure should look like a field professional from a pressure-world toyline. Not a mascot. Not a monster. Not a ninja. Not a lizard in a costume.

---

## Prototype Engineering Choice

For the first physical prototype, prioritize durability over maximum articulation.

The sculpt should use:

- broad scale texture zones instead of tiny fragile scale noise
- thickened claws with softened tips
- sturdy quills instead of needle-like hair spikes
- bold harness straps that can survive handling
- large goggles / shades that can be printed separately or sculpted onto an alternate head
- tail as balance structure, not decoration
- sockets and pegs that can be tested separately before final print

## Rejected / Do Not Use

Do not make Kavo:

- a generic raptor scout
- a ninja
- an assassin
- animalistic
- a dinosaur monster
- a human in reptile makeup
- a parody of any protected toyline
- overloaded with fantasy weapons
- dependent on literal ska-band props
- impossible to 3D print due to tiny brittle bits

## Core Design Sentence

Kavo "Forest" Rill is a calm Velocisapien Mountaineer with pressure-route swagger, field-earned precision, sly danger, and a tail that says he already knows where the terrain is lying.
