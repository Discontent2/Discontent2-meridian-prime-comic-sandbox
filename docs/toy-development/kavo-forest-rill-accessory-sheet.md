# Kavo "Forest" Rill Accessory Sheet

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Status:** First-pass accessory planning sheet  
**Figure Scale:** 5-inch retro collector prototype

---

## Accessory Design Rule

Accessories should come from Kavo's canon role and Traverse field culture.

He is a Mountaineer, route guide, wilderness EMT, GPR path-reader, and pressure-route professional. His accessories should feel useful, worn, procedural, and slightly charismatic. Avoid generic fantasy weapons. Avoid literal music props.

---

## Core Accessory Loadout

Recommended first prototype accessories:

1. Route goggles
2. GPR / route-reader device
3. Black Path flag marker
4. Compact EMT pouch
5. Route probe / collapsible staff

This gives him terrain truth, field medicine, Path culture, and hand-held toy play without turning him into an armory.

---

## Accessory Concepts

### 1. Black-Frame Shades

**Label:** Canon Reference / Toy Adaptation Choice

Purpose:

- establishes Velocisapien boiler-ska cool menace
- gives instant shelf read
- supports relaxed, sly expression

Prototype approach:

- Version A: sculpted onto head
- Version B: removable oversized frames
- Version C: separate display accessory only

Risk:

- removable glasses may be fragile at 5-inch scale

Recommendation:

- sculpted-on shades for first head
- removable shades as later test

---

### 2. Route Goggles

**Label:** Canon Reference / Toy Adaptation Choice

Purpose:

- alternate field look
- connects him to GPR prep, route reading, and Traverse work

Design notes:

- thick black or dark frames
- broad lenses
- strap detail can run around head or sit as sculpted strap fragments
- avoid ultra-thin elastic-style strap

Recommendation:

- alternate head with sculpted route goggles is safer than removable goggles for prototype one

---

### 3. GPR / Route-Reader Device

**Label:** Canon Reference / Prototype Engineering Choice

Purpose:

- visualizes Kavo as path-reader and terrain truth specialist
- gives one hand a non-weapon tool

Design notes:

- chunky handheld unit
- small screen or gauge face
- side grip sized for four-finger hand
- surface detail: route lines, small knobs, worn casing

Print notes:

- minimum thickness over delicate handles
- screen detail can be painted rather than fully sculpted

---

### 4. Black Path Flag Marker

**Label:** Canon Reference / Toy Adaptation Choice

Purpose:

- connects Kavo to The Path and black-flagged crevasse procedure
- simple, iconic silhouette accessory

Design notes:

- short field stake
- rectangular black flag
- slightly ragged but not shredded
- thickened pole for resin durability

Risk:

- thin pole may snap

Recommendation:

- use thickened pole, maybe oval in cross-section

---

### 5. Compact Mountaineer Field Pack

**Label:** Toy Adaptation Choice

Purpose:

- adds working-professional silhouette
- can hold implied route / EMT gear

Design notes:

- compact, close to back
- no giant backpack blob
- straps integrate with route harness
- sculpted buckles large enough to paint

Recommendation:

- sculpted-on field pack for first prototype, removable pack later if needed

---

### 6. EMT Pouch / Med-Kit

**Label:** Canon Reference / Toy Adaptation Choice

Purpose:

- preserves wilderness EMT role
- distinguishes him from generic scout / fighter

Design notes:

- hip pouch or belt pouch
- small cross, route medical mark, or field-medicine icon if later approved
- worn canvas or rubberized case feel

Canon caution:

- do not invent a final medical insignia as canon without review

---

### 7. Brass Whistle or Route Token

**Label:** Canon Reference / Toy-Only Continuity until approved

Purpose:

- compact cultural accessory
- pressure-route / boiler-ska detail without becoming a music prop

Design notes:

- small brass token on harness
- can be sculpted as badge detail rather than removable tiny part

Recommendation:

- sculpt on harness for first prototype

---

### 8. Route Probe / Collapsible Staff

**Label:** Toy Adaptation Choice

Purpose:

- field tool, not weapon
- gives action figure classic hand-held silhouette

Design notes:

- short collapsible probe extended for toy accessory
- blunt tip
- grip zone sized for hand
- etched route marks possible as paint detail

Guardrail:

- do not make it a spear, sword, or fantasy staff

---

### 9. Pressure-Route Badge Cluster

**Label:** Canon Reference / Candidate Canon

Purpose:

- identifies route culture and pressure-runner history
- adds visual texture

Design notes:

- 3 to 5 badge shapes on harness
- larger than real scale for paintability
- mix circle, rectangle, flag-tab shapes

Canon caution:

- individual badge meanings are not canon until approved

---

### 10. Alternate Hands

**Label:** Prototype Engineering Choice

Potential hand set:

- relaxed open hand
- route-reader grip hand
- probe grip hand
- black flag grip hand

Recommendation:

- start with two gripping hands only
- add alternates after fit tests

---

## First Prototype Accessory Priority

Must include:

- route-reader device
- black flag marker
- route probe

Should include:

- route goggles or shades
- EMT pouch

Nice later:

- alternate hands
- removable field pack
- route token as loose accessory

---

## Accessory Safety Notes

This prototype is not for children.

Even for adult handling:

- avoid needle-like points
- avoid brittle ultra-thin flag poles
- avoid razor-sharp claws or probe tips
- cure resin fully
- sand sharp edges
- test accessory grips before painting
