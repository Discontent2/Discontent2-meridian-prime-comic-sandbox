## Purpose

Page 06 is the **cab-interior grief / object-rhythm page**.

It is not an exterior machine page.  
It is not a crew-introduction page.  
It is not the Lindy packet-handoff page.  
It is not a route montage.  
It is not a literal ghost scene.  
It is not the explanation of Grandfather Ten's pistol.

It is the moment when practical field ritual lets grief enter the comic through objects.

This page should show:

- Tenet inside Red Umbrielor's cab
- dead / weak defrost
- frosted windshield
- yard lights smeared through glass
- improvised turkey roaster / auxiliary heater
- empty passenger seat
- Rob's absence as composition, not apparition
- under-seat compartment checked
- pistol presence planted as inheritance weight, not explained

Core page promise:

> Tiny domestic machinery meets grief. The turkey roaster is funny; the empty seat is not.

## Recommended Panel Breakdown

### Panel 1

Small or medium cab-interior panel.

Tenet sits or leans into Red Umbrielor's cab. Frosted windshield fills the view. Yard lights smear into blurry ghosts outside. Tenet reaches for the defrost control.

Required details:

- frosted / fogged windshield
- dim pre-dawn cab light
- analog/digital hybrid controls
- Tenet's gloved or half-gloved hand near defrost control
- no exterior establishing shot dominating the panel

Approved text:

```text
TK. TK.
```

```text
Come on.
```

---

### Panel 2

Small reaction / object panel.

The defrost vent gives almost nothing. Tenet gives the dash a flat look he learned from Rob.

Required details:

- dead or weak vent
- barely moving vapor / pathetic air puff
- Tenet's dry, experienced expression
- cab grime and old machine texture

Approved text:

```text
hhhff
```

```text
Fine.
```

Tone:

This is a familiar nuisance, not a catastrophe.

---

### Panel 3

Medium panel with warm center.

Tenet kicks on the turkey roaster: an improvised auxiliary heater in a bolted cage, glowing dull orange. It should look like a bad decision that survived enough winters to become procedure.

Required details:

- turkey roaster / Waviston-style auxiliary heater
- bolted safety cage
- dull orange glow
- improvised but stable field-fix logic
- old cab floor / bracket / wiring / scorch marks
- warm light beginning to fight the cold blue cab

Approved text:

```text
KLIK. RRRRATTLE.
```

Approved caption:

```text
The turkey roaster came alive with the confidence of a field fix nobody wanted to explain to insurance.
```

Important:

Do not make the turkey roaster a futuristic reactor, magic device, polished sci-fi heater, or clean factory component. It is a questionable field fix that became family.

---

### Panel 4

Wider quiet panel.

The windshield clears in a half-moon. Warm light touches the cab. The passenger seat sits empty.

Required details:

- half-moon cleared area on windshield
- warm heater light on cold cab surfaces
- empty passenger seat as emotional focus
- no Rob figure in the seat
- no literal ghost
- no dramatic supernatural glow

Approved caption:

```text
The passenger seat stayed empty.
```

Tone:

The panel should breathe. The empty seat is the emotional hit.

---

### Panel 5

Small memory-impression panel.

Rob's absence appears as composition, not apparition. Maybe a salmon sleeve implied by color memory, a duffel shape that is not really there, a warm indentation / remembered posture, or Tenet's glance avoiding the seat.

Allowed Rob-memory elements:

- faint salmon-color suggestion
- duffel-like shape that reads as memory, not literal object
- warm reflected color where Rob used to sit
- empty seat composition that implies Rob's old presence
- Tenet looking away too quickly

Disallowed Rob-memory elements:

- full ghost Rob
- transparent apparition
- supernatural figure
- Rob sitting there clearly
- Rob speaking from beyond the grave as a literal scene
- flashback that overtakes the page

Approved faint memory dialogue:

```text
Defrost's bullshit.
```

Lettering rule:

Rob memory dialogue should be faint or visually distinct from normal speech. It is memory pressure, not an active ghost conversation.

---

### Panel 6

Bottom close-up.

Tenet leans down and checks the under-seat compartment. Close on his hand near the latch. The compartment is closed and locked. The pistol does not need to be visible clearly. The page is about the weight of it.

Required details:

- under-seat compartment location
- Tenet's hand near latch
- closed / locked compartment
- practical cab hardware
- emotional restraint

Approved captions:

```text
Closed.
```

```text
Locked.
```

```text
Still there.
```

Reveal rule:

The pistol may be implied as a wrapped shape, shadow, weight, or hidden object. Do not show it clearly unless absolutely necessary, and do not explain what it does.

## Turkey Roaster / Auxiliary Heater Rules

The turkey roaster is a crew / NCI field term for an improvised or auxiliary cab-heating setup used when proper defrost or heat is lagging.

It should accomplish several things visually:

- establish practical field texture
- show Red Umbrielor as old, beloved, and imperfect
- show that NCI gear works because people know its defects
- provide a small funny beat before grief lands
- warm the cab enough to reveal the empty seat

It should feel:

- improvised
- bolted down
- caged for safety
- dull orange
- field-tested
- slightly absurd
- beloved by repeated use
- not fully safe-looking, but accepted as procedure

Do not over-explain it visually with labels. Let it appear as normal field language.

## Grandfather Ten's Pistol / Under-Seat Compartment Rules

The under-seat compartment is a planted inheritance-object beat.

Allowed:

- show the compartment latch
- show Tenet's hand checking it
- show that it is closed and locked
- imply a hidden weight inside
- show a wrapped shape or shadow only if subtle

Do not:

- show Tenet drawing the pistol
- show the pistol clearly as a hero prop
- explain the pistol's function
- show diagrams, mechanisms, lore symbols, or cosmology
- turn the pistol into a weapon glamour moment
- make the panel about gun coolness

The emotional function is:

> Tenet checks that the inheritance is still there before he is ready to admit why that matters.

## Common Failure Modes

These are likely image-generation failure modes for Page 06 and should be actively avoided:

1. **Exterior drift**  
   The generator turns Page 06 into another exterior Red Umbrielor / convoy page.

2. **Crew drift**  
   The generator introduces Lindy, Rudy, Forest, Ildi, or the full team too early.

3. **Packet drift**  
   The generator borrows Page 07 and makes Lindy bring the MITE II packet.

4. **Ghost drift**  
   The generator makes Rob a literal ghost instead of an empty-seat memory pressure.

5. **Gun drift**  
   The generator makes the under-seat pistol into a visible weapon moment.

6. **Sci-fi cockpit drift**  
   The cab becomes too sleek, futuristic, or spaceship-like.

7. **Polar drift**  
   Frosted glass becomes Antarctica / arctic expedition identity.

8. **Over-dialogue drift**  
   The generator invents exposition and mission philosophy rather than staying intimate.

Treat these as hard failures.
