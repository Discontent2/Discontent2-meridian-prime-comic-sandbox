## Overall Polish Diagnosis

The full 24-page draft has the right hype-machine architecture:

1. Rob's refusal opens the wound.
2. Tenet's cold start grounds the world in work.
3. Horus's approval stamp poisons the packet.
4. MITE II proves the crew and machine are real.
5. The ghost handshake moves wrongness from paper into signal.
6. The pistol memory binds Rob's death-chain to Tenet's route.
7. Terrain wrongness escalates into the hidden formation and footprints.
8. Page 22 delivers the emotional thesis.
9. Pages 23-24 close with Traverse culture and Rob's agency.

The draft is strong enough to move into a second script pass. The main polishing need is **compression**. Several lines are good but slightly too pleased with themselves. The comic should keep its teeth, but trim any line that makes the page wink too hard.

Guiding phrase for polish:

> Field document with teeth. Not lore lecture. Not quip storm.

## Character Voice Polish

### Rob Holliday

**Current strength:** Rob feels warm, blunt, funny, and already victorious. His dialogue has the right salmon-polo brass-knuckle energy.

**Risk:** Rob can become too aphoristic if every line lands like a perfect epitaph.

**Keep:**

- `Bullshit.`
- `Horus, you made everything smaller the day you started calling old friends assets.`
- `Ten liked you. That was never the same thing.`
- `You came for an answer. I sent you a route.`

**Tighten / Watch:**

- `For the question to catch up.` is strong, but very polished. Keep for now as Candidate Canon, but test whether it feels too oracle-like in lettering.
- `That's a fun question.` works if Rob is dry and tired. If it reads too cute, revise to: `That one took you long enough.`

**Polish target:** Rob should sound like a man who has already made his last move, not a man performing riddles for the reader.

---

### Tenet Nocsid III

**Current strength:** Tenet reads as field-grounded and practical. He logs, checks, asks for field language, preserves evidence, and does not speechify.

**Risk:** Page 22 risks turning him into a symbolic protagonist if the montage overexplains his realization.

**Keep:**

- `Say that in field language.`
- `Nobody steps near it.`
- `Nobody explains it before we preserve it.`
- `Then we do ugly once.`

**Tighten / Watch:**

- `If something squeals, lies, or develops religion, I want it logged.` is funny and field-flavored, but may be one clause too long. Possible tightened version: `If something squeals, lies, or prays, log it.`
- `Rob...` on Page 22 is simple and should remain simple.

**Polish target:** Tenet should process the impossible through procedure first and grief second. That restraint is the engine.

---

### Lindy Nocsid

**Current strength:** Lindy's entrance has bite, sibling rhythm, and practical dispatch competence.

**Risk:** Two consecutive jokes about defrost may crowd the emotional undertow of Page 7.

**Keep:**

- `Defrost never signed. Defrost had legal review.`
- `That unspecific.`
- `Yeah. Saw that.`

**Tighten / Watch:**

- `Runs in the family.` is good, but if the page feels too banter-heavy, cut it and let the turkey-roaster exchange carry the comedy.
- `Barely. And I improved it.` is cute. Keep only if Lindy's tone needs one more spark before Horus stamp darkness.

**Polish target:** Lindy should be funny because she is armored, not because the scene needs jokes.

---

### Horus Skelton

**Current strength:** Horus feels personal and late, which is better than cartoon-evil. He is an old friend turned institutional knife.

**Risk:** Horus does not yet show enough pain beneath control. He asks and demands, but his loss could cut deeper in one visual beat.

**Keep:**

- `Robert.`
- `Do not make this smaller than it is.`
- `Ten trusted me too.`

**Needs More Bite:**

- Add a visual note on Page 15 Panel 2 or 4: Horus almost says `Tenet`, but chooses `him`. This shows personal avoidance without adding exposition.
- Consider a silent panel of Horus seeing the empty table before asking `Where is it?`

**Polish target:** Horus should feel like someone who still believes his betrayal was responsibility.

---

### Rudy Calder

**Current strength:** Rudy enters as machine truth immediately. His humor comes from competence and dread.

**Keep:**

- `That defrost isn't broken. It's protesting management.`
- `Good. Then she's smarter than the packet.`
- `Machine can survive ugly. It cannot survive impossible twice.`

**Tighten / Watch:**

- `Still don't like a little survey hauling backup like it expects to feed teeth.` is very strong but dense. Possible tightened version: `Still don't like a little survey hauling backup like it expects teeth.`

**Polish target:** Rudy's lines should sound like wrench logic, not horror poetry, even when they are creepy.

---

### Kavo "Forest" Rill

**Current strength:** Forest is distinct, competent, and visually clear. He is not generic alien muscle.

**Keep:**

- `If the route was honest, they wouldn't need me this early.`
- `No. That's the annoying part.`
- `Terrain talks before it bites.`
- `The land has teeth where the map drew a line.`

**Tighten / Watch:**

- `This stretch is waiting for somebody else to speak first.` is atmospheric, but may overlap with `Terrain talks before it bites.` Consider cutting one if Page 12 feels over-written.

**Polish target:** Forest should be poetry by diagnosis, not by decoration.

---

### Ildi Vey

**Current strength:** Ildi is technical, sharp, and eerie without becoming mystical. Her signal language works.

**Keep:**

- `Your clean packet is bleeding old signal.`
- `Something old just shook our hand where the route says nothing has hands.`
- `Dead channels don't answer in rhythm.`
- `Where the packet gets quiet.`

**Tighten / Watch:**

- `It's answering us.` on Page 20 is direct and dramatic. Keep if the formation reveal needs a voice. Cut if the splash is stronger silent.

**Polish target:** Ildi should make wrongness measurable. The reader should feel haunted because the instruments are calm enough to be believed.

## Page Rhythm Polish

### Pages 1-2: Packaging / Artifact Framing

**Status:** Strong.

**Notes:** Page 2's field-packet credits concept is excellent. Keep the clean artifact layout. Do not over-corrupt it or the credits will become print-mud.

**Action:** Keep Page 2 as Candidate Canon visual language.

---

### Pages 3-4: Rob's Final Refusal

**Status:** Strong opening hook.

**Risk:** Too many Rob lines could make him feel invincible rather than dying.

**Action:** Let illness show visually so the dialogue does not need to remind us. Add a small physical beat: Rob hides a tremor before `Bullshit.`

---

### Pages 5-8: Cold Start / Packet Wrongness

**Status:** Strongest grounded section.

**Risk:** Page 7 has three joke beats before the packet handoff. Funny, but maybe too fizzy.

**Action:** On second draft, consider trimming one Lindy/Tenet joke if Page 8 needs more dread runway.

---

### Pages 9-13: MITE II / Crew Truth Gauges

**Status:** Necessary and working.

**Risk:** Page 10 may read like a lore inventory if not drawn dynamically.

**Action:** Make Page 10 a moving montage with crew hands, vibration, steam, warning labels, and lived-in details rather than static equipment closeups.

---

### Pages 14-15: Pistol Memory / Horus Pressure

**Status:** Emotional lock is good.

**Risk:** Rob's pistol dialogue is close to riddle territory.

**Action:** Keep mystery, but ground it with one concrete gesture: Rob checks that Tenet physically takes the weight correctly, not just symbolically.

---

### Pages 16-21: Terrain Wrongness / Formation / Footprints

**Status:** The escalation spine is strong.

**Risk:** The formation reveal may do too much in one image if described as door, wound, machine, and fossilized question all at once.

**Action:** Keep the artist brief evocative, but in final art prompt choose 2-3 primary design ideas, not all metaphors at once.

**Page 21:** Do not add dialogue. The current silence is correct.

---

### Page 22: Recognition Beat

**Status:** Correct emotional thesis.

**Risk:** Montage can become a lore diagram.

**Action:** Make it emotionally associative, not explanatory. Prefer fewer inset images if art gets crowded.

---

### Pages 23-24: Breath / Myth Sting

**Status:** Good closure.

**Risk:** Page 23's footer `THE FAMILY / THE COMPANY / FIELD WORK DONE UGLY` may be too tagline-y.

**Action:** Keep as optional only. Page 23 works best as an in-world warning, not a brand manifesto.

## Reveal Discipline Check

Even though the approved route is intentionally hype-forward, the draft still mostly avoids over-explanation.

### Preserve Mystery Around

- Core mechanics.
- Full Aeonos / Àæonos cosmology.
- Exact origin of footprints.
- Chrono-Floquet Distillate mechanics.
- Assassin identity inside NCI.
- Full Horus plan.
- Complete function of Grandfather Ten's pistol.
- Complete secrets of The House Ten Built.

### Watch Items

- Page 1 symbolic cover may show too much if it includes Rob, Horus, Aeonos / Àæonos, the formation, and Tenet all at once. Consider a cleaner cover variant focused on Red Umbrielor, Tenet, and formation only.
- Page 20 formation description should remain visually suggestive, not explanatory.
- Page 22 montage should not imply a solved causal chain.

- Keep radio balloons distinct from spoken dialogue.

- Page 20 should have very little lettering.
- Page 21 should be nearly silent.
