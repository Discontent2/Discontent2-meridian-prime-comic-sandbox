## Purpose

This file records what was discovered after the first Red Umbrielor / cover-prompt updates:

1. The matching **1980s glamour-shot character portrait style** works well as a fast character-read test.
2. The front-and-profile dual portrait format is strong for character comparison.
3. The mostly black background, neon grid, slightly fuzzy studio-photo finish, and weathered color-photo texture create a useful non-literal reference look.
4. The character set is beginning to feel emotionally coherent and distinct.
5. Tenet needed additional differentiation from Rudy.
6. The relaxed crew photo in front of 289 works as a team-tone reference, but must obey the Red Umbrielor side-marking / black mesh grille rule.

These images are **visual-development references**, not final model sheets and not literal in-universe publicity photos unless later adapted that way.

## Shared Glamour-Shot Style Lock

The successful character test format used:

- front portrait plus profile portrait in the same image
- mostly black background
- soft / fuzzy 1980s studio-photo finish
- neon grid or simple synthwave geometry
- subdued glamour lighting
- weathered color-photo texture
- character-specific clothing and props
- no heavy lore text
- no hard model-sheet labels

Best use:

- character vibe exploration
- facial-language tests
- costume direction tests
- model-sheet precursor images
- informal lineup comparison

Do not treat these as:

- final comic pages
- final model sheets
- literal canon photographs
- exact age / height / body-reference locks unless later approved

### Ildi Vey

**Status:** Strong success.

The Ildi glamour shot worked extremely well and should be treated as a high-value visual reference.

Locked / reinforced visual notes:

- no shades for this pass
- front portrait plus profile portrait
- blue-skinned Antisapien / Absconditian read

- chest-length stringy hair split down the middle
- hair resembles thin multicolored wires inside a phone cable

- subtle punk-tech wiring accents
- deadpan eerie baseline
- smile can show a chipped tooth

Ildi should remain technical and eerie rather than mystical. Her charm should feel strange, dry, and precise.

### Required Equipment Correction From Crew Photo

The crew-photo pass confirmed a key Red Umbrielor rule already added to the vehicle and cover files:

- `Red Umbrielor` vehicle name belongs on the side surfaces only.
- `289` call number belongs on side surfaces only.
- Neither should appear as readable head-on front text.
- The front grille is black metal mesh.
- The black mesh grille should not carry a large number, logo, name, or decorative label.

Group photos and field portraits in front of 289 should use side-visible markings only when the side plane is visible.

```text
Create a weathered color field photograph of the MITE II crew standing relaxed together in front of Red Umbrielor / call 289 during the height of the Traverse Mainbody season. The group should feel like a team that has worked together for seasons: comfortable, dusty, tired, competent, and quietly affectionate without posing like superheroes. Include Ildi, Forest, Rudy, Lindy, Rob, and Tenet. Horus is not present. Everyone wears shades. Tenet and Lindy wear caps, with Tenet's cap backward. The field is hot, bright, abrasive, and dusty, with crystalline regolith underfoot. Red Umbrielor looms behind them as a scarred red MMS Quadtrack 580 traverse hauler. The front grille is black metal mesh with no readable number or name on the front. The `289` call number and `Red Umbrielor` name are side markings only, visible only if side surfaces are visible. The image should feel like a worn real crew photo from a long field season, not a poster pose.
```

Avoid:

- Horus in the relaxed crew photo
- superhero lineup posing
- glossy fashion-team styling
- polar expedition identity
- readable `289` on front grille
- readable `Red Umbrielor` on front grille
- front grille as a logo panel
- treating Rob as a ghost or memory pressure in this crew-photo context
