## Adaptation Labels

Use these labels in all sandbox documents, scripts, art prompts, page plans, PDFs, and pitch materials.

### Canon Reference

Directly taken from the main repo. Must be preserved unless main canon changes.

### Adaptation Choice

Changed for pacing, clarity, visual impact, or comic readability. Valid inside the comic, but not main canon.

### Promo Exaggeration

Allowed for hype art, covers, teasers, pitch material, symbolic imagery, and back-cover drama.

### Comic-Only Continuity

Allowed inside the quarantined comic. Not valid main canon.

### Candidate Canon

A sandbox invention that might improve the larger Meridian Prime project, but must be reviewed before promotion.

### Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

Art that compresses, foreshadows, dramatizes, or symbolizes events beyond literal continuity.

The approved hype route may:

- Open with Rob Holliday's final refusal at The House Ten Built.

- Show Horus pressing Rob for old route truth earlier than the novella likely would.
- Make Grandfather Ten's pistol visually central from the beginning.
- Turn Horus's approval stamp into a major page-turn.
- Show the ghost handshake as a visual motif in panel gutters.
- Foreshadow the hidden formation, footprints, and Core-adjacent mystery earlier than the novella.

- End with a back-cover image that dramatizes The House Ten Built.

## Current Comic-Only Limits

Even in hype mode, do not change these core truths without separate approval:

- Tenet is a field-proven operator, not a rookie or chosen-one archetype.
- Rob's death remains a route, not a single simple murder beat.
- Horus is personal and tragic, not a random outside villain.
- Red Umbrielor remains MMS Quadtrack 289 and Tenet's signature machine.
- Grandfather Ten's pistol remains inheritance, key, test, trust marker, and route authority, not merely a firearm.
- NCI remains family-rooted, practical, capable, deniable, and haunted.
- Aeonos and Àæonos remain distinct.
- Species body plans and visual rules remain anchored to main canon references.
