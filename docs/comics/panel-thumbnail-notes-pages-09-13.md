## Global Thumbnail Rules

### Print Geometry

Use the Mixam framework measurements:

- Final canvas including bleed: **6.94 in x 10.49 in**
- Trim: **6.69 in x 10.24 in**
- Bleed: **0.125 in** on all sides
- Safe area: **0.25 in inside trim**
- Important elements at least **0.375 in from final canvas edge**

### Thumbnail Priorities

For each page, rough in:

- safe-area box
- bleed boundary
- major panel shapes
- main reading path
- text zones
- focal machine / face / terrain / signal element
- lettering risk areas
- how motion carries across the page

### Section Rhythm

Pages 09-13 should move like this:

1. **Page 09:** commitment and departure.
2. **Page 10:** machine/body montage.
3. **Page 11:** Rudy hears the machine disagree.
4. **Page 12:** Forest reads the quiet terrain.
5. **Page 13:** Ildi catches the old signal answering.

### Three Truth Gauges

This section must clearly establish:

- Rudy = machine truth.
- Forest = terrain truth.
- Ildi = signal truth.

Each character should enter through their expertise, not through an explanatory character card.

## Page 9: MITE II Rolls Out

**Page Role:** Interior page 7

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference / visual compression

**Panel Count:** 3 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Turn suspicion into motion. Red Umbrielor and the Mod train leave the NCI yard. The trap works because Tenet does the job correctly.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Wide cinematic departure page:

```text
Panel 1: low coupling detail / train tension
Panel 2: wide yard departure / Lindy watching
Panel 3: full-width route dawn / Red Umbrielor pulls Mod train out
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Low, wide horizontal panel.

**Focal Point:** couplings, cables, ice shaking loose, crew hand signal.

**Text Zone:** small radio balloon: `MITE II couplings green.` Place inside safe area, away from coupling detail.

**SFX Zone:** `KLANG. KNNNK.` may sit near couplings but should not obscure hitch shape.

**Mood:** metal taking responsibility.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Wide middle panel.

**Focal Point:** Red Umbrielor starting to pull out, yard lights behind, Lindy watching from dispatch area.

**Text Zone:** Dispatch and Tenet radio exchange. Keep balloons small and procedural.

**Composition Note:** Tenet visible through cab glass if possible. Lindy should be small but emotionally readable.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Largest panel, full-width bottom half or bottom third.

**Focal Point:** Red Umbrielor and Mod train rolling into cold route dawn.

**Text Zone:** two captions in open sky / exhaust negative space.

**Caption Rhythm:**

- `A clean packet could still move a dirty job.`
- `That was the problem with honest work.`

### Page Rhythm

This is not adventure-launch page. It is commitment. The machine leaves the known world while the legal world glows behind it like a bad alibi.

### Lettering Risk

Do not overcrowd with radio chatter. The departure image must breathe.

## Page 10: Mod Train as Survival Body

**Page Role:** Interior page 8

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference / Adaptation Choice

**Panel Count:** 6 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Show MITE II as a moving industrial survival organism. Avoid static gear catalog energy.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Moving montage with varied but orderly panels:

```text
Panel 1: fuel module in motion
Panel 2: comms module vibrating
Panel 3: tool storage rattling
Panel 4: GPR / survey prep while moving
Panel 5: berthing / kitchen life under vibration
Panel 6: Tenet in command rhythm
```

Suggested grid:

```text
[ P1 fuel wide ]
[ P2 comms ][ P3 tools ]
[ P4 GPR  ][ P5 kitchen ]
[ P6 Tenet command wide ]
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Wide top panel.

**Focal Point:** fuel bladder module flexing, straps shuddering, handwritten crew warning.

**Text Zone:** `Fuel first.` caption in open margin/sky/industrial negative space.

**Motion Note:** Show frost or straps moving so it does not feel like a still catalog image.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Medium panel.

**Focal Point:** comms module, paper checklist fluttering beside digital display.

**Text Zone:** `Signal second.` caption plus radio texture. Keep radio texture small and non-essential.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Medium panel.

**Focal Point:** tools knocking in racks, `NO, THE OTHER WRENCH` drawer label.

**Text Zone:** `Repair always.` caption. Avoid too many labels.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Medium panel.

**Focal Point:** GPR / survey prep, gloved hand calibrating while train lurches.

**Text Zone:** caption can sit over clean display edge.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Medium panel.

**Focal Point:** mugs in restraints, kettle steam trembling, bunk strap, lived-in machine detail.

**Text Zone:** caption should not cover tiny human-life details.

#### Panel 6

**Shape:** Wide bottom panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet in command rhythm, packet clipped nearby, route display open.

**Text Zone:** two Tenet radio balloons. The second line is important and should be clean:

`If something squeals, lies, or prays, log it.`

### Page Rhythm

MITE II is not just a convoy. It is a survival body with fuel, signal, repair, survey, human living, and command rhythm.

### Lettering Risk

This page can drown in labels. Keep only one or two text elements per panel unless the panel is primarily a screen/packet beat.

## Page 11: Rudy Calder / Machine Truth

**Page Role:** Interior page 9

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference

**Panel Count:** 4 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Introduce Rudy through touch, vibration, humor, and machine worry. If Rudy is worried, the equipment load is wrong.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Character entrance through machine space:

```text
Panel 1: Rudy in service crawl / mechanic module
Panel 2: cab reply / turkey roaster callback
Panel 3: Rudy sees redundant support line / smile fades
Panel 4: Rudy touches coupling housing / train stretches behind
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium panel, slightly cramped mechanical framing.

**Focal Point:** Rudy half in frame with diagnostic slate, listening to machinery.

**Text Zone:** Rudy radio balloon. Keep balloon above or beside hatch, not over face/hands.

**Character Note:** Rudy should look warm, blunt, and machine-literate, not generic mechanic wallpaper.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Small/medium cab panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet controls + turkey roaster background.

**Text Zone:** Tenet/Rudy exchange. Keep it compact.

**Continuity Note:** The turkey roaster callback ties Page 6 to crew rhythm.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Medium close-up / diagnostic panel.

**Focal Point:** Rudy's smile fading; redundant coupler/support line too clean and too ready.

**Text Zone:** `Who blessed the reefer support twice?` should sit near Rudy, not over the support line.

**Visual Beat:** This is the moment humor drains into concern.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Wide bottom panel or tall mechanical close-up.

**Focal Point:** Rudy's hand on coupling housing, Mod train stretching behind through a slit/window.

**Text Zone:** two Rudy lines. Keep final line readable:

`Still don't like a little survey hauling backup like it expects teeth.`

### Page Rhythm

Start with banter. End with metal giving Rudy bad news through his palm.

### Lettering Risk

Do not let Rudy's final line become a horror quote floating over nothing. It should be anchored to practical machine touch.

## Page 12: Kavo "Forest" Rill / Terrain Truth

**Page Role:** Interior page 10

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference

**Panel Count:** 4 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Introduce Forest through stillness, posture, terrain distrust, and the absence of obvious danger.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Stillness page with subtle tension:

```text
Panel 1: Forest relaxed at terrain station
Panel 2: clawed hand adjusts route/GPR overlay
Panel 3: Tenet screen/reflection in goggles
Panel 4: exterior terrain too quiet
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium-wide character intro panel.

**Focal Point:** Forest leaning at observation station, screens/viewport in background.

**Text Zone:** caption: `Kavo Rill watched terrain like it owed him money and might try running.`

**Character Requirements:** Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid body plan, universal tail, four-fingered clawed hands, expressive face, sparse red proto-quill mohawk, black-frame shades or route goggles, light harness, subtle checker trim, pressure-route badges, claw-wraps.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Close-up detail panel.

**Focal Point:** clawed hand adjusting GPR/route overlay. Official line is neat; Forest distrusts neat.

**Text Zone:** Forest line: `If the route was honest, they wouldn't need me this early.`

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Reflection/screen panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet reflected in Forest's goggles or on small screen.

**Text Zone:** Tenet asks; Forest answers: `No. That's the annoying part.` Keep strong deadpan timing.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Wide exterior feed panel.

**Focal Point:** proposed extension corridor too quiet, black markers/early flags, terrain holding still.

**Text Zone:** Forest line: `Terrain talks before it bites.`

### Page Rhythm

The page should make the lack of obvious danger feel dangerous.

Forest's expertise is a negative space reader: what terrain is not doing matters.

### Lettering Risk

Do not over-caption the terrain. Let Forest's single line do the work.

## Page 13: Ildi Vey / Signal Truth / Ghost Handshake

**Page Role:** Interior page 11

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference plus Candidate Canon visual motif

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Make the first field-level contradiction visual: old signal where the packet says no infrastructure exists.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Signal disruption page. Panel gutters begin normal, then subtly become part of the signal motif.

```text
Panel 1: Ildi at systems station
Panel 2: eyes / dead channel pulse
Panel 3: monitor handshake request / signal enters gutters
Panel 4: Tenet recalculates in cab
Panel 5: exterior wide / signal motif faint along border
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium-wide systems station panel.

**Focal Point:** Ildi among cables, old-route monitors, heat readouts, strain charts, comms equipment.

**Text Zone:** SFX `tik-tik-tik... kshh` should feel like texture, not big sound.

**Character Requirements:** blue-skinned Antisapien / Absconditian traits, black irises, magenta pupils, teal tapetum lucidum, signal-band aesthetic, exposed-wire punk practicality. Technical, not mystical.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Close-up on eyes / monitor reflection.

**Focal Point:** dead channel line pulsing once.

**Text Zone:** `No.` should be small and sharp.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Large central technical panel.

**Focal Point:** monitor showing legacy handshake request and packet contradiction.

**Critical Text:**

- `LEGACY HANDSHAKE REQUEST // SOURCE: UNREGISTERED`
- `INFRASTRUCTURE STATUS PER PACKET: NONE`
- `Your clean packet is bleeding old signal.`

**Signal Motif:** first clear gutter creep. Keep it restrained.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Cab reaction panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet's professional recalculation, packet nearby, Horus approval remembered by reader.

**Text Zone:** Tenet/Ildi exchange must be clean:

- `Say that in field language.`
- `Something old just shook our hand where the route says nothing has hands.`

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Wide exterior bottom panel.

**Focal Point:** MITE II moving through quiet corridor, signal motif threading faintly at border.

**Text Zone:** two captions in open sky/terrain negative space:

- `The paper lied first.`
- `Then the old systems answered.`

### Page Rhythm

This page should feel like the comic itself briefly becomes a receiver.

It is not magic. It is worse: the instruments are calm enough to be believed.

### Lettering Risk

The interface text is story-critical. Keep it legible and inside safe area. Signal motif must never obscure speech balloons or readable monitor text.

## Artist Notes for Pages 9-13

### Red Umbrielor / Mod Train

The train should feel like industrial survival strapped together:

- heavy
- ugly-useful
- field-patched
- modular
- vibrating with work
- built from practical compromises

Do not make it sleek or toy-like.

### Rudy Calder

Rudy enters through machine language.

Show:

- touch
- listening
- diagnostic habit
- humor fading into concern
- warmth without softness

### Kavo "Forest" Rill

Forest enters through terrain language.

Show:

- relaxed posture shifting to focus
- tail balance
- clawed hands on displays
- route goggles / shades
- terrain distrust
- stillness as warning

### Ildi Vey

Ildi enters through signal language.

Show:

- technical station density
- calm but wrong monitor behavior
- eye reflection from instruments
- old signal motif
- practical alarm, not mystic trance
