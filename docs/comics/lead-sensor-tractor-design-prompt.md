## Core Identity

409 is the lead sensor tractor for MITE II.

It is not a hauler.

It is not a cargo tractor.

It is not a snowcat in-world.

It is an **NCI-modded crystal groomer** adapted for crystalline-regolith Traverse work.

Core machine read:

> a compact tracked route-reading instrument platform built to put sensing hardware into the land ahead of the convoy before Red Umbrielor commits its mass.

409 should feel practical, tense, sensor-forward, and less sentimental than the other MITE II tractors.

---

## Machine Naming and Numbering

```text
409 = tractor number / call number
No name
No nickname
```

409 remains nameless.

This is important.

Red Umbrielor has a name. Rudy's tractor has inherited windshield archaeology and a verbal nickname. 409 is colder: trusted, useful, and functional, but not sentimental.

### Marking Rule

Current open question:

- exact placement of `409` remains unresolved.

Temporary prompt guidance:

- show `409` as a practical field number somewhere on the vehicle body or cab side, but do not over-emphasize until final model sheet placement is approved.

## Real-World Reference Use Policy

Real-world reference may include ski-resort snow shaper / groomer vehicle logic.

However, Meridian Prime is not a polar story.

409 is not an ice crawler, snowcat, or Antarctic vehicle.

Use real-world groomer reference only for:

- compact tracked utility shape
- front blade logic
- cab visibility
- low working stance
- utility chassis
- maintenance access
- field-grooming hardware logic

Translate everything into Meridian Prime:

- crystalline regolith
- crystal dust
- heat/cold cycle wear
- abrasive surface scoring
- glare and reflective grit
- pressure-route danger
- Growth Event terrain
- field modification by NCI

## Crew Association

### Ildi Vey

Ildi drives 409.

Her role in this machine:

- driver
- signal/system watcher
- infrastructure monitor
- old-route systems reader
- ghost-handshake detector

The driver position should allow her to read machine and signal behavior without looking mystical.

Her weirdness is technical.

### Kavo "Forest" Rill

Forest rides in the rear cab.

His role in this machine:

- Mountaineer
- route guide
- wilderness EMT
- GPR path-reader
- terrain interpreter

Forest reads terrain whenever the Traverse is in motion.

The rear cab should feel like a specialized terrain-reading station, not passenger seating.

### Tractor Rule

409 pulls nothing.

This helps visually distinguish it from:

- Red Umbrielor, which pulls life-support Mods and a fuel Tool train.
- 287 Discovery, which pulls repair/storage Mods and a fuel Tool train.

## Required Visual Anchors

### 1. NCI-Modded Crystal Groomer Base

409 should read as a compact tracked groomer-like machine modified for crystal-regolith Traverse work.

It should have:

- low tracked body
- compact work chassis
- front blade
- cab with forward visibility
- rear cab or rear reading station
- practical service panels
- field modification scars
- dust protection
- sensor hardware
- NCI rough competence

Avoid:

- high-track snowcat identity
- sleek sci-fi rover
- generic military scout vehicle
- farm tractor silhouette
- Red Umbrielor-style heavy Quadtrack mass
- Rudy's MT-style repair tractor silhouette

---

### 2. Front Blade

409 has a front blade.

The blade is not primarily for dramatic plowing in this design packet. It is a structural mount and field-work interface for the GPR boom system.

Blade should feel:

- practical
- broad enough to mount equipment
- scarred by regolith
- reinforced for mast/boom loads
- NCI-modified
- less iconic than Red Umbrielor's blade but still important

Do not make the blade cartoonishly huge.

---

### 3. 20-Foot GPR Boom

409's defining feature is the GPR boom system.

Required parts:

- **20-foot boom** mounted to the front blade
- **GPR unit** mounted at the leading end of the boom
- boom extends ahead of the tractor
- boom should look functional and field-engineered
- protected cable/data line routing
- hinge/pivot or mounting bracket at blade

The boom should make the machine instantly recognizable at silhouette scale.

It should show that 409 reads terrain before the convoy drives over it.

---

### 4. 8-Foot Mast, Pulley, and Winch Cable

Required support structure:

- **8-foot mast** mounted on the blade
- **pulley** at or near the top of the mast
- **electric winch cable** supporting the leading end of the boom

The boom tip / GPR unit should not look like it is magically floating.

Show the tension system clearly enough that an artist, reader, or modeler understands the boom is physically supported.

Suggested visual language:

```text
blade-mounted boom pivot
8' mast rising from blade
pulley at mast top
electric winch cable running down to support boom tip
GPR unit at leading end
```

Avoid:

- crane-like cargo boom
- weaponized lance
- magic scanner arm
- overbuilt sci-fi antenna spear

---

### 5. GPR Unit

GPR unit is mounted at the leading end of the 20-foot boom.

It should feel:

- rugged
- sensor-heavy
- shielded from dust
- close enough to terrain to read it
- field-replaceable
- not precious lab equipment

Possible visual elements:

- rectangular sensor block
- underside scan face
- dust shield
- cable gland
- protective cage
- worn leading edge
- small calibration marks

Do not make it glow like magic.

It may have small work lights or instrument LEDs, but the primary read should be functional field sensor.

---

### 6. Front Driver / Rear Terrain Station

409 has two functional crew zones:

1. front driving / systems position for Ildi
2. rear cab / terrain-read station for Forest

The rear station should not feel like casual seating.

It should suggest:

- GPR readout
- rear/side terrain feeds
- route overlays
- paper/digital hybrid notes
- harnesses
- field medical kit or emergency gear
- stable interpretation posture while moving

The exterior should imply this two-zone function through cab shape, windows, or station separation.

---

## Environmental Adaptation

409 operates on crystalline regolith.

Show:

- crystal dust packed into seams
- abrasive scoring on lower panels
- dust haze on glass
- scratched sensor housing
- heat/cold cycle stress
- glare shields
- filter housings
- reinforced vent screens
- lower-body wear from crystal grit
- functional grime rather than snowpack

It may be shown in cold dawn or hot traverse-season conditions, but it should never default into polar expedition identity.

---

## Story Function

409 gives the comic a visual way to show MITE II's terrain intelligence.

It does three story jobs:

1. It shows the route is being read before Red Umbrielor commits.
2. It gives Ildi and Forest a shared moving workspace.
3. It turns terrain wrongness into something visible on the page.

In issue rhythm, 409 is especially useful for:

- Page 9 convoy departure
- Page 10 crew/equipment truth-gauge montage
- Pages 16-19 terrain wrongness escalation
- Page 20 approach to formation reveal
- Page 21 footprint evidence discipline

## Variant A: Full Exterior Model Sheet

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Reference

### Best Use

Primary 409 reference for artists.

### Goal

Lock the full exterior identity of the lead sensor tractor.

### Prompt

Create a full exterior concept sheet for **409**, an **NCI-modded crystal groomer** used as the MITE II lead sensor tractor in the Meridian Prime comic sandbox. The machine is a compact tracked groomer-like vehicle adapted for crystalline-regolith Traverse work, not a snowcat in-world and not a sci-fi rover. It has a front blade, a blade-mounted 20-foot GPR boom extending forward, a rugged GPR unit on the leading end of the boom, an 8-foot mast mounted on the blade, a pulley at the mast top, and an electric winch cable supporting the leading end of the boom. Ildi drives from the forward cab position while Forest rides in a rear cab / terrain-reading station. The tractor pulls nothing. Show practical NCI field modifications, crystal dust, abrasive scoring, glare shields, reinforced vents, worn tracks, sensor cabling, and a utilitarian numbered body. Include the number `409` as a practical field marking, but keep the machine nameless and without a nickname. The design should feel compact, sensor-forward, rugged, and distinct from Red Umbrielor and Rudy's repair tractor.

### Notes

This is the baseline design. All other 409 depictions should derive from this silhouette.

## Variant B: GPR Boom Close-Up

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Reference

### Best Use

Technical detail reference for artists drawing Pages 16-21.

### Goal

Make the boom system clear and mechanically believable.

### Prompt

Create a detailed close-up concept image of the **409 lead sensor tractor's GPR boom system** for Meridian Prime. Focus on the front blade and mounted sensor hardware. Show a 20-foot boom attached to the blade by reinforced brackets or a hinge, with a rugged GPR unit at the leading end. An 8-foot mast is mounted on the blade, with a pulley at the top and an electric winch cable supporting the leading end of the boom. Include protected cable routing, dust-sealed connectors, worn metal, abrasive crystal-regolith scoring, field welds, and practical NCI modifications. The system should look functional and physically supported, not sleek, magical, weaponized, or decorative. The blade should show route-work scars, but the main focus is the boom, mast, pulley, cable, and GPR sensor relationship.

### Notes

This should answer the artist's question: "How is the boom held up?"

## Variant C: Crew Workspace View

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Reference

### Best Use

Interior / cutaway / panel reference for Ildi and Forest scenes.

### Goal

Show how Ildi and Forest work inside or through 409.

### Prompt

Create an interior or cutaway concept view of **409**, the MITE II lead sensor tractor. Show Ildi Vey in the forward driving / systems position and Kavo "Forest" Rill in the rear cab / terrain-reading station. The workspace should feel practical, cramped, field-modified, and built for reading terrain while moving. The environment is a crystalline-regolith Traverse machine: dusty, functional, and under vibration.

### Notes

This image should clarify the relationship between Ildi's signal truth and Forest's terrain truth.

## Artist Notes

### Silhouette Test

At thumbnail size, 409 should read as:

```text
compact tracked groomer-like machine
front blade
vertical mast
forward boom
small sensor at boom tip
no tow load
```

If those elements vanish, the design is too muddy.

### Mood Words

- practical
- sensor-forward
- compact
- modified
- nameless
- technical
- dusty
- tense
- route-reading
- unsentimental
- field-built

### Relationship to Other MITE II Tractors

409 should be visually distinct from:

- **Red Umbrielor:** huge red MMS Quadtrack 580, named, call 289, command/life-support tractor.
- **287 Discovery:** used MT-style crawler, front winch, rear crane, Rudy's repair/sustainment tractor.

409 is the lead instrument.

It is the thing MITE II sends ahead because the land lies.
