## Purpose

This document translates the first eight pages of the second-draft script into rough layout logic.

Pages 1-8 establish:

1. The issue's front-cover promise.
2. The leaked/field-packet artifact frame.
3. Rob Holliday's final refusal.
4. Horus Skelton as personal official wrongness.
5. Red Umbrielor's cold start.
6. Tenet's grief through machine ritual.
7. Lindy Nocsid and the MITE II packet.
8. Horus's approval stamp as the first major wrongness.

The goal is not final panel art. The goal is to define page rhythm, major silhouettes, safe text placement, and what each page must make the reader feel before final thumbnails are drawn.

## Global Thumbnail Rules

### Print Geometry

Use the Mixam framework measurements:

- Final canvas including bleed: **6.94 in x 10.49 in**
- Trim: **6.69 in x 10.24 in**
- Bleed: **0.125 in** on all sides
- Safe area: **0.25 in inside trim**
- Important elements at least **0.375 in from final canvas edge**

### Thumbnail Priorities

For each page, rough in:

- safe-area box
- bleed boundary
- major panel shapes
- main reading path
- text zones
- focal object / face / evidence
- lettering risk areas
- any full-bleed image requirements

### Opening Rhythm

The first eight pages should move like this:

1. Cover: impossible promise.
2. Credits: leaked document.
3. Quiet house.
4. Old friends as knives.
5. Hard cut to work.
6. Grief through objects.
7. Family / packet handoff.
8. Bureaucratic thunderclap.

### Lettering Philosophy

Use `docs/comics/lettering-and-safe-area-guide.md`.

Text should feel like field evidence, not decoration.

Do not let captions, packet text, or interface labels overwhelm facial acting or machine detail.

## Page 1: Front Cover

**Page Role:** Front cover

**Adaptation Label:** Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

**Panel Count:** 1 full-bleed image

**Thumbnail Goal:** Sell the entire issue at thumbnail size: Tenet, Red Umbrielor 289, the formation, and the dead-map route promise.

### Layout Concept

Single full-bleed vertical composition.

Suggested composition:

```text
[ SAFE TITLE ZONE ]

        bruised alien dawn / cold haze
        faint symbolic pressure, if using Variant A

        HIDDEN FORMATION
        huge, distant, partially obscured

    Red Umbrielor 289 angled toward formation
    massive lower-middle silhouette

Tenet small foreground / packet visible / pistol implied

[ TAGLINE + CREATOR / ISSUE ZONE ]
```

### Focal Hierarchy

1. Red Umbrielor 289
2. Hidden formation
3. Tenet as small field operator
4. Title / logo
5. Tagline

### Safe Text Zones

- Title/logo: top safe area
- Tagline: lower third or upper-middle safe area
- Creator/issue info: bottom safe area

### Must Preserve

- Red Umbrielor must not look sleek or generic.
- Tenet must not pose like a superhero.
- Pistol is implied, not brandished.
- Formation is mysterious, not explained.

### Thumbnail Notes

Make three cover thumbnail variants:

1. **Machine-dominant:** Red Umbrielor fills lower half, Tenet tiny.
2. **Operator-dominant:** Tenet in foreground, Red Umbrielor behind him.
3. **Formation-dominant:** formation towers over both machine and man.

Recommended first pass: **Machine-dominant** for strongest Meridian Prime identity.

## Page 2: Inside Front Cover / Credits Packet

**Page Role:** Inside front cover / credits

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon for packet-style design language

**Panel Count:** No traditional panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Make the issue feel like a leaked field packet while keeping credits readable.

### Layout Concept

Document design with clean hierarchy:

```text
[ Packet Header / NCI INTERNAL FIELD COPY ]

[ Series Title / Issue Title ]

[ Credits block left or centered ]
Writer
Artist
Colorist
Letterer
Editor
Cover Artist
Publisher / Imprint
Website / Socials

[ subtle route grid / packet texture behind ]

[ Footer: Field packets do not guarantee field truth. ]
```

### Focal Hierarchy

1. Series title / issue title
2. Creator credits
3. Packet artifact design
4. Small footer motto

### Safe Text Zones

All credits must remain inside safe area.

Packet texture may extend closer to trim if decorative only.

### Must Preserve

- Readability beats texture.
- Horus approval stamp may appear as faint texture, but the dramatic reveal waits for Page 8.
- Do not clutter with too much fake bureaucracy.

### Thumbnail Notes

Sketch credits in one stable block. Use texture around the block, not behind every line.

## Page 3: The House Ten Built

**Page Role:** Interior page 1

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** 4 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Establish the House, Rob's condition, and the sense that Rob has already acted.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Quiet, rectangular, no flashy angles.

```text
Panel 1: wide exterior, full-width top third
Panel 2: interior objects / history, medium-wide
Panel 3: Rob at table, medium close
Panel 4: Rob hears arrival / looks to door, bottom beat
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Wide establishing panel across page width.

**Focal Point:** House small in vast cold, one warm light.

**Text Zone:** Captions top-left or lower-left inside safe area.

**Avoid:** Placing captions over the one lit window.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Medium-wide horizontal.

**Focal Point:** old route maps, tools, dust, oilcloth absence, photograph.

**Text Zone:** caption box on clean wall/table negative space.

**Avoid:** Making prop text too tiny or plot-essential.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Medium close-up, Rob at table.

**Focal Point:** Rob's face and hand near missing oilcloth bundle.

**Text Zone:** no dialogue. Let Rob breathe.

**Must Show:** white hair swept to one side, square glasses, connected mustache/goatee, curled eyebrow tips, salmon polo under layers if visible.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Bottom horizontal or slightly compressed close-up.

**Focal Point:** Rob looking toward door / headlights outside.

**Text Zone:** Rob dialogue placed near face but not covering eyes or smile.

### Page Rhythm

Slow. Cold. Waiting.

The page should feel like a room holding its breath with an old heater clicking like a tiny countdown beetle.

## Page 4: Horus Arrives Too Late

**Page Role:** Interior page 2

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Establish Horus as personal, controlled, wounded, and too late. Let Rob's refusal land cleanly.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Tension page with narrowing focus:

```text
Panel 1: Horus in doorway, cold behind him
Panel 2: two-shot, Horus standing / Rob seated
Panel 3: Rob close-up
Panel 4: Horus hand gripping chair / photo in background
Panel 5: Rob final verdict, larger bottom panel
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Tall-ish doorway panel.

**Focal Point:** Horus silhouette with cold behind him.

**Text Zone:** minimal balloon placement. Keep doorway silhouette clean.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Wide two-shot.

**Focal Point:** power imbalance: Horus standing, Rob seated but owning room.

**Text Zone:** Horus left/right depending staging. Avoid balloon tails crossing faces.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Close-up on Rob.

**Focal Point:** warm smile, sharp eyes, curled eyebrow tips.

**Text Zone:** one Rob balloon. Do not crowd face.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Close detail panel.

**Focal Point:** Horus's hand gripping chair, old photo behind.

**Text Zone:** Horus/Rob exchange can sit in negative space above or beside chair.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Larger bottom panel for impact.

**Focal Point:** Rob, empty oilcloth space, hidden tremor.

**Text Zone:** `Bullshit.` needs room and clarity.

### Page Rhythm

Polite words as weapons. No big action. The panel compression should make the room feel smaller as the conversation sharpens.

## Page 5: Red Umbrielor Cold Start

**Page Role:** Interior page 3

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference with heightened visual pacing

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Hard cut from Rob's final refusal to Tenet's work morning. Establish machine scale and Tenet's competence.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Mechanical sequence with tactile progression:

```text
Panel 1: hand on scarred red steel, close-up
Panel 2: wide Red Umbrielor in NCI yard
Panel 3: diagnostic strip / MD-8 warning
Panel 4: Tenet watching warning clear
Panel 5: Tenet climbs toward cab / yard waking behind
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Narrow horizontal or square close-up.

**Focal Point:** gloved hand, frost, chipped red paint.

**Text Zone:** caption in clean corner.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Large wide panel.

**Focal Point:** Red Umbrielor 289 scale.

**Text Zone:** SFX can live around machine silhouette but not obscure number `289`.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Small inset diagnostic panel.

**Focal Point:** `MD-8 PRESSURE UNEVEN`.

**Text Zone:** diagnostic text must be readable.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Medium close / Tenet reaction.

**Focal Point:** Tenet calm, warning clears.

**Text Zone:** small dialogue balloon.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Wide bottom panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet climbing into Red Umbrielor, NCI yard waking.

**Text Zone:** caption should sit in open sky/exhaust negative space.

### Page Rhythm

Machine wakes first. Tenet follows procedure. The world is practical before it becomes impossible.

## Page 6: Turkey Roaster / Empty Seat / Under-Seat Weight

**Page Role:** Interior page 4

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference

**Panel Count:** 6 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Let grief enter through objects: dead defrost, turkey roaster, empty seat, under-seat compartment.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Intimate grid with object rhythm:

```text
Panel 1: frosted windshield / defrost control
Panel 2: dead vent / Tenet flat look
Panel 3: turkey roaster glows
Panel 4: empty passenger seat
Panel 5: memory-impression of Rob's absence
Panel 6: under-seat compartment / still there
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Small/medium cab panel.

**Focal Point:** defrost control, frosted glass.

**Text Zone:** small SFX / Tenet line.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Small reaction/object panel.

**Focal Point:** dead vent and Tenet's expression.

**Text Zone:** keep minimal.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Medium panel, warm center.

**Focal Point:** turkey roaster glow.

**Text Zone:** caption near roaster but not covering glow.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Wider, quiet panel.

**Focal Point:** empty passenger seat.

**Text Zone:** caption `The passenger seat stayed empty.` should breathe.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Small memory-impression panel.

**Focal Point:** absent Rob, salmon sleeve / duffel suggestion, not literal ghost.

**Text Zone:** faint memory dialogue distinct from normal speech.

#### Panel 6

**Shape:** Bottom close-up.

**Focal Point:** under-seat compartment latch, Tenet hand.

**Text Zone:** three short captions stacked cleanly: `Closed. Locked. Still there.`

### Page Rhythm

Tiny domestic machinery meets grief. The turkey roaster is funny; the empty seat is not.

## Page 7: Lindy Brings the Packet

**Page Role:** Interior page 5

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference with comic pacing

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Introduce Lindy through dispatch competence and sibling armor, then move quickly into the packet.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Threshold page. Lindy mostly stays on the edge of the cab/world.

```text
Panel 1: Lindy crosses yard with packet
Panel 2: Lindy in cab doorway / defrost exchange
Panel 3: turkey roaster exchange
Panel 4: packet handoff / digital twin appears
Panel 5: Lindy watches Tenet open packet
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium-wide exterior.

**Focal Point:** Lindy crossing yard, packet visible.

**Text Zone:** two captions. Keep them compact.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Medium panel, cab doorway.

**Focal Point:** Lindy threshold posture.

**Text Zone:** three short balloons. Avoid crowding cab doorway.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Small/medium interior comedy beat.

**Focal Point:** turkey roaster, Lindy's suspicion.

**Text Zone:** compact exchange. Do not let joke swallow page.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Larger handoff panel.

**Focal Point:** physical packet and digital twin appearing.

**Text Zone:** packet label readable inside safe area.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Quiet final panel.

**Focal Point:** Lindy watching Tenet's face, not the screen.

**Text Zone:** short remaining dialogue. Give the page a dread runway into Page 8.

### Page Rhythm

Banter as armor. Packet as weight. Lindy does not enter fully because the emotional room is already crowded.

## Page 8: Horus Approved It

**Page Role:** Interior page 6

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference / dramatic page-turn

**Panel Count:** 4 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Make Horus's approval stamp feel like a cold, official impact. This is bureaucratic thunder.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Build from packet scan to stamp shock:

```text
Panel 1: Tenet flips / scrolls packet, paperwork too clean
Panel 2: authorization chain / route language
Panel 3: huge Horus approval stamp / line
Panel 4: Tenet and Lindy reaction in cab
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium-wide panel.

**Focal Point:** physical/digital packet overlay.

**Text Zone:** two captions in clean negative space.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Close packet panel.

**Focal Point:** official route language.

**Text Zone:** packet text must be readable. Dialogue small and placed around document.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Largest panel on page, possibly full-width.

**Focal Point:** `FINAL APPROVAL: HORUS SKELTON / WORLD WORKS DIRECTOR OF TRAVERSE OPERATIONS`.

**Text Zone:** the approval line itself is the art. Do not clutter.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Bottom reaction panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet staring, Lindy already knowing, turkey roaster glow between them.

**Text Zone:** `What the-` / `Yeah. Saw that.` Balloons should be small and deadpan.

### Page Rhythm

The page should fall silent at Panel 3. Panel 4 is the breath after the stamp hits.

## Artist Notes for Pages 1-8

### Rob Holliday

Rob must retain:

- white hair swept to one side
- square glasses
- mustache connected to goatee
- curled eyebrow tips
- overweight but not obese body type
- warm smile
- salmon polo visible when possible

He is sick and physically fading, but his judgment is alive and rude.

### Horus Skelton

Horus must feel:

- official
- controlled
- personal
- late
- wounded under power

Do not make him cartoon evil.

### Tenet Nocsid III

Tenet must feel:

- practical
- experienced
- field-grounded
- emotionally controlled
- tired before work, not broken by it

Do not pose him like a superhero.

### Lindy Nocsid

Lindy must feel:

- sharp
- competent
- funny as armor
- threshold-oriented in Page 7

She brings the packet, but she also watches Tenet absorb it.

### Red Umbrielor 289

Red Umbrielor must feel:

- massive
- scarred
- patched
- frost-caked
- old but capable
- loved through complaint

Do not make it clean, sleek, or generic.
