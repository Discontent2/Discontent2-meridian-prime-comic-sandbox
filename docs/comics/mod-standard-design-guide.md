## Core Purpose

This file gives artists a consistent visual standard for MITE II Mods.

Mods are not generic sci-fi boxes.

Mods are not polished mobile homes.

Mods are not loose cargo containers dropped into panels by accident.

MITE II Mods are red, modified field containers mounted on unpowered straight-track platforms. In motion, they form fixed chains behind their tractors. At the end of shift, they become a temporary service camp called **Traverse Town**.

Core read:

> red field containers dragged across crystalline regolith, still on their tracked platforms, unfolded into a working survival town when the convoy stops.

---

## Mod Body Standard

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

### Base Body

MITE II Mods are modified conex / seavan-style containers.

They should look like field-converted freight containers, not futuristic pods.

### Platform

Each Mod rides on a platform attached to:

- four unpowered straight tracks

Important constraints:

- straight tracks
- not hi-tracks
- unpowered
- low platform-mounted
- pulled by tractors
- not self-propelled

### Color

Mods are red.

The red should be practical and worn:

- industrial red
- dust-muted
- abraded by crystalline regolith
- patched where needed
- not clean showroom red
- not glossy corporate red

### Shared Visual Features

Use:

- boxy container body
- reinforced frame
- welded modifications
- functional doors
- side hatches
- service ports
- utility hookups
- ladder rungs
- roof hardware
- foldable access stairs
- derricks
- vents and filters
- dust seals
- scratched lower edges
- thermal wear
- crystal dust in seams
- NCI / MITE II practical stenciling if approved later

Avoid:

- sleek sci-fi pods
- smooth mobile homes
- luxury expedition trailers
- military APC shapes
- polished corporate lab units
- visible powered drive units on the Mod tracks
- hi-track snow vehicle logic
- clean white science-module identity

## Mod Tracks

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

The tracks under each Mod are unpowered.

They are there to distribute weight, tolerate rough crystalline regolith, and let the tractor drag the Mod across route surfaces.

Visual notes:

- four straight track assemblies per Mod
- low, heavy, practical
- not tall snowcat tracks
- not active drive systems
- mounted to a platform beneath the container body
- dirty, worn, and scarred from being dragged

Artists should show that the Mod is heavy and reluctant. It does not glide.

## Fixed Pull Order

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

289 Red Umbrielor and 287 Discovery each pull exactly:

- two Mods
- followed by bladders

The order in which Mods are connected is the order in which they stay.

Do not casually rearrange Mods between moving and parked layouts unless a later story beat requires it.

### Red Umbrielor Chain

```text
289 Red Umbrielor
→ Comms Mod
→ Solar/Berthing Mod
→ Tool + two Magic Carpets + four black fuel bladders
```

### Discovery Chain

```text
287 Discovery
→ Refrigerator/Storage Mod
→ Shed
→ Tool + two Magic Carpets + four black fuel bladders
```

### 409 Lead Tractor

409 pulls nothing.

It is the lead sensor / terrain-reading tractor.

## Derricks and Foldable Access Stairs

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

Mods stay on their platforms when parked.

They are not unloaded onto the ground.

Each Mod has:

- derricks
- foldable access stairs

### Derricks

Derricks should feel like practical field lifting equipment.

Use for:

- moving crates
- handling hoses
- supporting service work
- rigging equipment
- assisting Aquifer Drill setup where appropriate
- utility lifts around camp

Avoid making derricks look like giant construction cranes.

### Foldable Access Stairs

Foldable stairs should:

- deploy from the Mod side
- look rugged
- handle dust and uneven terrain
- be visible in Traverse Town scenes
- help sell the difference between travel mode and parked mode

Travel mode:

- stairs folded / locked
- doors secured
- derricks stowed

Parked mode:

- stairs down
- derricks usable
- triplex hung
- service alley active

## Aquifer Drill

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

### Formal Equipment Term

```text
Aquifer Drill
```

### Field Command / Crew Phrase

```text
Set the tap.
```

### Function

In real polar Traverse logic, a kitchen module might use a snow melter for water.

Meridian Prime is not a polar story.

MITE II uses an **Aquifer Drill** to access prevalent aquifers beneath or within crystalline regolith.

The Aquifer Drill is associated with the kitchen side of the Comms Mod.

### Visual Identity

The Aquifer Drill should read as water-survival infrastructure, not mining spectacle.

Possible design features:

- mounted on or beside the Comms Mod
- fold-out or derrick-assisted deployment
- heated / rotary / pressure boring system
- rugged drill head
- mineral-crusted hose line
- intake / filter line back into the Mod
- small settling or filter box
- valve cluster
- dust-sealed ports
- work light
- thermal scarring
- regolith dust and mineral residue

Avoid:

- snow melter visual identity
- weapon-like drill
- massive mining rig
- fantasy crystal extractor
- bright warning-label clutter
- magical water device

### Dialogue Use

Preferred field phrases:

- "Set the tap."
- "Tap's set."
- "Kitchen wants the tap before lights-out."
- "Do not pull that hose unless you want Rudy to invent new weather."

The key phrase is:

> Set the tap.

## Traverse Town

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

At end of shift, the parked Mod layout becomes **Traverse Town**.

Traverse Town is not a permanent settlement.

It is a temporary field layout made by the parked Mods, tractor-width service lanes, power connections, and work habits.

### Core Layout Rules

At end of shift:

- Mods are parked side by side.
- An alley is left between Mods.
- The alley is wide enough for a tractor.
- A heavy triplex power umbilical is hung between the Mods to connect them.
- Mods stay on their platforms.
- Foldable access stairs are deployed.
- Derricks can be used.
- Tractors are parked on the other side of Traverse Town.
- Tractors plug into the Shed.

### Why This Matters

In motion, MITE II is a stretched industrial chain.

At rest, MITE II becomes a temporary work town.

This gives the comic two repeatable visual states:

1. **Convoy Mode:** stretched, vulnerable, procedural, moving.
2. **Traverse Town Mode:** compact, connected, livable, still under strain.

## Heavy Triplex Power Umbilical

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

At end of shift, Mods are connected by a heavy triplex power umbilical.

The triplex is:

- heavy
- hung between Mods
- visually obvious in parked scenes
- ruggedized
- dust-coated
- connected through exterior power ports
- part of the temporary camp infrastructure

It should not look like a thin extension cord.

Visual notes:

- sagging but controlled cable weight
- strain-relief hooks or clamps
- protected connector heads
- heavy ports on Mod sides
- dark cable or black industrial sheathing
- maybe colored bands only if approved later

The hung triplex should help artists immediately show that the parked Mods are connected into one working system.

## End-of-Shift Parking Layout

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

### Basic Parked Logic

The connection order stays fixed, but at end of shift the Mods are parked side by side to create Traverse Town.

The exact diagram may vary by terrain, but the key rules remain:

- paired Mods side by side
- tractor-width alley between them
- heavy triplex hung between Mods
- stairs down
- derricks usable
- tractors parked on the other side
- tractors plugged into Shed

### Red Umbrielor Side

Red Umbrielor's Mods:

```text
Comms Mod
Solar/Berthing Mod
```

These can form a living / command side of Traverse Town.

### Discovery Side

287 Discovery's Mods:

```text
Refrigerator/Storage Mod
Shed
```

These form a storage / repair side of Traverse Town.

### Tractor Parking

Tractors are parked on the other side of Traverse Town and plugged into the Shed.

This includes, as needed:

- 289 Red Umbrielor
- 287 Discovery
- 409 lead sensor tractor

The Shed acts as the mechanical plug-in hub.

### Tractor-Width Alley

The alley between Mods is wide enough for a tractor.

This is not a tight human walkway.

It should support:

- tractor movement
- service access
- loading / unloading
- repair work
- crew movement
- equipment staging
- cable routing
- emergency access

```text
409 pulls nothing.

289 Red Umbrielor
→ Comms Mod
→ Solar/Berthing Mod
→ Tool fuel train

287 Discovery
→ Refrigerator/Storage Mod
→ Shed
→ Tool fuel train
```

Visual cues:

- stairs folded
- derricks stowed
- triplex stowed
- Aquifer Drill stowed
- Mods stretched behind tractors
- tracks dragging through regolith
- dust, heat shimmer, or cold dawn vapor

### Parked Mode

At end of shift:

- Mods parked side by side
- tractor-width alley
- heavy triplex hung
- foldable stairs down
- derricks available
- Aquifer Drill can be deployed
- tractors parked on other side
- tractors plugged into Shed

Visual cues:

- temporary town silhouette
- red container bodies facing each other
- cable sag between Mods
- access stairs down
- service panels open
- crew moving through alley
- tractor silhouettes plugged into Shed side

## Production Reminder

Mods should always feel like useful weight under strain.

They are not scenery.

They are the temporary body of MITE II:

- Comms Mod keeps the crew connected, fed, washed, and tapped into water.
- Solar/Berthing Mod keeps people alive and powered.
- Refrigerator/Storage Mod keeps supplies survivable.
- Shed keeps the machines from becoming monuments.

When the convoy stops, the Mods become Traverse Town. When Traverse Town sleeps, the land keeps working underneath it.
