## Core Identity

Red Umbrielor is the issue's steel heartbeat.

If the hidden formation is the mystery, Red Umbrielor is the reader's anchor.

Red Umbrielor should feel like:

> old enough to complain, ugly enough to trust, and massive enough to matter until the land proves otherwise.

It is not a tank.

It is not a spaceship.

It is not a military glamour machine.

It is not a generic sci-fi crawler.

It is a brutally practical traverse workhorse.

---

## Locked Machine Identity

### In-World Hierarchy

```text
MMS Quadtrack 580 = tractor size / class
Red Umbrielor = vehicle name
289 = call number
```

### Standard Full Reference

Use this wording in sandbox documents when possible:

> **Red Umbrielor, an MMS Quadtrack 580 traverse hauler, call number 289.**

### Short Reference Forms

Acceptable shorthand:

- **Red Umbrielor**
- **Red Umbrielor / Quadtrack 580 / Call 289**
- **MMS Quadtrack 580 "Red Umbrielor," call 289**
- **Call 289**
- **Red Umbrielor 289**  
  Acceptable in dialogue or shorthand, but not as the formal machine-class line.

### Permanent Feature

Red Umbrielor **always has a front blade**.

This is not optional equipment.

This is part of its core silhouette and identity.

---

## Real-World Reference Use Policy

Real-world surface-traverse logistics may be used as **reference only** for:

- overland route logistics
- tractor-train hauling logic
- modular cargo/supply movement
- route proofing
- route flagging
- ground-survey workflow
- lead-hauler plus module-train relationship

That reference is structural only.

Meridian Prime is **not** a polar story.

Red Umbrielor operates on **crystalline regolith** and traverse routes that may be:

- freezing at dawn or night
- hot during the middle of traverse season
- abrasive
- reflective
- brittle
- dusty
- glassy
- pressure-active
- thermally punishing
- hostile to seals, optics, treads, and exposed surfaces

So Red Umbrielor should not read as a snow vehicle.

It should read as a **crystalline-regolith traverse tractor**.

---

## Base Visual Reference

Red Umbrielor should look **almost identical in core silhouette** to a real-world articulated four-track agricultural / industrial tractor in the **Case Quadtrac 580** family, but fictionalized for Meridian Prime.

That means the base visual DNA includes:

- articulated body
- high operator cab
- long front hood / engine body
- four major track units
- heavy industrial mass
- large work-machine proportions
- practical service geometry
- broad mechanical stance

However, it must be adapted for Meridian Prime and must not appear like a farm machine dropped into the setting with no changes.

---

## Design Summary

Red Umbrielor is:

- a red articulated traverse tractor
- an MMS Quadtrack 580
- call number 289
- permanently equipped with a front blade
- modified to pull the MITE II modular train
- weathered by crystal dust, pressure-route abrasion, heat/cold cycling, and constant field use

Core promise:

> A scarred red machine built to drag bad contracts across crystal dirt that cuts, shifts, reflects, and sometimes grows teeth.

## Required Visual Anchors

### 1. Overall Silhouette

Must read instantly as:

- heavy
- articulated
- four-track
- industrial
- practical
- haul-capable
- blade-fronted
- not sleek

The silhouette should remain readable in:

- full render
- mid-distance scene
- black silhouette
- poster graphic
- route-warning art
- small panel appearances

### 2. Front Blade

The blade is mandatory.

It should feel:

- broad
- heavy
- route-working
- permanently mounted
- used hard
- scarred
- replaceable-edged or segmented at the cutting lip
- practical rather than decorative

The blade's function should imply:

- route shaping
- crust breaking
- regolith pushing
- pressure-ridge testing
- growth-roller negotiation
- obstruction clearing

The blade should not feel cartoonishly oversized. It should feel like part of a serious work machine.

### 3. Track Layout

The quadtrack system should feel load-bearing and practical.

It should suggest:

- weight distribution
- traction over unstable surface
- tolerance for abrasive regolith
- maintenance burden
- severe route use

The tracks should not look toy-like or overly stylized.

### 4. Cab

The cab should feel:

- high enough for visibility
- practical for an operator
- industrial
- weather-sealed
- used daily
- equipped for ugly field work

Possible visible details:

- glare-shielded windows
- route lights
- mirrors
- hazard beacons
- grab rails
- ladder access
- dust-streaked glass
- seals and reinforced edges

### 5. Body / Hood

The body should feel:

- long and strong
- mechanically dense
- serviceable
- field-repaired
- built for work, not beauty

### 6. Rear Coupling / Tow Hardware

The rear must visually support the idea that Red Umbrielor pulls the MITE II modular train.

Required design cues:

- heavy rear hitch
- reinforced coupler architecture
- tow points
- cable routing
- utility hookups
- hydraulic or power-transfer implication
- structural logic for dragging multiple modules

This is crucial for Pages 9-10 and for the machine's role in the story.

### 7. Call Number 289

`289` should be visible as a field identifier.

It should feel:

- stenciled
- worn
- repainted or touched up over time
- practical rather than decorative

Possible placement:

- side of hood
- cab side
- body panel
- rear quarter
- blade flank
- multiple small placements if desired

### 8. Color and Wear

Primary color family:

- worn industrial red
- dark gray / black mechanical understructure
- grime-darkened joints
- metallic edges
- dust-muted surfaces

The red should not be clean or showroom bright.

Use:

- chipped paint
- patched panels
- mismatched reds from replacement plates
- abrasion scars
- mineral dust
- thermal wear
- route damage
- old work marks

---

## Meridian Prime Adaptation Details

Red Umbrielor should be visibly adapted to a crystalline-regolith world.

### Surface / Weathering Cues

Use:

- crystal dust packed into seams
- abrasive scoring on lower panels
- dust haze on glass
- heat-scorched edges from midday route exposure
- cold-crack stress in paint from dawn/night cycles
- mineral frosting in corners and joints
- regolith dulling over once-gloss surfaces
- scratches from hard crystal ridges
- replaceable wear plates
- dust-filtration hardware

### Functional Adaptation Cues

Possible features:

- glare shields
- reinforced vent screens
- dust-filter housings
- thermal shielding near engine areas
- field sensors
- pressure-route hardware
- blade edge wear indicators
- undercarriage guards
- sealed service points
- route marker storage
- tow cable anchors
- lighting suitable for low-visibility traverse conditions

### Interior / Operator Context

If the interior is shown, it should feel like a lived-in work cab, not a fighter cockpit.

Possible cues:

- analog-digital hybrid instrumentation
- checklists
- clipped route packet
- practical storage
- heater workaround culture
- field grime
- worn handles
- utilitarian seat wear
- thermos / roaster / survival clutter in story context

---

## What Red Umbrielor Is Not

Avoid:

- sleek spaceship styling
- generic sci-fi tank design
- clean future-tech panels
- military glamour
- toy-like proportions
- superhero vehicle posing
- ultramodern minimalism
- smooth glossy red finish
- agricultural cleanliness
- decorative concept-art nonsense with no mechanical logic
- tiny cab that wrecks scale
- blade omitted or treated as optional
- tracks too small to support the machine
- hyper-detailed nonsense that loses the silhouette
- polar/snow-tractor identity as the dominant read

## Variant A: Full Exterior Design

**Adaptation Label:** Active sandbox production reference

### Best Use

Primary machine reference for artists.

### Goal

Lock the full-body exterior design of Red Umbrielor as the baseline visual reference.

### Prompt

Create a full exterior design image of **Red Umbrielor**, a fictional **MMS Quadtrack 580 traverse hauler**, **call number 289**, used in the Meridian Prime comic sandbox. The machine should look almost identical in core silhouette to a real-world articulated four-track heavy tractor in the Case Quadtrac 580 family, but adapted for a crystalline-regolith traverse environment. It must be a massive red articulated machine with four heavy track assemblies, a high operator cab, long hood, industrial body mass, heavy rear towing/coupling hardware, and a **permanently mounted front blade**. The red bodywork should be weathered, patched, scarred, and dusty, with visible worn `289` markings. Add Meridian Prime-specific wear and adaptation: crystal-dust accumulation, abrasive scoring, heat/cold cycle damage, vent screens, glare protection, route-work grime, and practical field repairs. The machine should feel like a brutally reliable workhorse built to drag modular trains across unstable crystalline regolith. Not military. Not sleek. Not a spaceship. Not a clean farm tractor.

### Notes

This is the primary baseline reference.

All later versions should derive from this silhouette and identity.

## Variant B: Working Cold-Start View

**Adaptation Label:** Active sandbox production reference

### Best Use

Page 5 style imagery. Cold-start story reference. Operational mood reference.

### Goal

Show Red Umbrielor waking into route work, with strong story atmosphere and mechanical personality.

### Prompt

Create a story-focused exterior view of **Red Umbrielor**, an **MMS Quadtrack 580 traverse hauler**, **call number 289**, during cold-start conditions before route departure. The machine should be a massive red articulated four-track traverse tractor with a permanent front blade, heavy industrial proportions, and visible field wear. Show dawn chill, vapor, crystal dust, old lights, grime, chipped red paint, patched bodywork, and a worn `289` stencil. The machine is parked in a rough industrial yard or staging environment, preparing for traverse work. Emphasize practical mechanical atmosphere: cold exhaust, work lights, scarred blade edge, diagnostic readiness, route grime, and the feeling that this machine wakes before the crew does. It should feel old, useful, temperamental, and trusted.

### Notes

This version should carry character through machinery.

It is less about neutral design and more about operational presence.

## Variant C: Hot Traverse Season View

**Adaptation Label:** Active sandbox production reference

### Best Use

Mid-traverse environmental reference. Corrective counterweight against polar/snow assumptions.

### Goal

Show Red Umbrielor operating under hot traverse-season conditions on crystalline regolith.

### Prompt

Create an exterior action/reference image of **Red Umbrielor**, an **MMS Quadtrack 580 traverse hauler**, **call number 289**, working during hot traverse-season conditions on Meridian Prime's crystalline regolith. The machine is a red articulated four-track traverse tractor with a permanent front blade, heavy track assemblies, high cab, long hood, and rear train-coupling hardware. Show bright glare, heat shimmer, reflective crystal dust, abrasive regolith, dust haze, scorched wear on panel edges, filtered vents, glare shields, and lower-body abrasion from crystal grit. The blade is scarred and dust-packed from route shaping. The machine should feel heavy, practical, and field-modified, not polar, not military, not sleek, and not a clean farm tractor.

### Notes

This version helps artists remember Meridian Prime is not a polar setting. The Traverse can be brutally hot.

---

## Variant D: Poster / Silhouette Version

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon for poster/graphic shorthand

### Best Use

Page 23 poster/ad page. Warning-poster silhouette. Simplified branding shorthand. Graphic design use.

### Goal

Reduce Red Umbrielor to a highly readable silhouette while preserving identity.

### Prompt

Create a simplified silhouette/poster version of **Red Umbrielor**, the **MMS Quadtrack 580 traverse hauler**, **call number 289**. The design should remain instantly readable as a blade-fronted articulated four-track industrial machine. Emphasize the permanent front blade, high cab, long hood, four track assemblies, and heavy rear towing profile. The image should be clear enough to work in an in-world Traverse warning poster or graphic ad. It should feel practical, heavy, and iconic even with reduced detail. Avoid military styling, spaceship cues, snow-tractor assumptions, or generic tank shapes.

### Notes

The silhouette should still say "Red Umbrielor" even if all small details vanish.

## Negative Prompt / Avoid List

Avoid:

- no blade
- generic tank profile
- spaceship cockpit styling
- smooth sci-fi armor
- clean civilian tractor with no adaptation
- toy-like track modules
- tiny wheels or hybrid wheel/track confusion
- military gun mounts
- decorative weapons
- heroic action-vehicle framing
- clean glossy finish
- fantasy machine horns/spikes
- exaggerated monster-plow proportions
- random pipes with no purpose
- illegible over-detailing
- silhouette confusion
- polar expedition visual identity
- snowcat identity
- ice-tractor identity

It is not cute.
