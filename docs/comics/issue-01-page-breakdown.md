## Page 1: Front Cover

**Page role:** Front cover

**Adaptation label:** Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

**Visual objective:** Red Umbrielor faces a vast cracked formation under a bruised alien dawn. Tenet stands small in the foreground with the MITE II packet visible and the pistol implied by silhouette or body language.

**Story objective:** Sell the whole issue in one image: machine, operator, forbidden route, buried world, inherited grief.

**Panel density:** Single full-bleed cover image.

**Dialogue / text function:** Title, issue label, creator placeholders, tagline.

**Suggested cover text:**

> The job was legal. The map was dead. The route answered anyway.

**Reveal control:** May symbolically show the formation, Rob's ghostly presence, Horus as official shadow, and Aeonos / Àæonos mirrored imagery. Do not explain any of it.

**Artist notes:** Red Umbrielor should feel massive, scarred, loved through maintenance, and dangerous by sheer utility. Tenet should not look heroic in a superhero sense. He should look like a field operator realizing the land just filed a complaint in a language older than paperwork.

**Safe-area notes:** Title/logo, creator names, issue info, and tagline must stay inside safe area.

## Page 3: Rob at The House Ten Built, Opening Sting

**Page role:** Interior page 1

**Adaptation label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Visual objective:** The House Ten Built in cold isolation. One light inside. Rob Holliday alone or nearly alone, sick but composed.

**Story objective:** Begin with the emotional fuse: Rob's end is not an end, it is preparation.

**Panel density:** 4 panels, quiet cinematic pacing.

**Dialogue / text function:** Minimal. Let the place and Rob's body tell the first truth.

**Suggested beats:**

1. Exterior: The House Ten Built, tiny against lethal cold.
2. Interior: old maps, tools, dust, photographs, and a trace of NCI history.
3. Rob's hand near a table, maybe after moving or hiding something.
4. Rob hears arrival outside.

**Reveal control:** Show Rob is sick and waiting. Do not explain Chrono-Floquet Distillate, full poisoning mechanics, or exact secret he protected.

**Artist notes:** Rob visual anchor: white hair swept to one side, square glasses, mustache connected to goatee, curled eyebrow tips, overweight but not obese, warm smile, salmon polo shirt under cold-weather layers if needed.

**Safe-area notes:** Captions inside safe area. Avoid tiny text in prop documents unless decorative.

## Page 4: Horus Arrives Too Late

**Page role:** Interior page 2

**Adaptation label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Visual objective:** Horus Skelton enters or stands in the threshold. Rob meets him with exhaustion, warmth, contempt, and final judgment.

**Story objective:** Establish the old friendship turned wound. Rob is dying, but Horus has already lost the thing he came to take.

**Panel density:** 5 panels, conversational tension.

**Dialogue / text function:** Short, sharp confrontation. No lore lecture.

**Suggested dialogue direction:**

- Horus asks for records, coordinates, confirmation, or access.
- Rob refuses without naming everything.
- Rob's final line on the page may be: `Bullshit.`

**Reveal control:** May reveal Horus wants old route truth. Do not fully reveal Core mechanics, exact House secrets, or all of Rob's death-chain.

**Artist notes:** Horus should feel official and personal, not generic villainy in a coat. He should carry decades of control and one old bruise that never healed.

**Safe-area notes:** Keep the final Rob line large enough to land cleanly.
