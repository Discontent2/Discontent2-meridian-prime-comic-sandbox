## Page Objective

Page 20 is the issue's primary visual bombshell.

The page must deliver awe and dread without explaining the lore.

Core feeling:

> The map did not omit terrain.  
> It omitted a wound.

The reader should feel that the official packet, route map, and authority chain failed in one impossible visual instant.

This page should not answer what the formation is.

It should make the reader need Page 21.

## Required Visual Anchors

### Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289

Red Umbrielor must be visible for scale.

It should feel:

- massive by human standards
- small against the formation
- scarred
- frost-caked
- field-modified
- industrial
- practical
- old but capable

Required details if visible enough:

- patched red panels
- heavy track mass
- worn `289`
- cold exhaust / frost / field grime

Do not let Red Umbrielor become the dominant subject of the page. It is the measuring stick for the impossible.

---

### Growth Rollers / Pressure Ridges

The reveal happens as Red Umbrielor clears the angle between growth rollers.

Use foreground or near-foreground terrain to show:

- pressure ridges
- growth rollers under crust
- crystal pressure
- terrain that has moved against the map
- black route flags or markers made unreliable by the land

The terrain should feel physically dangerous, not decorative.

---

### Hidden Formation

The formation owns the page.

It must feel:

- vast
- old
- cracked
- forbidden
- partially buried or obscured
- not natural enough to be geology
- not readable enough to be a simple ruin
- not named by the world around it

Primary visual language for this issue:

**route-scar / wound in the land**

Possible supporting textures:

- frost
- crystal pressure
- cold haze
- old shadow
- signal distortion
- pressure-weather glow only if subtle

Do not make the formation a clean portal, fantasy gate, alien temple, obvious spaceship wreck, or solved mechanism.

---

### Old Signal Distortion

The ghost-signal motif may appear subtly around the formation or page gutters.

It should feel like old systems answering, not magic.

Allowed uses:

- faint linework in gutters
- signal shimmer around formation edges
- interference on Ildi's inset monitor
- subtle distortion in cold haze

Avoid:

- glowing rune language
- magical sigils
- lightning portal effects
- UI clutter over the formation core

## Page Layout Guidance

### Main Splash

The page should be splash-first.

Recommended structure:

```text
[ MAIN SPLASH: formation reveal dominates page ]

Small inset 1: Tenet in cab / Log visual.
Small inset 2: Ildi monitor / It's answering us.

Captions placed sparingly in safe negative space:
No marker.
No lease note.
No official name.
```

### Composition Priorities

1. Formation silhouette / shape.
2. Red Umbrielor for scale.
3. Growth rollers / pressure ridges as approach hazard.
4. Sparse captions.
5. Two small insets.
6. Ghost-signal motif as texture.

### Inset Placement

Insets should be small enough not to damage the splash.

Good placement options:

- one inset in upper-left or upper-right sky/cold haze
- one inset lower side area near Red Umbrielor but not over the formation
- both insets vertically stacked along one edge if the formation silhouette needs the center

Avoid placing insets over:

- formation core silhouette
- Red Umbrielor's scale relationship
- main approach route
- caption area

## Lettering Guidance

Use `docs/comics/lettering-and-safe-area-guide.md`.

### Required Caption Treatment

Captions should be sparse and field-report clean:

```text
No marker.
No lease note.
No official name.
```

They should feel like the map and packet losing authority, not like poetic narration.

### Text Placement

All captions and inset dialogue must remain inside safe area.

Keep the splash mostly open.

Recommended maximum text elements:

- 3 short captions
- 1 Tenet inset line
- 1 Ildi inset line

### Lettering Warning

Too much lettering kills the reveal.

The formation should make the reader pause before reading.

## Variant A: Route-Scar / Wound

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

### Best Use

Primary Page 20 direction. Most aligned with the second draft.

### Art Direction

Red Umbrielor clears the growth rollers and the land opens into something that looks less discovered than exposed. The formation dominates the page as a vast route-scar or wound. It appears old, cracked, pressure-buried, and partially veiled by frost and cold signal distortion.

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic splash page for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**, Page 20 hidden formation reveal. Red Umbrielor 289, a massive scarred red industrial quadtrack vehicle, clears dangerous growth rollers and pressure ridges in a freezing alien route corridor. Ahead, a vast hidden formation dominates the page, reading as a route-scar and wound in the land. It is cracked, old, partially buried, frost-caked, and surrounded by cold haze and subtle old signal distortion. It is not natural enough to be geology and not clear enough to be named. Red Umbrielor must remain visible for scale but small against the formation. Mood: awe, dread, official map failure, industrial survival, forbidden terrain. Include room inside the safe area for three small captions: `No marker. No lease note. No official name.` Include two tiny inset areas for Tenet in cab saying `Log visual.` and Ildi's monitor saying `It's answering us.` Do not make the formation a portal, fantasy gate, alien temple, or spaceship wreck.

### Notes

This variant should feel like the map's missing wound has opened in front of the crew.

## Variant B: Buried Machine Fossil

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

### Best Use

More industrial / ancient-technology version if the page needs stronger machine resonance.

### Art Direction

The formation looks like something partly mechanical and partly fossilized by pressure, but still not identifiable. It should not read as a spaceship wreck. Its machine quality should be ambiguous, as if the land and old structure have compressed into each other.

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic splash page for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**, Page 20 hidden formation reveal. Red Umbrielor 289 emerges from a corridor of growth rollers and pressure ridges into view of an enormous buried formation. The formation feels like a machine fossil fused into the land: cracked structural ribs, pressure-worn surfaces, frost, crystal stress lines, and ancient industrial geometry half-swallowed by terrain. It must remain mysterious and unnamed, not a recognizable spaceship, not a clean ruin, not an alien temple. Red Umbrielor is visible for scale, small and practical against the huge structure. The atmosphere is freezing, industrial, and full of restrained signal distortion. Leave safe-area space for the captions `No marker. No lease note. No official name.` and two small insets: Tenet in cab, `Log visual.` and Ildi monitor, `It's answering us.`

### Notes

Use this version only if the formation needs more material specificity. Keep the identity unresolved.

## Variant C: Negative Space Formation

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

### Best Use

Most mysterious version. Strong if the art direction should lean toward dread rather than spectacle.

### Art Direction

The formation is partly defined by absence: weather behaves wrong around it, frost refuses to cover certain planes, signal distortion outlines impossible edges, and the land seems to reveal a shape by failing to hide it.

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic splash page for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**, Page 20 hidden formation reveal. Red Umbrielor 289 clears growth rollers in a cold route corridor and faces a vast hidden formation that is partly defined by negative space. Frost, pressure haze, and old signal distortion reveal edges and cracks where the landscape refuses to behave normally. The formation should feel forbidden, huge, and old, like a route-scar emerging from absence. It is not a glowing portal, not a temple, not a spaceship wreck, not a fantasy monolith. Red Umbrielor must remain visible for scale near the bottom or lower third. The page should be quiet, enormous, and unsettling. Preserve safe-area space for three sparse captions: `No marker. No lease note. No official name.` Include two small insets only: Tenet in cab saying `Log visual.` and Ildi monitor saying `It's answering us.`

### Notes

This version works best if the artist can make emptiness feel engineered by pressure.

## Negative Prompt / Avoid List

Avoid:

- clean glowing portal
- fantasy gate
- magical doorway
- obvious alien temple
- obvious spaceship wreck
- generic monolith
- lore diagram
- map overlay explaining the formation
- giant rune circle
- glowing mystical symbols
- action movie explosion reveal
- superhero staging
- Red Umbrielor looking sleek or toy-like
- formation looking like a normal mountain
- formation looking like a city skyline
- over-lettering
- insets covering formation core
- captions placed near trim or bleed
- low-resolution filler textures

## Artist Notes

### Scale

The key relationship is:

```text
Tenet < Red Umbrielor < formation < Meridian Prime wrongness
```

Red Umbrielor is massive, but Page 20 should make it feel suddenly insufficient.

### Lighting

Use cold dawn / cold haze / pressure-weather light.

Avoid bright heroic sunlight.

The reveal should feel like the land has exposed something it did not want named.

### Color / Texture Direction

Suggested texture family:

- frost whites
- cold grays
- bruised dawn tones
- oxidized red on Red Umbrielor
- dark pressure cracks
- faint signal-blue distortion only if subtle

### Mood Words

- forbidden
- old
- unregistered
- pressure-buried
- industrial dread
- awe without explanation
- silence after machinery
