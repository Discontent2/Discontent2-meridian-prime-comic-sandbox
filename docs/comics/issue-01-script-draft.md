# PAGE 1

**Page Role:** Front Cover

**Adaptation Label:** Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

**Panel Count:** 1 full-bleed cover image

**Page Purpose:** Sell the entire hype route in one impossible image: Red Umbrielor, Tenet, the hidden formation, Rob's death-shadow, Horus's official pressure, and the sense that Meridian Prime itself has answered the route.

## Cover Image

**Visual:** Full-bleed cover. Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289 faces a vast cracked formation under a bruised alien dawn. The machine is massive, scarred, frost-caked, red panels patched in different shades, number `289` visible but weather-worn. Tenet stands small in the foreground, dressed as a field operator, not a superhero. One hand holds or rests near the MITE II packet. The pistol is implied by silhouette, coat line, or the weight of his stance rather than brandished.

Behind the formation, barely readable through cold haze and signal distortion: Rob Holliday's warm, sad face, Horus Skelton as a dark official shape, and mirrored but non-identical world-shapes suggesting Aeonos / Àæonos without explaining them.

**Cover Text:**

MERIDIAN PRIME  
THE ROUTE TO IT

**Tagline:**

The job was legal. The map was dead. The route answered anyway.

**Lettering Note:** Title, creator placeholders, issue number, and tagline must stay inside safe area. Any spectral background elements may bleed.

**Reveal Control:** Symbolic hype only. Do not explain the formation, Core, Aeonos / Àæonos, Rob's death-chain, or pistol function.

# PAGE 3

**Page Role:** Interior Page 1

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** 4 panels

**Page Purpose:** Open on Rob Holliday at The House Ten Built. Establish that the dead man has already made a move before Tenet knows he is on the board.

## Panel 1

**Visual:** Wide exterior. The House Ten Built sits alone in brutal Meridian Prime cold. Not famous, not grand, not picturesque. A small legacy cabin swallowed by pressure weather, old route dark, and endless hard distance. One warm light burns in a window.

**Caption:**

THE HOUSE TEN BUILT.

**Caption:**

Far enough off active routes that official maps had to squint before lying about it.

**Lettering Note:** Captions should feel like field-report narration with a human edge.

## Panel 2

**Visual:** Interior. Old route maps, tools, photographs, dust, oilcloth, field notes, and worn furniture. A place built by people who trusted objects more than records. A photograph is visible but not emphasized: three young men beside a military MMS Quadtrack.

**Caption:**

Rob Holliday had spent his life refusing bad paperwork, worse weather, and men who mistook access for trust.

## Panel 3

**Visual:** Rob Holliday at a table. White hair swept to one side, square glasses, mustache connected to goatee, curled eyebrow tips, overweight but not obese. He wears a salmon polo under cold-weather layers left open indoors. He looks sick: tired, thinner than he should be, but warm-eyed and present. One hand rests near an old oilcloth bundle's absence, as if something important was recently there and now is not.

**SFX:**

KTTK

**Lettering Note:** The sound is a small stove/heater tick, not a dramatic noise.

## Panel 4

**Visual:** Rob looks toward the door. Outside, headlights or field lamps cut through cold darkness. He smiles faintly, the way a man smiles when the last bad appointment finally arrives late.

**Rob Dialogue:**

Took you long enough.

**Reveal Control:** Show Rob is ill and waiting. Do not explain the poison, the Core, or what exactly he moved.

# PAGE 4

**Page Role:** Interior Page 2

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Page Purpose:** Establish Horus as personal, official, wounded, and too late. Rob's refusal becomes the issue's first moral detonation.

## Panel 1

**Visual:** Horus Skelton stands in the open doorway, cold behind him like another uniform. He is controlled, official, weathered by age and power. His silhouette should suggest decades of Traverse authority, not random villainy.

**Horus Dialogue:**

Robert.

**Rob Dialogue, off-panel:**

That's the voice people use when they want a favor and brought a threat in case manners fail.

## Panel 2

**Visual:** Horus inside now, door closed behind him. He does not remove all his outer gear. He is not staying. Rob remains seated, conserving strength, but he owns the room by refusing to be impressed.

**Horus Dialogue:**

Where are the records?

**Rob Dialogue:**

Which ones?

**Horus Dialogue:**

Do not make this smaller than it is.

## Panel 3

**Visual:** Close on Rob. Warm smile. Eyes sharp behind square glasses. Eyebrow tips curled like tiny weather hooks.

**Rob Dialogue:**

Horus, you made everything smaller the day you started calling old friends assets.

## Panel 4

**Visual:** Close on Horus's gloved hand on the back of a chair. He grips it too hard. In the background, the photograph of the three young men is now slightly clearer, but still not explained.

**Horus Dialogue:**

Ten left something here.

**Rob Dialogue, off-panel:**

Ten left a lot of things. Tools. Debt. Bad coffee. Better judgment than either of us deserved.

## Panel 5

**Visual:** Rob leans back. Tired, sick, but victorious in a way Horus cannot yet tolerate. The empty place where the oilcloth bundle once rested sits between them like a missing tooth.

**Horus Dialogue:**

I need what he left.

**Rob Dialogue:**

Bullshit.

**Lettering Note:** Rob's final word should be large enough to hit but not superhero-sized. It is a verdict, not a punchline.

**Reveal Control:** May imply Horus came for route truth or access. Do not reveal full House secrets, Core mechanics, or exact pistol function.

# PAGE 5

**Page Role:** Interior Page 3

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference with heightened visual pacing

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Page Purpose:** Hard cut from Rob's final refusal to Tenet's working morning. Establish Red Umbrielor, Tenet's competence, the cold, and the practical texture of the world.

## Panel 1

**Visual:** Tenet's gloved hand pressed against scarred red steel. Frost catches in chipped paint. The panel is tactile: cold metal, field grime, worn glove seams.

**Caption:**

NCI STAGING YARD.

**Caption:**

Before dawn, Red Umbrielor complained correctly.

## Panel 2

**Visual:** Wider shot. Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289 sits massive in the half-asleep NCI yard. Field lamps, vapor, fuel crews, old Mods, and dark blocks of machinery in the background. The number `289` is stenciled beneath frost, partly worn away.

**SFX:**

KUNK. TICK. WHRRRRR.

## Panel 3

**Visual:** Diagnostic strip on the machine. Amber warning.

**Diagnostic Text:**

MD-8 PRESSURE UNEVEN

**Tenet Dialogue, off-panel:**

Yeah.

## Panel 4

**Visual:** Tenet watches the warning for a few breaths. He is calm, experienced, and tired in the way people are tired before work rather than after it. The warning clears.

**Diagnostic Text:**

STATUS: STABILIZING

**Tenet Dialogue:**

Thought so.

## Panel 5

**Visual:** Tenet climbs the ladder toward the cab. Behind him, NCI wakes in grudges: fuel handlers, mechanics arguing quietly, Mods being coupled, cold exhaust. Red Umbrielor's scale dominates the page.

**Caption:**

Catastrophe had a different sound.

**Reveal Control:** No Rob explanation yet. The cut itself implies connection.

The turkey roaster came alive with the confidence of a field fix nobody wanted to explain to insurance.

The passenger seat stayed empty.

# PAGE 7

**Page Role:** Interior Page 5

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference with comic pacing

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Page Purpose:** Bring Lindy into the cab threshold with family friction, dispatch competence, and the MITE II packet.

## Panel 1

**Visual:** Lindy Nocsid crosses the staging yard with a dispatch slate tucked under one arm and a hardcopy packet under the other. She moves like irritation learned to wear boots. Field crews part without making a ceremony of it.

**Caption:**

Lindy Nocsid did not wave.

**Caption:**

Lindy considered waving an admission that people were far away from each other.

## Panel 2

**Visual:** Lindy climbs the side step and leans into Red Umbrielor's cab doorway. Thermal cap low, sharp eyes, small frame carrying more authority than her job title admits.

**Lindy Dialogue:**

Your defrost still dead?

**Tenet Dialogue:**

Resting.

**Lindy Dialogue:**

Runs in the family.

## Panel 3

**Visual:** Lindy sniffs the cab air with theatrical suspicion. The turkey roaster glows behind Tenet.

**Lindy Dialogue:**

Turkey roaster?

**Tenet Dialogue:**

Until the defrost remembers the contract.

**Lindy Dialogue:**

Defrost never signed. Defrost had legal review.

## Panel 4

**Visual:** She shoves the packet at him. The hardcopy is in a field sleeve, stiff with cold. At the same time, the digital twin blinks onto Red Umbrielor's dash.

**SFX:**

CHIME

**Packet Text:**

MITE II PROPOSED ROUTE ACCESS / SURVEYING AND PROOFING PACKAGE  
NCI INTERNAL FIELD COPY  
CONTRACT DESIGNATION: HEO-IC / FIELD LEAD

**Tenet Dialogue:**

What did they change?

## Panel 5

**Visual:** Lindy braces in the doorway, not fully entering. Threshold posture. She has delivered the packet, but not surrendered her distance.

**Lindy Dialogue:**

Good morning to you too.

**Tenet Dialogue:**

It was morning before you opened the door.

**Lindy Dialogue:**

Barely. And I improved it.

The mission read clean.

**Caption:**

**Tenet Dialogue:**

Yeah.
