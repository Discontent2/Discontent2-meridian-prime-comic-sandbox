## Page Objective

Page 21 is the quiet evidence page after Page 20's visual bombshell.

Page 20 says the formation should not be there.

Page 21 says something used it.

Core feeling:

> The formation was impossible.  
> The footprint made it personal.

This page must sharpen the mystery without solving it.

The reader should leave Page 21 with a better question, not an answer.

## Script Anchor

**Page Role:** Interior Page 19

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference revealed early for hype / Promo Exaggeration

**Panel Count:** 3 panels

**Page Purpose:** Quiet impossible evidence. The formation is not just geology. Something used it.

Required caption:

```text
The formation was not just geology.
```

Required Tenet dialogue:

```text

```

Small exchange:


## Required Visual Anchors

### Low Exterior Evidence View

The page should begin close to the ground near the formation approach.

Use:

- snow
- crystal dust
- disrupted frost
- hard surface
- pressure-weather grit
- faint formation shadow or presence nearby

The surface should feel cold, brittle, and evidence-bearing.

---

### The Footprint

The footprint is the page's core object.

It must be:

- clear enough to be evidence
- ambiguous enough not to identify the maker
- shaped wrong enough to raise questions
- preserved in terrain texture
- not a monster reveal

Avoid making the footprint clearly:

- human
- Meridian
- Saurasapien
- Antisapien
- fresh in an obvious way
- ancient in an obvious way
- animalistic
- monstrous
- symbolic rune / magic mark

It should read as a physical trace, not a logo.

---

### Scale Reference

Include one subtle scale marker:

- crawler probe
- crew boot at edge of frame
- sensor wand
- measuring marker
- gloved hand held back from touching

The scale marker must not disturb the print.

The crew is preserving evidence, not trampling it.

---

### Tenet's Field Instinct

Tenet must remain practical and controlled.

He should be shown through one of these modes:

- cab/exterior feed view
- distant field view
- reflected monitor image
- command posture in Red Umbrielor

He does not solve the footprint.

He protects the evidence.

---

### Formation Presence

The formation may appear as:

- blurred background mass
- shadow over terrain
- edge of structure outside focus
- pressure haze silhouette
- implied scale beyond panel edge

The footprint is the evidence; the formation is the pressure behind it.

## Page Layout Guidance

### Recommended 3-Panel Structure

```text
Panel 1: low exterior close-up near formation approach
Panel 2: footprint evidence close-up, largest and quietest panel
Panel 3: Tenet / feed view looking from print toward formation
```

### Panel 1 Guidance

Low, ground-level view.

Show disrupted frost and crystal dust before the reader fully understands what they are seeing.

Include the tiny exchange:

```text
Forest: Tenet.
Tenet: I see it.
```

This panel should feel like someone noticing the floor has started speaking.

### Panel 2 Guidance

The footprint dominates.

This should be the largest panel or visual center of the page.

Place caption carefully:

```text
The formation was not just geology.
```

Caption should not cover the footprint's most important shape.

### Panel 3 Guidance

Show Tenet's preservation order.

Possible view:

- Tenet framed by cab/exterior feed
- Tenet seen in reflection over a preserved footprint tag
- Red Umbrielor's systems view showing the footprint area marked off
- distant crew perspective looking from print toward formation

Dialogue:

```text
Nobody steps near it.
Nobody explains it before we preserve it.
```

The line should feel procedural, not dramatic.

## Lettering Guidance

Use `docs/comics/lettering-and-safe-area-guide.md`.

### Text Density

Keep this page nearly silent.

Maximum suggested text elements:

1. Forest/Tenet small exchange.
2. One caption.
3. Tenet preservation order.
4. Tiny radio texture only if needed.

Do not add extra exposition.

### Caption Treatment

The caption should be clean and field-report restrained:

```text
The formation was not just geology.
```

It should feel like evidence changing classification.

### Dialogue Treatment

Tenet's line should be in radio/comms style if he is speaking from the cab or through field comms.

If he is physically near the evidence, normal speech balloon is acceptable, but the page should still feel controlled and procedural.

## Variant A: Evidence Close-Up

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

### Best Use

Primary Page 21 direction. Cleanest and strongest.

### Art Direction

The footprint dominates the page as physical evidence. The formation is mostly implied through terrain pressure and shadow. The page is quiet, close, and forensic.

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic page for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**, Page 21 footprint reveal. The page is almost silent and evidence-focused. Near the hidden formation approach, show snow, crystal dust, disrupted frost, and a single ambiguous footprint pressed into hard cold terrain. The footprint must be clear enough to read as evidence but not identifiable as human, alien, animal, monster, old, or fresh. It should be shaped wrong enough to raise questions. Include a small scale reference, such as a crawler probe, sensor marker, or crew boot at the edge of frame, carefully kept away from the print. The mood is forensic, cold, and unsettling. Preserve room for sparse lettering: `The formation was not just geology.` and Tenet's line, `Nobody steps near it. Nobody explains it before we preserve it.` Do not reveal the maker. No monster, no explanation, no magic symbol.

### Notes

This variant should make the reader lean closer.

## Variant B: Probe Scale

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

### Best Use

Use if the page needs clearer scale and field-procedure emphasis.

### Art Direction

A crawler probe or field sensor sits near the print without touching it. Tenet's preservation instinct is visually reinforced by distance, marker placement, and controlled approach.

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic page for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**, Page 21 footprint reveal. Show a cold ground-level evidence scene near the hidden formation. A small crawler probe or field sensor has stopped beside an ambiguous footprint in snow, crystal dust, and disrupted frost. The probe provides scale but does not touch the footprint. The print is clear enough to be evidence and strange enough to be unsettling, but it must not identify the maker. In the distance or through a subtle feed view, suggest Red Umbrielor / MITE II holding position and Tenet ordering preservation. The page should feel procedural and tense, not action-oriented. Keep text sparse: `The formation was not just geology.` and `Nobody steps near it. Nobody explains it before we preserve it.` No monster reveal, no explanation, no obvious species footprint.

### Notes

This variant emphasizes Tenet's competence and evidence discipline.

## Variant C: Formation Shadow

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

### Best Use

Use if the page needs stronger visual continuity from Page 20.

### Art Direction

The footprint sits in the foreground while the hidden formation looms blurred or shadowed behind it. The formation's presence makes the small evidence feel worse.

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic page for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**, Page 21 footprint reveal. In the foreground, show an ambiguous footprint preserved in snow, crystal dust, and disrupted frost near the hidden formation approach. The footprint is clear physical evidence but not identifiable as human, alien, animal, monster, old, or fresh. Behind it, out of focus or partially obscured by cold haze, the vast hidden formation looms as a route-scar / wound in the land. Include a small scale marker at the edge of the evidence field, such as a boot, probe, or sensor wand, without disturbing the print. The composition should be quiet, cold, and deeply unsettling. Leave clean safe-area room for the caption `The formation was not just geology.` and Tenet's preservation order. No explanation, no creature, no magical symbol.

### Notes

This variant makes the footprint feel like the formation's smallest accusation.

## Negative Prompt / Avoid List

Avoid:

- monster reveal
- visible creature
- clear human footprint
- clear dinosaur/reptile footprint
- clear alien species footprint
- glowing magic mark
- rune / sigil footprint
- blood trail
- horror jump scare
- action pose
- crew member stepping in the print
- Tenet solving the print
- explanatory diagram
- measurement clutter that covers the evidence
- oversized UI labels
- muddy unreadable ground texture
- caption covering the footprint
- full lore answer

## Artist Notes

### Evidence Tone

This page should feel forensic, not spectacular.

The dread comes from restraint.

The footprint is not a monster's entrance. It is a question the ground preserved.

### Scale

The print must be readable at comic size.

Use one clear scale marker, but keep the evidence visually dominant.

### Surface Texture

Use specific terrain materials:

- snow crust
- crystal dust
- hard pressure surface
- broken frost edge
- small displaced particles
- subtle compression around print

### Mood Words

- quiet
- forensic
- impossible
- preserved
- cold
- personal
- procedural dread
- evidence without answer
