## Purpose

This document translates Pages 23-24 of the second-draft script into rough layout logic.

Pages 23-24 close the issue as an object:

1. Page 23 lets the reader breathe through an in-world Traverse warning poster / ad framework.
2. Page 24 returns to The House Ten Built and closes on Rob's agency.

Together, these pages form the pressure valve after Page 22's emotional recognition.

Page 23 says:

> This is the kind of world where work survives by warning itself.

## Global Thumbnail Rules

### Print Geometry

Use the Mixam framework measurements:

- Final canvas including bleed: **6.94 in x 10.49 in**
- Trim: **6.69 in x 10.24 in**
- Bleed: **0.125 in** on all sides
- Safe area: **0.25 in inside trim**
- Important elements at least **0.375 in from final canvas edge**

### Closing-Page Priorities

For each page, rough in:

- safe-area box
- bleed boundary
- major poster/image zones
- text hierarchy
- focal object / image anchor
- safe production note placement
- caption/footer placement
- where background texture may bleed

### Closing Rhythm

Pages 23-24 should move like this:

1. **Page 23:** breath, poster, Traverse culture.
2. **Page 24:** silence, House, Rob's choice.

Do not add new story mechanics here. These pages should close the pressure case, not open another trapdoor.

## Page 23: Inside Back Cover / In-World Traverse Warning Ad

**Page Role:** Inside back cover / in-world warning ad

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Candidate Canon for poster language

**Panel Count:** No traditional panels. Poster / ad page.

**Thumbnail Goal:** Create a readable in-world poster that also works as an ad/template page. It should reinforce NCI / Traverse culture without feeling like a normal story panel.

### Required Text

Primary poster headline:

```text
THINGS ARE DIFFERENT ON TRAVERSE.
```

Secondary poster text:

```text
NCI accepts private route contracts, field repairs, survey support, and bad decisions with paperwork.
```

Small in-world text:

```text
Ask your Lead before trusting the map.
Ask your mechanic before trusting the machine.
Ask your signal tech before trusting the silence.
```

Required production note:

```text
Keep text/logos inside the safe area (0.25 in from trim). Backgrounds may extend to bleed.
```

Optional footer:

```text
NOCSID CONTRACTORS INC.
THE FAMILY / THE COMPANY / FIELD WORK DONE UGLY
```

### Recommended Layout Concept

Poster hierarchy, not panel sequence:

```text
[ TOP SAFE AREA ]
Headline: THINGS ARE DIFFERENT ON TRAVERSE.

[ MID FIELD ]
Cold field-paper texture / route grid / packet fragments
Optional ghost-signal motif as low-contrast background

[ LOWER THIRD ]
Red Umbrielor + Mod train silhouette crossing black-flagged route

[ LOWER SAFE AREA ]
Secondary text / small in-world warnings
Required production note
Optional NCI footer
```

### Visual Anchors

#### Red Umbrielor / Mod Train Silhouette

Place in lower third or lower-middle.

Must read as:

- heavy
- industrial
- field-useful
- moving through a black-flagged route
- simplified enough for poster clarity

Do not over-detail the silhouette. The page is a poster, not a technical cutaway.

#### Black-Flagged Route Motif

Use as a strong horizontal or diagonal path through the lower third.

It should suggest:

- route danger
- field procedure
- Traverse work culture
- warning-sign identity

#### Packet Fragments / Approval Blocks

Use as background texture only.

Possible elements:

- route-grid lines
- redacted bars
- clipped approval blocks
- faint NCI form fields
- low-contrast ghost of MITE II packet structure

Keep all texture subordinate to text readability.

#### Ghost-Signal Texture

May appear as faint linework threading through the background or near the route silhouette.

Rule: if it starts fighting text, kill it. The poster must be readable first.

### Text Hierarchy

1. Headline: largest, top safe area.
2. Secondary text: medium, below headline or above silhouette.
3. Three `Ask your...` lines: small but readable, grouped cleanly.
4. Production note: small but readable, bottom safe area.
5. Optional footer: smallest brand/signature area.

### Safe-Area Requirements

All text must sit inside safe area:

- headline
- secondary text
- warning text
- production note
- optional footer
- any logo/mark

Background route grid, field-paper texture, and silhouettes may extend toward trim or bleed only if they are non-essential.

### Thumbnail Variants

Create three rough thumbnails:

#### Variant A: Classic Warning Poster

- Big headline at top.
- Red Umbrielor silhouette lower third.
- warning copy stacked below.
- cleanest and most readable.

#### Variant B: Route-Map Poster

- headline top-left.
- route line diagonally crosses page.
- silhouette travels along route.
- ghost-signal texture follows route.

#### Variant C: Corrupted Field Ad

- poster embedded in packet/document texture.
- approval blocks and form fragments visible.
- silhouette appears as stamped/printed graphic.

Recommended first pass: **Variant A** for print clarity.

### Lettering Risk

This page can accidentally become design soup. Keep the headline dominant and the production note legible.

### Page Rhythm

After Page 22, this page should feel like a breath through official paper: strange, useful, and a little funny in the jaw.

## Page 24: Back Cover / The House Ten Built

**Page Role:** Back cover / back-story image page

**Adaptation Label:** Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

**Panel Count:** 1 full-bleed back-cover image

**Thumbnail Goal:** Close the issue on The House Ten Built, one warm light, fading tracks, and Rob's final agency. Quiet myth sting, no literal ghost scene.

### Required Text

Main caption:

```text
Rob did not die because he lost.
He died because he chose.
```

Optional footer:

```text
MERIDIAN PRIME WILL RETURN
```

### Recommended Layout Concept

Full-bleed landscape with safe caption zone:

```text
[ cold sky / pressure haze / negative space ]

        The House Ten Built
        small, distant, one warm window

        old tracks fading under frost

[ lower safe caption zone ]
Rob did not die because he lost.
He died because he chose.

[ optional footer ]
MERIDIAN PRIME WILL RETURN
```

### Visual Anchors

#### The House Ten Built

Place slightly above center or in the middle third, small against the cold.

Must feel:

- remote
- practical
- legacy-heavy
- secret-bearing
- stubborn against the landscape

Avoid gothic haunted-house staging.

#### One Warm Light

This is the emotional anchor. It should be easy to find even at thumbnail size.

It should suggest:

- Rob's choice still matters
- the house remembers
- warmth inside enormous cold
- agency, not haunting

Do not cover the warm window with caption text.

#### Old Tracks Fading Under Frost

Use as a subtle diagonal or foreground path leading away from the house.

They should suggest:

- Red Umbrielor / Traverse memory
- route history
- something already moved
- evidence being swallowed by weather

Do not make them too diagrammatic.

#### Optional Memory Pressure

If included, keep it non-literal:

- slight salmon warmth near window
- a shape that could be interior shadow
- track-memory in snow
- cold haze where a figure might have been, but is not clearly a ghost

Do not show literal ghost Rob.

### Text Placement

Main caption should sit inside safe area, preferably lower third or lower-middle, depending on landscape balance.

Requirements:

- high contrast
- enough breathing room
- not over busy frost/track texture
- not too close to trim
- optional footer smaller and below or separated from main caption

### Thumbnail Variants

Create three rough thumbnails:

#### Variant A: Quiet Mythic House

- House small, central or slightly upper-center.
- one warm light.
- tracks fade toward bottom foreground.
- caption lower third.

Recommended primary version.

#### Variant B: Track-Led Composition

- tracks dominate foreground and lead eye to house.
- house sits in upper-middle distance.
- caption in cold sky or lower safe area depending contrast.

Good if the route-memory motif needs emphasis.

#### Variant C: Wide Cold Negative Space

- house very small.
- massive sky/land dominates.
- caption floats in clean negative space.

Best for quiet literary pitch mood.

### Lettering Risk

Do not let the caption sit too close to trim. Do not place white/light text over pale frost without a subtle backing shape or contrast strategy.

### Page Rhythm

The back cover should feel like the last warm window after the machine noise stops.

It should not answer what happened. It should insist that Rob chose.

### From Page 23 to Page 24

## Artist Notes for Pages 23-24

### Page 23 Poster Mood

The poster should feel official enough to be believable and ugly enough to be used.

Think:

- field office wall
- route safety culture
- NCI practical humor
- warning poster that has survived cold, hands, and bad tape

Avoid:

- glossy corporate ad
- fake recruitment cheer
- over-designed sci-fi UI
- cluttered fake bureaucracy

### Page 24 House Mood

The House should feel real, not theatrical.

Think:

- practical shelter
- too much history
- old cold
- one decision still warm
- route memory fading into frost

Avoid:

- gothic horror mansion
- fantasy cottage charm
- literal ghost in window
- sentimental glow overload
