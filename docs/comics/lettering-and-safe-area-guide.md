## Core Lettering Philosophy

Text should feel like field evidence, not decoration.

Meridian Prime lettering should support the sense that readers are handling a route packet, listening to crew traffic, and watching field truth overrule official language.

Use text to clarify pressure, not to explain the whole world.

Guiding rules:

- Keep spoken dialogue readable and human.
- Keep radio traffic distinct from normal speech.
- Treat packet/interface text as evidence, not wallpaper, when it carries story meaning.
- Let big visual pages breathe.
- Do not turn technical texture into confetti.
- Keep all essential words inside the safe area.

## Required Page Geometry

Physical inch measurements are the source of truth.

| Area | Width | Height |
|---|---:|---:|
| Final canvas including bleed | 6.94 in | 10.49 in |
| Trim size | 6.69 in | 10.24 in |
| Bleed | 0.125 in on all sides | 0.125 in on all sides |
| Safe / quiet area | 0.25 in inside trim | 0.25 in inside trim |

Because bleed is 0.125 in and safe area starts 0.25 in inside trim, all important elements should sit at least:

**0.375 in from the final canvas edge**

on all sides.

## Safe-Area Rules

### Must Stay Inside Safe Area

- Speech balloons
- Radio balloons
- Captions
- Memory dialogue
- Page numbers
- Creator names
- Cover logo / title
- Issue info
- Back-cover teaser captions
- Poster/ad text
- Required production notes
- Important packet text
- Important diagnostic/interface text
- Sound effects that carry story information

### May Extend Through Bleed

- Full-bleed art
- Background textures
- Signal motifs used as design texture
- Weather / frost / pressure effects
- Non-essential packet texture
- Decorative route-map fragments
- Non-story SFX if intentionally cropped

### Avoid

- Balloons touching trim.
- Tails crossing too many panels.
- Tiny packet/interface text that must be read to understand the scene.
- Captions sitting on busy art without contrast.
- Important SFX placed under fold, trim, or bleed.
- Cover/back-cover text near the page edge.

## Text Hierarchy

### 1. Normal Speech Balloons

Use for in-person dialogue inside scenes.

**Primary pages:** Rob/Horus pages, cab scenes, Lindy/Tenet exchange, direct character moments.

**Treatment:** Clean comic balloons. Smooth edges. High readability. Avoid overly stylized shapes.

**Placement:** Always inside safe area. Balloon tails should clearly indicate speaker without crossing critical art.

**Tone:** Human, restrained, grounded.

---

### 2. Radio / Comms Balloons

Use for MITE II crew traffic, dispatch calls, and module-to-cab communication.

**Primary pages:** Pages 9-21.

**Treatment:** Distinct from normal balloons. Suggested options:

- Slightly squared balloon corners.
- Thin broken or technical outline.
- Small radio tag or speaker label when needed.
- Subtle waveform or comms bracket styling.

**Examples:**

- `Tenet Radio:`
- `Rudy Radio:`
- `Ildi Radio:`
- `Dispatch Radio:`

**Placement:** Inside safe area. Avoid stacking too many comms balloons vertically unless the page is intentionally procedural.

**Rule:** Radio traffic should feel like work communication, not narration wearing a headset.

---

### 3. Memory Dialogue

Use for remembered Rob lines or emotionally overlaid dialogue.

**Primary pages:** Page 6, Page 14, Page 22.

**Treatment:** Visually distinct from present-tense speech. Suggested options:

- Softer balloon edge.
- Slightly faded outline.
- Caption-like balloon if spoken memory is not tied to a visible mouth.
- Small label only if timeline confusion is possible.

**Placement:** Inside safe area. Keep memory dialogue sparse.

**Rule:** Memory dialogue is not ghost dialogue. Avoid making Rob look like a literal apparition unless a page is explicitly labeled as symbolic art.

---

### 4. Field-Report Captions

Use for dry, pressure-haunted narration and route-document texture.

**Primary pages:** Pages 3, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 13, 16, 20, 21, 22.

**Treatment:** Rectangular or clipped-corner caption boxes. Should feel like field notes with human residue.

**Examples:**

- `Field packets do not guarantee field truth.`
- `The route stopped lying quietly.`
- `Rob did not leave him an answer. Rob left him a route.`

**Placement:** Inside safe area. Captions should not cover key facial acting or evidence details.

**Rule:** Captions should sharpen meaning, not repeat the art.

---

### 5. Packet / Interface Text

Use for mission packet excerpts, authorization chains, route display text, diagnostic overlays, and source contradictions.

**Primary pages:** Pages 2, 7, 8, 13, 16, 18, 19, 22, 23.

**Treatment:** Monospaced or clean technical lettering. Use larger type for story-critical text.

**Story-critical packet text includes:**

- MITE II packet label.
- Horus approval stamp.
- `INFRASTRUCTURE STATUS PER PACKET: NONE`
- route display contradictions.
- diagnostic warnings that affect decisions.

**Texture-only packet text may be smaller**, but it should not be required reading.

**Placement:** Critical text inside safe area. Decorative packet fragments may approach trim or bleed.

**Rule:** If the reader must read it, letter it like dialogue. If it is texture, let it be texture.

---

### 6. Diagnostic Text

Use for Red Umbrielor systems, Mod strain, route displays, and equipment readings.

**Primary pages:** Pages 5, 8, 10, 13, 16, 18, 19.

**Treatment:** Technical and crisp. Avoid tiny unreadable diagnostic clutter.

**Examples:**

- `MD-8 PRESSURE UNEVEN`
- `STATUS: STABILIZING`
- `COUPLING STRAIN: RISING`
- `TRACK LOAD: UNEVEN`

**Placement:** Inside safe area if story-critical. May be embedded in art if legibility is preserved.

**Rule:** Diagnostics should reveal operational pressure, not decorate screens with gibberish.

---

### 7. Sound Effects / SFX

Use for machine sounds, environment, signal glitches, and radio texture.

**Primary pages:** Pages 3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 13, 17, 19, 21.

**Treatment:** Integrated with art but readable. SFX should feel mechanical, cold, and material.

**Examples:**

- `KTTK`
- `KUNK. TICK. WHRRRRR.`
- `KLANG. KNNNK.`
- `GRRNNNNK`
- `kshh... tik... tik... kshh...`

**Placement:** Essential SFX inside safe area. Decorative or environmental SFX may drift closer to trim if not required for story.

**Rule:** SFX should carry texture. Do not over-letter every machine noise.

---

### 8. Poster / Ad Text

Use for Page 23 and future in-world back-matter.

**Primary page:** Page 23.

**Treatment:** Large, readable, poster-like typography. It should feel like an in-world safety/recruitment warning, not a normal panel page.

**Required headline:**

`THINGS ARE DIFFERENT ON TRAVERSE.`

**Required production note:**

`Keep text/logos inside the safe area (0.25 in from trim). Backgrounds may extend to bleed.`

**Placement:** All poster text and production notes inside safe area.

**Rule:** Page 23 can be stylish, but it must still work as a print-safe ad/template page.

---

### 9. Cover / Back-Cover Typography

Use for Pages 1 and 24.

**Primary pages:** Page 1, Page 24.

**Page 1 cover text:**

- `MERIDIAN PRIME`
- `THE ROUTE TO IT`
- tagline / issue info / creator names

**Page 24 teaser text:**

- `Rob did not die because he lost.`
- `He died because he chose.`
- optional `MERIDIAN PRIME WILL RETURN`

**Placement:** All title, logo, tagline, creator, website, and teaser text inside safe area.

**Rule:** Background art can go feral into bleed. Words stay civilized inside the fence.

## Page-Specific Lettering Risks

### Page 1: Front Cover

**Risk:** Too many symbolic elements plus title/tagline can crowd the cover.

**Rule:** Prioritize title/logo, tagline, Tenet, Red Umbrielor, and formation. Keep all text inside safe area.

---

### Page 2: Credits / Packet Artifact

**Risk:** Credits become unreadable over packet texture.

**Rule:** Credits remain clean. Packet texture must stay low-contrast behind live text.

---

### Page 3: The House Ten Built

**Risk:** Captions over-explain the mood.

**Rule:** Let the house and Rob's body language carry silence. Captions should feel field-document sharp, not novelistic overflow.

---

### Page 4: Rob / Horus Confrontation

**Risk:** Dialogue density can smother facial acting.

**Rule:** Preserve breathing room around `Bullshit.` Rob's hidden tremor should be visible.

---

### Page 7: Lindy Packet Handoff

**Risk:** Banter stacks too high before the Horus reveal.

**Rule:** Keep balloons compact. Let Lindy's watchfulness in Panel 5 create the dread runway.

---

### Page 8: Horus Approval Stamp

**Risk:** Approval text loses impact if too small or surrounded by clutter.

**Rule:** The Horus approval line must be one of the crispest text moments in the issue.

---

### Page 10: Mod Train Montage

**Risk:** Montage becomes a labeled equipment catalog.

**Rule:** Use captions sparingly and let motion connect the details. Do not crowd every panel with labels.

---

### Page 13: Ghost Handshake

**Risk:** Ghost-signal gutter motif obscures dialogue or interface text.

**Rule:** Signal can creep through gutters and borders, but dialogue and story-critical interface text stay readable.

---

### Page 20: Hidden Formation Reveal

**Risk:** Lettering weakens the splash.

**Rule:** Use minimal captions. Let the formation breathe. Do not cover the central silhouette/shape.

---

### Page 21: Footprints

**Risk:** Over-lettering kills dread.

**Rule:** Keep almost silent. Preserve `Nobody explains it before we preserve it.` No extra explanatory captions.

---

### Page 22: Recognition Beat

**Risk:** Montage becomes a lore diagram.

**Rule:** Four emotional elements only: Horus approval line, under-seat pistol, formation/footprint, Rob memory. Keep final captions clean.

---

### Page 23: Warning Poster / Ad Page

**Risk:** In-world poster language conflicts with production note readability.

**Rule:** Required production note stays inside safe area and remains readable. Poster headline can dominate, but not at the cost of template clarity.

---

### Page 24: Back Cover

**Risk:** Final caption too close to trim or too small against cold landscape.

**Rule:** Place caption inside safe area with enough contrast and breathing room. Let the House image extend to bleed.

## Balloon and Caption Density Guide

| Page Type | Recommended Text Density |
|---|---|
| Cover / back cover | Very low |
| Credits / packet artifact | Medium, structured |
| Rob/Horus confrontation | Medium-low |
| Cold start / cab scenes | Medium |
| Crew intro pages | Medium |
| Technical montage | Medium-low |
| Formation reveal | Very low |
| Footprint page | Very low |
| Recognition montage | Low |
| Poster/ad page | Medium, poster-structured |

### Maximum Practical Limits

These are not hard laws, but they keep pages readable:

- Avoid more than 6 balloons/captions on a normal interior page.
- Avoid more than 3 text elements on splash-heavy pages.
- Avoid more than 2 captions on Page 21.
- Avoid more than 4 total text elements on Page 20.
- If a panel already has interface text, reduce dialogue in that panel.
