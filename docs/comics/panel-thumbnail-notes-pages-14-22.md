## Purpose

This document translates Pages 14-22 of the second-draft script into rough layout logic.

Pages 14-22 establish:

1. Grandfather Ten's pistol as inheritance, weight, and route authority without explanation.
2. Horus realizing Rob already moved the thing he came to take.
3. Paper wrongness becoming physical field danger.
4. Forest, Rudy, and Ildi continuing as terrain, machine, and signal truth.
5. The hidden formation as the issue's visual bombshell.
6. Footprints as quiet impossible evidence.
7. Page 22 as emotional recognition, not lore solution.

This is the issue's pressure cooker. The layouts must keep awe, grief, and danger legible without turning the climax into a lore traffic jam.

## Global Thumbnail Rules

### Print Geometry

Use the Mixam framework measurements:

- Final canvas including bleed: **6.94 in x 10.49 in**
- Trim: **6.69 in x 10.24 in**
- Bleed: **0.125 in** on all sides
- Safe area: **0.25 in inside trim**
- Important elements at least **0.375 in from final canvas edge**

### Thumbnail Priorities

For each page, rough in:

- safe-area box
- bleed boundary
- major panel shapes
- main reading path
- text zones
- focal object / face / terrain / evidence
- lettering risk areas
- whether the page needs quiet, compression, or spectacle

### Section Rhythm

Pages 14-22 should move like this:

1. **Page 14:** memory handoff, warm but heavy.
2. **Page 15:** Horus pressure, cold and personal.
3. **Page 16:** route display contradiction, field wrongness begins.
4. **Page 17:** Forest reads terrain teeth.
5. **Page 18:** Rudy measures machine survival.
6. **Page 19:** Ildi triangulates signal toward the blank.
7. **Page 20:** formation reveal, maximum visual scale.
8. **Page 21:** footprints, almost silent dread.
9. **Page 22:** emotional click, four pressure elements only.

### Climax Design Rule

Do not explain the world while the world is opening.

Let the reader feel:

- the route lied
- Rob prepared Tenet
- Horus arrived too late
- the land is wrong
- the signal is old
- the formation should not be there
- something used it

Do not solve why.

## Page 14: Pistol Memory / The Last Traverse

**Page Role:** Interior page 12

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** 4 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Memory page. Rob transfers the pistol as weight, trust, and time without gun glamour or full explanation.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Warm-memory grid with controlled intimacy:

```text
Panel 1: Red Umbrielor near The House Ten Built / memory establishing
Panel 2: Rob unwraps oilcloth / adjusts Tenet's hand position
Panel 3: pistol between them, responsibility not weapon pose
Panel 4: transfer into Tenet's hands / Rob's line lands
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Wide top panel.

**Focal Point:** Red Umbrielor parked near The House Ten Built, cold outside, warmer cab interior implied.

**Text Zone:** memory captions in quiet negative space.

**Mood:** goodbye before Tenet knows it is goodbye.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Medium panel, hands and oilcloth.

**Focal Point:** Rob's hands unwrapping bundle and physically adjusting Tenet's grip.

**Text Zone:** Rob/Tenet dialogue. Keep balloons away from hands.

**Avoid:** gun glamour, hero-prop lighting, action-poster framing.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Close object panel.

**Focal Point:** pistol resting across Rob's palms like responsibility.

**Text Zone:** short exchange: `It is not just a gun.` / `No.`

**Rule:** The pistol is old, cared for, and heavy. It is not sexy. It is not brandished.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Bottom panel, emotional transfer.

**Focal Point:** wrapped pistol entering Tenet's hands, Rob's face carrying warmth and pain.

**Text Zone:** Rob's `You still have time.` and `For the question to catch up.` need room, but avoid making Rob oracle-like through overly dramatic lettering.

### Page Rhythm

The page should feel like Rob teaching Tenet how to hold a question without naming it.

### Lettering Risk

Too many mystery captions would over-explain the pistol. Let the object and hands do the work.

## Page 15: Horus Presses Rob

**Page Role:** Interior page 13

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Horus realizes Rob already moved the thing he came to take. The empty table lands before the dialogue.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Controlled confrontation, mirroring Page 4 but colder:

```text
Panel 1: Horus sees empty table / missing oilcloth space
Panel 2: Horus close-up, avoids saying Tenet's name
Panel 3: Rob pain beat / refusal of weakness
Panel 4: old photo behind Horus / Ten, Rob, Horus
Panel 5: Rob final route line
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium-wide table panel.

**Focal Point:** the empty space where the oilcloth bundle was.

**Text Zone:** `Where is it?` after the visual beat. Let the silence hit first.

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Close on Horus.

**Focal Point:** control cracking; he almost says Tenet's name, then chooses `him`.

**Text Zone:** Horus balloon small and tight.

**Mood:** personal avoidance wearing official control.

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Medium Rob panel.

**Focal Point:** Rob coughs/tightens, hand gripping table, refuses weakness.

**Text Zone:** keep around face but not over eyes/hands.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Slightly wider panel.

**Focal Point:** Horus leaning closer, old photograph of Tenet I, Rob, and Horus visible behind him.

**Text Zone:** `Ten trusted me too.` / `Ten liked you. That was never the same thing.`

**Rule:** The photograph should be readable enough to hurt, not so dominant it becomes exposition.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Larger bottom panel.

**Focal Point:** Rob's warm smile, thin but victorious; Horus too late.

**Text Zone:** `You came for an answer. I sent you a route.` must have clean space.

### Page Rhythm

Horus arrived with authority. Rob already spent the truth.

### Lettering Risk

Do not overstack Rob zingers. The final route line should be the page's blade.

## Page 17: Forest Reads the Teeth in the Land

**Page Role:** Interior page 15

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference / Promo Exaggeration if visualized aggressively

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Forest's terrain truth becomes central. Pressure ridges and growth rollers become live hazard.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Terrain diagnosis sequence:

```text
Panel 1: Forest posture changes / command to stop
Panel 2: exterior feed pressure ridge
Panel 3: Red Umbrielor brakes / Mods sway
Panel 4: Forest reads GPR + land
Panel 5: wide exterior, land bristles
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium character panel.

**Focal Point:** Forest no longer lounging. Tail angled, clawed hand over display.

**Text Zone:** `Stop short of the next marker.`

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Close exterior feed panel.

**Focal Point:** pressure ridge where passable surface should be.

**Text Zone:** `Because that marker is lying by about thirty meters.`

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Dynamic wide panel.

**Focal Point:** Red Umbrielor braking, tracks biting, Mods swaying within tolerance.

**Text Zone:** Rudy radio `Easy, easy, easy.`

**SFX:** `GRRNNNNK` integrated with track movement, not over vehicles.

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Split-feel diagnostic panel.

**Focal Point:** Forest reading both GPR and land. Display + exterior image.

**Text Zone:** pressure ridges / growth rollers lines.

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Wide bottom panel.

**Focal Point:** land ahead bristling, Red Umbrielor dwarfed by terrain scale.

**Text Zone:** `The land has teeth where the map drew a line.`

### Page Rhythm

Forest sees the bite before the bite. His authority should come from diagnosis, not volume.

### Lettering Risk

Do not let terrain terminology become textbook. Pair every term with an image that makes it obvious.

## Page 18: Rudy Measures Survival

**Page Role:** Interior page 16

**Adaptation Label:** Canon Reference / Adaptation Choice

**Panel Count:** 5 panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Ground danger in machinery: couplings, load, tracks, brakes, weight, and ugly math.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Operational tension:

```text
Panel 1: Rudy diagnostics / amber warnings gather
Panel 2: Tenet asks if they can crawl it
Panel 3: exterior Mod train side view / machine can survive ugly
Panel 4: Tenet marks slow approach path
Panel 5: Rudy braced as train creeps forward
```

### Panel Notes

#### Panel 1

**Shape:** Medium diagnostic panel.

**Focal Point:** Rudy braced in mechanic module, warning lights multiplying but not catastrophic.

**Critical Text:**

- `COUPLING STRAIN: RISING`
- `TRACK LOAD: UNEVEN`
- `MOD SWAY: WITHIN LIMITS`

#### Panel 2

**Shape:** Cab/Rudy comms panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet with packet clipped where it can be glared at.

**Text Zone:** `Can we crawl it?` / `Define crawl like a man with insurance.`

#### Panel 3

**Shape:** Wide exterior side panel.

**Focal Point:** Mod train vulnerable against ridge lines and roller shadows.

**Text Zone:** Rudy line:

`Machine can survive ugly. It cannot survive impossible twice.`

#### Panel 4

**Shape:** Route display / hand panel.

**Focal Point:** Tenet overriding official route with field-noted corrections.

**Text Zone:** `Then we do ugly once.` / `Forest, talk me through teeth.`

#### Panel 5

**Shape:** Bottom close/medium panel.

**Focal Point:** Rudy braced, hand tight on grab rail, smile without comfort.

**Text Zone:** `I hate when leadership makes sense.`

### Page Rhythm

Ugly is survivable. Impossible is a debt they cannot pay twice.

### Lettering Risk

Diagnostics must be readable but not so large they steal Rudy's expression.

## Page 20: Hidden Formation Reveal

**Page Role:** Interior page 18

**Adaptation Label:** Promo Exaggeration / Comic-Only Continuity

**Panel Count:** Mostly splash page with 2 inset panels

**Thumbnail Goal:** Major visual bombshell. The hidden formation appears and the official map fails completely.

### Recommended Panel Layout

Splash-first page:

```text
[ MAIN SPLASH: Red Umbrielor clears rollers, formation revealed ]

Inset 1: Tenet reaction / Log visual
Inset 2: Ildi monitor / ghost signal steadies
```

### Main Splash Notes

**Shape:** Full-page or near full-page splash, with two small insets placed where they do not damage scale.

**Focal Point:** hidden formation as route-scar / wound in the land.

**Composition:** Red Umbrielor should be visible enough to provide scale, but the formation owns the page.

**Visual Language:**

- vast
- old
- cracked
- scarred open by pressure
- frost, crystal pressure, old shadow, signal distortion
- not natural enough to be geology
- not clear enough to be named

**Avoid:** fantasy portal, glowing magic gate, obvious alien temple, clean spaceship wreck.

### Caption Placement

Use only three short captions:

- `No marker.`
- `No lease note.`
- `No official name.`

Place them inside safe area, preferably in quiet sky/terrain space, not over the formation's central silhouette.

### Inset 1

**Focal Point:** Tenet in cab, recalculating danger and grief.

**Text Zone:** `Log visual.` small.

### Inset 2

**Focal Point:** Ildi monitor loses clean map layer, ghost-signal motif spikes and steadies.

**Text Zone:** `It's answering us.`

### Page Rhythm

Let the page roar by not talking much.

### Lettering Risk

Too much lettering kills the reveal. This page should feel huge enough that the captions look small.

### Panel Notes

## Artist Notes for Pages 14-22

### Pistol / Grandfather Ten's Object

The pistol is an inheritance object, not an action prop.

Show:

- weight
- care
- age
- transfer
- Rob's practicality
- Tenet's uncertainty

Avoid:

- heroic gun pose
- barrel-forward framing
- fetish lighting
- combat implication

### Horus and Rob

The House scenes should feel intimate and personal.

Rob is physically fading but morally sharp.

Horus is official, late, and trying not to show the wound.

### Terrain Wrongness

Pressure ridges and growth rollers should feel dangerous and specific, not generic bad terrain.

They are not just obstacles. They are the land contradicting the paper.

### Hidden Formation

The formation must be visually unforgettable but not explanatory.

It should feel like:

- route-scar
- wound in land
- old pressure
- policy absence made visible

Do not make it a solved portal.

### Footprint Evidence

The footprint must be legible, ambiguous, and unsettling.

It is evidence, not a monster reveal.

### Page 22 Recognition

The page should feel like four pieces clicking together inside Tenet, not a narrated plot chart.
