## Cover Objective

The front cover must sell the Issue 01 hype route in one image:

- Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289 as the giant visual anchor.
- Tenet Nocsid III as a field operator, not a superhero.
- The hidden formation as mystery, danger, and route wrongness.
- Rob Holliday's death-shadow as emotional pressure.
- Horus Skelton as official wrongness, not cartoon evil.
- Aeonos / Àæonos as symbolic, non-explanatory background texture only if using the maximalist variant.

Core cover promise:

> The job was legal. The map was dead. The route answered anyway.

## Required Visual Anchors

### Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289

Must feel:

- massive
- scarred
- frost-caked
- industrial
- field-modified
- loved through complaint
- dangerous by utility, not sleekness

Required details:

- patched red panels
- visible weather-worn `289`
- heavy treads / quadtrack mass
- old lights, frost, exhaust, field grime
- no clean corporate spaceship or generic sci-fi tank design

---

### Tenet Nocsid III

Must read as:

- experienced field operator
- practical
- tired but controlled
- grounded in work
- not a chosen-one pose
- not a superhero silhouette

Suggested pose:

Tenet stands small in the foreground or near Red Umbrielor's scale, holding or carrying the MITE II packet / route document. The pistol is implied by coat line, under-jacket weight, holster silhouette, or stance, not brandished.

---

### Hidden Formation

Must feel:

- forbidden
- partially concealed
- massive enough to humble the machine
- not clearly named
- not fully explained
- route-scar / wound in the land

Avoid making it a clean portal, fantasy gate, alien temple, or obvious spaceship wreck.

---

### Rob Holliday

If included:

- white hair swept to one side
- square glasses
- mustache connected to goatee
- curled eyebrow tips
- overweight but not obese
- warm smile
- salmon polo, if visible

Use Rob as emotional pressure, not a literal ghost. He may appear as a faint face in cold haze, reflection, packet texture, or backlit memory shape.

---

### Horus Skelton

If included:

- controlled
- official
- dark procedural presence
- tragic old-friend pressure
- not a moustache-twirling villain

Use Horus as stamp, silhouette, official shadow, or cold bureaucratic geometry.

---

### Aeonos / Àæonos Symbolism

Use only in maximalist or symbolic versions.

Must remain:

- non-explanatory
- mirrored but not identical
- atmospheric
- secondary to Red Umbrielor, Tenet, and the formation

Do not depict the full cosmology as solved cover lore.

## Variant A: Hype Maximalist Cover

**Adaptation Label:** Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

### Best Use

Pitch hype, internal excitement, select-reader teaser, dramatic promo image.

### Art Direction

Vertical comic cover. Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289 dominates the lower half, angled toward a vast hidden formation under a bruised alien dawn. Tenet stands small in the foreground with the MITE II packet in hand. The pistol is implied by his stance or coat line, not drawn as a weapon pose.

The formation rises ahead like a route-scar and wound in the land, partly obscured by pressure weather, frost, and old signal distortion. Behind or within the cold haze, Rob Holliday's warm, sad face appears faintly as memory pressure. Horus Skelton appears as a dark official silhouette or approval-stamp geometry. In the far upper background, mirrored but non-identical world-shapes suggest Aeonos / Àæonos without explanation.

Mood: industrial grief, cold route danger, official wrongness, hidden-world pressure.

### Prompt

Create a vertical US standard comic cover for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**. A massive scarred red industrial MMS Quadtrack vehicle, **Red Umbrielor 289**, faces a vast hidden formation under a bruised alien dawn. The vehicle is frost-caked, patched, field-modified, heavy, old, and practical, with visible weathered number **289**. Tenet Nocsid III stands small in the foreground as an experienced field operator, holding a MITE II route packet; he is grounded, controlled, and not superheroic. The pistol is only implied by silhouette or coat weight, never brandished. The hidden formation ahead looks like a route-scar and wound in the land, mysterious and dangerous, not a fantasy portal. In the cold haze behind the formation, faint symbolic elements suggest Rob Holliday's memory, Horus Skelton's official pressure, and mirrored-but-not-identical Aeonos / Àæonos world imagery. Diesel-punk industrial survival mood, cold pressure weather, frost, signal distortion, official packet fragments, cinematic but grounded, print-comic cover composition, room for title at top and tagline inside safe area.

### Text Placement

- Title/logo at top inside safe area.
- Tagline lower third or upper middle inside safe area.
- Creator names/issue info at bottom inside safe area.
- Do not place Rob/Horus/Aeonos symbolism where title will cover it.

## Variant B: Clean Publisher Pitch Cover

**Adaptation Label:** Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

### Best Use

Publisher pitch, cleaner sample PDF, less cluttered first impression.

### Art Direction

Focus on three elements only:

1. Tenet
2. Red Umbrielor 289
3. Hidden formation

No Rob face, no Horus silhouette, no Aeonos / Àæonos imagery unless barely suggested through atmosphere.

This version should read instantly from thumbnail size.

### Prompt

Create a clean vertical comic cover for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**. In a cold industrial alien landscape, **Red Umbrielor 289**, a massive scarred red quadtrack field vehicle, faces a hidden formation at dawn. The machine is old, practical, patched, frost-caked, and field-modified, not sleek or futuristic. Tenet Nocsid III stands in the foreground as a tired but controlled field operator holding a route packet. He is small against the machine and the formation. The formation ahead reads as a mysterious route-scar in the land, not a portal or fantasy gate. Strong silhouette, readable composition, dramatic cold light, dieselpunk survival texture, no lore clutter. Leave clear safe-area space for title/logo at top, tagline, issue info, and creator names.

### Text Placement

- Strong title zone at top.
- Tagline can sit beneath title or above Tenet.
- Lower safe area reserved for issue/creator information.

### Variant B Front-Blade Composition Lock

**Adaptation Label:** Sandbox Production Direction / Non-Canon Promo Art Composition Rule

For the clean publisher pitch cover, prefer the head-on / near-head-on composition when the front blade needs to read correctly.

Locked staging:

- Tenet stands in front of the blade, holding the MITE II packet.
- The matte-black rear face of Red Umbrielor's front blade sits behind Tenet and spans the lower foreground.
- Red Umbrielor looms behind the blade.
- The tractor body should be mostly front-facing or front-left three-quarter, not side-profile.
- The blade is mounted directly in front of the tractor, not on the side.
- The viewer sees the back / rear face of the blade, not the front cutting face.
- The rear face of the blade is matte black.
- Black hydraulic lift arms run from the rear of the blade back to mounting points below the front engine compartment, in front of the front tracks.
- The lift arms protrude slightly outward from the chassis so they clear the engine compartment when actuated.
- The hidden formation remains distant beyond the machine and landscape.

Marking rule for this head-on cover composition:

- Do **not** show `Red Umbrielor` or `289` on the front face of the tractor.
- `Red Umbrielor` and `289` cannot be cleanly read from a head-on view because they are side markings only.
- If a side surface is barely visible, a partial faded glimpse may appear, but it should not become a clear front-facing label.
- The front grille of Red Umbrielor is black mesh.
- The black mesh grille should carry no large number, name, logo, or decorative label.

Avoid in Variant B cover generations:

- side-profile tractor staging when blade placement is critical
- blade appearing attached to the side of 289
- blade flank reading as the main blade face
- readable `289` on the front grille
- readable `Red Umbrielor` on the front grille or front hood
- billboard-style front labels created by the image model

## Variant C: Artifact / Field Packet Cover

**Adaptation Label:** Adaptation Choice / Candidate Canon for packet design language

### Best Use

Limited pitch packet, alternate cover, production mockup, in-world artifact presentation.

### Art Direction

The cover appears as a corrupted NCI / World Works route packet rather than a conventional painted scene.

Elements:

- clipped field document structure
- Red Umbrielor silhouette or blueprint
- route-line diagram going wrong
- Horus approval stamp
- MITE II packet label
- ghost signal line crossing gutters / folds
- formation hinted as a blank/black region on map

### Prompt

Create a vertical comic cover designed like a corrupted NCI / World Works field packet for **Meridian Prime: The Route To It**. The design is a print-comic cover but styled as an in-world route-access document: pale field-paper texture, clipped forms, route grid, redacted bars, approval stamps, cold blue interface traces, and worn industrial marks. Include a silhouette or technical outline of **Red Umbrielor 289**, a heavy scarred quadtrack field vehicle. Show the MITE II proposed route line crossing into a blank unmapped region where the hidden formation is implied but not shown. Include a visible but stylized Horus Skelton approval stamp as procedural wrongness. Add subtle ghost-signal linework threading through the design. The result should feel official, corrupted, cold, and dangerous. Leave safe-area space for title, tagline, issue info, and creator names.

### Text Placement

- Title/logo can be integrated into packet header.
- Tagline may appear as stamped warning or redacted note.
- Creator credits can appear as document footer.
