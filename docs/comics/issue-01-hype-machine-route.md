## Core Hype Premise

**Rob's death was a map.**

The hype comic treats Issue 01 as a forbidden route document rather than a normal first chapter. It intercuts Rob Holliday's final refusal, Tenet Nocsid III's cold start, and the MITE II route discovering that the proposed extension corridor is not just a job. It is the next door.

Reader-facing engine:

> Tenet thinks he accepted a job.  
> Rob had already sent him to a door.

---

## Primary Threads

### 1. Past Route: Rob's Final Refusal

Rob Holliday is at The House Ten Built, physically near the end, but still clear enough to refuse Horus Skelton.

Rob's death should feel like a route, not a single event:

- old friendship
- betrayal
- suspicion
- poisoning
- preparation
- pistol handoff
- refusal
- final victory through timing

Rob should remain warm, blunt, funny, and judgment-rich. His final resistance is not spectacle. It is refusal.

Signature final beat:

> Bullshit.

### 2. Present Route: Tenet's Cold Start

Tenet begins before dawn with Red Umbrielor.

Required visual anchors:

- cold scarred steel
- MD-8 system complaints
- dead defrost
- turkey roaster
- empty passenger seat
- under-seat compartment
- Grandfather Ten's pistol
- Lindy Nocsid delivering the MITE II packet
- Horus Skelton's approval stamp

Tenet is not a chosen-one figure. He is a field operator accepting work that has become too clean to trust.

### 3. Secret Route: Door Under the World

The comic visually teases the hidden formation, ghost handshake, footprints, and Core-adjacent mystery earlier and harder than the novella would.

Allowed teaser imagery:

- signal lines threading through panel gutters
- pressure ridges rising like a bad answer
- growth rollers obscuring what official maps deny
- black flags bending wrong
- footprints in impossible terrain
- symbolic double-world imagery for Aeonos / Àæonos
- back-cover image of The House Ten Built with one light still burning

Do not present the teaser imagery as clean main-canon exposition. It is hype-comic dramatization.

## 24-Page Hype Structure

### Page 1: Front Cover

Red Umbrielor faces a vast cracked formation under a bruised alien dawn. Tenet stands small before it with the MITE II packet and the inheritance weight of the pistol implied.

Cover label:

**Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art**

Suggested title:

# Meridian Prime: The Route To It

Suggested tagline:

> The job was legal. The map was dead. The route answered anyway.

### Page 2: Credits / Corrupted Field Packet

Credits page styled as an NCI / World Works field packet with light corruption, approval stamps, packet fragments, and safe-area production text.

### Pages 3-4: Rob at The House Ten Built

Rob is sick, smiling, and already winning. Horus arrives too late. Their old friendship should be visible under the threat.

### Pages 5-6: Tenet Cold Start

Red Umbrielor wakes before dawn. MD-8 complains. The turkey roaster rattles. Rob's empty passenger seat lands as grief through absence.

### Page 7: Lindy and the Packet

Lindy arrives with the MITE II packet. Sibling friction, dark humor, and family bruises should carry the scene.

### Page 8: Horus Approval Stamp

The approval line becomes the first major comic page-turn.

Tenet line:

> What the-

### Pages 9-10: MITE II Rolls Out

Red Umbrielor and the Mod train become the hero machine-organism: tractor, Mods, fuel, lights, frost, crew, and route procedure moving as one survival body.

### Pages 11-12: Three Truth Gauges

Introduce Rudy Calder, Kavo "Forest" Rill, and Ildi Vey through action.

- Rudy = machine truth
- Forest = terrain truth
- Ildi = signal truth

### Page 13: Ghost Handshake

Ildi detects old signal / ghost handshake where the packet says infrastructure should not exist. Let the signal visually invade the panel design.

### Page 14: Pistol Memory

Intercut Rob giving Tenet Grandfather Ten's pistol during The Last Traverse.

The pistol is inheritance, key, test, trust marker, and route authority. Do not reduce it to a weapon.

### Page 15: Horus Wants the Old Route Truth

Intercut Horus pressing Rob for records, access, coordinates, or confirmation. Rob refuses without explaining everything.

### Page 16: The Land Starts Behaving Wrong

MITE II enters terrain wrongness. Official map language begins to visually fail.

### Page 17: Forest Reads the Teeth in the Land

Pressure ridges and growth rollers appear where the route should be passable.

### Page 18: Rudy Measures Survival

Rudy reads the Mod train's strain and confirms the machinery can survive some things, but not a route that keeps changing its mind.

### Page 19: Ildi Tracks the Signal Toward the Formation

The ghost handshake becomes a direction rather than just an anomaly.

### Page 20: Hidden Formation Reveal

The formation appears as the comic's major visual bombshell.

Label the sequence as **Promo Exaggeration** if it reveals more than the novella would this early.

### Page 21: Footprints

The footprints make the formation impossible to dismiss as geology.

This page should be quiet, eerie, and devastating.

### Page 22: Rob's Death and MITE II Align

Tenet realizes the present job and Rob's death are part of the same route.

Meaning:

> Rob did not leave him an answer. Rob left him a route.

### Page 23: Inside Back Cover / Ad Page

In-world ad or warning poster:

> Things are different On Traverse.

This may double as an NCI recruitment / safety / warning page.

### Page 24: Back Cover Teaser

The House Ten Built stands tiny against the cold with one light still burning.

Suggested caption:

> Rob did not die because he lost.  
> He died because he chose.

## Tone Mandate

The comic should feel like:

- dieselpunk field survival
- family grief under procedure
- official paperwork hiding blood
- machinery with memory
- signal horror without mysticism
- cosmic pressure glimpsed through work culture
- a route document that should not have been printed

---

## Guardrails Even In Hype Mode

Even with lore restrictions removed for this sandbox route, preserve these character truths:

- Tenet remains practical, experienced, and field-grounded.
- Rob remains warm, funny, blunt, and judgment-rich.
- Horus remains tragic and personal, not random evil.
- Red Umbrielor remains Tenet's signature machine.
- The pistol remains more than a weapon.
- NCI remains family-rooted, practical, capable, deniable, and haunted.
- Aeonos / Àæonos distinction should be teased, not collapsed.
- Any invented material remains quarantined until promoted.
