## Core Definition

Traverse Town is the end-of-shift parked layout of MITE II.

It is formed when the Mod trains park into two long parallel rows, leaving a wide tractor alley between them. The arrangement is still visibly a convoy system: Mods remain on their tracked platforms, tongue/drawbar connections remain visible, fuel tails remain attached, and tractors either remain attached during arrival or detach and back into service positions for the night.

Traverse Town must read as:

> a parked working convoy, not a base.

Important rules:

- Traverse Town is not a permanent settlement.
- Traverse Town is not a literal town with a sign.
- `Traverse Town` is a crew / layout term.
- No Traverse Town sign should appear because signs add useless weight.
- Everything visible should feel hauled there, plugged in, folded down, backed into place, or still attached because survival logistics demanded it.

## Two Traverse Town States

Traverse Town has two related layout states:

1. **State A: Attached Arrival Layout**
2. **State B: Detached Service Layout**

The real-world traverse reference clarified an important workflow: Traverse Town first appears while the tractors are still attached. The town shape already exists before detachment. Detachment is a secondary service-phase action, not the act that creates the town.

Core rule:

> Traverse Town is a parked working convoy, not a base.

### Why This Matters

Without the two-state distinction, art prompts tend to collapse the layout into a generic camp or mining base.

The two states preserve the real machine choreography:

- First, the convoy parks into town formation.
- Then, if needed, tractors detach and back into service positions.
- The central alley exists in both states.
- The Mod rows create the town shape, not the detached tractors.

## State A: Attached Arrival Layout

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

### Definition

State A is the freshly-arrived end-of-shift layout where the Mod rows have been parked into town formation, the central alley is visible, and the tractors remain attached to the heads of their respective rows.

This is the moment when Traverse Town looks most like a convoy that has just become a town.

### State A Visual Rules

- Two long parallel red Mod rows already exist.
- The central alley is already established.
- The alley is wide enough for a tractor to drive through.
- Tractors are still attached to the heads of their Mod trains.
- All Mod segments remain connected by tongues/drawbars.
- Fuel tails remain attached at the backs of the train rows.
- Bladders are not detached into camp pallets.
- Mod-mounted lights may be on.
- No freestanding street lights.
- No Traverse Town sign.
- No permanent base infrastructure.
- This state should feel like a convoy that has just parked, not a camp that was built.


## Mod Requirements

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

Mods are:

- red
- modded double-wide 45-foot high-cube containers
- mounted on platforms with four unpowered straight tracks
- tongue/drawbar connected
- equipped with derricks
- equipped with foldable access stairs
- fitted with Mod-mounted work lights
- equipped with ports for heavy triplex umbilical connections
- not self-propelled
- not buildings detached from platforms


## Tractor Requirements

### 409

409 must be shown as:

- red
- NCI-modded crystal groomer
- no name
- pulls nothing
- backed in in State B
- 20-foot GPR boom remains attached
- boom is blade-mounted
- boom has GPR unit at leading end
- boom is supported by 8-foot mast, pulley, and electric winch cable

409 must not be shortened, duplicated, stripped of its boom, or converted into a generic utility crawler.

### 289 Red Umbrielor

289 Red Umbrielor must be shown as:

- MMS Quadtrack 580
- call number 289
- attached to its train in State A
- backed in and plugged into Shed in State B
- not duplicated or shortened
- retaining heavy tractor scale
- visibly distinct from 409 and 287

289 should not become a generic tank, snowcat, or small crawler.

### 287 Discovery

287 Discovery must be shown as:

- used MT-style crawler
- front winch
- red knuckle boom / crane
- `DISCOVERY` windshield decal from original owner
- verbal nickname Tight Tracks, not visibly painted
- attached to its train in State A
- backed in and plugged into Shed in State B
- not duplicated or shortened
- visually distinct from 409 and Red Umbrielor

## Aquifer Drill

**Formal term:** Aquifer Drill

**Field phrase:** `Set the tap.`

The Aquifer Drill is:

- permanently attached to kitchen side / tongue area of the Comms/Kitchen Mod
- a Meridian Prime replacement for polar snow-melter logic
- used to access prevalent aquifers beneath or within crystalline regolith
- water-survival infrastructure

The Aquifer Drill is not:

- a standalone drill rig
- 409's GPR boom
- a mining spectacle
- a weapon
- a loose camp prop

### Placement Rule

If shown in Traverse Town art, the Aquifer Drill should remain attached to or integrated with the Comms/Kitchen Mod, preferably near the kitchen side / tongue area.

---

## Lighting

Lighting rules:

- Lights are attached to Mods.
- No freestanding deployed street lights.
- No decorative camp light towers.
- Work lights must look like Mod-mounted infrastructure.

This preserves weight discipline:

> if it is not part of the train, they probably did not haul it.

---

## Fuel Tails / Bladders

Fuel tail rules:

- Bladders stay attached at the back of the Traverse train.
- Each train tail uses:
  - Tool
  - two Magic Carpets side by side
  - four black MD-8 fuel bladders
- Bladders are black.
- Patches are black.
- No warning labels.
- Tool numbers are only small engraving on the tongue.
- Bladder numbers are engraved on hose connectors.
- Do not detach bladders into separate camp pallets unless a specific scene requires service handling.

### Fuel Tail Production Grammar

```text
Mod row
→ Tool
→ Magic Carpet A with two black bladders front-to-back
→ Magic Carpet B with two black bladders front-to-back
```

The two Magic Carpets ride side by side behind the Tool.

Avoid:

- treating Traverse Town as a permanent base
- making it a sci-fi mining camp
- adding a Traverse Town sign
- adding freestanding street lights
- adding decorative light towers
- detaching fuel tails into pallets
- arranging four Mods as loose buildings
- removing tongue/drawbar connections
- removing Mod platforms
- unloading Mods onto ground
- narrowing the alley to pedestrian width
- duplicating tractors
- shortening tractors
- turning 409 into a generic small crawler
- removing 409's 20-foot GPR boom
- making 409 pull cargo
- shrinking 289 Red Umbrielor
- duplicating Red Umbrielor
- duplicating Discovery
- making 287's knuckle boom non-red
- turning Aquifer Drill into a freestanding mining rig
- confusing Aquifer Drill with 409's GPR boom
- adding polar camp identity
- adding snow-base identity
