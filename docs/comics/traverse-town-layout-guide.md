## Core Definition

Traverse Town is the end-of-shift parked layout of MITE II, where the Mod trains remain connected, the four Mods form two long parallel rows, a wide tractor alley is left between them, tractors are backed in on the service side, and the Shed becomes the mechanical plug-in hub.

Traverse Town must read as:

> a parked working convoy, not a base.

Important rules:

- Traverse Town is not a permanent settlement.
- Traverse Town is not a literal town with a sign.
- `Traverse Town` is a crew / layout term.
- No Traverse Town sign should appear because signs add useless weight.
- Everything visible should feel hauled there, plugged in, folded down, or backed into place because survival logistics demanded it.

## Locked Layout Facts

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

1. All Mods are red.
2. Each Mod is a modded double-wide 45-foot high-cube container.
3. Each Mod remains on a platform with four unpowered straight tracks.
4. Mods are not unloaded onto the ground.
5. Mods stay tongue/drawbar attached to each other.
6. Each segment of each tractor train is connected by tongues/drawbars.
7. The bladders remain attached to the back of each Traverse train.
8. The connection order stays fixed.
9. The Mods are parked in two long parallel rows.
10. One side of the alley has two Mods connected end-to-end:
    - Comms/Kitchen Mod
    - Solar/Berthing Mod
11. The other side of the alley has two Mods connected end-to-end:
    - Refrigerator/Storage Mod
    - Shed
12. The central alley is wide enough for a tractor to drive through.
13. A heavy triplex power umbilical is hung between the two Mod rows.
14. Lights are attached to Mods.
15. There are no freestanding deployed street lights.
16. All tractors are backed in.
17. Tractors park on the service side / other side of Traverse Town.
18. Tractors plug into the Shed.
19. 409 remains red.
20. 409 remains the NCI-modded crystal groomer.
21. 409 pulls nothing.
22. 409 keeps its 20-foot GPR boom attached.
23. The Aquifer Drill is permanently attached to the kitchen side / tongue area of the Comms/Kitchen Mod.
24. Field phrase for the Aquifer Drill is: `Set the tap.`

## Travel Mode

```text
409
pulls nothing

289 Red Umbrielor
→ Comms/Kitchen Mod
→ Solar/Berthing Mod
→ Tool
→ twin Magic Carpets
→ four black MD-8 bladders

287 Discovery
→ Refrigerator/Storage Mod
→ Shed
→ Tool
→ twin Magic Carpets
→ four black MD-8 bladders
```

### Travel Mode Notes

- Mods remain in fixed connection order.
- Every segment uses tongue/drawbar logic.
- 409 leads as sensor tractor and pulls nothing.
- 289 pulls life-support / command Mods and its fuel tail.
- 287 pulls storage / repair Mods and its fuel tail.
- Bladders remain attached at the back of each train.

## Parked / Traverse Town Mode

```text
Comms/Kitchen Mod and Solar/Berthing Mod form one long red Mod row.

Refrigerator/Storage Mod and Shed form the other long red Mod row.

A wide tractor alley runs between the two rows.

Tractors are backed in on the service side and plugged into the Shed.

Bladders remain attached to the backs of their train rows.

Aquifer Drill remains permanently attached to the Comms/Kitchen Mod.
```

### Parked Mode Notes

- Mods remain on platforms.
- Foldable access stairs may be deployed.
- Derricks may be available.
- Mod-mounted lights provide work light.
- No freestanding light towers or street lights.
- No decorative Traverse Town signage.
- The Shed acts as the mechanical plug-in hub.

## Mod Requirements

**Adaptation Label:** Candidate Canon / Sandbox Production Decision

Mods are:

- red
- modded double-wide 45-foot high-cube containers
- mounted on platforms with four unpowered straight tracks
- tongue/drawbar connected
- equipped with derricks
- equipped with foldable access stairs
- fitted with Mod-mounted work lights
- equipped with ports for heavy triplex umbilical connections
- not self-propelled
- not buildings detached from platforms

### Mod Layout Rule

The Mods are not four loose buildings.

They are two connected end-to-end rows:

```text
[Comms/Kitchen Mod]--tongue/drawbar--[Solar/Berthing Mod]

[Refrigerator/Storage Mod]--tongue/drawbar--[Shed]
```

## Tractor Requirements

### 409

409 must be shown as:

- red
- NCI-modded crystal groomer
- no name
- pulls nothing
- backed in
- 20-foot GPR boom remains attached
- boom is blade-mounted
- boom has GPR unit at leading end
- boom is supported by 8-foot mast, pulley, and electric winch cable

409 must not be shortened, duplicated, or converted into a generic utility crawler.

### 289 Red Umbrielor

289 Red Umbrielor must be shown as:

- MMS Quadtrack 580
- call number 289
- backed in
- plugged into Shed when parked
- not duplicated or shortened
- retaining heavy tractor scale
- visibly distinct from 409 and 287

289 should not become a generic tank, snowcat, or small crawler.

### 287 Discovery

287 Discovery must be shown as:

- used MT-style crawler
- front winch
- red knuckle boom / crane
- `DISCOVERY` windshield decal from original owner
- verbal nickname Tight Tracks, not visibly painted
- backed in
- plugged into Shed when parked
- not duplicated or shortened
- visually distinct from 409 and Red Umbrielor

## Aquifer Drill

**Formal term:** Aquifer Drill

**Field phrase:** `Set the tap.`

The Aquifer Drill is:

- permanently attached to kitchen side / tongue area of the Comms/Kitchen Mod
- a Meridian Prime replacement for polar snow-melter logic
- used to access prevalent aquifers beneath or within crystalline regolith
- water-survival infrastructure

The Aquifer Drill is not:

- a standalone drill rig
- 409's GPR boom
- a mining spectacle
- a weapon
- a loose camp prop

### Placement Rule

If shown in bird's-eye Traverse Town art, the Aquifer Drill should remain attached to or integrated with the Comms/Kitchen Mod, preferably near the kitchen side / tongue area.

---

## Lighting

Lighting rules:

- Lights are attached to Mods.
- No freestanding deployed street lights.
- No decorative camp light towers.
- Work lights must look like Mod-mounted infrastructure.

This preserves weight discipline:

> if it is not part of the train, they probably did not haul it.

---

## Fuel Tails / Bladders

Fuel tail rules:

- Bladders stay attached at the back of the Traverse train.
- Each train tail uses:
  - Tool
  - two Magic Carpets side by side
  - four black MD-8 fuel bladders
- Bladders are black.
- Patches are black.
- No warning labels.
- Tool numbers are only small engraving on the tongue.
- Bladder numbers are engraved on hose connectors.
- Do not detach bladders into separate camp pallets unless a specific scene requires service handling.

### Fuel Tail Production Grammar

```text
Mod row
→ Tool
→ Magic Carpet A with two black bladders front-to-back
→ Magic Carpet B with two black bladders front-to-back
```

The two Magic Carpets ride side by side behind the Tool.

## Negative Layout / Avoid List

Avoid:

- four loose Mods arranged as separate buildings
- sci-fi mining-base layout
- permanent settlement feel
- Traverse Town sign
- freestanding street lights
- duplicate tractors
- shrunken tractors
- random extra vehicles
- 409 without its 20-foot boom
- 409 pulling a Mod or fuel train
- Red Umbrielor duplicated
- Discovery duplicated
- loose bladders detached from train tails
- bladders shown as cargo pallets
- Mods without tongue/drawbar connections
- Mods floating without platforms
- Mods unloaded onto ground
- narrow pedestrian alley
- Mod lights replaced by light towers
- Aquifer Drill as freestanding rig
- Aquifer Drill confused with 409 GPR boom
- high-tech sci-fi base cleanliness
- polar camp visual identity
