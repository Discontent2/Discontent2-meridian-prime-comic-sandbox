## Purpose

This file captures the current Save the Cat-style shape for Ildi Vey's Conjugate Chimera creation backstory.

The revised concept: Ildi is in her twenties, living on the fringes with a close friend. Daily life exposes them to vulnerability, exploitation, debt, dangerous work, and predatory contracts. A close friend betrays Ildi for monetary gain, leading her into what appears to be a voluntary paid experiment. The consent is real only on paper. She does not understand what she has volunteered for.

The experiment transforms her into a Conjugate Chimera. Afterward, involuntary autobiographical memory mind-pops pull her into dreamlike flashback states. During these states, her many scars biofluoresce. The color depends on scar location, memory type, intensity, and nearby system pressure. Before she can escape, she must learn to control these memory states enough to read her transformed body as a map of the hostile facility and the world around her.

Corrected visibility rule:

> Conjugate Chimeras and Aeonolacertians can visually perceive / interpret Conjugate Chimera scar biofluorescence without blacklight. All other races require a strong enough UV light source, such as a black light, or diagnostic / sensor mediation to visualize it.

Forest implication:

> Ildi later chooses to trust Forest partly because he is Aeonolacertian. He can recognize her scar-state changes during an extreme involuntary mind-pop without needing blacklight, without forcing her to expose herself to equipment, and without demanding an explanation before helping.

Core escape principle:

> The experiment turned her body into a record. Ildi turned the record into a map.

## Working Title

**Ildi Vey and the Voluntary Lie**

## Logline

In her twenties, fringe signal-reader Ildi Vey is betrayed by a close friend for money and unknowingly enters a predatory Bi-Phase Integration experiment. Transformed into a Conjugate Chimera, she must learn to control scar-lit autobiographical memory flashes and read her own body as a boundary map before she can escape.

## Core Theme

**Consent without truth is only paperwork.**

Secondary theme:

**Being known can mean being owned, but being unknown can mean being abandoned.**

## Beat Sheet

### 1. Opening Image

Ildi in her twenties crouches in a half-dead service corridor, splicing into an old signal line with her close friend beside her.

They are fringe operators: clever, underpaid, hungry, and too useful to be safe. Their work exposes them to predatory institutions, bad jobs, debt pressure, and systems that treat vulnerability as an invitation.

Her friend jokes that one day they will get paid enough to stop crawling through other people's rot.

Ildi answers with something like:

> People like us do not get paid to leave the rot. We get paid to prove it exists.

This is Ildi before the transformation: suspicious, brilliant, vulnerable through poverty and underclass precarity, and still capable of trusting one person too much.

### 2. Theme Stated

An older fringe contact, Antisapien archivist, route medic, or underclass protector warns her:

> Never sign anything when hunger is in the room.

Ildi thinks she understands. She believes she can read any trap if she gets close enough.

The tragic gap: she can read systems, but she does not yet know a friend can become part of one.

### 3. Set-Up

Establish Ildi's life before the experiment.

She and her close friend survive on the edge of official protection. They expose corruption, pull data from abandoned infrastructure, sell information when survival requires it, and sometimes protect people by making records messy.

They are not saints. They hustle, trespass, lie, and survive.

Set-up elements:

- Ildi is already talented at signal-reading and infrastructure logic.
- She trusts old systems more than clean reports.
- She and the friend have shared rooms, shared debts, shared danger, and shared jokes.
- Money is constant pressure.
- Exploitation is ordinary weather.
- Ildi has scars already from fringe life, but they are not yet the biofluorescent archive they later become.
- Her friend knows exactly where Ildi's hope, hunger, and caution live.

Character flaw:

> Ildi thinks betrayal is something systems do, not something intimacy can do.

### 4. Catalyst

The friend brings Ildi an opportunity.

It is framed as a short-term paid research contract: signal tolerance, boundary exposure diagnostics, infrastructure resonance mapping, or some other clean phrase with hazard pay folded inside.

Ildi agrees because:

- the job seems adjacent to skills she already uses;
- the money could clear debt or buy temporary safety;
- the friend vouches for it;
- the contract technically asks for consent;
- she believes she can walk away if the job turns wrong.

But she volunteers only for the wrapper, not the truth inside it.

Catalyst horror:

> Consent becomes a door that locks behind her.

### 5. Debate

Inside the facility, Ildi senses wrongness.

The staff avoid her name. The forms shift terms too often: participant, subject, candidate, exposure case. Her friend is kept beyond a threshold. Equipment behaves like it was waiting for her.

She almost leaves.

Then the friend appears one last time and persuades her to stay.

They use intimacy to override her instincts.

Possible friend line:

> One night, Ildi. One ugly little night and we never crawl for scraps again.

Ildi stays.

### 6. Break into Two

The true experiment begins.

Ildi is placed into a Bi-Phase Integration Trial without informed consent. The researchers are not merely testing her signal tolerance. They are testing whether a living Antisapien signal-band body can host sealed conjugate structures under controlled boundary conditions.

The transformation begins as procedure and becomes violation.

Early signs:

- the room temperature divides around her;
- old scars begin responding and biofluorescing;
- Conjugate Chimeras and Aeonolacertians could see the scar-light unaided, but other personnel need blacklight or diagnostic translation;
- machines disagree about whether she is stable;
- she hears systems as arguments through her own body;
- memories arrive without invitation;
- the staff become excited before they become afraid.

Story turn:

> Ildi crosses from exploited fringe survivor into living boundary horror.

### 7. B Story

The emotional B Story is Ildi's relationship with the betrayer, refracted through involuntary autobiographical memory mind-pops.

The friend may be physically absent for much of the middle, but they haunt the story through memory.

At first the memory flashes are ordinary:

- shared food;
- jokes in utility tunnels;
- patched clothes;
- old promises;
- mutual survival rituals.

Then the memories sharpen:

- the friend looking away during a payment discussion;
- a nervous hand on a contract packet;
- a rehearsed reassurance;
- a moment where they almost confessed and chose not to.

B Story lesson:

> Trust is not the same as safety.

### 8. Fun and Games

The promise of the premise, in horror form: Ildi inside the facility, transformed and trapped, trying to understand what she has become before the researchers do.

#### Scar Biofluorescence

Her scars biofluoresce during involuntary memory mind-pops. The color depends on scar location, memory type, intensity, and nearby system pressure.

The visual rule matters:

- Conjugate Chimeras can see / interpret the biofluorescence without blacklight.
- Aeonolacertians can also see / interpret the biofluorescence without blacklight.
- All other races require strong UV / blacklight exposure, cameras, sensor translation, or diagnostic displays.

Candidate color logic:

- **Teal / blue-green:** survival memories, route escapes, old signal contact.
- **Magenta:** betrayal memories, intimacy corrupted, names used as leverage.
- **Amber / gold:** technical insight, pattern recognition, moments of self-command.
- **White-blue:** medical trauma, containment shock, sterile-room memories.
- **Violet:** conjugate boundary contact, cosmic pressure, memories that may not be entirely hers.
- **Sickly green:** false safety, bad consent, institutional language.
- **Red-orange:** anger, pain, body alarm, immediate danger.

Her body becomes an archive she cannot choose when to open.

#### Mind-Pop Dream States

The flashbacks are not ordinary dreams. They seize the present.

During a mind-pop:

- the current room overlays with a past corridor;
- staff voices become the friend's voice;
- a scanner tone becomes laughter from an old job;
- scar-light reveals the memory before her mind can reject it;
- she speaks to someone who is not there;
- systems respond to her memory-state as if it is input.

The researchers first treat this as a side effect. Then they realize the memory-states produce useful data.

Second violation:

> Even her trauma becomes instrumented.

### 9. Midpoint

Ildi learns the truth.

She was not randomly selected. Her friend sold her profile, signal history, vulnerabilities, and access. The voluntary experiment was engineered around what she would accept, what she needed, and who she trusted.

She is now a viable Conjugate Chimera.

Midpoint revelation:

> Her survival is the experiment's success. Her captivity is the experiment's next phase.

Possible midpoint image:

Ildi sees footage of herself during transformation. Staff use UV / blacklight-assisted imaging so non-Chimera / non-Aeonolacertian personnel can visualize her scar patterns. A researcher says:

> She is remembering in boundary light.

That is when Ildi understands they are not merely observing her suffering. They are learning how to use it.

### 10. Bad Guys Close In

External pressure tightens:

- the facility moves toward permanent containment;
- the friend tries to collect, vanish, justify, or erase involvement;
- institutional language replaces medical language;
- Ildi is reclassified from participant to asset-risk;
- escape routes close;
- memory states become triggerable through sound, signal, phrase, or setting.

Internal pressure tightens:

- flashbacks become more disorienting;
- scar responses escalate before she can hide them;
- she loses time;
- every memory feels like a handle someone else can pull;
- she doubts which memories are original and which were shaped by the experiment.

The researchers discover that certain memory states make her more responsive to old signal and boundary systems.

Core nightmare:

> Weaponized autobiography.

### 11. All Is Lost

The friend returns, or their full betrayal is revealed through recording, payment log, staged message, or facility transcript.

Ildi finally sees the truth: the friend may not have known every detail, but they knew enough. They knew she was vulnerable. They knew the contract was predatory. They knew she trusted them.

Then the facility uses a memory of the friend to trigger Ildi's strongest mind-pop.

Her scars flare in multiple states at once. Conjugate Chimeras and Aeonolacertians could see the overlapping color-states unaided. Other observers see them only under blacklight / UV or through instruments.

She is trapped in overlapping memory states: old corridor, consent room, transformation chamber, the friend's final lie.

The result is a catastrophic loss of control. Someone may be harmed, lost, or sacrificed by the containment response. The exact consequence can remain expandable.

All Is Lost feeling:

> Her body is no longer only hers, and her memories are no longer safe even inside her.

### 12. Dark Night of the Soul

Ildi is isolated.

The facility decides she cannot be allowed to escape until she controls the flashbacks. Not because they care about her, but because uncontrolled memory-light makes her dangerous and difficult to transport, classify, or exploit.

Ildi realizes she cannot stop the memories by suppressing them. Suppression makes them arrive harder.

She also cannot obey the facility's control methods. Their version of control means becoming readable on command.

She needs a third method.

Not forgetting.  
Not surrendering.  
Not performing stability for her captors.

She must learn to choose the frame around the memory before the memory chooses the room.

This becomes the birth of adult Ildi's restraint.

### 13. Break into Three

Ildi discovers the escape principle.

The scar-light is not merely output. It is not only symptom, evidence, data leakage, or proof of memory states.

It is also input.

Her scars are responding to the world around her. Color, location, rhythm, intensity, and memory-pressure form a hostile interface she can partially read. She can feel and interpret aspects of the response internally. Conjugate Chimera or Aeonolacertian observers could see the pattern unaided; everyone else would require UV / blacklight or equipment.

She realizes:

- teal / blue-green can point toward old signal routes or survival pathways;
- magenta can mark betrayal-memory triggers, manipulation architecture, or human-pressure traps;
- amber / gold can stabilize technical pattern recognition;
- white-blue can identify medical systems, containment protocols, and sterile-room logic;
- violet can indicate boundary pressure or conjugate-adjacent systems;
- sickly green can signal false consent, bad safety language, legal traps, and institutional predation;
- red-orange can warn of immediate bodily danger or rupture pressure.

She does not master this cleanly. It hurts, glitches, misleads, and overwhelms.

But she learns enough to ask:

- What is this scar telling me about the room?
- What memory is the building trying to use?
- Which pain is a warning, and which pain is a leash?

Breakthrough line:

> You do not get to play me back.

### 14. Finale

Ildi escapes by reading her body to translate the facility into a map.

#### Step 1: Pretend Compliance

She lets the facility believe she has learned their version of control.

#### Step 2: Corrupt the Category

She manipulates diagnostic systems so they cannot decide whether she is participant, patient, hazard, asset, or equipment.

Safety protocols begin arguing with each other.

#### Step 3: Read the Scar Map

A mind-pop begins. This time, Ildi does not merely endure it.

She reads it.

The scars become route language. Their colors and rhythms reveal hidden lines in the facility: old service paths, sealed conduits, medical locks, boundary pressure zones, surveillance blind spots, and institutional traps.

During the escape, Ildi can read the map internally. If the sequence shows others seeing the scar pattern, viewer rules apply: Conjugate Chimeras and Aeonolacertians can see it unaided; all others need blacklight / UV or mediated diagnostics.

Final escape principle enacted:

> The experiment turned her body into a record. Ildi turned the record into a map.

#### Step 4: Confront the Betrayal

Ildi finds proof of the friend's role.

The friend may plead, justify, or reveal they were also squeezed by money, debt, or threat. Ildi may understand the pressure without forgiving the sale.

Possible Ildi line:

> You did not sell my body. You sold the fact that I trusted you with it.

#### Step 5: The Locked Door

Ildi must seal off part of the facility to prevent pursuit, stop continuation of the trial, protect other vulnerable people, or keep hostile systems from reclaiming her.

This becomes the origin of her locked-door trauma.

Someone may be left behind. The friend may be on the wrong side. Or the complete truth of what happened to her may remain behind that door.

She chooses escape and containment over total truth.

#### Step 6: Escape

She escapes with incomplete records, many scars, partial control, and a new law inside herself:

> Never let a system be the first thing to know you.

### 15. Final Image

Adult Ildi in 409.

The Traverse is in motion. She reads 409, module systems, comms, heat, dead channels, old signal behavior, and ghost-handshake anomalies.

A signal pulse causes one scar beneath her sleeve or collar to respond.

Most crew may see nothing without blacklight or sensors.

Forest, because he is Aeonolacertian, can see enough to know the response is not ordinary pain. He notices the scar-state and the room around her changing. He does not ask what she is. He asks:

> Ugly version?

This is one reason Ildi later chooses to trust him: he can recognize when an extreme involuntary mind-pop means she needs help, and he can act before ordinary observers or equipment understand the danger.

Ildi studies the clean packet, the dead channel, and the route that should not be answering.

She says:

> The system knows a word for me. That does not mean it gets to use it.

Then she gets to work.

## Revised Character Arc

### Starts As

A brilliant fringe survivor who believes she can read any system well enough to avoid being owned by it.

### Becomes

A betrayed, transformed, classified body whose memories can be triggered and studied.

### Ends As

A restrained, dangerous systems reader who has learned that control is not forgetting. Control is deciding what a memory is allowed to do next.

## Betrayer Role Options

The betrayer should be close enough that the wound feels intimate, not mechanical.

### Option A: The Desperate Friend

They sold Ildi's profile believing the experiment was exploitative but survivable. They did not expect the full transformation.

Best for tragic complexity.

### Option B: The Jealous Friend

They resented Ildi's talent and survival instincts. Money gave them permission to do what envy already wanted.

Best for psychological horror.

### Option C: The Compromised Friend

They were already trapped by debt, blackmail, or institutional pressure. They betrayed Ildi to save themself or someone else.

Best for morally gray fringe-world realism.

### Option D: The Deliberate Recruiter

They knew more than they admitted and helped design the lure.

Best for sharper betrayal-horror energy, but less tragic.

Recommended current version:

> Option A with traces of C. They knew enough to be guilty, not enough to understand the full horror. That keeps the betrayal human, ugly, and hard to file cleanly.

## Scar Biofluorescence System

Ildi's scars are memory-reactive boundary sites.

During involuntary autobiographical memory mind-pops, the scars biofluoresce according to:

- memory type;
- scar location;
- intensity;
- nearby system pressure;
- whether the memory is acting as warning, lure, map, or wound.

Visibility rule:

> Conjugate Chimeras and Aeonolacertians can perceive / interpret the biofluorescence without blacklight. All other races require a strong enough UV light source, such as a black light, or diagnostic / sensor mediation.

Story uses:

- visualizes trauma without exposition;
- gives comics and animation a signature visual language;
- gives prose a recurring image that can shift meaning;
- supports game mechanics around flashback control and environmental mapping;
- makes her body an unwilling archive;
- later lets her convert that archive into navigational agency;
- creates suspense around who can actually see the evidence and under what conditions;
- explains why Forest's Aeonolacertian perception matters to Ildi's eventual trust in him.

Rule:

> The glow should not be pretty first. It should be revealing first.

## Control Requirement Before Escape

The facility will not allow Ildi to escape until she controls the flashback states.

Their reason is not compassion. Uncontrolled memory-light makes her difficult to move, classify, exploit, or safely contain.

Their control model:

- trigger memory;
- read glow;
- stabilize response;
- convert Ildi into repeatable data.

Ildi's control model:

- recognize the memory;
- identify whether the pain is warning or leash;
- read the room through the scar response;
- choose what the memory is allowed to do next.

This is the engine of her escape.

## Final Backstory Principle

**Ildi Vey was not transformed into a map. She was transformed into a record. Her victory was learning how to read the record before anyone else could use it to own her.**
