**File Type:** Modeler start-here handoff

---

## Start Here

This is the practical kickoff brief for the artist / modeler opening Blender, ZBrush, Nomad Sculpt, or an equivalent sculpting tool.

Your first job is **not** final detail.

Your first job is to prove that Kavo "Forest" Rill works as a readable, printable, balanced 5-inch retro collector figure in blockout form.

Think: big silhouette first, tiny scale gremlins later.

---

## One-Sentence Figure Goal

Create a 5-inch adult collectible prototype of Kavo "Forest" Rill: a calm, precise, streetwise Velocisapien Mountaineer route-reader with a functional tail, black shades, black fingerless gloves, cropped route jacket, cargo pants, field boots, compact left-wrist GPR watch, and route tools.

---

## Non-Negotiable Identity Markers

Preserve these from the first blockout:

- Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid read.
- Route-Dust Olive Teal scaled skin on exposed body areas, never human skin.
- Saurian head, snout, and brow.
- Short sparse red proto-quill mohawk.
- Black-frame shades as primary look.
- Mostly humanoid saurian body plan.
- Four-finger clawed hands.
- Four-toed field-boot / claw read.
- Universal functional tail.
- Black fingerless gloves, not wrist warmers.
- Cropped cold-weather route jacket.
- Black field-knit / thermal under-layer.
- Dark route-worker cargo pants.
- Light route harness with checker trim and badges.
- Compact left-wrist route-reader watch / wrist GPR unit.
- Relaxed route-readiness, not sprint pose.

Do not make him a ninja, assassin, generic raptor scout, dinosaur monster, human in reptile makeup, military operator, or over-armed survival figure.

## Scene Setup

Before sculpting:

- Set units to **millimeters**.
- Set figure height target to **5 inches / 127 mm**.
- Add a ground plane.
- Start in a neutral relaxed stance, not action sprint.
- Keep origin / pivot conventions consistent.
- Work symmetrically only where appropriate. The left wrist has the watch.
- Keep all file and part names aligned with the STL manifest.

- arms and four-finger hand masses

Do not detail scales, zippers, stitches, badge icons, checker squares, UI graphics, or tiny watch screws until the blockout review passes.

## Suggested Blockout Sequence

### 1. Body Mass and Stance

- Block feet first so the figure can stand.
- Use broad boots and stable hips.
- Place the tail early because it affects balance, pelvis, silhouette, and back view.
- Keep the pose relaxed and ready, not mid-run.

### 2. Head Identity

- Block saurian head, snout, and brow before eyewear.
- Add thick shades as a separate mass.
- Add short sparse proto-quill mass.
- Keep expression sly, calm, dangerous, and socially awake.

### 3. Torso and Garments

- Block cropped route jacket over torso.
- Add black field-knit under-layer as visible base.
- Add route harness over jacket / under-layer.
- Reserve checker trim and badge zones, but do not detail them yet.

### 4. Legs and Boots

- Block cargo pants with broad reinforcement panels.
- Add pocket volumes without blocking hip movement.
- Taper cuffs into field boot collars.
- Reserve four-toe claw clearance at boot fronts.

### 5. Arms, Hands, Watch

- Block shoulder and wrist joint volumes.
- Block four-finger hands.
- Add black fingerless glove cuff forms.
- Add compact left-wrist watch only after glove and wrist proportions are readable.
- Watch must read as a small hard device, not a wrist warmer or wrist brick.

### 6. Tail and Socket

- Confirm tail base and keyed socket volume.
- Tail should support balance and attitude.
- Tail tip stays blunt.
- Tail must clear jacket hem and harness lower straps.

### 7. Accessory Grip Placeholders

- Add route probe / staff handle cylinder.
- Add black Path flag marker handle cylinder.
- Optional handheld route-reader backup should be smaller and secondary to the wrist watch.

## Watch Rules

KFR-REF-014B has hard corrections. Follow these exactly:

- Watch goes on **left wrist**.
- Watch is **compact**.
- Watch is sculpted on for Prototype A.
- Watch does not become removable in first build.
- Watch does not become wrist warmer.
- Watch does not cover the black fingerless glove read.
- Watch does not erase four-finger hand identity.
- Watch face is simple and non-canon.
- Exposed skin around watch remains Route-Dust Olive Teal Velocisapien scaled skin.
- No real-world watch branding or exact product trade dress.
- No kill-switch, stealth, or military command UI.

---

## Tail Rules

- Tail is essential, not decoration.
- Tail must be planned before final pelvis shape.
- Tail base must be thick.
- Tail tapers to a blunt safe tip.
- Tail must support balance or near-balance.
- Tail should communicate attitude and route-readiness.
- Tail should not look like a weapon spike.
- Tail must be keyed removable for Prototype A.
- Segmented tail is deferred.

---

## Garment Rules

### Jacket

- Cropped black cold-weather route jacket.
- Weathered leather / waxed shell read.
- Sturdy collar mass, shearling-style texture later.
- Jacket hem must clear hips, tail base, and harness lower straps.
- Jacket must not block shoulder movement.
- Avoid biker parody, superhero costume, or military uniform read.

### Under-Layer

- Black thermal / field-knit layer.
- Minimal graphic treatment.
- Do not add shirt logos during blockout.

### Pants

- Dark route-worker cargo pants.
- Reinforced thigh and knee panel masses.
- Pocket masses must not block hip movement.
- Cuffs taper into boot collars.
- Broad fabric planes, not tiny wrinkle noise.
- No dangling straps, chains, or fragile cords.

## Immediate Modeler Tasks

Start with these in order:

1. Create the 127 mm neutral blockout scene with ground plane.
2. Build the body mass, broad boot stance, and tail balance together.
3. Block the saurian head, snout, brow, shades, and proto-quill mass.
4. Block cropped jacket, under-layer, cargo pants, and harness volumes.
5. Block arms, four-finger hands, black glove cuffs, and compact left-wrist watch.
6. Place joint socket placeholders and accessory grip cylinders.
7. Export the required review views.
8. Fill the blockout review record in `kavo-forest-rill-sculpt-blockout-checklist.md`.
9. Update `kavo-forest-rill-stl-part-manifest.md` status fields as parts move from `Not Started` to `Blockout In Progress`.

---

## Stop Signs

Stop and request review if any of these happen:

- Kavo starts reading human.
- The watch grows too large.
- The watch reads like a wrist warmer.
- The tail cannot help balance.
- Boots are too narrow to stand.
- Jacket blocks shoulder range.
- Cargo pockets block hip range.
- Hands lose four-finger identity.
- Feet lose four-toe / claw identity.
- The design drifts into ninja, assassin, monster, military, or generic survival-figure territory.
- Any real-world branding appears in geometry.
