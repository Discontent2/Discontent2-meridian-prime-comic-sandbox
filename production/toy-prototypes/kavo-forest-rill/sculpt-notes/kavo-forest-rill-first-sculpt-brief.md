# Kavo "Forest" Rill First Sculpt Brief

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Folder:** `production/toy-prototypes/kavo-forest-rill/sculpt-notes/`  
**Status:** First sculptor handoff brief  
**Prototype Target:** 5-inch retro collector action figure  
**Canon Boundary:** Sandbox production file. Does not alter main canon.

---

## Purpose

This is the main sculptor handoff brief for the first Kavo "Forest" Rill 3D-printable action figure prototype.

Use this brief to begin a build in Blender, ZBrush, Nomad Sculpt, or equivalent. It combines canon markers, toy design requirements, turnaround expectations, parts breakdown, STL planning, articulation goals, and print constraints into one build instruction file.

---

## Prototype Goal

Create a 5-inch retro collector action figure prototype of Kavo "Forest" Rill that is:

- canon-protective
- sculptor-ready
- 3D-printable
- durable enough for test assembly
- expressive at shelf distance
- practical enough for future articulation and paint testing

The figure should have chunky retro toy presence without becoming goofy, derivative, over-armed, or impossible to print.

---

## Character Summary

Kavo Rill is his real name.

Forest Rill is his human / crew name.

He is a Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid Mountaineer, route guide, wilderness EMT, and GPR path-reader.

He represents terrain truth among the first MITE II crew anchors.

Character read:

- relaxed menace
- precise
- streetwise
- impossible to rush
- quiet until funny
- funny until dangerous
- dangerous until useful

Toy read:

> A calm pressure-route professional who already knows where the terrain is lying.

---

## Required Canon Markers

The sculpt must preserve:

- mostly humanoid saurian body plan
- expressive saurian face
- saurian snout and brow structure
- scaled skin
- universal tail
- four-fingered clawed hands
- four-toed clawed feet
- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades when not using route goggles
- route goggles as alternate look or future head
- subtle checker trim
- light route harness
- pressure-route badge cluster
- claw-wraps
- relaxed posture that can become dangerous instantly

Do not omit the tail. Do not treat it as decoration only.

---

## Design Guardrails

Do not make Kavo:

- a ninja
- an assassin
- a generic raptor scout
- an animalistic alien
- a dinosaur monster
- a human in reptile makeup
- a clone of any protected toyline
- a literal musician with stage props
- an over-armed fantasy combat figure

Do not include:

- turtle shell
- mask
- sewer imagery
- pizza jokes
- ooze / mutagen concepts
- ninja vocabulary
- literal guitars or microphones
- hidden Book One reveals
- unapproved badge meanings

---

## Figure Format

**Scale:** 5 inches, approximately 127 mm from bottom of feet to top of head / quill silhouette.  
**Style:** Retro collector action figure.  
**Audience:** Adult prototype / collector, not child-safe.  
**Pose:** Relaxed route-readiness.  
**Build priority:** Durable first, deluxe articulation later.

Recommended shelf silhouette:

- slightly oversized expressive head
- confident saurian brow and snout
- short red quill crest
- black-frame shades
- light route harness across torso
- badge cluster
- wrapped clawed hands
- broad four-toed feet
- long sturdy tail with attitude

---

## Proportion Direction

Use toyetic, chunky proportions:

- Head: slightly enlarged for expression and shelf read.
- Neck: sturdy saurian neck, not thin human neck.
- Torso: mostly humanoid saurian, compact and field-strong.
- Arms: tool-capable, not thin.
- Hands: four fingers, thickened claws, grip-ready.
- Legs: agile, powerful, not monster-heavy.
- Feet: wide four-toed stance for balance.
- Tail: long, sturdy, slightly curved, visible in silhouette.

Avoid hyper-real paleo anatomy. Kavo is a person with saurian morphology, field gear, social presence, and professional competence.

---

## Pose Requirement

Primary sculpt pose:

- neutral enough for action figure articulation
- relaxed route-readiness
- slight confident lean possible in hero render
- shoulders calm, not attack-raised
- head able to look sideways through neck joint
- hands capable of holding route tools
- feet flat and broad enough to stand
- tail curved for balance and attitude

Do not sculpt him mid-sprint or mid-attack for prototype one.

---

## Head Sculpt Instructions

Build the head first after body blockout is approved.

Required:

- saurian snout
- strong expressive brow
- sly controlled mouth line
- almost vestigial lips, not mammalian lips
- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades, sculpted-on for prototype one
- visible intelligence and humor

Expression target:

> Calm enough to make you think he is joking, sharp enough to make you wonder whether you are standing in the wrong place.

Optional later:

- alternate route-goggles head
- plain sculpt-review head without shades

Prototype one preference:

- primary head with sculpted shades for durability

---

## Body and Gear Instructions

Required body gear:

- light route harness
- pressure-route badge cluster
- subtle checker trim
- claw-wraps
- compact EMT pouch or pouch detail
- optional compact field pack if silhouette allows

Harness rules:

- should not block shoulder articulation
- should help hide torso and shoulder seams
- should read as field-useful, not costume-only
- should support badge cluster placement

Checker trim rules:

- subtle and limited
- use on one harness tab, cuff, small garment stripe, or badge-adjacent patch
- do not cover the figure in checkerboard

Badge rules:

- make badges large enough to paint
- do not assign canon meanings unless approved
- place where visible from front or three-quarter view

---

## Tail Instructions

The tail is essential.

Tail must communicate:

- balance
- attitude
- emotional control
- combat posture
- route-readiness

Prototype one tail:

- keyed removable tail preferred
- thick peg
- future-friendly swivel potential
- broad anatomical base
- gentle curve that helps standing balance
- blunt, durable tip

Avoid:

- decorative noodle tail
- needle tip
- tail too heavy to stand
- plugged-on look
- socket wall too thin

---

## Hands and Feet Instructions

### Hands

Required:

- four fingers including thumb-equivalent
- claws thick enough to print
- gripping hand compatible with route probe / flag pole
- hand shape not human-with-claws
- wrist wraps hiding wrist seam

Grip standard:

- use a shared test cylinder for route probe and flag marker
- route-reader should have compatible grip or side handle

### Feet

Required:

- four toes per foot
- claws blunted for safety
- broad stable footprint
- saurian tool-culture anatomy, not human feet with claws pasted on

Feet should allow figure to stand without a display base if tail balance succeeds.

Minimum first sculpt parts:

1. Head with sculpted shades
2. Torso with harness and badge cluster
3. Pelvis / hip block with tail socket
4. Left full arm
5. Right full arm
6. Left gripping hand
7. Right gripping hand
8. Left leg with integrated foot
9. Right leg with integrated foot
10. Keyed removable tail
11. Route-reader device
12. Black Path flag marker
13. Route probe

Optional:

- route-goggles alternate head
- separate field pack
- separate EMT pouch
- alternate hands
- display base only if balance fails

---

## Articulation Target

First prototype target:

- neck swivel or simple ball
- shoulder swivels
- wrist swivels if strong enough
- simple hip joints
- keyed removable or swivel-ready tail

Optional later:

- waist swivel
- elbows
- knees
- ball-jointed tail

Do not add elbow or knee joints until the core prototype proves silhouette, balance, and material strength.

---

## Accessory Sculpt Instructions

Required first accessories:

### Route-Reader Device

- chunky handheld field tool
- screen or gauge face
- grip compatible with four-fingered hand
- no tiny breakable antennae
- screen detail may be paint-defined

### Black Path Flag Marker

- thickened pole
- matte black flag in paint plan
- blunt stake tip
- scaled for hand grip

### Route Probe

- collapsible field-tool feel
- blunt tip
- grip zone
- not a spear, staff weapon, or fantasy weapon

Optional accessory detail:

- brass whistle or route token sculpted onto harness, not loose tiny part for prototype one

---

## Texture Strategy

Use broad, print-friendly detail tiers.

Must sculpt clearly:

- head / snout / brow
- four-finger hands
- four-toed feet
- tail curve
- route harness
- shades / goggles shape
- proto-quill mohawk

Should sculpt clearly:

- claw-wraps
- badge cluster
- checker trim zone
- EMT pouch
- route-reader body
- black flag
- route probe grip

Can be paint-only:

- tiny badge symbols
- micro scratches
- route-reader screen graphics
- cloth weave
- very small buckle scuffs

Avoid microscopic scale texture everywhere. Use readable scale zones.

---

## Printability Rules

Sculpt for resin prototype durability:

- no needle-thin quills
- no razor claws
- no ultra-thin glasses arms
- no floating strap slivers
- no tiny loose badges
- no thin flag pole
- no hollow sockets with weak walls
- no hidden internal geometry
- no non-manifold meshes
- pegs and sockets must be testable separately

Use thickened features where needed. A slightly chunky Kavo is better than a tragic pile of elegant splinters.

---

## STL / Source Handoff Requirements

Export units:

- millimeters
- real print scale

Use file naming convention:

`kfr_[part-id]_[part-name]_[side-or-type]_[version]_[status].stl`

Required source handoff:

- source sculpt file, such as `.blend`, `.ztl`, Nomad source, or equivalent
- separated named objects matching STL names
- neutral assembled model
- exploded parts layout
- scale reference ruler
- articulation axes or helper objects
- notes on non-print-ready decorative layers

Do not use `final` in file names until a printed and painted prototype has passed review.

---

## Required Test Coupons

Before full body print, create / export:

- neck joint coupon
- shoulder joint coupon
- wrist joint coupon
- hip joint coupon
- tail joint coupon
- standard grip cylinder
- route-reader grip test
- black flag pole grip test
- route probe grip test
- paint-rub sample joint

Clearance ladder:

- 0.10 mm
- 0.15 mm
- 0.20 mm
- optional 0.25 mm for tail or painted joints

Sculpt should support these paint zones:

- muted field-ready scaled skin
- red proto-quills
- black shades
- optional route goggles lenses
- worn dark harness
- subtle checker trim
- pressure-route badges
- claw-wraps
- claws
- EMT pouch
- route-reader screen
- black Path flag

Do not sculpt detail so tiny that paint cannot reach it.
