## Purpose

This packet translates the approved Kavo "Forest" Rill toy-development references into sculptor-ready parts, joints, engineering notes, garment logic, accessory compatibility rules, and first-print build priorities.

Use this file before blocking out the Blender / ZBrush / Nomad Sculpt model.

The goal is a physical 3D printed prototype that reads as Kavo: relaxed menace, precise route-reader, Mountaineer field professional, and Velocisapien pressure-runner without becoming a generic raptor, ninja, assassin, dinosaur monster, human in reptile makeup, or over-armed survival figure.

## Build Philosophy

Prototype A should prioritize:

1. strong silhouette
2. durable printability
3. recognizable Kavo identity
4. stable standing pose
5. useful tail balance
6. accessory handling
7. clean garment and harness layering
8. clean paint masking
9. simple reliable joints

A durable prototype is better than a tiny joint carnival that shatters when the tail side-eyes gravity.

**Scale:** 5-inch retro collector figure  
**Approximate height:** 5 inches / 127 mm to top of head or proto-quill silhouette  
**Proportion language:** chunky, toyetic, retro-action, not goofy

## Garment Sculpt Notes: KFR-REF-014A

### Jacket

- Cropped black cold-weather route jacket.
- Weathered leather / waxed shell read.
- Shearling-style collar translated as sturdy toy texture.
- Jacket hem short enough to avoid hips, harness lower straps, and tail base.
- Open-front layering may reveal black field-knit under-layer and harness.
- Cuffs must be thick enough to print.
- Jacket bulk must not block shoulder movement.
- Back yoke / seam shape should be visible but not over-detailed.

### Under-Layer

- Black thermal / field-knit layer under jacket and harness.
- Minimal graphic treatment unless separately approved.
- No generated shirt graphic becomes canon.

### Pants

- Dark brown / worn brown route-worker cargo pants.
- Reinforced thigh and knee panels.
- Cargo pocket on one thigh and optional smaller utility pocket if sculpt space allows.
- Wide belt loops and belt line.
- Tapered legs meet or tuck into the field boot collar.
- Fabric folds should be broad toy-readable planes, not hyper-real wrinkle noodles.
- No dangling straps or cords.
- Pants pockets must not block hip movement.

## Watch Sculpt Notes: KFR-REF-014B

Prototype A uses a **compact sculpted-on left-wrist route-reader watch / wrist GPR unit**.

Hard corrections:

- Left wrist only.
- Compact size, not the oversized watch from earlier generated views.
- Forest does not wear wrist warmers.
- Forest wears black fingerless gloves.
- On-figure views must show Route-Dust Olive Teal Velocisapien scaled skin, never human skin.
- The watch sits over or just above the glove cuff and jacket cuff.
- The watch must not block the four-finger glove read or hand grip.
- No real-world branding or exact trade dress.
- No kill switch, stealth mode, military-assassin UI, or canon-making screen text.

Sculpt approach:

- Matte black rectangular case.
- Raised protective bezel.
- Recessed screen plane.
- Three chunky side buttons.
- Simplified screw / fastener circles.
- Thick hook-and-loop style strap texture.
- Sculpted on for durability; not removable in Prototype A.

## Joint Engineering Notes

- Neck: thick peg and sturdy head socket; preserve KFR-REF-005 clearance.
- Shoulders: check jacket sleeve and harness clearance before finalizing sockets.
- Wrists: seam under glove cuff; left watch must not make the wrist brittle.
- Hips: stable retro hips; cargo pockets and belt line must clear movement.
- Knees: optional only; omit if knee weakens stance.
- Ankles / boots: boot collar seam only; boots stand flat and wide.
- Tail: thick keyed base, blunt tip, balance support.

## Sculpt Detail Requirements

### Head

- Strong saurian brow.
- Expressive snout and mouth line.
- Sly, calm, precise expression.
- Thick black-frame shades.
- Short sparse red proto-quills, sturdy and not needle-thin.

### Body / Torso

- Chunky retro toyetic build.
- Cropped jacket, black field-knit layer, and route harness integrated cleanly.
- Chest and back panels visible.
- Checker trim zones raised or recessed enough to paint cleanly.
- Badge details sculpted as simple readable shapes, not tiny flakes.
- Pouches and straps can be sculpted nonfunctional.

### Hands / Watch

- Exactly four fingers per hand.
- Black fingerless glove shape.
- Visible saurian fingers and dark horn claws.
- Left-wrist watch compact and sculpted on.
- No wrist warmers.
- Watch and cuff must not erase the glove silhouette.

### Feet / Boots / Pants

- Exactly four-toe / front claw read per foot.
- Field boot silhouette from KFR-REF-008.
- Pants taper to boot collar.
- Cargo pocket and knee panels sculpted as robust raised forms.
- Tread blocks simplified into durable lugs.
- No real boot or garment branding.

### Tail

- Functional balance silhouette.
- Thick base and taper.
- Blunt tip.
- Simple paint zones: upper Route-Dust Olive Teal, underside Sage Green.
- Red proto-quill / ridge accents only if sculpted short, sparse, and sturdy.

## Accessory Compatibility

KFR-REF-014B changes the route-reader hierarchy:

- The left-wrist route-reader watch is Forest's primary route-reading interface.
- The handheld GPR / route-reader from KFR-REF-011 becomes optional backup, compact variant, or tray accessory.
- Do not include both a giant wrist device and giant handheld device.

Approved Prototype A accessory pool:

1. compact wrist route-reader watch, sculpted-on
2. black Path flag marker
3. route probe / collapsible staff
4. compact Mountaineer field pack
5. dark canvas EMT pouch, no symbol
6. route goggles
7. brass route token / whistle
8. carabiner / route clip
9. folded terrain map / route card
10. optional alternate gripping hand pair
11. optional compact handheld route-reader backup

Do not add generic survival weapons, ice axes, knives, fantasy blades, firearms, or ninja gear.

## Paint Integration Notes

Use:

- `docs/toy-development/kavo-forest-rill-paint-guide.md`

Critical paint-engineering considerations:

- Keep jacket and pants muted and field-worn.
- Keep orange primarily on harness panels, boot uppers, and selected route-tool accents.
- Keep checker trim in paintable controlled zones.
- Keep watch screen and UI non-canon and simple.
- Keep badge symbols readable but not canonized.
- Use W-02 Light Route Dust lightly on boots, lower pants, jacket hem, tail contact, harness edges, watch edges, and tools.
- Avoid tight paint rub at neck, shoulders, wrists, hips, tail socket, watch edges, and optional knees.

## Safety / Prototype Handling Notes

This is an adult collectible prototype.

Do not describe it as child-safe, kid-friendly, or toy-certified.

3D print safety:

- use ventilation
- use gloves and eye protection when handling resin
- avoid skin contact with uncured resin
- cure parts properly
- control sanding dust
- blunt sharp claws, quills, tail tip, flag marker, route probe, watch corners, and boot claws
- keep brittle test parts away from children
