# Kavo "Forest" Rill Turnaround Checklist

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Folder:** `production/toy-prototypes/kavo-forest-rill/sculpt-notes/`  
**Status:** Sculptor-ready turnaround checklist  
**Prototype Target:** 5-inch retro collector action figure  
**Canon Boundary:** Sandbox production file. Does not alter main canon.

---

## Purpose

This checklist defines what the first sculptor-ready model sheet / turnaround packet must show before a 3D sculpt begins.

The goal is to prevent Kavo from becoming a generic raptor scout, a ninja, a dinosaur monster, or a human in reptile makeup. The sculpt should deliver a durable, printable, toyetic Mountaineer with pressure-route swagger and field-earned precision.

---

## Required Turnaround Views

Minimum required views:

1. Front view
2. Left side view
3. Right side view
4. Back view
5. Three-quarter front hero view
6. Three-quarter back view showing tail, pack, harness, and socket logic
7. Neutral T-pose or low A-pose engineering view
8. Relaxed route-readiness pose view

Optional but recommended:

- head close-up front / side / three-quarter
- hand close-up showing four fingers and grip shape
- foot close-up showing four toes and standing footprint
- tail base close-up showing joint or keyed socket plan
- accessory scale lineup beside hand

---

## Scale and Proportion Requirements

**Prototype scale:** 5-inch figure.

Suggested toy proportions:

- Head: slightly oversized for expression and shelf read.
- Torso: mostly humanoid saurian, compact and strong.
- Arms: tool-capable, not noodle-thin.
- Hands: four-fingered, clawed, thick enough for grip sockets.
- Legs: agile and strong, not monster-thigh exaggeration.
- Feet: broad four-toed stance for shelf stability.
- Tail: long, sturdy, visible from front silhouette, useful for balance.

Checklist:

- [ ] Figure stands with feet alone in neutral pose.
- [ ] Figure can use tail as visual balance support.
- [ ] Tail does not look like an afterthought.
- [ ] Saurian head reads as intelligent and expressive.
- [ ] Body does not read as human body plus reptile mask.
- [ ] Hands and feet are not ordinary human anatomy with claws glued on.

## Character Read Targets

The turnaround should communicate:

- relaxed menace
- precise field competence
- streetwise patience
- impossible-to-rush body language
- quiet until funny
- funny until dangerous
- dangerous until useful

Expression target:

> Calm enough to make you think he is joking, sharp enough to make you wonder whether you are standing in the wrong place.

Pose target:

> Leaning into route-readiness, not sprinting, crouching, or attacking.

---

## Head Sheet Requirements

The head sheet should include:

- front head view
- side head view
- three-quarter head view
- brow / snout profile
- proto-quill mohawk placement
- shades version
- route goggles version
- plain face version if needed for sculpt review

Head sculpt checklist:

- [ ] brow is expressive but not human eyebrow anatomy
- [ ] snout is saurian, not dog-like or monster-dinosaur only
- [ ] mouth can imply sly humor without mammalian lips
- [ ] proto-quills are short, sparse, and red
- [ ] shades or goggles do not erase facial attitude
- [ ] neck can support swivel or ball joint

---

## Body and Gear Sheet Requirements

The body sheet should clearly show:

- route harness path across torso
- harness connection points
- badge cluster placement
- checker trim placement
- claw-wrap design
- EMT pouch location
- optional compact field pack location
- tail base and socket / seam planning
- clothing / skin boundary
- scale texture zones

Gear placement notes:

- Harness should frame the torso without strangling articulation.
- Badge cluster should be visible from front or three-quarter view.
- Checker trim should be subtle and readable, not all-over costume pattern.
- EMT pouch should distinguish Kavo as Mountaineer / wilderness EMT, not generic fighter.

---

## Tail Sheet Requirements

The tail sheet must show:

- top view curve
- side view curve
- base thickness
- socket or keyed peg plan
- contact / near-contact point for balance
- tail tip thickness
- tail texture direction

Tail checklist:

- [ ] tail supports balance and attitude
- [ ] tail is thick enough to print
- [ ] tail does not taper to a brittle needle
- [ ] tail socket does not destroy hip strength
- [ ] tail can be removed or rotated depending on prototype version
- [ ] tail silhouette is visible from front and back

Recommended first prototype:

- keyed removable tail with future-friendly swivel potential

---

## Hands and Feet Requirements

### Hands

Show:

- relaxed hand
- gripping hand around route probe
- gripping hand around route-reader or flag marker
- palm structure
- claw thickness
- four-finger count

Checklist:

- [ ] four fingers including thumb-equivalent
- [ ] grip diameter defined
- [ ] claws thickened and blunted for print safety
- [ ] wrist joint or seam hidden by wraps

### Feet

Show:

- front foot view
- side foot view
- sole / footprint shape if possible
- four-toe count
- claw thickness
- standing width

Checklist:

- [ ] four toes per foot
- [ ] broad enough to stand
- [ ] claws not dangerously sharp
- [ ] foot stance supports relaxed pose
- [ ] feet feel saurian but tool-culture compatible

- [ ] route goggles or shades

- [ ] handles fit four-fingered gripping hands

- [ ] black flag marker pole is thick enough
- [ ] route-reader device has a clear grip zone
- [ ] route probe has a blunt field-tool tip

## Articulation Planning Overlay

Add an articulation overlay page or layer showing:

- neck joint
- shoulder joints
- wrist joints
- hip joints
- tail joint / keyed socket
- optional waist joint
- optional elbow / knee future cuts

Checklist:

- [ ] joint seams do not break core silhouette
- [ ] harness does not block shoulders
- [ ] hip articulation does not destroy stance
- [ ] wrist wraps can disguise wrist seam
- [ ] tail base can survive repeated assembly

---

## Printability Notes for Sculptor

Keep these visible during model-sheet approval:

- no needle-thin quills
- no thin glasses arms unless optional display-only part
- no floating strap slivers
- no razor claws
- no ultra-thin flag poles
- avoid unsupported tiny badges
- use bold texture zones instead of microscopic scale texture everywhere
- design pegs and sockets as testable pieces
- preserve part separation lines where they can hide under gear

## Guardrails

### Rejected / Do Not Use

Do not depict Kavo as:

- ninja
- assassin
- generic raptor scout
- dinosaur monster
- animalistic alien
- human in reptile makeup
- parody of an existing protected toyline
- over-armed fantasy combat figure
- ska musician with literal performance props

### Canon Protection

Do not imply:

- hidden Book One revelations
- full Core / gateway mechanics
- secret assassin knowledge
- new species anatomy not supported by Aeonolacertian canon
- canon badge meanings without approval
