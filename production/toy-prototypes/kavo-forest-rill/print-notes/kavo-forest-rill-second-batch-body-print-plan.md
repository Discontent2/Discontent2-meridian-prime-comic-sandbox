## Purpose

This file plans the second physical print batch for the Kavo "Forest" Rill action figure prototype.

Batch A proves joints, grips, head readability, and core accessory printability. Batch B should test the first partial body assembly: torso, pelvis, legs, arms, tail, stance, and balance. This is where the figure starts becoming a small person instead of a tray of clever coupons.

---

## Batch B Rule

Do not print Batch B until Batch A has selected or revised:

- neck tolerance
- shoulder tolerance
- wrist plan or fixed-hand decision
- tail tolerance
- standard grip diameter
- head support strategy
- route-reader / flag / probe print strategy

If Batch A fails any structural test, revise and repeat Batch A before printing body parts.

---

## Batch B Objectives

Batch B should answer:

1. Does the torso print cleanly with harness and badge details?
2. Does the pelvis hold hip sockets and tail socket without cracking?
3. Do the legs and broad four-toed feet support a neutral stance?
4. Do the arms fit shoulders and clear the route harness?
5. Does the keyed tail fit and support balance?
6. Can the partial figure stand without a display base?
7. Do seams hide under harness, wraps, belt line, and tail base folds?
8. Are paint zones still accessible after part separation?
9. Does Kavo still read as relaxed route-readiness when assembled?

## Raw Inspection Checklist

After washing and curing:

- [ ] Torso printed without major support damage to harness.
- [ ] Badge cluster survived and remains paintable.
- [ ] Shoulder sockets / pegs are round enough.
- [ ] Pelvis printed without cracking.
- [ ] Tail socket is clean.
- [ ] Hip sockets are clean.
- [ ] Arms survived support removal.
- [ ] Wrist sockets or hand connections survived.
- [ ] Legs printed without warping.
- [ ] Feet have flat enough contact points.
- [ ] Tail is not warped.
- [ ] Tail peg is intact.
- [ ] No claws or toes are dangerously sharp.
- [ ] No uncured resin remains trapped.

---

## Assembly Test Order

Test assembly in this order:

1. Pelvis to legs.
2. Pelvis to tail.
3. Torso to pelvis if applicable.
4. Arms to torso.
5. Hands to wrists or fixed-hand arms.
6. Head to neck, only if Batch A head is ready.
7. Accessories in hands.
8. Full neutral stance.
9. Relaxed route-readiness stance.
10. Tail-assisted balance stance.

Do not force any joint. If a part resists, stop and log it.

## Go / No-Go for Full Prototype Batch

### Go to Batch C if:

- [ ] Torso prints cleanly enough.
- [ ] Pelvis sockets survive.
- [ ] Tail fits and supports balance.
- [ ] Legs support neutral stance.
- [ ] Arms attach and clear harness.
- [ ] Hands hold selected accessories.
- [ ] Partial figure reads as Kavo.
- [ ] No structural part requires major redesign.

### Conditional Go if:

- [ ] Minor sanding solves fit.
- [ ] Foot soles need small adjustment.
- [ ] Tail balance needs curve adjustment.
- [ ] Harness detail needs small cleanup.
- [ ] One joint needs tolerance tweak.

### No-Go if:

- [ ] Pelvis cracks.
- [ ] Tail socket fails.
- [ ] Figure cannot stand at all.
- [ ] Legs warp badly.
- [ ] Arms cannot clear harness.
- [ ] Body silhouette loses Kavo's character read.
- [ ] Required canon markers are missing or unreadable.
