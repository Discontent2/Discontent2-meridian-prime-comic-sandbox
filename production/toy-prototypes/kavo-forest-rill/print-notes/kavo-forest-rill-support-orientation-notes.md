## Purpose

Use this file to plan print orientation, support placement, and cleanup risk before slicing each Prototype A part.

The goal is to protect the face, shades, proto-quills, watch face, hand grips, checker trim, badge faces, cargo pockets, boot claws, tail socket, and accessory handles from support-scar goblin bites.

---

## Global Support Rules

- Use heavier supports on hidden structural areas.
- Use lighter supports near visible detail, then inspect for warping.
- Avoid support contact on face, shades lenses, watch screen, badge faces, checker trim, hand grip interiors, visible tail top line, and route-reader screens.
- Add extra support to heavy tail base and underside curve.
- Print delicate accessories in multiples.
- Keep small handles slightly oversized for sanding and fit tuning.
- Keep watch buttons, strap texture, cargo pocket flaps, and jacket cuffs robust rather than tiny.
- Do not hollow small parts, hands, watch wrist sample, accessories, heads, or joints.
- Do not leave uncured resin trapped inside any hollowed part.

---

## Surface Protection Priority

### Protect at All Costs

Avoid supports on:

- face
- snout bridge
- brow expression
- shades / goggle lenses
- mouth line
- proto-quill mohawk tips
- watch screen and front bezel
- visible black fingerless glove knuckles
- exposed Velocisapien wrist scales
- badge faces
- checker trim blocks
- hand grip interiors
- route-reader screen face
- black flag face
- cargo pocket faces
- jacket collar / lapel face
- top visible tail curve
- tail socket key surfaces

### Support Acceptable With Cleanup

Supports may touch:

- back of head
- underside of jaw if hidden
- torso back under pack area
- inside upper arms
- underside of forearms
- watch strap underside
- glove cuff underside
- inner thighs
- underside of feet
- boot soles
- underside of tail curve
- accessory backs

## Watch-Specific Orientation Notes

For KFR-REF-014B:

- Watch must stay compact.
- Watch face should be protected from supports.
- Side buttons should be chunky and not brittle.
- Strap texture should be broad and sturdy.
- Exposed wrist skin should remain Velocisapien scaled skin.
- No human skin coloration or reference is allowed.
- Black fingerless glove must remain readable.
- Watch should not look like a wrist warmer.

---

## Garment-Specific Orientation Notes

For KFR-REF-014A:

- Protect cropped jacket collar and lapel read.
- Avoid support scars on front jacket panels if visible.
- Cargo pocket faces should stay clean.
- Reinforced knee panels should remain paintable.
- Pant cuffs should meet boot collar cleanly.
- Shoulder supports must not destroy jacket / harness clearance.
