# Kavo "Forest" Rill Support Orientation Notes

**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab  
**Folder:** `production/toy-prototypes/kavo-forest-rill/print-notes/`  
**Status:** First-pass support and orientation plan  
**Prototype Target:** 5-inch retro collector action figure  
**Canon Boundary:** Sandbox production file. Does not alter main canon.

---

## Purpose

This file defines suggested resin print orientation, support placement rules, protected surface zones, and risk areas for each planned Kavo "Forest" Rill figure part and accessory before slicing.

The goal is to protect the face, hands, tail, gear, and small route tools while still producing strong, clean parts for fit testing.

---

## Safety Positioning

This workflow assumes adult prototype production only.

3D print safety requirements:

- use ventilation
- wear gloves when handling uncured resin
- avoid skin contact with uncured resin
- wear eye protection when clipping supports
- cure parts fully
- control sanding dust
- wet-sand resin where appropriate
- blunt sharp points before handling
- keep uncured resin and fragile test pieces away from children

---

## General Resin Orientation Rules

Use these as starting points, not sacred tablets.

- Avoid printing broad flat surfaces parallel to the build plate.
- Angle most parts 25 to 45 degrees to reduce suction and visible layer artifacts.
- Place supports on hidden or less-visible surfaces when possible.
- Use heavier supports on sockets, pegs, tail base, and underside load areas.
- Use lighter supports on cosmetic surfaces only when unavoidable.
- Avoid support marks on the face, shades lenses, snout bridge, badge faces, checker trim, and hand grip interiors.
- Print tiny accessories in multiples because the workshop floor eats small parts with religious conviction.

---

## Surface Protection Priority

### Protect at All Costs

Avoid supports on:

- face
- snout bridge
- brow expression
- shades / goggle lenses
- mouth line
- proto-quill mohawk tips
- badge faces
- checker trim blocks
- hand grip interiors
- route-reader screen face
- black flag face
- top visible tail curve

### Support Acceptable With Cleanup

Supports may touch:

- back of head
- underside of jaw if hidden
- torso back under pack area
- inside upper arms
- underside of forearms
- inner thighs
- underside of feet
- underside of tail
- back of route-reader casing
- bottom of flag pole
- underside of route probe

## Body Part Orientation Plan

### P-01 Head, Shades Version

Recommended orientation:

- tilt backward 25 to 35 degrees
- rotate slightly so supports favor the back of head and underside of jaw
- keep face and shades lenses away from support contact

Support placement:

- back of skull
- underside of jaw if needed
- underside of neck socket
- rear base of proto-quills only if necessary

Protect:

- snout bridge
- brow ridge
- mouth line
- shades lenses
- front of proto-quill mohawk

Risk notes:

- shades may trap resin if sculpted with deep recesses
- mohawk quills must not be needle-thin
- neck socket needs enough support to stay round

---

### P-14 Head, Route Goggles Version

Recommended orientation:

- same as P-01, but with extra lens protection
- angle so goggle lenses face upward and away from dense supports

Support placement:

- back of head
- underside of neck
- rear strap area if needed

Protect:

- goggle lenses
- goggle frame front
- snout
- brow
- mouth line

Risk notes:

- goggle strap can become fragile if separated from head
- sculpt strap as broad relief or attached band for first prototype

---

### P-02 Torso

Recommended orientation:

- angle 30 to 45 degrees backward or diagonally
- keep front harness and badge cluster as clean as possible
- orient to minimize suction inside neck, shoulder, waist, or hip cavities

Support placement:

- torso back
- lower torso underside
- inside shoulder sockets if needed
- under harness edges only if unavoidable

Protect:

- front harness path
- pressure-route badge cluster
- checker trim
- front chest scale texture

Risk notes:

- shoulder sockets must stay round
- harness relief can catch supports
- badge cluster should be oversized enough to survive sanding near it

---

### P-03 Pelvis / Hip Block

Recommended orientation:

- angle 30 to 45 degrees
- prioritize accurate hip sockets and tail socket
- orient sockets so trapped resin can drain

Support placement:

- underside of pelvis
- inner surfaces not visible in assembly
- lower back around tail socket, avoiding socket mouth if possible

Protect:

- visible belt / harness transitions
- tail socket rim
- hip socket edges

Risk notes:

- pelvis is structurally critical
- tail socket wall must not warp
- test coupons should validate socket geometry before full print

---

### P-04 / P-05 Arms

Recommended orientation:

- angle along length of arm at 30 to 45 degrees
- place visible outer arm surface upward if possible
- place supports along inner arm or back of arm

Support placement:

- inside upper arm
- underside of forearm
- shoulder peg underside
- wrist socket underside

Protect:

- outer arm silhouette
- claw-wrap transition if sculpted onto forearm
- shoulder peg cylinder
- wrist socket mouth

Risk notes:

- shoulder pegs need heavier support
- wrist sockets can ovalize if unsupported
- arms should be printed in mirrored pair only after left / right details are locked

---

### P-06 / P-07 Hands

Recommended orientation:

- angle hand so palm and grip cavity avoid supports
- support from back of hand and wrist peg
- print multiple copies of hands in early tests

Support placement:

- back of hand
- wrist peg base
- underside of claws only if necessary and easy to sand

Protect:

- grip interior
- finger gaps
- claw tips
- palm detail

Risk notes:

- fingers must be thick enough for handling
- grip cavity must not fill with support scars
- claws should be blunt and sandable

---

### P-08 / P-09 Legs and Feet

Recommended orientation:

- angle legs along their length
- keep soles or undersides as support-friendly zones
- avoid supports on visible toe tops if possible

Support placement:

- underside of feet
- back of calves
- inner thighs
- hip peg underside

Protect:

- top of feet
- toe structure
- claws
- visible shin / thigh silhouette

Risk notes:

- feet are stability-critical
- toe claws should not become spikes
- soles can be sanded flat after curing if needed

---

### P-10 Tail

Recommended orientation:

- angle diagonally across the build volume
- support the underside along the curve
- avoid support marks on the top visible curve
- keep tail peg well supported

Support placement:

- underside of tail
- lower side planes
- tail peg base
- thick base area

Protect:

- top visible tail line
- tail tip
- keying surfaces
- socket interface surfaces

Risk notes:

- tail may warp if under-supported
- tail peg is high-risk and should be printed in tough resin
- orientation should reduce peel forces across the long curve
- print at least one tail test before final body assembly

---

## Accessory Orientation Plan

### P-11 Route-Reader Device

Recommended orientation:

- angle 30 degrees with screen facing away from supports
- support from back casing and lower handle

Support placement:

- back of device
- underside of handle
- bottom corners

Protect:

- screen face
- button panel
- grip surface where hand contacts

Risk notes:

- handle must remain clean enough to fit hand
- screen detail can be paint-defined if too small

---

### P-12 Black Path Flag Marker

Recommended orientation:

- print diagonally, not straight vertical if pole is thin
- support pole in multiple places
- support flag from back side or bottom edge

Support placement:

- back of flag
- lower pole
- stake base
- underside of flag edge

Protect:

- front flag face
- hand grip zone
- top flag silhouette if possible

Risk notes:

- pole is snap-prone
- print multiples
- consider thicker oval pole for durability

---

### P-13 Route Probe

Recommended orientation:

- diagonal angle along length
- avoid long unsupported spans
- support underside and blunt tip area

Support placement:

- underside of shaft
- underside of handle
- blunt tip support

Protect:

- grip zone
- any painted route marks
- straightness of shaft

Risk notes:

- probe can warp if printed too thin
- do not sharpen tip into spear-like point
- print multiples for handling tests

## Test Coupon Orientation

Joint test coupons should be printed in the same orientation style as their final parts when possible.

Critical coupon notes:

- neck coupon should mimic head / torso socket direction
- shoulder coupon should mimic torso wall thickness
- wrist coupon should mimic wrap-hidden wrist geometry
- hip coupon should mimic pelvis wall thickness
- tail coupon should mimic actual tail peg base and socket wall
- grip coupons should preserve hand insertion direction

Do not create perfect lab coupons that lie about the real part.

---

## Support Density Guidance

### Heavy Supports

Use for:

- tail peg
- shoulder pegs
- hip sockets
- pelvis block
- large underside tail curve
- foot soles if needed

### Medium Supports

Use for:

- torso back
- arm undersides
- leg backs
- head back
- route-reader casing

### Light Supports

Use sparingly for:

- cosmetic edges
- small accessory corners
- flag edge
- probe shaft

Avoid light supports as the only support on structural pegs.

---

## Drainage and Hollowing Notes

For first prototype, prefer solid small parts unless resin cost or suction risk demands hollowing.

If hollowing larger pieces:

- add drainage holes in hidden locations
- avoid drainage holes on face, badge cluster, or visible tail top
- ensure uncured resin can escape
- document hole locations in print log

Likely solid parts:

- head
- hands
- arms
- legs
- tail if not too massive
- route-reader
- flag marker
- probe

Potential hollow part:

- torso, only if thick and suction-prone

---

## Post-Processing Notes

After printing:

1. Remove supports before full cure when appropriate for resin type.
2. Clip supports carefully, do not twist delicate parts.
3. Cure fully after wash and support removal.
4. Sand support scars away from visible surfaces.
5. Check pegs and sockets before painting.
6. Test standing balance with raw parts.
7. Photograph failures and log orientation before reprinting.

Do not force joints before resin is fully cured.

---

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Face support scars | Bad head orientation | Move supports to back / underside. |
| Tail warped | Under-supported long curve | Add underside supports; change angle. |
| Peg snapped | Too thin or under-supported | Thicken peg; use tough resin; heavier supports. |
| Socket ovalized | Poor support or suction | Reorient, support socket rim, test coupon. |
| Flag pole snapped | Too thin | Thicken pole; print multiples. |
| Grip scarred | Supports in hand cavity | Reorient hand; move supports to back. |
| Feet uneven | Sole supports or cure warp | Sand soles; adjust support contact. |
| Paint rub extreme | Tolerance too tight | Increase clearance or mask joint surfaces. |

## Orientation Review Checklist

Before slicing the first full test batch:

- [ ] face is protected
- [ ] shades / goggles lenses are protected
- [ ] head socket is supported
- [ ] shoulder pegs are supported
- [ ] wrist pegs / sockets are supported
- [ ] hip sockets are supported
- [ ] tail peg and underside curve are supported
- [ ] feet soles can be sanded flat if needed
- [ ] hand grip interiors are clean
- [ ] route-reader screen is protected
- [ ] flag pole is thick enough and supported
- [ ] route probe is not needle-thin
- [ ] test coupons match final orientation logic
- [ ] resin safety notes are visible in print log

---

## Recommended First Print Batch

Print first:

- joint tolerance coupons
- one head test
- one tail test
- one gripping hand test
- route probe
- black flag marker
- route-reader device

Delay full body print until:

- neck tolerance is chosen
- wrist / grip test passes
- tail peg / socket test passes
- support strategy for head and tail is proven
