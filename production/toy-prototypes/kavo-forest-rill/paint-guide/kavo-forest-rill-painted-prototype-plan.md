**Room:** Meridian Prime Toy Development Room / Action Figure Prototype Lab

**Status:** Painted prototype planning file  
**Prototype Target:** 5-inch retro collector action figure  
**Canon Boundary:** Sandbox production file. Does not alter main canon.

---

## Purpose

This file plans the painted prototype stage for the Kavo "Forest" Rill action figure after Batch C has produced a Go or Conditional Go for paint.

The painted prototype should prove color read, canon markers, material finish, gear weathering, lens treatment, accessory paint, sealant behavior, and final assembly without damaging the figure's joints or losing Kavo's character read.

---

## Paint Stage Rule

Do not begin full paint until Batch C confirms:

- full prototype assembles without cracking
- figure stands without required display base or has an approved support solution
- tail supports balance and attitude
- head expression reads correctly
- hands hold selected accessories
- paint zones are accessible
- paint-rub samples pass or a masking plan exists

If Batch C produces a No-Go, revise and reprint before painting.

---

## Painted Prototype Objectives

The painted prototype should answer:

1. Does the skin palette preserve Velocisapien identity and shelf read?
2. Does the short sparse red proto-quill mohawk remain visible and iconic?
3. Do shades or route goggles read cleanly without hiding the expression?
4. Does the route harness remain practical and not costume-loud?
5. Does checker trim stay subtle?
6. Do badges, wraps, EMT pouch, and tools read at one foot?
7. Does the weathering suggest field use, not neglect?
8. Do joints still move or assemble after primer, paint, and sealant?
9. Does the finished figure still stand with accessories?
10. Does the prototype feel like Kavo, not a generic raptor toy?

---

## Safety Notes

This is an adult collectible prototype workflow.

Paint and finishing safety:

- [ ] Work in a ventilated area.
- [ ] Use respiratory protection when sanding primer or cured resin.
- [ ] Wear gloves when handling uncured resin or solvent-heavy paints.
- [ ] Follow paint, primer, thinner, and sealant safety labels.
- [ ] Keep paints, solvents, sealants, resin parts, and sharp tools away from children and pets.
- [ ] Let parts cure fully before sealing or final assembly.
- [ ] Do not treat prototype paints or materials as child-safe.

## Paint Preparation Sequence

Before primer:

- [ ] Confirm parts are fully cured.
- [ ] Wash and dry any remaining residue.
- [ ] Remove remaining support nubs.
- [ ] Sand rough support scars.
- [ ] Blunt any sharp claws, flag stakes, probe tips, or tail contact points.
- [ ] Test raw assembly one final time.
- [ ] Mark any joint surfaces to mask.
- [ ] Decide which parts will be painted separately.
- [ ] Photograph raw parts before primer.

Recommended parts to paint separately:

- head
- torso
- arms / hands if removable
- pelvis / legs if practical
- tail
- route-reader
- black Path flag marker
- route probe
- optional route-goggles head

---

## Masking Strategy

Mask or protect:

- neck peg / socket contact surfaces
- shoulder peg / socket contact surfaces
- wrist pegs / sockets, if used
- hip pegs / sockets
- tail peg / socket
- hand grip interiors
- route-reader handle
- route probe grip
- black flag pole grip

Masking options:

- painter's tape trimmed small
- liquid mask for awkward sockets
- temporary poster tack in sockets
- sacrificial peg inserts for sockets
- no-primer zones on high-friction joints

Do not let primer, paint, or sealant build up inside sockets unless the tolerance plan expects it.

---

## Primer Plan

Recommended approach:

1. Light primer coat on all visible surfaces.
2. Inspect for print lines, support scars, and missed sanding.
3. Sand problem areas gently.
4. Apply second light primer coat if needed.
5. Re-test joint and socket fit before color paint.

Primer checklist:

- [ ] Face details remain readable.
- [ ] Shades / lenses are not flooded.
- [ ] Badge details remain visible.
- [ ] Checker trim sculpt zones remain crisp.
- [ ] Scale texture is not filled in.
- [ ] Joint areas still fit or are masked.

---

## Skin Paint Sequence

Recommended skin workflow:

1. Base coat: route-dust olive teal.
2. Recess wash: dark green-gray into scale zones, brow, jawline, knuckles, tail base.
3. Cleanup base tone on broad planes.
4. Dry-brush: route-dust tan / pale olive on brow, cheek, shoulders, hands, knees, feet, tail ridge.
5. Optional subtle warmer tone around face planes if expression needs life.
6. Matte or satin interim protection only if needed.

Avoid:

- bright monster green
- wet all-over reptile gloss
- human blush logic
- scale speckling so dense it muddies the toy read

---

## Head and Face Paint Plan

Priority order:

1. Preserve brow / snout expression.
2. Paint black-frame shades cleanly.
3. Paint smoky gloss lenses after surrounding matte colors are stable.
4. Paint red proto-quills with darker root or underside shading.
5. Keep mouth line subtle and controlled.
6. Touch up snout and cheek planes.

Checks:

- [ ] Expression still reads sly, calm, dangerous.
- [ ] Shades do not erase the brow.
- [ ] Proto-quills are red and visible.
- [ ] Snout reads saurian, not mammalian.
- [ ] Face remains readable at three feet.

---

## Harness, Badges, and Checker Trim

### Harness

Recommended:

- worn black-brown field material
- edge wear on raised strap edges
- subtle dust, not mud bath

### Badges

Recommended:

- brass / copper base
- one teal or amber accent maximum unless approved
- no assigned canon meanings unless separately approved

### Checker Trim

Recommended:

- one harness tab and / or cuff edge
- black and off-white
- crisp but limited

Do not turn checker trim into an all-over costume pattern.

---

## Claw-Wraps, Claws, and Feet

### Claw-Wraps

Recommended:

- route-dust tan or dirty off-white
- light wash in wrap recesses
- slight edge dry-brush

### Claws

Recommended:

- dark horn or bone-gray
- satin finish
- blunt tips

### Feet

Recommended:

- slightly dustier than upper body
- darker recess wash around toes
- contact points sanded safe before paint

Check feet after paint:

- [ ] Figure still stands.
- [ ] Sole paint does not create rocking.
- [ ] Toe claws are not sharp.

---

## Tail Paint Plan

The tail should support balance, attitude, and emotional read.

Recommended:

- same base skin tone as body
- darker dorsal ridge or top curve
- lighter side / underside planes
- route-dust at lower contact point
- dry-brush along curve to emphasize motion

Avoid:

- making tail look like a weapon spike
- glossing entire tail
- hiding the socket seam with paint buildup that blocks assembly

---

## Accessory Paint Plan

### Route-Reader Device

- dark casing
- amber or muted teal screen
- small metallic buttons
- light edge wear
- gloss on screen only

### Black Path Flag Marker

- matte black flag
- dusty lower edge
- weathered pole
- blunt stake tip

### Route Probe

- worn steel shaft
- black grip
- brass collar
- blunt dark tip
- no spear-like bright edge

### EMT Pouch / Field Pack

- dark canvas
- worn flap edges
- no permanent medical symbol unless approved
- optional toy-only field mark only if labeled

## Weathering Level

Recommended first painted prototype: **W-02 Light Route Dust**.

Apply to:

- lower feet
- lower tail contact point
- harness edges
- route-reader corners
- EMT pouch flap
- route probe grip
- black flag lower pole

Avoid:

- random blood effects
- heavy mud covering silhouette
- excessive rust
- face-obscuring grime
- comedy grime splatter

---

## Final Assembly After Paint

Assembly order:

1. Confirm paint and sealant are fully cured.
2. Clear masked joint surfaces carefully.
3. Test head fit.
4. Test shoulders.
5. Test wrists / hands.
6. Test hips.
7. Test tail.
8. Assemble full figure.
9. Add accessories.
10. Re-test standing balance.
11. Photograph final painted prototype.

Do not force painted joints. If fit is tight, stop and inspect for paint buildup.

## Go / No-Go for Final Prototype Review

### Go if:

- [ ] Paint preserves canon markers.
- [ ] Figure still assembles safely.
- [ ] Figure stands with final accessories.
- [ ] Shelf-read succeeds at 6 feet, 3 feet, and 1 foot.
- [ ] Paint finish supports toyetic retro collector feel.
- [ ] Weathering is restrained and field-practical.
- [ ] No major paint rub or joint lock occurs.

### Conditional Go if:

- [ ] minor touch-ups are needed
- [ ] one accessory needs repaint or reprint
- [ ] one joint requires paint cleanup
- [ ] sealant needs finish correction

### No-Go if:

- [ ] paint obscures expression
- [ ] figure no longer stands
- [ ] joints are frozen or damaged
- [ ] canon markers become unreadable
- [ ] Kavo reads as generic raptor / monster / ninja / protected-toyline clone

Decision:

- [ ] Go to final prototype review
- [ ] Conditional Go, touch up first
- [ ] No-Go, revise paint or print
